1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Motor Vehicle or Vessel – Criminal Negligence
3 (Sergeant Patrick Keep Act)
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from driving, operating, or controlling a motor
5 vehicle or vessel in a criminally negligent manner; providing that certain conduct
6 does not constitute a violation of this Act; and generally relating to motor vehicles or
7 vessels and criminal negligence.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments, adding to
9 Article – Criminal Law
10 Section 3–212.1 3–212.2
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Criminal Law
16 3–212.1.
17 (a) (1) In this section, “vessel” means any watercraft that is used or is capable
18 of being used as a means of transportation on water or ice.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0710*
2 SENATE BILL 710
1 (2) “Vessel” does not include a seaplane.
2 (b) A person may not:
3 (1) DRIVE, OPERATE, OR CONTROL A MOTOR VEHICLE OR VESSEL IN
4 A CRIMINALLY NEGLIGENT MANNER; OR
5 (2) cause a life–threatening injury to another as a result of the person’s
6 driving, operating, or controlling a motor vehicle or vessel in a criminally negligent manner.
7 (c) For the purpose of this section, a person acts in a criminally negligent manner
8 with respect to a result or a circumstance when:
9 (1) the person should be aware, but fails to perceive, that the person’s
10 conduct creates a substantial and unjustifiable risk that such a result will occur; and
11 (2) the failure to perceive constitutes a gross deviation from the standard
12 of care that would be exercised by a reasonable person.
13 (d) It is not a violation of this section for a person to:
14 (1) DRIVE, OPERATE, OR CONTROL A MOTOR VEHICLE OR VESSEL IN
15 A NEGLIGENT MANNER AS DEFINED IN § 21–901.1 OF THE TRANSPORTATION
16 ARTICLE; OR
17 (2) cause a life–threatening injury to another as a result of the person’s
18 driving, operating, or controlling a motor vehicle or vessel in a negligent manner.
19 (e) (1) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES SUBSECTION (B)(1) OF THIS SECTION IS
20 GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT
21 NOT EXCEEDING 6 MONTHS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $5,000 OR BOTH.
22 (2) A person who violates SUBSECTION (B)(2) OF this section is guilty of
23 a misdemeanor and on conviction is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 1 year or a fine
24 not exceeding $5,000 or both.

26 (A) A PERSON MAY NOT DRIVE, OPERATE, OR CONTROL A MOTOR VEHICLE
27 IN A CRIMINALLY NEGLIGENT MANNER.
28 (B) FOR THE PURPOSE OF THIS SECTION, A PERSON ACTS IN A CRIMINALLY
29 NEGLIGENT MANNER WHEN:
