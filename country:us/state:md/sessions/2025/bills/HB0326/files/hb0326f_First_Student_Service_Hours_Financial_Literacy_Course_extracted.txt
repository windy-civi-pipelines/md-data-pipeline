A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Student Service Hours – Financial Literacy Course
3 FOR the purpose of requiring each county board of education, beginning in a certain school
4 year, to allow successful completion of certain financial literacy courses to be applied
5 toward fulfillment of the student service hours high school graduation requirement
6 for a certain number of hours; and generally relating to financial literacy courses and
7 student service hours.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Education
10 Section 7–213
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Education
16 7–213.
17 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
18 INDICATED.
19 (2) “ELIGIBLE FINANCIAL LITERACY COURSE” MEANS A COURSE
20 THAT PROVIDES INSTRUCTIONAL CONTENT ON FINANCIAL LITERACY CONCEPTS
21 THAT IS:
22 (I) OFFERED AT AN INSTITUTION OF HIGHER EDUCATION;
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb0326*
2 HOUSE BILL 326
1 (II) OFFERED BY AN ORGANIZATION APPROVED BY THE COUNTY
2 BOARD; AND
3 (III) APPROVED BY THE COUNTY BOARD.
4 (3) “INSTITUTION OF HIGHER EDUCATION” HAS THE MEANING
5 STATED IN § 10–101 OF THIS ARTICLE.
6 (B) BEGINNING IN THE 2026–2027 SCHOOL YEAR, EACH COUNTY BOARD
7 SHALL ALLOW SUCCESSFUL COMPLETION OF AN ELIGIBLE FINANCIAL LITERACY
8 COURSE TO BE APPLIED TOWARD FULFILLMENT OF THE STUDENT SERVICE HOURS
9 HIGH SCHOOL GRADUATION REQUIREMENT FOR THE NUMBER OF HOURS
10 DETERMINED BY THE COUNTY BOARD.
11 (C) THE STATE BOARD SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT THIS
12 SECTION.
13 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
14 1, 2025.
