1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Destroying Funerary Objects and Cemetery Elements – Statute
3 of Limitations and Prohibition
4 FOR the purpose of increasing the statute of limitations for prosecutions relating to
5 destroying funerary objects and other elements of a cemetery to begin at the time
6 local authorities knew or reasonably should have known of the violation; prohibiting
7 a person from willfully destroying, damaging, defacing, or removing landscaping or
8 part of the grounds of a cemetery; and generally relating to the statute of limitations
9 for and prosecution of the crime of destroying funerary objects and other elements of
10 a cemetery.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
12 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
13 Section 5–106(a)
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
16 BY adding to
17 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
18 Section 5–106(jj)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
21 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
22 Article – Criminal Law

9 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
10 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
11 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
12 5–106.
13 (a) Except as provided by this section, § 1–303 of the Environment Article, and §
14 8–1815 of the Natural Resources Article, a prosecution for a misdemeanor shall be
15 instituted within 1 year after the offense was committed.
16 (JJ) A PROSECUTION FOR A VIOLATION OF § 10–404(A) OR (B) OF THE
17 CRIMINAL LAW ARTICLE RELATING TO THE DESTRUCTION OF FUNERARY OBJECTS
18 AND OTHER ELEMENTS OF A CEMETERY SHALL BE INSTITUTED WITHIN 3 YEARS
19 AFTER THE LOCAL AUTHORITIES IN FACT KNEW OR REASONABLY SHOULD HAVE
20 KNOWN OF THE VIOLATION.
21 Article – Criminal Law
22 10–404.
23 (a) (1) Subject to the provisions of paragraph (2) of this subsection, a person
24 may not willfully destroy, damage, deface, or remove:
25 (i) an associated funerary object or another structure placed in a
26 cemetery; [or]
27 (ii) a building, wall, fence, railing, or other work, for the use,
28 protection, or ornamentation of a cemetery; OR
29 (III) LANDSCAPING OR ANY OTHER PART OF THE GROUNDS OF A
30 CEMETERY.
31 (2) The provisions of paragraph (1) of this subsection do not prohibit the
32 removal of a funerary object or a building, wall, fence, railing, LANDSCAPING, or other
33 object installed for the use, protection, or ornamentation of a cemetery or burial site, OR
SENATE BILL 421 3
1 OTHER PART OF THE GROUNDS OF THE CEMETERY for the purpose of repair or
2 replacement, either at the request of or with the permission of heirs or descendants of the
3 deceased or the owner or manager of the cemetery or burial site.
4 (b) (1) Subject to the provisions of paragraph (2) of this subsection, a person
5 may not willfully destroy, damage, or remove a tree, plant, or shrub in a cemetery.
6 (2) The provisions of paragraph (1) of this subsection do not prohibit
7 normal maintenance of a cemetery or burial site, including trimming of trees and shrubs,
8 removal of weeds or noxious growths, grass cutting, or other routine care and maintenance.
9 (d) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and on conviction
10 is subject to:
11 (1) for a violation of subsection (a) of this section, imprisonment not
12 exceeding 5 years or a fine not exceeding $10,000 or both; and
13 (2) for a violation of subsection (b) or (c) of this section, imprisonment not
14 exceeding 2 years or a fine not exceeding $500 or both.
15 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
16 October 1, 2025.
