By: Delegate S. Johnson
Introduced and read first time: February 13, 2025
Assigned to: Rules and Executive Nominations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Procurement – Construction and Services – Contract Modification
3 FOR the purpose of requiring a procurement contract for construction or services to include
4 a clause requiring contract modification for increased costs for compensation and
5 benefits under certain circumstances; and generally relating to procurement
6 contracts for construction and services.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – State Finance and Procurement
9 Section 13–218(b)
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – State Finance and Procurement
15 13–218.
16 (b) (1) In addition to the clauses required under subsection (a) of this section,
17 a procurement contract for construction shall include:
18 [(1)] (I) a clause providing for contract modification if the condition of a
19 site differs from the condition described in the specifications; [and]
20 [(2)] (II) a clause covering the requirements for notice of contract claims,
21 submission of contract claims, and resolution of contract claims under § 15–219 of this
22 article; AND
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb1499*
2 HOUSE BILL 1499
1 (III) A CLAUSE REQUIRING CONTRACT MODIFICATION FOR THE
2 EQUITABLE ADJUSTMENT OF THE CONTRACT FOR INCREASED COSTS FOR
3 COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS AS A RESULT OF:
4 1. STATUTORY CHANGES INCREASING REQUIRED
5 COMPENSATION OR BENEFITS; OR
6 2. COLLECTIVE BARGAINING.
7 (2) IN ADDITION TO THE CLAUSES REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (A)
8 OF THIS SECTION, A PROCUREMENT CONTRACT FOR SERVICES SHALL INCLUDE A
9 CLAUSE REQUIRING CONTRACT MODIFICATION FOR THE EQUITABLE ADJUSTMENT
10 OF THE CONTRACT FOR INCREASED COSTS FOR COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS AS A
11 RESULT OF:
12 (I) STATUTORY CHANGES INCREASING REQUIRED
13 COMPENSATION OR BENEFITS; OR
14 (II) COLLECTIVE BARGAINING.
15 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
16 October 1, 2025.
