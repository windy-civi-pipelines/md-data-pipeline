CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Fisheries – Striped Bass or Rockfish – Juvenile Survey
3 FOR the purpose of expanding the scope of the annual young–of–the–year juvenile survey
4 of striped bass or rockfish by requiring the Department of Natural Resources to
5 conduct the survey at at least a certain number of additional sampling sites in the
6 central region of the Maryland waters of the Chesapeake Bay in addition to sampling
7 sites in the northern and southern regions of the Maryland waters of the Chesapeake
8 Bay; establishing certain requirements for the central region sampling sites; and
9 generally relating to striped bass or rockfish.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
11 Article – Natural Resources
12 Section 4–746
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 BY adding to
16 Article – Natural Resources
17 Section 4–746
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

2 HOUSE BILL 889
1 Article – Natural Resources
2 4–746.
3 (A) (1) The Department shall conduct annually a scientific survey to determine
4 the relative abundance of striped bass or rockfish of approximately 18 inches in length that
5 are in the Chesapeake Bay and its tributaries.
6 (2) The survey shall be conducted in areas that are used by the Department
7 to determine its young–of–the–year index of striped bass or rockfish.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
9 as follows:
10 Article – Natural Resources
11 4–746.
12 (A) IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 4–215 OF THIS TITLE, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL
13 CONDUCT SCIENTIFIC MONITORING TO COLLECT BIOLOGICAL INFORMATION TO
14 DETERMINE THE RELATIVE ABUNDANCE OF STRIPED BASS OR ROCKFISH IN AREAS
15 THROUGHOUT THE CHESAPEAKE BAY AND ITS TIDAL TRIBUTARIES.
16 (B) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL:
17 (1) CONDUCT THE ANNUAL YOUNG–OF–THE–YEAR JUVENILE SURVEY
18 OF STRIPED BASS OR ROCKFISH AT BETWEEN 12 AND 20, INCLUSIVE, SAMPLING
19 SITES LOCATED IN THE CENTRAL REGION OF THE MARYLAND WATERS OF THE
20 CHESAPEAKE BAY IN ADDITION TO SAMPLING SITES IN THE NORTHERN AND
21 SOUTHERN REGIONS OF THE CHESAPEAKE BAY; AND
22 (2) LOCATE THE CENTRAL REGION SAMPLING SITES IN ANY OF THE
23 FOLLOWING WATERS OF THE STATE:
24 (1) CONDUCT SAMPLING USING THE SAME OR EQUIVALENT METHODS
25 AS THE YOUNG–OF–THE–YEAR JUVENILE INDEX SURVEY OF STRIPED BASS OR
26 ROCKFISH IN AT LEAST TWO SITES LOCATED IN THE CENTRAL REGION OF THE
27 MARYLAND WATERS OF THE CHESAPEAKE BAY AND ITS TIDAL TRIBUTARIES; AND
28 (2) LOCATE THE CENTRAL REGION SAMPLING SITES IN AREAS OTHER
29 THAN THE AREAS SAMPLED FOR THE YOUNG–OF–THE–YEAR JUVENILE INDEX
30 SURVEY OF STRIPED BASS OR ROCKFISH AND IN ANY OF THE FOLLOWING WATERS
31 OF THE STATE:
HOUSE BILL 889 3
1 (I) THE MAGOTHY RIVER;
2 (II) THE SEVERN RIVER;
3 (III) THE CHESTER RIVER;
4 (IV) THE SOUTH RIVER;
5 (V) THE WEST RIVER;
6 (VI) EASTERN BAY; OR
7 (VII) THE MILES RIVER.;
8 (VIII) THE PATAPSCO RIVER;
9 (IX) THE RHODE RIVER; OR
10 (X) THE TRED AVON RIVER.
11 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
12 as follows:
13 Article – Natural Resources
14 4–746.
15 IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 4–215 OF THIS TITLE, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL
16 CONDUCT SCIENTIFIC MONITORING TO COLLECT BIOLOGICAL INFORMATION TO
17 DETERMINE THE RELATIVE ABUNDANCE OF STRIPED BASS OR ROCKFISH IN AREAS
18 THROUGHOUT THE CHESAPEAKE BAY AND ITS TIDAL TRIBUTARIES.
19 SECTION 4. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Department of Natural
20 Resources shall annually examine the survey data collected under § 4–746(b) of the Natural
21 Resources Article, as enacted under Section 2 of this Act, to ensure the distribution of
22 striped bass or rockfish has not shifted from the established sites of the young–of–the–year
23 juvenile index survey of striped bass or rockfish.
24 SECTION 5. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before December 31
25 each year, the Department of Natural Resources shall report its results from its
26 examination of the survey data conducted under Section 4 of this Act to the Sport Fisheries
27 Advisory Commission, the Tidal Fisheries Advisory Commission, and, in accordance with §
28 2–1257 of the State Government Article, the Senate Committee on Education, Energy, and
29 the Environment and the House Environment and Transportation Committee.
4 HOUSE BILL 889
1 SECTION 6. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That Section 3 of this Act shall take
2 effect July 1, 2027.
3 SECTION 2. 7. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, except as provided in
4 Section 6 of this Act, this Act shall take effect June 1, 2025. Sections 2, 4, and 5 of this Act
5 shall remain effective for a period of 2 years and 1 month and, at the end of June 30, 2027,
6 Sections 2, 4, and 5 of this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly,
7 shall be abrogated and of no further force and effect.
Approved:
