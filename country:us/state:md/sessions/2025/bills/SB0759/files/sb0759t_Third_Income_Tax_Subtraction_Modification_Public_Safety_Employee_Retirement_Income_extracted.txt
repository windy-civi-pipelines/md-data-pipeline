1 AN ACT concerning
2 Income Tax – Subtraction Modification – Public Safety Employee Retirement
3 Income
4 FOR the purpose of altering a certain subtraction modification under the Maryland income
5 tax for retirement income attributable to an individual’s employment as a public
6 safety employee to include certain retirement income attributable to an individual’s
7 employment as a 9–1–1 specialist; and generally relating to a subtraction
8 modification under the Maryland income tax for the retirement income of public
9 safety employees.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
11 Article – Tax – General
12 Section 10–207(a)
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
16 Article – Tax – General
17 Section 10–207(mm)
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
21 Article – Tax – Property
22 Section 9–262(a)(1) and (3)

2 SENATE BILL 759
1 Annotated Code of Maryland
2 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
3 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
4 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
5 Article – Tax – General
6 10–207.
7 (a) To the extent included in federal adjusted gross income, the amounts under
8 this section are subtracted from the federal adjusted gross income of a resident to determine
9 Maryland adjusted gross income.
10 (mm) (1) (i) In this subsection the following words have the meanings
11 indicated.
12 (ii) “Correctional officer” means an individual who:
13 1. was employed in:
14 A. a State correctional facility, as defined in § 1–101 of the
15 Correctional Services Article;
16 B. a local correctional facility, as defined in § 1–101 of the
17 Correctional Services Article;
18 C. a juvenile facility included in § 9–226 of the Human
19 Services Article; or
20 D. a facility of the United States that is equivalent to a State
21 or local correctional facility or a juvenile facility included in § 9–226 of the Human Services
22 Article; and
23 2. is eligible to receive retirement income attributable to the
24 individual’s employment under item 1 of this subparagraph.
25 (iii) “Emergency services personnel” means emergency medical
26 technicians [or], paramedics, OR 9–1–1 SPECIALISTS.
27 (iv) “Employee retirement system” has the meaning stated under §
28 10–209(a) of this subtitle.
29 (V) “9–1–1 SPECIALIST” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN §
30 9–262(A) OF THE TAX – PROPERTY ARTICLE.
SENATE BILL 759 3
1 [(v)] (VI) “Public safety employee” means an individual who is a
2 retired correctional officer, law enforcement officer, or fire, rescue, or emergency services
3 personnel of the United States, the State, or a political subdivision of the State.
4 (2) The subtraction under subsection (a) of this section includes the first
5 $15,000 of income from an employee retirement system that is attributable to service as a
6 public safety employee, if the income is received by an individual who is at least 55 years
7 old on the last day of the taxable year.
8 Article – Tax – Property
9 9–262.
10 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
11 (3) “9–1–1 specialist” means an employee of a county public safety
12 answering point, or an employee working in a county safety answering point, whose duties
13 and responsibilities include:
14 (i) receiving and processing 9–1–1 requests for emergency
15 assistance;
16 (ii) other support functions directly related to 9–1–1 requests for
17 emergency assistance; or
18 (iii) dispatching law enforcement officers, fire rescue services,
19 emergency medical services, and other public safety services to the scene of an emergency.
20 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
21 1, 2025, and shall be applicable to all taxable years beginning after December 31, 2024.
Approved:
