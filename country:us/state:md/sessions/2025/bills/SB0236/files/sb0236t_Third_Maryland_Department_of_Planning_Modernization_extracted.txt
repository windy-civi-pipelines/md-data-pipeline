CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Department of Planning – Modernization
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the State Administrator of Elections to submit certain
4 information to the Secretary of Planning under certain circumstances; altering a
5 requirement that the Department of the Environment submit certain proposals to
6 the Department of Planning under certain circumstances; altering certain
7 requirements related to the Department of Planning preparing certain population
8 projections; repealing a provision authorizing the Secretary of Planning or staff
9 member of the Department of Planning to access certain capital facilities planning
10 information and to enter certain State–owned land under certain circumstances;
11 repealing a certain provision authorizing the Department of Planning to include
12 certain funding in the Department’s budget for the Maryland–National Capital Park
13 and Planning Commission; repealing a provision requiring the Department of
14 Planning to establish certain statewide classification standards; altering certain
15 provisions requiring the Department of Planning to be a repository and
16 clearinghouse for information relating to State–owned real property; repealing an
17 obsolete reference to the State Economic Growth, Resource Protection, and Planning
18 Commission; repealing a requirement that each local jurisdiction file a certain report
19 with the State Economic Growth, Resource Protection, and Planning Commission;
20 and generally relating to the Department of Planning and State planning.
21 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
22 Article – Election Law

1 (b) The Department shall correlate information concerning real property owned
2 by the State [or any political subdivision of the State].
3 (c) (1) The Department shall maintain a current, updated list of real property
4 owned by the State [or any political subdivision of the State].
5 (2) The list shall include pertinent details concerning size, facilities, and
6 value.
7 (3) A copy of the list and any related information shall be provided to any
8 State agency and the General Assembly upon request.
9 5–7A–02.
10 (a) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, with respect to
11 a State public works, transportation, or major capital improvement project funded through
12 State or federal funds, the State may not provide State funding for the project if the project
13 is not consistent with:
14 (i) the State Economic Growth, Resource Protection, and Planning
15 Policy established in § 5–7A–01 of this subtitle; or
16 (ii) the local plan of the jurisdiction in which the project is located.
17 (2) The State may provide State funding for a State public works,
18 transportation, or major capital improvement project funded through State or federal funds
19 which is not consistent with the policy or plan under paragraph (1) of this subsection if the
20 State determines that extraordinary circumstances exist that warrant proceeding with the
21 project and that no reasonably feasible alternative exists.
22 (b) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection with respect to a
23 local construction project involving the use of State funds, grants, loans, loan guaranties,
24 or insurance, a local jurisdiction may not approve or construct the project unless the project
25 is consistent with the local plan.
26 (2) A local jurisdiction may approve and construct a local construction
27 project which is inconsistent with the local plan under paragraph (1) of this subsection if
28 the local jurisdiction determines that extraordinary circumstances exist that warrant
29 proceeding with the project and that no reasonably feasible alternative exists.
30 (c) By December 1, 1992 the Governor shall establish procedures for review of
31 State projects under subsection (a) of this section, and each local jurisdiction shall establish
32 procedures for the review of local projects under subsection (b) of this section, to:
33 (1) ensure that the projects are consistent with their respective policy and
34 plans; and

1 (2) evaluate extraordinary circumstances under subsections (a)(2) and
2 (b)(2) of this section, respectively.
3 [(d) Each local jurisdiction shall report on or before February 1 of each year to the
4 State Economic Growth, Resource Protection, and Planning Commission on the number
5 and type of projects approved under subsection (b)(2) of this section during the preceding
6 calendar year, including a description of the extraordinary circumstances existing and the
7 alternatives considered, together with any additional information required by the
8 Commission.]

10 October 1, 2025.
