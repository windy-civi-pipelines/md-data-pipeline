11 (a) Each year, no later than 60 days before the General Assembly convenes for its
12 regular session, the Department shall submit a report to the Governor.
13 (b) The annual report shall include:
14 (1) a summary and description of the nature of every section of the State
15 Development Plan that has been:
16 (i) added, deleted, or revised since the last annual report; and
17 (ii) filed by the Governor under § 5–605 of this title;
18 (2) a summary of each important study wholly or partly completed by the
19 Department since the last annual report; and
20 (3) [summaries of the work of the Department and of the State Economic
21 Growth, Resource Protection, and Planning Commission] A SUMMARY OF THE WORK OF
22 THE DEPARTMENT.
23 (c) The Department shall distribute copies of the annual report:
24 (1) subject to § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, to the General
25 Assembly;
26 (2) to the head of each department of the State government;
27 (3) to the head of each local or regional planning agency in the State; and
28 (4) on request, to any federal agency.
29 (d) The Department shall make copies of the annual report available for general
30 distribution or sale.

1 [5–311.
2 (a) The Secretary or a staff member of the Department designated by the
3 Secretary shall have access to information that relates to capital facilities planning in the
4 possession of any unit of the State government.

6 Secretary or a staff member of the Department designated by the Secretary may enter, at
7 any reasonable hour, on any State–owned land in order to make examinations and surveys
8 that relate to capital facilities planning.]
9 [5–407.
10 (a) In this section, “Commission” means the Maryland–National Capital Park
11 and Planning Commission.
12 (b) To provide regional planning in the area of the State subject to the jurisdiction
13 of the Commission, and except as otherwise provided in this section, the Department may
14 include in the annual budget of the Department the amount the Secretary considers
15 appropriate as the State’s share of the cost of regional planning projects programmed by
16 the Commission for the following fiscal year.
17 (c) The amount included in the annual budget under subsection (b) of this section
18 may not exceed $100,000 in any single year.
19 (d) The Secretary shall determine which regional planning projects programmed
20 by the Commission qualify for State assistance.
21 (e) (1) The Commission shall provide the Secretary with any information the
22 Secretary requires to determine which regional planning projects programmed by the
23 Commission qualify for State assistance.
24 (2) On or before August 1 of each year, the Commission shall provide the
25 Secretary with a copy of the proposed budget of the Commission for the following fiscal
26 year. After the copy of the proposed budget is provided to the Secretary, the Commission
27 shall promptly notify the Secretary and obtain the approval of the Secretary for any
28 changes in the proposed budget for regional projects funded under this section.
29 (f) The Secretary may require an annual audit of the operations of the
30 Commission that relate to any project financed wholly or partly by payments to the
31 Commission from the Department.]

6 SENATE BILL 236
1 (a) The Department shall establish statewide classification standards for
2 geographically referencing all basic planning information collected by any unit of the State
3 government.
4 (b) The classification standards may be used by any unit of the State or of a local
5 government.]
6 5–503.
7 (a) The Department shall be a repository [and clearinghouse] for information
8 about STATE–OWNED real property [available for public use].
9 (b) The Department shall correlate information concerning real property owned
10 by the State [or any political subdivision of the State].
11 (c) (1) The Department shall maintain a current, updated list of real property
12 owned by the State [or any political subdivision of the State].
13 (2) The list shall include pertinent details concerning size, facilities, and
14 value.
15 (3) A copy of the list and any related information shall be provided to any
16 State agency and the General Assembly upon request.
17 5–7A–02.
18 (a) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, with respect to
19 a State public works, transportation, or major capital improvement project funded through
20 State or federal funds, the State may not provide State funding for the project if the project
21 is not consistent with:
22 (i) the State Economic Growth, Resource Protection, and Planning
23 Policy established in § 5–7A–01 of this subtitle; or
24 (ii) the local plan of the jurisdiction in which the project is located.
25 (2) The State may provide State funding for a State public works,
26 transportation, or major capital improvement project funded through State or federal funds
27 which is not consistent with the policy or plan under paragraph (1) of this subsection if the
28 State determines that extraordinary circumstances exist that warrant proceeding with the
29 project and that no reasonably feasible alternative exists.
30 (b) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection with respect to a
31 local construction project involving the use of State funds, grants, loans, loan guaranties,
32 or insurance, a local jurisdiction may not approve or construct the project unless the project
33 is consistent with the local plan.
SENATE BILL 236 7
1 (2) A local jurisdiction may approve and construct a local construction
2 project which is inconsistent with the local plan under paragraph (1) of this subsection if
3 the local jurisdiction determines that extraordinary circumstances exist that warrant
4 proceeding with the project and that no reasonably feasible alternative exists.
5 (c) By December 1, 1992 the Governor shall establish procedures for review of
6 State projects under subsection (a) of this section, and each local jurisdiction shall establish
7 procedures for the review of local projects under subsection (b) of this section, to:
8 (1) ensure that the projects are consistent with their respective policy and
9 plans; and
10 (2) evaluate extraordinary circumstances under subsections (a)(2) and
11 (b)(2) of this section, respectively.
12 [(d) Each local jurisdiction shall report on or before February 1 of each year to the
13 State Economic Growth, Resource Protection, and Planning Commission on the number
14 and type of projects approved under subsection (b)(2) of this section during the preceding
15 calendar year, including a description of the extraordinary circumstances existing and the
16 alternatives considered, together with any additional information required by the
17 Commission.]
18 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
19 October 1, 2025.
