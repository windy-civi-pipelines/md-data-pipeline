CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Care Facilities – Warrior Service Member Community Members
3 (SFC Matthew Fast Act for Warrior Healthcare)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring authorizing each health care facility to screen each patient
5 for information on whether the patient is a warrior service member community
6 member, include on the patient’s medical record whether the patient is a warrior
7 service member community member, and meet certain standards established by The
8 Joint Commission for the purpose of improving health care equity for warrior service
9 member community members; and generally relating to health care facilities and
10 veterans, service members, and their families.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Health – General
13 Section 24–2501 and 24–2502 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 25. Health Care
14 Equity for Warrior Service Member Community Members”
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Health – General

2 SENATE BILL 740
1 SUBTITLE 25. HEALTH CARE EQUITY FOR WARRIOR SERVICE MEMBER
2 COMMUNITY MEMBERS.
3 24–2501.
4 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
5 INDICATED.
6 (B) (1) “HEALTH CARE FACILITY” MEANS A FACILITY OR AN OFFICE
7 WHERE HEALTH CARE IS PROVIDED TO PATIENTS BY A HEALTH CARE PRACTITIONER
8 OR GROUP OF HEALTH CARE PRACTITIONERS LICENSED, CERTIFIED, OR OTHERWISE
9 AUTHORIZED BY LAW TO PROVIDE HEALTH CARE SERVICES.
10 (2) “HEALTH CARE FACILITY” INCLUDES:
11 (I) A HOSPITAL, AS DEFINED IN § 19–301 OF THIS ARTICLE;
12 (II) A FREESTANDING MEDICAL FACILITY, AS DEFINED IN §
13 19–3A–01 OF THIS ARTICLE;
14 (III) A NURSING FACILITY;
15 (IV) A RESIDENTIAL TREATMENT CENTER;
16 (V) A REHABILITATION FACILITY; AND
17 (VI) A FACILITY OPERATED BY THE DEPARTMENT, THE
18 BEHAVIORAL HEALTH ADMINISTRATION, OR A HEALTH OFFICER.
19 (C) “SERVICE MEMBER” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL WHO IS AN ACTIVE DUTY
20 MEMBER OF:
21 (1) THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED STATES;
22 (2) A RESERVE COMPONENT OF THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED
23 STATES; OR
24 (3) THE NATIONAL GUARD OF ANY STATE.
25 (D) “VETERAN” MEANS A FORMER SERVICE MEMBER WHO WAS
26 DISCHARGED FROM ACTIVE DUTY.
27 (E) (D) “WARRIOR SERVICE MEMBER COMMUNITY MEMBER” MEANS:
28 (1) A SERVICE MEMBER;
SENATE BILL 740 3
1 (2) A VETERAN;
2 (3) THE SPOUSE OF A SERVICE MEMBER OR VETERAN;
3 (4) A CHILD OF A SERVICE MEMBER OR VETERAN; OR
4 (5) A STEPCHILD OF A SERVICE MEMBER OR VETERAN.
5 (E) “VETERAN” MEANS A FORMER SERVICE MEMBER WHO WAS
6 DISCHARGED FROM ACTIVE DUTY.
7 24–2502.
8 (A) EACH HEALTH CARE FACILITY SHALL MAY SCREEN EACH PATIENT FOR
9 INFORMATION ON THE PATIENT’S ELIGIBILITY AS A WARRIOR SERVICE MEMBER
10 COMMUNITY MEMBER AND INCLUDE IN THE PATIENT’S MEDICAL RECORD WHETHER
11 THE PATIENT IS A WARRIOR SERVICE MEMBER COMMUNITY MEMBER.
12 (B) FOR THE PURPOSE OF IMPROVING HEALTH CARE EQUITY FOR WARRIOR
13 SERVICE MEMBER COMMUNITY MEMBERS, EACH HEALTH CARE FACILITY SHALL
14 MAY IDENTIFY WARRIOR SERVICE MEMBER COMMUNITY MEMBERS AS A
15 VULNERABLE POPULATION AND COMPLY WITH THE STANDARDS ASSOCIATED WITH
16 THE NATIONAL PATIENT SAFETY GOAL TO IMPROVE HEALTH CARE EQUITY
17 ESTABLISHED BY THE JOINT COMMISSION.
18 (C) ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 1 EACH YEAR, BEGINNING IN 2026, THE
19 DEPARTMENT SHALL REPORT TO THE GOVERNOR AND, IN ACCORDANCE WITH §
20 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY ON:
21 (1) THE NEEDS OF WARRIOR COMMUNITY MEMBERS;
22 (2) THE EXTENT TO WHICH EACH HEALTH CARE FACILITY MEETS THE
23 NEEDS OF WARRIOR COMMUNITY MEMBERS IDENTIFIED UNDER ITEM (1) OF THIS
24 SUBSECTION; AND
25 (3) A DESCRIPTION OF PROGRESS MADE TOWARD HEALTH CARE
26 EQUITY FOR WARRIOR COMMUNITY MEMBERS.
27 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
28 October 1, 2025.
