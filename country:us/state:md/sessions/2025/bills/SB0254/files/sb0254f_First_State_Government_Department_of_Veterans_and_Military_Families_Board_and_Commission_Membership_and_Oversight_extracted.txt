A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Government – Department of Veterans and Military Families – Board and
3 Commission – Membership and Oversight
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Veterans and Military Families to provide
5 oversight of the War Memorial Building; altering the membership of the Maryland
6 Veterans Trust Fund Board of Trustees; altering the membership of the Maryland
7 Veterans Commission; repealing the War Memorial Commission; and generally
8 relating to the Department of Veterans and Military Families.
9 BY repealing
10 Article – State Government
11 Section 9–932 through 9–939 and the part “Part IV. War Memorial Commission”
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 BY adding to
15 Article – State Government
16 Section 9–908.1
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
19 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
20 Article – State Government
21 Section 9–914, 9–914.1, and 9–917
22 Annotated Code of Maryland
23 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
24 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
25 Article – State Government

1 Section 9–915

4 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
5 That Section(s) 9–932 through 9–939 and the part “Part IV. War Memorial Commission” of
6 Article – State Government of the Annotated Code of Maryland be repealed.
7 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
8 as follows:
9 Article – State Government
10 9–908.1.
11 (A) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE OVERSIGHT OF THE WAR
12 MEMORIAL BUILDING AS PROVIDED IN THIS SECTION.
13 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS THAT PROVIDE
14 FOR THE MANAGEMENT OF THE WAR MEMORIAL BUILDING IN A MANNER THAT:
15 (I) PERMITS THE GREATEST POSSIBLE USE OF THE BUILDING
16 BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE AND BY PATRIOTIC ORGANIZATIONS IN THE STATE;
17 AND
18 (II) TO THE EXTENT POSSIBLE, PERMITS THE USE BY THE
19 PATRIOTIC ORGANIZATIONS WITHOUT COST.
20 (2) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ENFORCE THE REGULATIONS.
21 (C) THE DEPARTMENT MAY USE, FOR THE MAINTENANCE AND
22 ADMINISTRATION OF THE BUILDING, ANY FUNDS THAT THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OR
23 THE MAYOR AND CITY COUNCIL OF BALTIMORE APPROPRIATE FOR THOSE
24 PURPOSES.
25 (D) THE STATE AND BALTIMORE CITY JOINTLY SHALL HOLD TITLE TO THE
26 WAR MEMORIAL BUILDING AND THE MEMORIAL SITE.
27 9–914.
28 (a) The powers and duties of the Trust shall rest in and be exercised by a Board
29 of Trustees.
30 (b) The Board of Trustees shall consist of the following 11 members:
SENATE BILL 254 3
1 (1) the Secretary, ex officio, or the Secretary’s designee, who shall serve as
2 the chair;
3 (2) the Secretary of Aging, ex officio, or the Secretary’s designee;
4 (3) the Secretary of Labor, ex officio, or the Secretary’s designee;
5 (4) the Secretary of Health, ex officio, or the Secretary’s designee;
6 (5) the Secretary of Human Services, ex officio, or the Secretary’s designee;
7 (6) the Adjutant General of the Military Department, ex officio, or the
8 Adjutant General’s designee;
9 (7) one representative of each of the following organizations, appointed by
10 the Governor:
11 (i) a veterans service organization;
12 (ii) a nonprofit organization that serves veterans; and
13 (iii) the business community;
14 (8) AS A NONVOTING TRUSTEE, a member of the House of Delegates
15 appointed by the Speaker of the House; and
16 (9) AS A NONVOTING TRUSTEE, a member of the Senate appointed by the
17 President of the Senate.
18 (c) The Governor shall consider geographical balance in making appointments to
19 the Board of Trustees.
20 (d) Except for the ex officio members or their designees:
21 (1) the term of a member is 4 years;
22 (2) the terms of members are staggered as required by the terms provided
23 for members of the Board on July 1, 2013;
24 (3) at the end of a term, a member continues to serve until a successor is
25 appointed and qualifies;
26 (4) a member who is appointed after a term has begun serves for the rest
27 of the term and until a successor is appointed and qualifies; and
28 (5) a member may serve no more than 2 terms.

1 9–914.1.
2 (a) The Board shall meet at places and dates to be determined by the Board, but
3 not less than 2 times a year.
4 (b) A majority of the VOTING trustees is a quorum.
5 (c) A trustee:
6 (1) may not receive compensation as a trustee; but
7 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
8 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.
9 (d) The Department shall provide staff, supplies, and office space for the Board.

11 In this Part II of this subtitle, “Commission” means the Maryland Veterans
12 Commission.
