J5 5lr2577
By: Delegates Hill, Pena–Melnyk, Kaiser, Kaufman, J. Lewis, J. Long, Ruff, and
Young
Introduced and read first time: January 29, 2025
Assigned to: Health and Government Operations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Insurance – Utilization Review – Use of Artificial Intelligence
3 FOR the purpose of requiring that certain carriers, pharmacy benefits managers, and
4 private review agents ensure that artificial intelligence, algorithm, or other software
5 tools are used in a certain manner when used for conducting utilization review; and
6 generally relating to utilization review and artificial intelligence.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – Insurance
9 Section 15–10A–06
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2017 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
12 BY adding to
13 Article – Insurance
14 Section 15–10B–05.1
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2017 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Insurance
20 15–10A–06.
21 (a) On a quarterly basis, each carrier shall submit to the Commissioner, on the
22 form the Commissioner requires, a report that describes:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 820
1 (1) the number of members entitled to health care benefits under a policy,
2 plan, or certificate issued or delivered in the State by the carrier;
3 (2) the number of clean claims for reimbursement processed by the carrier;
4 (3) the activities of the carrier under this subtitle, including:
5 (i) the outcome of each grievance filed with the carrier;
6 (ii) the number and outcomes of cases that were considered
7 emergency cases under § 15–10A–02(b)(2)(i) of this subtitle;
8 (iii) the time within which the carrier made a grievance decision on
9 each emergency case;
10 (iv) the time within which the carrier made a grievance decision on
11 all other cases that were not considered emergency cases;
12 (v) the number of grievances filed with the carrier that resulted from
13 an adverse decision involving length of stay for inpatient hospitalization as related to the
14 medical procedure involved;
15 (vi) the number of adverse decisions issued by the carrier under §
16 15–10A–02(f) of this subtitle, whether the adverse decision involved a prior authorization
17 or step therapy protocol, [and] the type of service at issue in the adverse decisions, AND
18 WHETHER AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER SOFTWARE TOOL
19 WAS USED IN MAKING THE ADVERSE DECISION;
20 (vii) the number of adverse decisions overturned after a
21 reconsideration request under § 15–10B–06 of this title; and
22 (viii) the number of requests made and granted under § 15–831(c)(1)
23 and (2) of this title; and
24 (4) the number and outcome of all other cases that are not subject to
25 activities of the carrier under this subtitle that resulted from an adverse decision involving
26 the length of stay for inpatient hospitalization as related to the medical procedure involved.
27 (b) The Commissioner shall:
28 (1) compile an annual summary report based on the information provided:
29 (i) under subsection (a) of this section; and
30 (ii) by the Secretary under § 19–705.2(e) of the Health – General
31 Article;
HOUSE BILL 820 3
1 (2) report any violations or actions taken under § 15–10B–11 of this title;
2 and
3 (3) provide copies of the summary report to the Governor and, subject to §
4 2–1257 of the State Government Article, to the General Assembly.
5 15–10B–05.1.
6 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
7 INDICATED.
8 (2) “ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE” MEANS AN ENGINEERED OR
9 MACHINE–BASED SYSTEM THAT VARIES IN ITS LEVEL OF AUTONOMY AND THAT CAN,
10 FOR EXPLICIT OR IMPLICIT OBJECTIVES, INFER FROM THE INPUT IT RECEIVES HOW
11 TO GENERATE OUTPUTS THAT CAN INFLUENCE PHYSICAL OR VIRTUAL
12 ENVIRONMENTS.
13 (3) “CARRIER” MEANS:
14 (I) AN INSURER;
15 (II) A NONPROFIT HEALTH SERVICE PLAN;
16 (III) A HEALTH MAINTENANCE ORGANIZATION;
17 (IV) A DENTAL PLAN ORGANIZATION; OR
18 (V) ANY OTHER PERSON THAT PROVIDES HEALTH BENEFIT
19 PLANS SUBJECT TO REGULATION BY THE STATE.
20 (B) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO:
21 (1) A CARRIER THAT:
22 (I) USES AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR
23 OTHER SOFTWARE TOOL FOR THE PURPOSE OF UTILIZATION REVIEW; OR
24 (II) CONTRACTS WITH OR OTHERWISE WORKS THROUGH AN
25 ENTITY THAT USES AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER
26 SOFTWARE TOOL FOR THE PURPOSE OF UTILIZATION REVIEW; AND
27 (2) A PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER OR PRIVATE REVIEW AGENT
28 THAT:
4 HOUSE BILL 820
1 (I) CONTRACTS WITH A CARRIER TO PROVIDE UTILIZATION
2 REVIEW ON BEHALF OF THE CARRIER; AND
3 (II) USES AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR
4 OTHER SOFTWARE TOOL FOR THE PURPOSE OF CONDUCTING UTILIZATION REVIEW
5 ON BEHALF OF THE CARRIER.
6 (C) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION, AN ENTITY SUBJECT TO
7 THIS SECTION SHALL ENSURE THAT:
8 (1) AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER
9 SOFTWARE TOOL BASES ITS DETERMINATIONS ON:
10 (I) AN ENROLLEE’S MEDICAL OR OTHER CLINICAL HISTORY;
11 (II) INDIVIDUAL CLINICAL CIRCUMSTANCES AS PRESENTED BY
12 A REQUESTING PROVIDER; OR
13 (III) OTHER RELEVANT CLINICAL INFORMATION CONTAINED IN
14 THE ENROLLEE’S MEDICAL OR OTHER CLINICAL RECORD;
15 (2) AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER
16 SOFTWARE TOOL DOES NOT BASE ITS DETERMINATIONS SOLELY ON A GROUP
17 DATASET;
18 (3) THE CRITERIA AND GUIDELINES FOR USING AN ARTIFICIAL
19 INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER SOFTWARE TOOL FOR MAKING
20 DETERMINATIONS COMPLY WITH THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS TITLE;
21 (4) AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER
22 SOFTWARE TOOL DOES NOT REPLACE THE ROLE OF A HEALTH CARE PROVIDER IN
23 THE DETERMINATION PROCESS UNDER § 15–10B–07 OF THIS SUBTITLE;
24 (5) THE USE OF AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR
25 OTHER SOFTWARE TOOL DOES NOT RESULT IN DISCRIMINATION AGAINST
26 ENROLLEES THAT IS PROHIBITED BY FEDERAL OR STATE LAW;
27 (6) AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER
28 SOFTWARE TOOL IS FAIRLY AND EQUITABLY APPLIED, INCLUDING IN ACCORDANCE
29 WITH ANY APPLICABLE REGULATIONS AND GUIDANCE ISSUED BY THE FEDERAL
30 DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES;
HOUSE BILL 820 5
1 (7) AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER
2 SOFTWARE TOOL IS OPEN TO INSPECTION FOR AUDIT OR COMPLIANCE REVIEWS BY
3 THE COMMISSIONER;
4 (8) WRITTEN POLICIES AND PROCEDURES ARE INCLUDED IN THE
5 UTILIZATION PLAN SUBMITTED UNDER § 15–10B–05 OF THIS SUBTITLE, INCLUDING
6 HOW AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER SOFTWARE TOOL WILL
7 BE USED AND WHAT OVERSIGHT WILL BE PROVIDED;
8 (9) THE PERFORMANCE, USE, AND OUTCOMES OF AN ARTIFICIAL
9 INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER SOFTWARE TOOL ARE REVIEWED AND
10 REVISED, IF NECESSARY AND AT LEAST ON A QUARTERLY BASIS, TO MAXIMIZE
11 ACCURACY AND RELIABILITY;
12 (10) PATIENT DATA IS NOT USED BEYOND ITS INTENDED AND STATED
13 PURPOSE, CONSISTENT WITH THE FEDERAL HEALTH INSURANCE PORTABILITY AND
14 ACCOUNTABILITY ACT OF 1996, AS APPLICABLE; AND
15 (11) AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER
16 SOFTWARE TOOL DOES NOT DIRECTLY OR INDIRECTLY CAUSE HARM TO AN
17 ENROLLEE.
18 (D) AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ALGORITHM, OR OTHER SOFTWARE
19 TOOL MAY NOT DENY, DELAY OR MODIFY HEALTH CARE SERVICES.
20 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
21 October 1, 2025.
