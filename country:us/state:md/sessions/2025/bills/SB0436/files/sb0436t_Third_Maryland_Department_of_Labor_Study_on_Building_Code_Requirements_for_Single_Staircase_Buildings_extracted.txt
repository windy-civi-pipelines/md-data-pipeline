1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Department of Labor – Study on Building Code Requirements for
3 Single–Staircase Buildings
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Labor to study and make
5 recommendations regarding building code requirements for single–staircase
6 buildings; and generally relating to building code requirements for single–staircase
7 buildings.
8 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
9 That:
10 (a) In this section, “Department” means the Maryland Department of Labor.
11 (b) The Department shall:
12 (1) conduct a study on building code requirements for single–staircase
13 buildings in the State and other states; and
14 (2) make recommendations regarding potential legislative or regulatory
15 changes to authorize the construction of single–staircase buildings up to six stories above
16 grade plane in the State for the purpose of increasing the affordable housing supply by
17 reducing barriers to multifamily housing development.
18 (c) The study shall:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.

amendment. *sb0436*
2 SENATE BILL 436
1 (1) survey and document a representative sampling of state and local laws,
2 ordinances, and policies from across North America;
3 (2) analyze best practices for state and local governments when drafting
4 building code requirements for single–staircase buildings up to six stories above grade,
5 taking into consideration the following factors:
6 (i) the availability of an adequate water supply;
7 (ii) the presence and response capability of a professional fire
8 department;
9 (iii) the practicability or feasibility of compliance with modern fire
10 codes, including a comparison to the safety of the existing housing supply;
11 (iv) the practicability or feasibility of building in urban or infill
12 settings;
13 (v) the viability of redevelopment or adaptive reuse of historic
14 properties;
15 (vi) any impacts of laws, ordinances, or policies on:
16 1. housing development; or
17 2. the cost of housing; and
18 (vii) any other relevant factors, as determined by the Department;
19 and
20 (3) make legislative and regulatory recommendations, including potential
21 amendments to the Maryland Building Performance Standards, to authorize the
22 construction of single–staircase buildings up to six stories above grade plane in the State
23 for the purpose of increasing the affordable housing supply by reducing barriers to
24 multifamily housing development.
25 (d) In carrying out the study, the Department shall consult with:
26 (1) a fire engineer who is a faculty member at a public institution of higher
27 education that has a fire protection program; and
28 (2) a representative of a union that represents firefighters the Professional
29 Fire Fighters of Maryland, designated by the President of the Professional Fire Fighters of
30 Maryland;
31 (3) a representative of the Maryland State Firefighters Association,
32 designated by the President of the Association;
SENATE BILL 436 3
1 (4) a representative of the Maryland Fire Chiefs Association, designated by
2 the President of the Association;
3 (5) a representative of the Metropolitan Fire Chiefs Association,
4 designated by the President of the Association;
5 (6) a representative of the Office of the State Fire Marshal; and
6 (7) a representative of a Maryland nonprofit organization that advocates
7 for safe and affordable housing.
8 (e) On or before December 1, 2025 2026, the Department shall report its findings
9 and recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
10 Government Article, the General Assembly, the Senate Education, Energy, and the
11 Environment Committee, and the House Environment and Transportation Committee.
12 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
13 1, 2025.
