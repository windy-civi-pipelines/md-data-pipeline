1 AN ACT concerning
2 Workgroup to Study the Rise in Adverse Decisions in the State Health Care
3 System – Establishment
4 FOR the purpose of establishing the Workgroup to Study the Rise in Adverse Decisions in
5 the State Health Care System; and generally relating to the Workgroup to Study the
6 Rise in Adverse Decisions in the State Health Care System.
7 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
8 That:
9 (a) There is a Workgroup to Study the Rise in Adverse Decisions in the State
10 Health Care System.
11 (b) The Workgroup consists of the following members:
12 (1) one member of the Senate of Maryland, appointed by the President of
13 the Senate;
14 (2) one member of the House of Delegates, appointed by the Speaker of the
15 House;

1 (3) the Maryland Insurance Commissioner, or the Commissioner’s
2 designee;
3 (4) the Deputy Secretary of the Maryland Medicaid Program, or the Deputy
4 Secretary’s designee;
5 (5) the Executive Director of the Health Services Cost Review Commission,
6 or the Executive Director’s designee;
7 (6) the Executive Director of the Maryland Health Care Commission, or the
8 Executive Director’s designee;
9 (7) the Executive Director of the Chesapeake Regional Information System
10 for our Patients, or the Executive Director’s designee; and
11 (8) the Director of the Health Education and Advocacy Unit of the Office of
12 the Attorney General, or the Director’s designee; and
13 (8) (9) the following members, appointed by the President of the Senate and
14 Speaker of the House Governor:
15 (i) one representative of the Maryland Hospital Association;
16 (ii) one representative of the League of Life and Health Insurers;
17 (iii) one representative of a managed care plan;
18 (iv) two representatives of Maryland hospitals, with one
19 representative from a large hospital system and one representative from a community
20 hospital;
21 (v) one pharmacy services provider;
22 (vi) one behavioral health provider;
23 (vii) one representative of a commercial carrier;
24 (viii) one representative of a patient advocacy organization;
25 (ix) one physician; and
26 (x) one representative of MedChi; and
27 (xi) one representative of a federally qualified health center.
28 (c) The Workgroup members shall elect the chair of the Workgroup.
HOUSE BILL 995 3
1 (d) The Health Services Cost Review Commission and the Maryland Insurance
2 Administration, jointly and in consultation with the Maryland Hospital Association, shall
3 provide staff for the Workgroup.
4 (e) A member of the Workgroup:
5 (1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Workgroup; but
6 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
7 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.
8 (f) The Workgroup shall:
9 (1) review existing State adverse decision reporting requirements for all
10 health payers in the State and include in its final report:
11 (i) the number of adverse decisions compared to the total number of
12 claims processed each year on average;
13 (ii) the number of enrollees in each health plan offered in the State;
14 (iii) the diagnostic and procedure information for each adverse
15 decision; and
16 (iv) any other data used to inform the Workgroup’s goal of reducing
17 adverse decisions;
18 (2) make recommendations to improve State reporting on adverse
19 decisions, including recommendations regarding:
20 (i) standardized definitions of:
21 1. medical service categories;
22 2. health settings;
23 3. adverse decisions; and
24 4. medical necessity;
25 (ii) a standardized method for categorizing adverse decisions and
26 prior authorization denials; and
27 (iii) a standardized process for reporting grievances or filing
28 complaints and appealing adverse decisions;
4 HOUSE BILL 995
1 (3) develop strategies for, and make recommendations to reduce, the
2 number of adverse decisions; and
3 (4) develop recommendations for legislation to address the rise in adverse
4 decisions and standardize State reporting requirements regarding adverse decisions across
5 all payers.
6 (g) On or before December 1, 2025, the Workgroup shall report its findings and
7 recommendations to the Senate Finance Committee and the House Health and
8 Government Operations Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government
9 Article.
10 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
11 1, 2025. It shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and 1 month and, at the end of June
12 30, 2026, this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be
13 abrogated and of no further force and effect.
Approved:
