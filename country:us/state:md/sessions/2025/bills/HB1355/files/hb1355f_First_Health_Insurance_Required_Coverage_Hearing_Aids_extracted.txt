Assigned to: Health and Government Operations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Insurance – Required Coverage – Hearing Aids
3 FOR the purpose of altering the circumstances under which certain insurers, nonprofit
4 health service plans, and health maintenance organizations are required to provide
5 coverage for medically appropriate and necessary hearing aids for adults to require
6 coverage if the hearing aid is ordered, fitted, and dispensed by a licensed hearing aid
7 dispenser; and generally relating to health insurance coverage for hearing aids.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Insurance
10 Section 15–838.1
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2017 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Insurance
16 15–838.1.
17 (a) In this section, “hearing aid” means a device that:
18 (1) is of a design and circuitry to optimize audibility and listening skills in
19 the environment commonly experienced by adults; and
20 (2) is nondisposable.
21 (b) This section applies to:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1355
1 (1) insurers and nonprofit health service plans that provide hospital,
2 medical, or surgical benefits to individuals or groups on an expense–incurred basis under
3 health insurance policies or contracts that are issued or delivered in the State; and
4 (2) health maintenance organizations that provide hospital, medical, or
5 surgical benefits to individuals or groups under contracts that are issued or delivered in
6 the State.
7 (c) An entity subject to this section shall provide coverage for all medically
8 appropriate and necessary hearing aids for an adult who is covered under a policy or
9 contract if the hearing aids are:
10 (1) prescribed, fitted, and dispensed by a licensed audiologist; OR
11 (2) ORDERED, FITTED, AND DISPENSED BY A LICENSED HEARING AID
12 DISPENSER.
13 (d) (1) An entity subject to this section may limit the benefit payable under
14 subsection (c) of this section to $1,400 per hearing aid for each hearing–impaired ear every
15 36 months.
16 (2) An insured or enrollee may choose a hearing aid that is priced higher
17 than the benefit payable under this subsection and may pay the difference between the
18 price of the hearing aid and the benefit payable under this subsection, without financial or
19 contractual penalty to the provider of the hearing aid.
20 (e) This section does not prohibit an entity subject to this section from providing
21 coverage that is greater or more favorable to an insured or enrollee than the coverage
22 required under this section.
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall apply to all
24 policies, contracts, and health benefit plans issued, delivered, or renewed in the State on or
25 after January 1, 2026.
26 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
27 January 1, 2026.
