(PRE–FILED) CF SB 8
By: Delegate Vogel
Requested: September 6, 2024
Introduced and read first time: January 8, 2025
Assigned to: Ways and Means
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Maryland Civic Excellence Program – Established
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland Civic Excellence Program as a voluntary
4 program in which local school systems may participate to recognize students and
5 public schools that promote the civic readiness and civic engagement of students in
6 public schools in the State; and generally relating to the establishment of the
7 Maryland Civic Excellence Program.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Education
10 Section 4–147
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Education
16 4–147.
17 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “PROGRAM” MEANS THE MARYLAND CIVIC
18 EXCELLENCE PROGRAM ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SECTION.
19 (B) (1) THERE IS A MARYLAND CIVIC EXCELLENCE PROGRAM.
20 (2) THE PURPOSE OF THE PROGRAM IS TO RECOGNIZE STUDENTS
21 AND PUBLIC SCHOOLS THAT PROMOTE THE CIVIC READINESS AND CIVIC
22 ENGAGEMENT OF STUDENTS IN PUBLIC SCHOOLS IN THE STATE.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb0072*
2 HOUSE BILL 72
1 (C) THE PROVISIONS OF THIS SECTION APPLY ONLY TO A LOCAL SCHOOL
2 SYSTEM THAT CHOOSES TO PARTICIPATE IN AND ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM.
3 (D) BEGINNING WITH THE 2026–2027 SCHOOL YEAR, EACH PARTICIPATING
4 LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL:
5 (1) AWARD A STUDENT A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE IF THE
6 STUDENT APPLIES AND MEETS THE CRITERIA ESTABLISHED BY THE LOCAL SCHOOL
7 SYSTEM UNDER SUBSECTION (E) OF THIS SECTION; AND
8 (2) DESIGNATE A PUBLIC SCHOOL AS A CENTER OF CIVIC
9 EXCELLENCE IF THE SCHOOL APPLIES AND MEETS THE CRITERIA ADOPTED BY THE
10 LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM UNDER SUBSECTION (G) OF THIS SECTION.
11 (E) AT MINIMUM, A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM’S CRITERIA
12 FOR A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE SHALL REQUIRE A STUDENT TO:
13 (1) (I) TAKE THE MARYLAND COMPREHENSIVE ASSESSMENT FOR
14 GOVERNMENT; AND
15 (II) MEET THE ASSESSMENT REQUIREMENTS FOR
16 GRADUATION;
17 (2) EARN A GRADE OF A OR B IN EACH OF THE FOLLOWING HIGH
18 SCHOOL SOCIAL STUDIES COURSES:
19 (I) UNITED STATES HISTORY;
20 (II) MODERN WORLD HISTORY; AND
21 (III) AMERICAN GOVERNMENT; AND
22 (3) COMPLETE AND DEMONSTRATE EVIDENCE OF AT LEAST 75 HOURS
23 OF STUDENT SERVICE, OF WHICH AT LEAST 30 HOURS SHALL BE DEDICATED TO A
24 SERVICE LEARNING PROJECT WITH A FOCUS ON COMMUNITY AND CIVIC
25 ENGAGEMENT.
26 (F) A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL ESTABLISH
27 APPLICATION CRITERIA TO ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM.
28 (G) AT MINIMUM, A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM’S CRITERIA
29 FOR A CENTER OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE DESIGNATION SHALL REQUIRE A PUBLIC
HOUSE BILL 72 3
1 SCHOOL TO HAVE AT LEAST 50% OF THE STUDENTS IN ITS GRADUATING CLASS WHO
2 HAVE EARNED A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE.
3 (H) (1) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM THAT DETERMINES A STUDENT HAS
4 FULFILLED THE CRITERIA UNDER THE PROGRAM SHALL:
5 (I) AWARD THE STUDENT A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE;
6 (II) RECORD THE AWARD ON THE STUDENT’S OFFICIAL HIGH
7 SCHOOL TRANSCRIPT; AND
8 (III) RECOGNIZE THE AWARD BY AWARDING A CORD OR MEDAL
9 OR BY ATTACHING OTHER INSIGNIA ON THE STUDENT’S HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA.
10 (2) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM THAT DETERMINES THAT A PUBLIC
11 SCHOOL HAS FULFILLED THE CRITERIA UNDER THE PROGRAM SHALL:
12 (I) DESIGNATE THE SCHOOL AS A CENTER OF CIVIC
13 EXCELLENCE; AND
14 (II) RECOGNIZE THE DESIGNATION BY PROVIDING ANY OF THE
15 FOLLOWING:
16 1. A BANNER;
17 2. A GOVERNOR’S CITATION; OR
18 3. A CITATION FROM THE DEPARTMENT.
19 (I) ON OR BEFORE JULY 1, 2027, AND EACH JULY 1 THEREAFTER, EACH
20 PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL SUBMIT TO THE DEPARTMENT A
21 REPORT ON THE PROGRAM BASED ON THE MOST RECENTLY COMPLETED SCHOOL
22 YEAR.
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
24 1, 2025.
