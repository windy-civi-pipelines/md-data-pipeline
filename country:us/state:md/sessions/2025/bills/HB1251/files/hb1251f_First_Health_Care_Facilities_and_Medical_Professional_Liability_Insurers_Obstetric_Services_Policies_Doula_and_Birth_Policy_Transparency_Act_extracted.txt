By: Delegates Toles, Amprey, Atterbeary, Boafo, Boyce, Hill, D. Jones, J. Long,
Pasteur, Patterson, Smith, Taylor, Wells, White Holland, Wilkins, and
Williams
Introduced and read first time: February 7, 2025
Assigned to: Health and Government Operations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Care Facilities and Medical Professional Liability Insurers – Obstetric
3 Services Policies
4 (Doula and Birth Policy Transparency Act)
5 FOR the purpose of requiring certain health care facilities to adopt and provide evidence to
6 the Maryland Department of Health that the health care facility has a certain policy
7 relating to obstetric services; requiring insurers that issue or deliver medical
8 professional liability insurance policies in the State, on request, to provide the
9 Maryland Department of Health with information regarding the insurer’s policy
10 regarding coverage of obstetric services; and generally relating to obstetric services.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Health – General
13 Section 19–2601 and 19–2602 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 26. Health Care
14 Facility Obstetric Services Policy”
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 BY adding to
18 Article – Insurance
19 Section 19–104.1
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2017 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
22 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
23 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
24 Article – Health – General
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1251
1 SUBTITLE 26. HEALTH CARE FACILITY OBSTETRIC SERVICES POLICY.
2 19–2601.
3 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
4 INDICATED.
5 (B) “FREESTANDING AMBULATORY CARE FACILITY” HAS THE MEANING
6 STATED IN § 19–3B–01 OF THIS TITLE.
7 (C) “HEALTH CARE FACILITY” MEANS A HOSPITAL OR FREESTANDING
8 AMBULATORY CARE FACILITY THAT PROVIDES OBSTETRIC CARE.
9 (D) “HOSPITAL” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 19–301 OF THIS TITLE.
10 19–2602.
11 (A) A HEALTH CARE FACILITY SHALL ADOPT AND PROVIDE EVIDENCE TO
12 THE DEPARTMENT THAT THE HEALTH CARE FACILITY HAS A POLICY THAT:
13 (1) ALLOWS EVERY BIRTHING PARENT TO HAVE A CERTIFIED DOULA,
14 AS DEFINED IN § 15–141.4 OF THIS ARTICLE, PRESENT DURING BIRTH, IN ADDITION
15 TO ANY AUTHORIZED GUESTS;
16 (2) PRIORITIZES NEWBORN BONDING WITH THE FAMILY OF THE
17 NEWBORN;
18 (3) PROHIBITS THE HEALTH CARE FACILITY FROM USING
19 SIGNIFICANT MEDICAL INTERVENTION IN THE BIRTHING PROCESS WITHOUT THE
20 INFORMED CONSENT OF THE BIRTHING PARENT, INCLUDING:
21 (I) THE ADMINISTRATION OF A MEDICATION THAT WILL
22 INDUCE LABOR;
23 (II) A CESAREAN SECTION; AND
24 (III) USE OF FORCEPS;
25 (4) DETAILS THE HEALTH CARE FACILITY’S PROCESS FOR RECEIVING
26 A BIRTHING PARENT’S MEDICAL INFORMATION FROM A HEALTH CARE PROVIDER
27 REGULATED UNDER TITLE 8 OF THE HEALTH OCCUPATIONS ARTICLE WHO HAS
28 PROVIDED SERVICES TO THE BIRTHING PERSON; AND
HOUSE BILL 1251 3
1 (5) ESTABLISHES A PROCESS TO TRANSFER AND RECEIVE PREGNANT
2 PERSONS ACROSS THE HEALTH CARE FACILITY’S LEVELS OF CARE WITHIN THE
3 FACILITY’S CAPACITY AND CAPABILITY.
4 (B) THE PROCESS DETAILED IN THE POLICY REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION
5 (A)(4) OF THIS SECTION SHALL INCLUDE A PROCESS FOR TRANSFERRING MEDICAL
6 RECORDS WHEN THE BIRTHING PARENT WAS RECEIVING SERVICES FROM THE
7 HEALTH CARE PROVIDER IN A HOME BIRTH SETTING.
8 (C) A HEALTH CARE FACILITY SHALL PROVIDE EVIDENCE TO THE
9 DEPARTMENT THAT THE HEALTH CARE FACILITY HAS ADOPTED A POLICY UNDER
10 SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION:
11 (1) (I) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1, 2026; OR
12 (II) IF THE HEALTH CARE FACILITY IS ESTABLISHED AFTER
13 JANUARY 1, 2026, ON THE ESTABLISHMENT OF THE HEALTH CARE FACILITY; AND
14 (2) WHENEVER THE POLICY IS UPDATED.
15 (D) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT THIS
16 SECTION, INCLUDING REGULATIONS THAT ESTABLISH THE FORM AND MANNER IN
17 WHICH A HEALTH CARE FACILITY MAY PROVE TO THE DEPARTMENT THAT THE
18 HEALTH CARE FACILITY HAS ADOPTED A POLICY IN COMPLIANCE WITH THIS
19 SECTION.
20 Article – Insurance
21 19–104.1.
22 ON REQUEST, AN INSURER THAT ISSUES OR DELIVERS MEDICAL
23 PROFESSIONAL LIABILITY INSURANCE POLICIES IN THE STATE SHALL PROVIDE THE
24 MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH WITH INFORMATION REGARDING THE
25 INSURER’S POLICY RELATED TO COVERAGE OF OBSTETRIC SERVICES, INCLUDING
26 COVERAGE FOR A VAGINAL BIRTH AFTER CESAREAN.
27 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
28 October 1, 2025.
