Assigned to: Rules and Executive Nominations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Baltimore County and Baltimore City – Vehicle Laws – Lighting on Privately
3 Owned Vehicles
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing certain officers from certain volunteer emergency medical
5 services organizations located in Baltimore County and Baltimore City to have their
6 privately owned vehicles equipped with certain lighting devices; and generally
7 relating to vehicle lighting and volunteer emergency services.
8 BY renumbering
9 Article – Transportation
10 Section 22–218(c)(4) through (13)
11 to be Section 22–218(c)(5) through (14), respectively
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – Transportation
16 Section 22–218(c)(1)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
19 BY adding to
20 Article – Transportation
21 Section 22–218(c)(4)
22 Annotated Code of Maryland
23 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
24 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
25 That Section(s) 22–218(c)(4) through (13) of Article – Transportation of the Annotated Code
26 of Maryland be renumbered to be Section(s) 22–218(c)(5) through (14), respectively.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb1520*
2 HOUSE BILL 1520
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
2 as follows:
3 Article – Transportation
4 22–218.
5 (c) (1) A person may not drive or move on any highway any vehicle or
6 equipment that is equipped with or displays any light or signal device designed to emit an
7 oscillating, rotating, blinking, or other type of emission of light, unless designated and
8 authorized by the Administrator as indicated in paragraphs (2) through [(13)] (14) of this
9 subsection. The provisions of this section do not prohibit the display and use of any lighting
10 device that may be permitted or required elsewhere in the Maryland Vehicle Law.
11 (4) IN EACH NONPROFIT VOLUNTEER EMERGENCY MEDICAL
12 ORGANIZATION THAT IS LOCATED IN BALTIMORE COUNTY OR BALTIMORE CITY AND
13 OPERATES UNDER A COMMERCIAL AMBULANCE SERVICE LICENSE, NO MORE THAN
14 FIVE OF THE HIGHEST–RANKING OFFICERS MAY HAVE THEIR PRIVATELY OWNED
15 VEHICLES EQUIPPED WITH RED OR RED AND WHITE LIGHTS OR SIGNAL DEVICES
16 WHICH MAY BE DISPLAYED ONLY WHILE ON ROUTE TO OR AT THE SCENE OF AN
17 EMERGENCY.
18 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
19 October 1, 2025.
