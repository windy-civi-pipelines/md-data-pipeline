A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Cyber Maryland Program – Revisions
3 FOR the purpose of transferring the Cyber Maryland Program from the Maryland
4 Technology Development Corporation to the Maryland Department of Labor;
5 altering the duties of the Program; altering the purposes for which the Cyber
6 Maryland Fund may be used; repealing certain funding requirements related to the
7 Program; altering the membership of the Cyber Maryland Board; and generally
8 relating to the Cyber Maryland Program.
9 BY transferring
10 Article – Economic Development
11 Section 10–491 and the part “Part XI. Miscellaneous”
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 to be
15 Article – Labor and Employment
16 Section 11–1701 and the subtitle “Subtitle 17. Miscellaneous”
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
19 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
20 Article – Labor and Employment
21 Section 11–1701 to be under the amended subtitle “Subtitle 17. Cyber Maryland
22 Program”

24 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

16 11–1702.
17 [(b)] (A) There is a Cyber Maryland Program in the [Corporation]
18 DEPARTMENT.
19 [(c)] (B) The purposes of the Program are to:
20 (1) create and execute a talent pipeline that materially reduces workforce
21 vacancies by July 1, 2026;
22 (2) serve as a one–stop shop for employers seeking to leverage cyber
23 workforce development programs offered by the State and its partners;
24 (3) inform cybersecurity training and education programs operated by
25 public or private entities with industry–driven needs;
26 (4) build the most advanced local and State information technology
27 workforce in the nation, which, to the maximum extent possible, reflects the racial, gender,
28 ethnic, and geographic diversity of the State;
29 (5) coordinate and accelerate cybersecurity research and innovation in the
30 State; and
HOUSE BILL 82 3
1 (6) support the efforts of the Department of Information Technology to
2 improve the State government’s cybersecurity posture, including State agencies, local
3 government units, and critical infrastructure.
4 [(d)] (C) The Program shall:
5 (1) conduct ongoing research by collaborating with the Cybersecurity
6 Association [of Maryland], Inc., Cybersecurity Talent Advisory Board to collect and analyze
7 real–time industry data to identify cybersecurity workforce needs as described in the U.S.
8 Chamber of Commerce Talent Pipeline Management Approach;
9 (2) use the results of the research described in item (1) of this subsection
10 to increase the effectiveness of existing State cybersecurity workforce programs for
11 employers in the State;
12 (3) facilitate partnerships for new training and education programs to
13 address the workforce needs identified under item (1) of this subsection;
14 (4) develop a statewide strategic plan for cybersecurity workforce
15 development, using the results of the research described in item (1) of this subsection and
16 with input from the Cybersecurity Association [of Maryland], Inc., Cybersecurity Talent
17 Advisory Board on or before December 1, 2023; [and]
18 (5) DEVELOP, PROMOTE, SUPPORT, AND INVEST IN TALENT
19 IMPROVEMENT STRATEGIES, SUCH AS STRATEGIES THAT LEVERAGE THE U.S.
20 CHAMBER OF COMMERCE TALENT PIPELINE MANAGEMENT APPROACH; AND
21 [(5)] (6) ensure that outcomes of the Program are inclusive and, to the
22 maximum extent practicable, reflect the racial, gender, ethnic, and geographic diversity of
23 the State.

27 [(i)] (1) achieve the purposes identified under [subsection (c) of
28 this section] § 11–1702(B) OF THIS SUBTITLE; and
29 [(ii)] (2) support innovative approaches to meeting cybersecurity
30 workforce needs.
31 [(3)] (C) The [Corporation] DEPARTMENT shall administer the Fund.
