1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Department of Health – Workgroup Behavioral Health Advisory
3 Council and the Commission on Behavioral Health Care Treatment and Access –
4 Plan to Implement Early and Periodic Screening, Diagnostic, and Treatment
5 Requirements
6 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Health to convene a Behavioral
7 Health Advisory Council and the Commission on Behavioral Health Care Treatment
8 and Access, through its workgroup on youth behavioral health, individuals with
9 developmental disabilities, and individuals with complex behavioral health needs, to
10 provide recommendations to implement Early and Periodic Screening, Diagnostic,
11 and Treatment requirements; and generally relating to a workgroup plan to
12 implement Early and Periodic Screening, Diagnostic, and Treatment requirements.
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That:
15 (a) The Maryland Department of Health shall convene a workgroup to implement
16 Early and Periodic Screening, Diagnostic, and Treatment requirements.
17 (b) The workgroup shall include the following members:
18 (1) behavioral health professionals who work with children and families;
19 (2) childhood mental health specialists;

1 (3) clinicians who work with children and families;
2 (4) children with behavioral health issues;
3 (5) behavioral health advocates;
4 (6) parents and caregivers with experience navigating the behavioral
5 health system on behalf of children; and
6 (7) other interested stakeholders.
7 (c) (a) The Behavioral Health Advisory Council and the Commission on Behavioral
8 Health Care Treatment and Access, through its workgroup on youth behavioral health,
9 individuals with developmental disabilities, and individuals with complex behavioral
10 health needs, shall create a plan provide recommendations to implement the
11 recommendations in Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services State Health Official
12 letter #24–005: Best Practices for Adhering to Early and Periodic Screening, Diagnostic,
13 and Treatment (EPSDT) Requirements for children, including:
14 (1) screening and assessment of behavioral health conditions;
15 (2) the feasibility of implementing the DC:0–5 Diagnostic Classification of
16 Mental Health and Developmental Disorders of Infancy and Early Childhood as a
17 diagnostic tool for patients under the age of 5 years;
18 (3) community–based services to correct and ameliorate a wide range of
19 behavioral health conditions; and
20 (4) services to:
21 (i) ensure children’s behavioral health;
22 (ii) address early symptoms of concern, with or without a diagnosis;
23 and
24 (iii) address urgent and crisis needs.
25 (b) In performing the duties described in subsection (a) of this section, the Council
26 and Commission, through its workgroup, shall consult with:
27 (1) behavioral health professionals who work with children and families;
28 (2) early childhood mental health specialists;
29 (3) clinicians who work with children and families;
30 (4) children with behavioral health issues;

1 (5) behavioral health advocates;
2 (6) parents and caregivers with experience navigating the behavioral
3 health system on behalf of children; and
4 (7) other interested stakeholders.
5 (d) (c) On or before December 1, 2025 January 1, 2026, the Maryland Department of
6 Health shall submit a report of the its findings and recommendations of the workgroup to
7 the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
9 1, 2025. It shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and, at the end of June 30, 2026, this
10 Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no
11 further force and effect.
