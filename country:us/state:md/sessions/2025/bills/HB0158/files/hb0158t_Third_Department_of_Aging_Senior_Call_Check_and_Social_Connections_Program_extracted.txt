1 AN ACT concerning
2 Department of Aging – Social Connections Program – Establishment
3 Senior Call–Check and Social Connections Program
4 FOR the purpose of renaming the Senior Call–Check Service and Notification Program to
5 be the Senior Call–Check and Social Connections Program; repealing altering
6 certain provisions of law related to the Senior Call–Check Service and Notification
7 Program; establishing the Social Connections Program to provide certain
8 communication to eligible older adults to promote wellness and purposeful social
9 engagement; authorizing the integration of services between the Program and
10 Telecommunications Access of Maryland; authorizing the Department of Aging and
11 the Department of Disabilities to collaborate on developing the Program budget; and
12 generally relating to the Department of Aging and the Senior Call–Check and Social
13 Connections Program.
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – State Finance and Procurement
16 Section 3.5–701 and 3.5–702 to be under the amended subtitle “Subtitle 7. Senior
17 Call–Check and Social Connections Program”

19 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
21 Article – Human Services

15 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
16 (b) “Eligible participant” means a resident of the State who is [at least 65 60
17 years old] AN OLDER ADULT WHO:
18 (1) IS AT RISK OF SOCIAL ISOLATION; AND
19 (2) WOULD BENEFIT FROM ADDITIONAL PURPOSEFUL SOCIAL
20 ENGAGEMENT AND WELLNESS CHECKS.
21 (c) “Person of record” includes a local law enforcement unit or other local
22 government agency OR A DESIGNEE OF THE PARTICIPANT that chooses to participate in
23 the Program.
24 (d) “Program” means the [Senior Call–Check Service and Notification] AND
25 SOCIAL CONNECTIONS Program.
26 [(e) (1) “Senior call–check service and notification AND SOCIAL
27 CONNECTIONS” means a telephone call made or received each day at a regularly scheduled
28 time by REGULAR, DIRECT COMMUNICATION FROM the Department of Aging or the
29 Department of Aging’s designee to the residence of an eligible participant to verify that the
30 participant is able to receive notifications and answer the telephone or place a call from the
31 telephone.
32 (2) “Senior call–check service and notification AND SOCIAL
33 CONNECTIONS” includes:

1 (i) an automated or live telephone call OR OTHER DIRECT
2 COMMUNICATION placed INITIATED by an eligible participant or received by an eligible
3 participant at a regularly scheduled time each day;
4 (ii) if the eligible participant does not answer or place INITIATE the
5 regularly scheduled call OR OTHER DIRECT COMMUNICATION and the Secretary of Aging
6 designs the Program to require this action, one or more automated or live telephone calls
7 OR OTHER DIRECT COMMUNICATIONS to the eligible participant;
8 (iii) if the eligible participant does not answer a telephone call OR
9 OTHER DIRECT COMMUNICATION made under item (ii) of this paragraph, an additional
10 automated or live telephone call OR OTHER DIRECT COMMUNICATION to notify a person
11 of record whose name has been provided to the Department of Aging; and
12 (iv) a notification to the eligible participant regarding information
13 that the Secretary of Aging has determined to be relevant.]
14 (E) (F) “REGULAR, DIRECT COMMUNICATION” MEANS REGULAR
15 INTERACTIONS INITIATED OR RECEIVED BY THE DEPARTMENT OF AGING OR THE
16 DEPARTMENT’S DESIGNEE THAT:
17 (1) MAY INCLUDE TELEPHONE CALLS, TEXT MESSAGES, AND
18 WEB–BASED, VIRTUAL, VIDEO, OR IN–PERSON COMMUNICATIONS, AND OTHER
19 FORMS OF COMMUNICATION APPROVED BY THE DEPARTMENT; AND
20 (2) ARE ACCESSIBLE TO ELIGIBLE PARTICIPANTS WITH DISABILITIES.
21 (F) (G) “VILLAGE” MEANS LOCAL, VOLUNTEER–LED ORGANIZATIONS
22 THAT AIM TO SUPPORT COMMUNITY MEMBERS WHO CHOOSE TO AGE IN PLACE,

25 (2) FOSTERING SOCIAL CONNECTIONS THROUGH ACTIVITIES AND
26 COORDINATING VOLUNTEER HELP AT HOME USING THE
27 NEIGHBOR–HELPING–NEIGHBOR MODEL.

10 (1) AN AUTOMATED OR LIVE TELEPHONE CALL PLACED BY AN

14 THE REGULAR, DIRECT COMMUNICATION, A FOLLOW–UP DIRECT COMMUNICATION

17 (3) A NOTIFICATION TO THE ELIGIBLE PARTICIPANT REGARDING

25 ACCESS OF MARYLAND PROGRAM.

29 STATE, AN AREA AGENCY ON AGING, OR ANY OTHER PERSON to provide [the senior
30 call–check service and notification required under subsection (a) of this section] PROGRAM
31 SERVICES.

33 AGREEMENTS WITH OTHER STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENTAL ENTITIES TO

7 under the Program in accordance with regulations of the Department of Aging.

18 7–806.
19 (a) (1) Subject to paragraph (2) of this subsection, the programs under §
20 7–804(a) of this subtitle, § 7–902(a) of this title, and § 3.5–702 of the State Finance and
21 Procurement Article shall be funded as provided in the State budget.
22 (2) For fiscal year 2019 and each fiscal year thereafter, the program under
23 § 3.5–702 of the State Finance and Procurement Article shall be funded at an amount that:
24 (i) is equal to the cost that the Department of Aging is expected to
25 incur for the upcoming fiscal year to provide the service and administer the program; [and]
26 (ii) does not exceed 5 cents per month for each account out of the
27 surcharge amount authorized under subsection (c) of this section; AND
28 (III) IS BASED ON A BUDGET DEVELOPED BY THE DEPARTMENT

30 CONSIDERATION OF THE FISCAL NEEDS OF THE PROGRAMS FUNDED THROUGH THE
31 SURCHARGES PAID UNDER SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION.

33 paying the costs of maintaining and operating the programs under:

1 (i) § 7–804(a) of this subtitle, subject to the limitations and controls

10 (3) Money in the Universal Service Trust Fund may only be used:
11 (i) to fund the costs of the programs specified in paragraph (1) of
12 this subsection; and
13 (ii) to pay for the administration of the Universal Service Trust
14 Fund.

16 of this title, and § 3.5–702 of the State Finance and Procurement Article shall be funded by
17 revenues generated by:
18 (i) a surcharge to be paid by the subscribers to a communications
19 service; and
20 (ii) other funds as provided in the State budget.
21 (2) (i) The surcharge may not exceed 18 cents per month for each
22 account and shall be applied to all current bills rendered for a communications service in
23 the State.
24 (ii) The surcharge is payable at the time the bills for a
25 communications service are due.
26 (3) The surcharge to be collected under this section applies only to a
27 communications service for which charges are billed by, or on behalf of, a communications
28 company to a subscriber of the communications service.
29 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall be construed to
