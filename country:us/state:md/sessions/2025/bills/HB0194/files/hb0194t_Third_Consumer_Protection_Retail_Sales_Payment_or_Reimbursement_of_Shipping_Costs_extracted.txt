CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – Retail Sales – Payment or Reimbursement of Shipping
3 Costs
4 FOR the purpose of requiring that a merchant provide a prepaid shipping label or a refund
5 for shipping costs to a consumer who received an incorrect item that is damaged,
6 faulty, or sent in error within a certain period of time after a certain request is made
7 or a certain item is received; making a violation of this Act an unfair, abusive, or
8 deceptive trade practice that is subject to enforcement and certain civil penalties
9 under the Maryland Consumer Protection Act; and generally relating to consumer
10 retail transactions.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
12 Article – Commercial Law
13 Section 13–301(14)(xliv)
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
16 (As enacted by Chapters 262, 454, 455, 460, 461, 463, and 962 of the Acts of the
17 General Assembly of 2024)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
19 Article – Commercial Law
20 Section 13–301(14)(xlv)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

2 HOUSE BILL 194
1 (As enacted by Chapters 262, 454, 455, 460, 461, 463, and 962 of the Acts of the
2 General Assembly of 2024)
3 BY adding to
4 Article – Commercial Law
5 Section 13–301(14)(xlvi) and 14–1328
6 Annotated Code of Maryland
7 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
8 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
9 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
10 Article – Commercial Law
11 13–301.
12 Unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practices include any:
13 (14) Violation of a provision of:
14 (xliv) Title 14, Subtitle 49 of this article; [or]
15 (xlv) Section 12–6C–09.1 of the Health Occupations Article; or
16 (XLVI) SECTION 14–1328 OF THIS ARTICLE; OR
17 14–1328.
18 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
19 INDICATED.
20 (2) “CONSUMER” MEANS THE PURCHASER, OTHER THAN FOR
21 PURPOSES OF RESALE, OF CONSUMER GOODS, CONSUMER SERVICES, OR CONSUMER
22 CREDIT.
23 (3) “MERCHANT” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 13–101 OF THIS
24 ARTICLE.
25 (4) “RETAIL SALE” MEANS THE SALE OF CONSUMER GOODS BY A
26 MERCHANT TO A CONSUMER FOR:
27 (I) THE USE OR CONSUMPTION OF THE CONSUMER GOODS BY
28 THE CONSUMER; OR
29 (II) THE BENEFIT OR SATISFACTION THAT THE CONSUMER MAY
30 DERIVE FROM THE USE OR CONSUMPTION OF THE CONSUMER GOODS BY ANOTHER.
HOUSE BILL 194 3
1 (B) A CONSUMER MAY REQUEST THAT A MERCHANT PAY OR REFUND THE
2 FULL AMOUNT OF SHIPPING COSTS THE CONSUMER INCURS TO RETURN AN
3 INCORRECT ITEM THAT IS DAMAGED, FAULTY, OR SENT IN ERROR THAT THE
4 CONSUMER RECEIVES FROM THE MERCHANT IN CONNECTION WITH A RETAIL SALE.
5 (C) A MERCHANT THAT RECEIVES A PAYMENT OR REFUND REQUEST FROM
6 A CONSUMER UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION SHALL:
7 (1) PROVIDE THE CONSUMER A PREPAID SHIPPING LABEL AFTER
8 RECEIVING THE REFUND REQUEST; OR
9 (2) REFUND REFUND THE CONSUMER’S FULL INCURRED SHIPPING
10 COSTS TO THE CONSUMER WITHIN 30 DAYS AFTER RECEIVING THE REFUND
11 REQUEST RETURNED ITEM FROM THE CONSUMER IN ITS ORIGINAL CONDITION.
12 (D) A VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION IS:
13 (1) AN UNFAIR, ABUSIVE, OR DECEPTIVE TRADE PRACTICE WITHIN
14 THE MEANING OF TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE; AND
15 (2) SUBJECT TO THE ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTY PROVISIONS
16 CONTAINED IN TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE, EXCEPT § 13–411 OF THIS ARTICLE.
17 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
18 October 1, 2025.
Approved:
