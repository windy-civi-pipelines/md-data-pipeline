Assigned to: Economic Matters
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – Retail Sales – Reimbursement of Shipping Costs
3 FOR the purpose of requiring that a merchant provide a refund for shipping costs to a
4 consumer who received an incorrect item within a certain period of time after a
5 certain request is made; making a violation of this Act an unfair, abusive, or
6 deceptive trade practice that is subject to enforcement and penalties under the
7 Maryland Consumer Protection Act; and generally relating to consumer retail
8 transactions.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
10 Article – Commercial Law
11 Section 13–301(14)(xliv)
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 (As enacted by Chapters 262, 454, 455, 460, 461, 463, and 962 of the Acts of the
15 General Assembly of 2024)
16 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
17 Article – Commercial Law
18 Section 13–301(14)(xlv)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
21 (As enacted by Chapters 262, 454, 455, 460, 461, 463, and 962 of the Acts of the
22 General Assembly of 2024)
23 BY adding to
24 Article – Commercial Law
25 Section 13–301(14)(xlvi) and 14–1328
26 Annotated Code of Maryland
27 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

2 HOUSE BILL 194
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Commercial Law
4 13–301.
5 Unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practices include any:
6 (14) Violation of a provision of:
7 (xliv) Title 14, Subtitle 49 of this article; [or]
8 (xlv) Section 12–6C–09.1 of the Health Occupations Article; or
9 (XLVI) SECTION 14–1328 OF THIS ARTICLE; OR
10 14–1328.
11 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
12 INDICATED.
13 (2) “CONSUMER” MEANS THE PURCHASER, OTHER THAN FOR
14 PURPOSES OF RESALE, OF CONSUMER GOODS, CONSUMER SERVICES, OR CONSUMER
15 CREDIT.
16 (3) “MERCHANT” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 13–101 OF THIS
17 ARTICLE.
18 (4) “RETAIL SALE” MEANS THE SALE OF CONSUMER GOODS BY A
19 MERCHANT TO A CONSUMER FOR:
20 (I) THE USE OR CONSUMPTION OF THE CONSUMER GOODS BY
21 THE CONSUMER; OR
22 (II) THE BENEFIT OR SATISFACTION THAT THE CONSUMER MAY
23 DERIVE FROM THE USE OR CONSUMPTION OF THE CONSUMER GOODS BY ANOTHER.
24 (B) A CONSUMER MAY REQUEST THAT A MERCHANT REFUND THE FULL
25 AMOUNT OF SHIPPING COSTS THE CONSUMER INCURS TO RETURN AN INCORRECT
26 ITEM THAT THE CONSUMER RECEIVES FROM THE MERCHANT IN CONNECTION WITH
27 A RETAIL SALE.
HOUSE BILL 194 3
1 (C) A MERCHANT THAT RECEIVES A REFUND REQUEST FROM A CONSUMER
2 UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION SHALL REFUND THE CONSUMER’S FULL
3 INCURRED SHIPPING COSTS TO THE CONSUMER WITHIN 30 DAYS AFTER RECEIVING
4 THE REFUND REQUEST.
5 (D) A VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION IS:
6 (1) AN UNFAIR, ABUSIVE, OR DECEPTIVE TRADE PRACTICE WITHIN
7 THE MEANING OF TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE; AND
8 (2) SUBJECT TO THE ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTY PROVISIONS
9 CONTAINED IN TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE.
10 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
11 October 1, 2025.
