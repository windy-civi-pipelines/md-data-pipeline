A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Baltimore City – Office of the Sheriff – Neighborhood Services Unit
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Neighborhood Services Unit within the Office of the
4 Sheriff of Baltimore City; requiring the Neighborhood Services Unit and the Board
5 of License Commissioners of Baltimore City to work in partnership to enforce the
6 alcoholic beverages laws of Baltimore City and to enter into a memorandum of
7 understanding that specifies the respective roles and responsibilities of the parties
8 in connection with the partnership; requiring the Neighborhood Services Unit to
9 perform any other duties as directed by the Sheriff of Baltimore City; expanding the
10 authorized uses of certain revenue to include the work of the Neighborhood Services
11 Unit; and generally relating to the Office of the Sheriff of Baltimore City.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
13 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
14 Section 12–101(a) and (b)
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2024 Replacement Volume)
17 BY adding to
18 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
19 Section 12–2605
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2024 Replacement Volume)
22 BY adding to
23 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
24 Section 2–316.1
25 Annotated Code of Maryland
26 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
27 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,

28 1. For a surcharge assessed under item (i)1 of this
29 paragraph:
4 HOUSE BILL 1293
1 A. 45% into the Statewide Rental Assistance Voucher
2 Program established under § 4–2902 of the Housing and Community Development Article;
3 B. 45% into the Maryland Legal Services Corporation Fund
4 established under § 11–402 of the Human Services Article; and
5 C. 10% into the Rental Assistance for Community Schools
6 Families Fund established under § 9.9–104.1 of the Education Article; and
7 2. For a surcharge assessed under item (i)2 of this
8 paragraph, into the Maryland Legal Services Corporation Fund established under §
9 11–402 of the Human Services Article.
10 (3) (i) In addition to the surcharge assessed under paragraph (2) of this
11 subsection, the Chief Judge of the District Court shall assess a surcharge that may not be
12 more than $10 per case for the following cases filed in Baltimore City:
13 1. Summary ejectment;
14 2. Tenant holding over;
15 3. Breach of lease; and
16 4. Warrant of restitution.
17 (ii) The revenue generated from the surcharge on filing fees collected
18 by the District Court in Baltimore City under subparagraph (i) of this paragraph shall be:
19 1. Remitted quarterly to the Baltimore City Director of
20 Finance; and
21 2. Used to fund the enhancement of sheriff benefits [and],
22 the increase in sheriff personnel to enhance the service of domestic violence orders, AND
23 THE WORK OF THE NEIGHBORHOOD SERVICES UNIT WITHIN THE OFFICE OF THE
24 SHERIFF OF BALTIMORE CITY.
25 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
26 October 1, 2025.
