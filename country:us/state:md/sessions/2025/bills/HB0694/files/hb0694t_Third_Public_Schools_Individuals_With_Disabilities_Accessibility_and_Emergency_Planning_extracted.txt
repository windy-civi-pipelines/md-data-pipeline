CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Schools – Individuals With Disabilities – Main Entrance Accessibility and
3 Emergency Planning
4 FOR the purpose of requiring each county board of education to publish on its website and
5 report to the General Assembly on the number of main entrances to public school
6 buildings in the county that are not accessible for individuals with disabilities and
7 not in compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act; requiring each local
8 school system to send a copy of the local school system’s emergency plan to the State
9 Department of Education; requiring the Department to report certain information to
10 the General Assembly on or before a certain date each year; local school system to
11 identify in its regular safety evaluation issues of accessibility for individuals with
12 disabilities; requiring local school systems to include in a certain annual report
13 instances in which a public school facility became inaccessible for a student with a
14 disability in a manner that could impede evacuation or an emergency response; and
15 generally relating to school building main entrance accessibility and emergency
16 planning for individuals with disabilities.
17 BY adding to
18 Article – Education
19 Section 7–136
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

14 7–136.
15 ON OR BEFORE SEPTEMBER 1, 2025, AND EACH SEPTEMBER 1 THEREAFTER,
16 EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL PUBLISH ON ITS WEBSITE AND REPORT, IN
17 ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, TO THE
18 GENERAL ASSEMBLY ON THE NUMBER OF MAIN ENTRANCES TO PUBLIC SCHOOL
19 BUILDINGS IN THE COUNTY THAT ARE NOT ACCESSIBLE FOR INDIVIDUALS WITH
20 DISABILITIES AND NOT IN COMPLIANCE WITH THE FEDERAL AMERICANS WITH
21 DISABILITIES ACT.
22 7–435.
23 (a) On or before December 1, 2017, the Department shall, in consultation with
24 disability advocacy groups, update the Emergency Planning Guidelines for Local School
25 Systems and Schools to accommodate, safeguard, and evacuate students, staff, and visitors
26 with disabilities on public school grounds in accordance with the federal Americans with
27 Disabilities Act.
28 (b) (1) On or before July 1, 2018, each local school system shall update the local
29 school system’s emergency plan to comply with the update of the Department’s guidelines
30 and regulations under subsection (a) of this section.
31 (2) (I) ON OR BEFORE SEPTEMBER 1, 2025, AND EACH
32 SEPTEMBER 1 THEREAFTER, EACH LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL SEND A COPY OF
33 THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM’S EMERGENCY PLAN TO THE DEPARTMENT.
34 (II) ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 1, 2025, AND EACH DECEMBER
35 1 THEREAFTER, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL:

1 1. ANALYZE THE EMERGENCY PLANS RECEIVED UNDER
2 SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH; AND
3 2. REPORT TO THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY, IN
4 ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, ON WHETHER
5 EACH LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM IS IN COMPLIANCE WITH THE DEPARTMENT’S
6 GUIDELINES AND REGULATIONS UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION.
7 (c) If a student with an IEP requires specific accommodations for evacuation in
8 an emergency, the student’s IEP shall include accommodations for the student during an
9 emergency.
10 (d) If a student with a 504 plan requires specific accommodations for evacuation
11 in an emergency, the student’s 504 team shall discuss and address the student’s needs as
12 necessary.
13 7–1510.
14 (a) On or before June 15, 2019, and regularly thereafter, each local school system
15 shall conduct a safety evaluation of each public school under the local school system’s
16 jurisdiction to:
17 (1) Identify and, if necessary, develop solutions for physical safety
18 concerns, including issues with building security AND ASSOCIATED ISSUES OF
19 ACCESSIBILITY FOR INDIVIDUALS WITH DISABILITIES; and
20 (2) Identify and evaluate any patterns of safety concerns on school property
21 or at school–sponsored events.
22 (b) In performing the safety evaluations, each designated safety coordinator shall:
23 (1) Consult with the Center for guidance;
24 (2) Coordinate with the Interagency Commission on School Construction’s
25 facility assessment process, established under § 5–310 of this article in identifying issues
26 with public school facilities that could impact school safety; and
27 (3) Submit a summary of the completed safety evaluations to the Center.
28 (d) On or before August 1, 2020, and regularly thereafter, each local school system
29 shall update the school emergency plan for each public school in the school system’s
30 jurisdiction to:
31 (1) Include detailed plans for the manner in which each public school will
32 address:

1 (i) Behavioral threats;
2 (ii) Emergency events; and
3 (iii) Accommodations for students with disabilities in emergency
4 events;
5 (2) Conform with the Emergency Planning Guidelines updated under
6 subsection (c) of this section; and
7 (3) Incorporate any changes required under subsection (f) of this section.
8 (e) Each local school system shall submit the plans updated under subsection (d)
9 of this section to the Center for review and comment.
10 (f) (1) On or before August 1, 2020, and each August 1 thereafter, each local
11 school system shall submit a report to the Center that includes, for the immediately
12 preceding school year:
13 (i) Aggregate data about threats made against any school or school
14 system facility;
15 (ii) Information about any school lockdowns, evacuations, or other
16 emergency responses that occurred;
17 (III) INSTANCES IN WHICH A PUBLIC SCHOOL FACILITY BECAME
18 INACCESSIBLE FOR A STUDENT WITH A DISABILITY IN A MANNER THAT COULD
19 IMPEDE EVACUATION OR AN EMERGENCY RESPONSE;
20 [(iii)] (IV) Incidents in which a public school’s emergency plan failed
21 in part or in whole to function as anticipated in an emergency or an emergency drill; and
22 [(iv)] (V) School hours spent in an emergency or an emergency drill.
23 (2) Each local school system shall, in consultation with the Center, update
24 each emergency plan to correct weaknesses identified under paragraph (1) of this
25 subsection.
26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
27 1, 2025.
