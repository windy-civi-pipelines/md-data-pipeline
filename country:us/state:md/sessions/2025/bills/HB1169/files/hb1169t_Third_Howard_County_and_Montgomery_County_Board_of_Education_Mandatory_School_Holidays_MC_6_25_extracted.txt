4 MC 6–25
5 FOR the purpose of requiring the Howard County Board of Education and the Montgomery
6 County Board of Education to close public schools in the county to students to observe
7 certain holidays beginning in a certain school year; and generally relating to the
8 Howard County Board of Education and the Montgomery County Board of Education
9 and mandatory school holidays.

2 HOUSE BILL 1169
1 Article – Education
2 7–103.
3 (a) Except as provided in subsections (b), (e), and (f) of this section, each public
4 school under the jurisdiction of a county board:
5 (1) (i) Shall be open for pupil attendance for at least 180 actual school
6 days and a minimum of 1,080 school hours during a 10–month period in each school year;
7 or
8 (ii) If normal school attendance is prevented because of conditions
9 described in subsection (b) of this section, shall be open for at least 1,080 hours during a
10 10–month period;
11 (2) Shall be open for pupil attendance a minimum of 3 hours during each
12 school day; and
13 (3) May not be open on Saturdays, Sundays, or holidays in order to meet
14 the 180–day or 1,080–hour requirement of this subsection.
15 (c) (1) The following days are public school holidays:
16 (i) Thanksgiving Day and the day after;
17 (ii) Christmas Eve and from then through January 1;
18 (iii) Martin Luther King, Jr. Day;
19 (iv) Presidents’ Day;
20 (v) The Friday before Easter and from then through the Monday
21 after Easter;
22 (vi) Memorial Day; and
23 (vii) Primary and general election days.
24 (2) If the federal and State observances of a holiday are on different days,
25 the board of education of each county shall determine which date shall be the date of
26 observance for the public schools within the county.
27 (3) The public schools shall devote a part of at least one day to appropriate
28 exercises for each of the following:
29 (i) Washington’s Birthday;

1 (ii) Lincoln’s Birthday;
2 (iii) Veterans’ Day;
3 (iv) Columbus Day;
4 (v) Arbor Day;
5 (vi) Black History Month, with an emphasis on Harriet Tubman and
6 Frederick Douglass and the contributions they made in the fight against slavery; and
7 (vii) Any other day of national significance.
8 (4) Notwithstanding any other provisions of this article, the public schools,
9 in the following counties, may remain open and in session on primary and general election
10 days:
11 (i) Calvert;
12 (ii) Caroline;
13 (iii) Dorchester;
14 (iv) Kent;
15 (v) Talbot; and
16 (vi) Worcester.
17 (5) (I) THIS PARAGRAPH APPLIES ONLY IN HOWARD AND
18 MONTGOMERY COUNTY COUNTIES.
19 (II) THE BEGINNING IN THE 2026–2027 SCHOOL YEAR, THE
20 HOWARD COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION AND THE MONTGOMERY COUNTY BOARD
21 OF EDUCATION SHALL CLOSE PUBLIC SCHOOLS IN THE COUNTY, INCLUDING ALL
22 BUILDINGS AND OFFICES, FOR STUDENT ATTENDANCE ONLY TO OBSERVE THE
23 FOLLOWING HOLIDAYS:
24 1. IF ANY OF THE FOLLOWING HOLIDAYS BEGIN ON A
25 WEEKDAY, THE THE FIRST DAY ONLY OF:
