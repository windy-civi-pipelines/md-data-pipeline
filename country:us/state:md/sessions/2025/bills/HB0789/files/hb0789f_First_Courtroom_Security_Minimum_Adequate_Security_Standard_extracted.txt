Assigned to: Judiciary
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Courtroom Security – Minimum Adequate Security Standard
3 FOR the purpose of establishing minimum adequate security standards for courtroom
4 security at all courthouse facilities; and generally relating to courtroom security.
5 BY adding to
6 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
7 Section 1–206
8 Annotated Code of Maryland
9 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
10 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
11 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
12 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
13 1–206.
14 (A) THERE IS A MINIMUM ADEQUATE SECURITY STANDARD FOR
15 COURTROOM SECURITY AT ALL COURTHOUSE FACILITIES IN THE STATE.
16 (B) FOR PURPOSES OF THIS SECTION, A COURT SECURITY OFFICER MUST
17 MEET THE QUALIFICATIONS FOR A SPECIAL POLICE OFFICER, AS DEFINED IN §
18 3–301 OF THE PUBLIC SAFETY ARTICLE.
19 (C) BEGINNING JULY 1, 2026, THERE SHALL BE PRESENT IN EVERY
20 COURTROOM DURING PROCEEDINGS AT LEAST:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb0789*
2 HOUSE BILL 789
1 (1) ONE COURT SECURITY OFFICER FOR ANY CRIMINAL, FAMILY, OR
2 JUVENILE PROCEEDING;
3 (2) TWO COURT SECURITY OFFICERS FOR ANY PROCEEDING
4 INVOLVING AN INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL OR AN INDIVIDUAL WHO MAY BE
5 REMANDED TO CUSTODY AS A RESULT OF THE PROCEEDING; AND
6 (3) ONE ADDITIONAL COURT SECURITY OFFICER FOR EVERY FOUR OR
7 FEWER COURTROOMS ON EACH FLOOR OF A COURTHOUSE.
8 (D) BEGINNING JULY 1, 2028, THERE SHALL BE PRESENT IN EVERY
9 COURTROOM DURING PROCEEDINGS AT LEAST:
10 (1) ONE COURT SECURITY OFFICER FOR ANY PROCEEDING;
11 (2) TWO COURT SECURITY OFFICERS FOR ANY PROCEEDING
12 INVOLVING AN INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL OR AN INDIVIDUAL WHO MAY BE
13 REMANDED TO CUSTODY AS A RESULT OF THE PROCEEDING; AND
14 (3) ONE ADDITIONAL COURT SECURITY OFFICER FOR EVERY FOUR OR
15 FEWER COURTROOMS ON EACH FLOOR OF A COURTHOUSE.
16 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before September 30,
17 2025, each law enforcement agency, department, or entity providing security at a
18 courthouse facility shall submit a report to the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of
19 Maryland and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, the General
20 Assembly identifying:
21 (1) the current number of court security officers available to provide
22 courthouse security;
23 (2) the minimum number of court security officers necessary to meet the
24 minimum adequate security standards, accounting for annual leave and absences;
25 (3) the current number of courthouse security positions for which funding
26 has been allocated;
27 (4) the current number of vacancies in the agency, department, or entity;
28 (5) a viable plan to meet the minimum adequate security standards under
29 Section 1 of this Act; and
30 (6) any obstacles to the agency, department, or entity meeting those
31 minimum adequate security standards.
HOUSE BILL 789 3
1 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
2 1, 2025.
