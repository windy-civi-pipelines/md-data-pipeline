A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Safety – Immigration Enforcement
3 (Maryland Values Act)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring an employee or agent of a State or local correctional facility
5 and a law enforcement agent to detain a certain individual and transfer the
6 individual to federal immigration authorities under certain circumstances;
7 prohibiting the State, a unit of local government, a county sheriff, or any agency,
8 officer, employee, or agent of the State or a unit of local government from entering
9 into a certain immigration enforcement agreement; requiring the termination of an
10 existing immigration enforcement agreement on or before a certain date; and
11 generally relating to immigration enforcement.
12 BY adding to
13 Article – Correctional Services
14 Section 9–309
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2017 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
18 Article – Criminal Procedure
19 Section 5–104
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2018 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1222
1 BY adding to
2 Article – Criminal Procedure
3 Section 5–104.1
4 Annotated Code of Maryland
5 (2018 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
8 Article – Correctional Services
9 9–309.
10 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
11 INDICATED.
12 (2) “COVERED INDIVIDUAL” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL WHO:
13 (I) IS NOT LAWFULLY PRESENT IN THE UNITED STATES; AND
14 (II) HAS BEEN CONVICTED OF A CRIME OF VIOLENCE.
15 (3) “CRIME OF VIOLENCE” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 14–101 OF
16 THE CRIMINAL LAW ARTICLE.
17 (B) IF REQUESTED BY FEDERAL IMMIGRATION AUTHORITIES, AN
18 EMPLOYEE OR AGENT OF A STATE OR LOCAL CORRECTIONAL FACILITY SHALL:
19 (1) DETAIN A COVERED INDIVIDUAL FOR UP TO 48 HOURS IN ORDER
20 TO FACILITATE THE TRANSFER OF THE INDIVIDUAL TO FEDERAL IMMIGRATION
21 AUTHORITIES; AND
22 (2) TRANSFER A COVERED INDIVIDUAL TO FEDERAL IMMIGRATION
23 AUTHORITIES.
24 Article – Criminal Procedure
25 5–104.
26 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
27 (2) “Civil immigration violation” means a violation of federal civil
28 immigration law.
HOUSE BILL 1222 3
1 (3) “Family member” means a relative by blood, adoption, or marriage.
2 (4) “Household member” means a person who lives with another or is a
3 regular presence in the home of another.
4 (5) (i) “Law enforcement agent” means an individual who is certified by
5 the Maryland Police Training and Standards Commission under § 3–209 of the Public
6 Safety Article.
7 (ii) “Law enforcement agent” does not include an agent or employee
8 of a State correctional facility or a local correctional facility.
9 (6) “Local correctional facility” has the meaning stated in § 1–101 of the
10 Correctional Services Article.
11 (7) “State correctional facility” has the meaning stated in § 1–101 of the
12 Correctional Services Article.
13 (b) (1) In this subsection, “arrest” does not include a routine booking
14 procedure.
15 (2) Except as provided in paragraphs (3) [and], (4), AND (5) of this
16 subsection, a law enforcement agent may not, during the performance of regular police
17 functions:
18 (i) inquire about an individual’s citizenship, immigration status, or
19 place of birth during a stop, a search, or an arrest;
20 (ii) detain, or prolong the detention of, an individual:
21 1. for the purpose of investigating the individual’s
22 citizenship or immigration status; or
23 2. based on the suspicion that the individual has committed
24 a civil immigration violation;
25 (iii) transfer an individual to federal immigration authorities unless
26 required by federal law; or
27 (iv) coerce, intimidate, or threaten any individual based on the actual
28 or perceived citizenship or immigration status of the individual or:
29 1. the individual’s family member;
30 2. the individual’s household member;
31 3. the individual’s legal guardian; or
4 HOUSE BILL 1222
1 4. another individual for whom the individual is a legal
2 guardian.
3 (3) Nothing in this subsection shall prevent a law enforcement agent from
4 inquiring about any information that is material to a criminal investigation.
5 (4) If the citizenship or immigration status of an individual is relevant to a
6 protection accorded to the individual under State or federal law, or subject to a requirement
7 imposed by international treaty, a law enforcement agent may:
8 (i) notify the individual of the protection or requirement; and
9 (ii) provide the individual an opportunity to voluntarily disclose the
10 individual’s citizenship or immigration status for the purpose of receiving the protection or
11 complying with the requirement.
12 (5) (I) IN THIS PARAGRAPH, “COVERED INDIVIDUAL” HAS THE
13 MEANING STATED IN § 9–309 OF THE CORRECTIONAL SERVICES ARTICLE.
14 (II) IF REQUESTED BY FEDERAL IMMIGRATION AUTHORITIES, A
15 LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENT SHALL:
16 1. DETAIN A COVERED INDIVIDUAL FOR UP TO 48 HOURS
17 TO FACILITATE THE TRANSFER OF THE INDIVIDUAL TO FEDERAL IMMIGRATION
18 AUTHORITIES; AND
19 2. TRANSFER A COVERED INDIVIDUAL TO FEDERAL
20 IMMIGRATION AUTHORITIES.
21 5–104.1.
22 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION, “IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT AGREEMENT”
23 MEANS A CONTRACT, AN AGREEMENT, AN INTERGOVERNMENTAL SERVICE
24 AGREEMENT, OR A MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING WITH THE FEDERAL
25 GOVERNMENT THAT AUTHORIZES THE STATE, A UNIT OF LOCAL GOVERNMENT, A
26 COUNTY SHERIFF, OR ANY AGENCY, OFFICER, EMPLOYEE, OR AGENT OF THE STATE
27 OR A UNIT OF LOCAL GOVERNMENT TO ENFORCE CIVIL IMMIGRATION LAW.
28 (2) “IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT AGREEMENT” INCLUDES AN
29 AGREEMENT MADE IN ACCORDANCE WITH:
30 (I) 8 U.S.C. § 1103;
31 (II) 8 U.S.C. § 1357; OR
HOUSE BILL 1222 5
1 (III) ANY OTHER FEDERAL LAW.
2 (B) THE STATE, A UNIT OF LOCAL GOVERNMENT, A COUNTY SHERIFF, OR
3 ANY AGENCY, OFFICER, EMPLOYEE, OR AGENT OF THE STATE OR A UNIT OF LOCAL
4 GOVERNMENT MAY NOT ENTER INTO AN IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT AGREEMENT.
5 (C) THE STATE, A UNIT OF LOCAL GOVERNMENT, A COUNTY SHERIFF, OR
6 ANY AGENCY, OFFICER, EMPLOYEE, OR AGENT OF THE STATE OR A UNIT OF LOCAL
7 GOVERNMENT WITH AN EXISTING IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT AGREEMENT SHALL
8 EXERCISE THE TERMINATION PROVISION CONTAINED IN THE IMMIGRATION
9 ENFORCEMENT AGREEMENT NOT LATER THAN JULY 1, 2025.
10 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
11 1, 2025.
