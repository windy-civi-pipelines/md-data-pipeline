A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Safety – State Clearinghouse for Missing Persons
3 FOR the purpose of renaming and transferring the State Clearinghouse for Missing
4 Children to be the State Clearinghouse for Missing Persons; and generally relating
5 to the State Clearinghouse for Missing Persons.
6 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments, and transferring
7 Article – Family Law
8 Section 9–403
9 Annotated Code of Maryland
10 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
11 to be
12 Article – Public Safety
13 Section 3–609
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
16 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
17 That Section(s) 9–403 of Article – Family Law of the Annotated Code of Maryland be
18 repealed and reenacted, with amendments, and transferred to be Section(s) 3–609 of Article
19 – Public Safety of the Annotated Code of Maryland, to read as follows:
20 Article – Public Safety
21 3–609.
22 (a) There is a State Clearinghouse for Missing [Children] PERSONS operated by
23 the Department of State Police that is responsible for:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb1116*
2 HOUSE BILL 1116
1 (1) the receipt, collection, and distribution of general information and
2 annual statistics regarding missing [children] PERSONS; and
3 (2) coordination of law enforcement agencies and other interested
4 [persons] INDIVIDUALS or groups within and outside the State regarding information on
5 [children] PERSONS who have disappeared from, or are thought to be located in, Maryland.
6 (b) For [children] PERSONS who have disappeared from or are thought to be
7 located in the State, the State Clearinghouse for Missing [Children] PERSONS:
8 (1) shall publish:
9 (i) the names of and relevant available information on missing
10 [children] PERSONS; and
11 (ii) annual statistics regarding missing [children] PERSONS; and
12 (2) may establish and maintain a list of organizations and groups that
13 provide volunteer search teams or resources relating to missing [children] PERSONS.
14 (c) The Secretary of State Police may develop, in cooperation with local law
15 enforcement agencies, a plan for voluntary fingerprinting programs for children.
16 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
17 October 1, 2025.
