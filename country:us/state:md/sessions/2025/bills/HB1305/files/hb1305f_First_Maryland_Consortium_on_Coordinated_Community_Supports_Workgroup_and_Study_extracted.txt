A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Consortium on Coordinated Community Supports – Workgroup and
3 Study
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Consortium on Coordinated Community Supports to
5 convene a workgroup to study the effectiveness of the framework for community
6 supports partnerships in meeting the needs of local school systems and equitable
7 distribution of grant funding across the State; prohibiting the Consortium from
8 awarding grants in a certain fiscal year; and generally relating to a workgroup and
9 study and the Consortium on Coordinated Community Supports.
10 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
11 That:
12 (a) The Consortium on Coordinated Community Supports shall convene a
13 stakeholder workgroup to examine whether the framework established by the Consortium
14 for the creation of coordinated community supports partnerships for the awarding of grants
15 to providers for the provision of community behavioral health services in local school
16 systems is effective in granting awards to community providers that meet the needs
17 determined by a local school system and whether grant awards are distributed equitably
18 across the State.
19 (b) The workgroup shall consist of the following members:
20 (1) the Chair of the Consortium on Coordinated Community Supports;
21 (2) the State Superintendent of Schools, or the State Superintendent’s
22 designee;
23 (3) the Chair of the Accountability and Implementation Board, or the
24 Chair’s designee;

2 HOUSE BILL 1305
1 (4) one representative each from a small, midsized, and large local school
2 system; and
3 (5) any other members as determined by the Chair of the Consortium.
4 (c) The workgroup shall:
5 (1) analyze the effectiveness of the community supports partnerships pilot
6 hub model in matching community behavioral health service providers with the needs of
7 local school systems as determined by a local school system;
8 (2) examine reports of grantees on key performance measures to determine
9 whether grantees are providing services that have anticipated behavioral health outcomes;
10 (3) review existing requirements for provider and Consortium
11 collaboration with local school systems to determine whether the service needs of every
12 local school system are being acknowledged and considered in the awarding of grants; and
13 (4) review all the grants awarded by the Consortium in February 2024 to
14 determine whether the number and amount of grants awarded were equitably distributed
15 throughout the State.
16 (d) On or before December 1, 2025, the workgroup shall submit a report of its
17 findings and recommendations, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government
18 Article, to the General Assembly.
19 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
20 (a) The Consortium on Coordinated Community Supports may not award any
21 grant funding for fiscal year 2026 under § 7–447.1 of the Education Article.
22 (b) The Consortium may resume awarding grants for fiscal year 2027 if the report
23 of the workgroup under Section 1 of this Act finds that:
24 (1) the pilot hub model is an effective method for organizing community
25 supports partnerships because it successfully matches community behavioral health
26 service providers to the needs determined by local school systems; and
27 (2) the grants awarded by the Consortium are equitably distributed
28 throughout the State to meet the needs of all school children and their families in every
29 local school system.
30 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
31 1, 2025. It shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and, at the end of June 30, 2026, this
32 Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no
33 further force and effect.
