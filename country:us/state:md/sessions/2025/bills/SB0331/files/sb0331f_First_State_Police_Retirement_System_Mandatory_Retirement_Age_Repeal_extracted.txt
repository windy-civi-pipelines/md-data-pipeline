A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Police Retirement System – Mandatory Retirement Age – Repeal
3 FOR the purpose of repealing the requirement that certain members of the State Police
4 Retirement System retire at a certain age; clarifying the eligibility requirement of
5 State Police Retirement System members in the Deferred Retirement Option
6 Program (DROP); repealing the requirement that certain members of the State
7 Police Retirement System may participate in the DROP up to a certain age; and
8 generally relating to certain members of the State Police Retirement System.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
10 Article – State Personnel and Pensions
11 Section 24–401 and 24–401.1(a), (c), (d), and (g)
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
15 Article – State Personnel and Pensions
16 Section 24–401.1(b)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
21 Article – State Personnel and Pensions
22 24–401.
23 (a) (1) (i) This paragraph applies to an individual who is a member on or
24 before June 30, 2011.

1 (ii) A member may retire with a normal service retirement allowance
2 if:
3 1. on or before the date of retirement, the member:
4 A. has at least 22 years of eligibility service; or
5 B. is at least 50 years old; and
6 2. the member completes and submits a written application
7 to the Board of Trustees, on the form that the Board of Trustees provides, stating the date
8 when the member desires to retire.
9 (2) (i) This paragraph applies to an individual who becomes a member
10 on or after July 1, 2011.
11 (ii) A member may retire with a normal service retirement allowance
12 if:
13 1. on or before the date of retirement, the member:
14 A. has at least 25 years of eligibility service; or
15 B. is at least 50 years old; and
16 2. the member completes and submits a written application
17 to the Board of Trustees, on the form that the Board of Trustees provides, stating the date
18 when the member desires to retire.
19 (b) (1) Subject to the approval of the Board of Trustees, the Secretary of State
20 Police may order a member who is at least 50 years old to retire on the first day of the
21 month after the member is notified of the Secretary’s order.
22 (2) Before approving the Secretary’s order, the Board of Trustees shall give
23 the member at least 30 days’ notice and an opportunity to be heard.
24 (c) [Except for the Secretary of State Police, a member shall retire with a normal
25 service retirement allowance not later than the first day of the month after the member
26 becomes 60 years old.
27 (d)] (1) Except as provided in paragraphs (2) and (3) of this subsection, on
28 retirement under this section, a member is entitled to receive a normal service retirement
29 allowance that equals 2.55% of the member’s average final compensation multiplied by each
30 year of the member’s years of creditable service.
31 (2) A member’s normal service retirement allowance may not exceed 71.4%
32 of the member’s average final compensation.
