CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Election Law – State Administrator of Elections – Municipal Elections –
3 Authority of State Administrator of Elections and Use of State Ballots
4 Administration
5 FOR the purpose of authorizing the State Administrator of Elections to enter into
6 memoranda of understanding with municipalities municipal corporations that allow
7 the State Administrator to work collaboratively with a municipality municipal
8 corporation to support the administration of the municipality’s municipal
9 corporation’s elections; authorizing the State Administrator to lease a voting system
10 or other equipment used to administer an election to a municipal corporation for use
11 in a municipal election; repealing the authority of a local board of elections to lease
12 a voting system to a governmental or nongovernmental entity within the county;
13 altering the deadline for municipalities municipal corporations to request that the
14 State Board of Elections include on a ballot offices and questions to be voted on in a
15 municipal election and the certification that must be included in the request; and
16 generally relating to the administration of municipal elections.
17 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
18 Article – Election Law
19 Section 2–103(a)
20 Annotated Code of Maryland

14 Article – Local Government
15 4–108.3.
16 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
17 (2) “Ballot” means a ballot prepared by the State Board of Elections under
18 Title 9 of the Election Law Article.
19 (3) “State Board” means the State Board of Elections.
20 (b) A municipality may request that the State Board include on a ballot the offices
21 and questions to be voted on in a municipal election.
22 (c) (1) A municipality that makes a request under this section shall:
23 (i) file the request with the State Board on or before the day that is
24 [18] 9 months before the deadline date applicable for individuals who are required to file a
25 certificate of candidacy as required under § 5–303 of the Election Law Article; and
26 (ii) certify as part of the request that the charter of the municipality
27 requires, and the municipality has established, deadlines and procedures for the
28 administration of municipal elections for the municipality that are consistent with the
4 SENATE BILL 88
1 deadlines and procedures for State and county elections established by the State Board
2 with regard to:
3 1. the filing of certificates of candidacy;
4 2. the filling of a vacancy in office;
5 3. the filing of a petition; [and]
6 4. the certification of a ballot question;
7 5. VOTER REGISTRATION; AND
8 6. VOTING.
9 (2) (i) Except as provided in subparagraph (ii) of this paragraph, if the
10 State Board previously included a municipal election on the ballot, that municipality’s
11 elections may continue to appear on the ballot without the municipality filing an additional
12 request under this section.
13 (ii) A municipality shall file a request under this section if, since the
14 municipality’s election last appeared on the ballot, there has been a significant change in
15 the method the municipality uses to conduct its elections.
16 (3) Within 30 days after receipt of a municipality’s request under this
17 section, the State Board, after consultation with the local board in the county where the
18 municipality is located, shall notify the municipality of its decision whether to include the
19 municipal election on the ballot.
20 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act is an emergency
21 measure, is necessary for the immediate preservation of the public health or safety, has
22 been passed by a yea and nay vote supported by three–fifths of all the members elected to
23 each of the two Houses of the General Assembly, and shall take effect from the date it is
24 enacted.
