Q3 5lr1807
By: Delegates Ziegler, Allen, Crosby, Guyton, Hill, Hinebaugh, Kerr, T. Morgan,
Ruth, and Schindler
Introduced and read first time: January 20, 2025
Assigned to: Ways and Means
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Income Tax Credit – Venison Donation – Alterations
3 FOR the purpose of repealing the limitation on the maximum amount of a credit against
4 the State income tax that an individual may claim for certain expenses incurred for
5 a donation of deer meat to certain organizations; and generally relating to an income
6 tax credit for the donation of deer meat.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – Tax – General
9 Section 10–746
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Tax – General
15 10–746.
16 (a) In this section, “qualified expenses” means expenses incurred to butcher and
17 process an antlerless deer for human consumption.
18 (b) Subject to the limitations of this section, an individual who hunts and harvests
19 an antlerless deer may claim a credit against the State income tax for up to $75 of the
20 qualified expenses incurred by the individual if:
21 (1) the hunting and harvesting of the deer complies with State hunting
22 laws and regulations; and
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 483
1 (2) the individual donates the processed deer meat to a venison donation
2 program administered by an organization that is exempt from taxation under § 501(c)(3) of
3 the Internal Revenue Code.
4 (c) [(1) For any taxable year, the total amount of credits that an individual may
5 claim under this section may not exceed $300, unless the individual harvested each deer
6 for which the credits are claimed in accordance with a deer management permit.
7 (2)] The unused amount of the credit for any taxable year may not be carried
8 over to any other taxable year.
9 (d) An individual who claims the credit under this section shall have the
10 immunity from liability described under § 5–634 of the Courts Article for donated food.
11 (e) On or before January 31 each year, each venison donation program that
12 accepts a donation under this section shall report to the Comptroller the following
13 information for the immediately preceding taxable year:
14 (1) the name and home address of each donor who intended to claim the
15 income tax credit under this section; and
16 (2) the number of deer donated by each donor who intended to claim the
17 income tax credit under this section.
18 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
19 1, 2025, and shall be applicable to all taxable years beginning after December 31, 2024. It
20 shall remain effective until the taking effect of the termination provision specified in
21 Section 2 of Chapters 769 and 770 of the Acts of the General Assembly of 2024. If that
22 termination provision takes effect, this Act, with no further action required by the General
23 Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no further force and effect. This Act may not be
24 interpreted to have any effect on that termination provision.
