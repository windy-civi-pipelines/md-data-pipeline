A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Schools – Venue–Specific Emergency Action Plans – High School Football
3 Games
4 FOR the purpose of altering the requirements of a venue–specific emergency action plan to
5 include the on–site presence of a licensed athletic trainer or an ambulance at each
6 high school football game; and generally relating to venue–specific emergency action
7 plan requirements.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Education
10 Section 7–451
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Education
16 7–451.
17 (a) (1) Each middle school and high school shall develop a venue–specific
18 emergency action plan for all athletic facilities.
19 (2) A venue–specific emergency action plan includes:
20 (i) The operation and use of automatic external defibrillators in
21 accordance with § 7–425 of this subtitle;
22 (ii) Heat acclimatization in accordance with § 7–434 of this subtitle;

1 (iii) Coordination of care for other emergent injuries, including
2 cervical spinal injury, concussion and closed head injury, major orthopedic injuries, and
3 severe weather for outdoor facilities; [and]
4 (IV) FOR HIGH SCHOOL FOOTBALL PROGRAMS ONLY, THE
5 PRESENCE OF ONE OF THE FOLLOWING ON–SITE DURING A GAME:
6 1. AN ATHLETIC TRAINER LICENSED UNDER TITLE 14,
7 SUBTITLE 5D OF THE HEALTH OCCUPATIONS ARTICLE; OR
8 2. AN AMBULANCE STAFFED WITH THE APPROPRIATE
9 EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES PERSONNEL; AND
10 [(iv)] (V) A cardiac emergency response plan in accordance with
11 subsection (e) of this section.
12 (b) A venue–specific emergency action plan developed under this section shall be:
13 (1) Posted at each athletic facility at the school;
14 (2) Available on the school’s website for viewing by a student athlete’s
15 parent or guardian at the beginning of each school year;
16 (3) Distributed to each member of the coaching staff; and
17 (4) Rehearsed in person and interactively by all of the coaching staff of each
18 sport before each of their respective seasons.
19 (c) The provisions of a venue–specific emergency action plan for the operation and
20 use of automatic external defibrillators shall ensure that:
21 (1) An automated external defibrillator is provided on–site and is located
22 at or within a brief walk from an athletic practice or event on school property;
23 (2) An automated external defibrillator is freely accessible during all school
24 functions;
25 (3) All members of the coaching staff are trained in the operation and use
26 of an automated external defibrillator; and
27 (4) A trained member of the coaching staff is present at all athletic
28 practices and events on school property.
29 (d) The provisions of a venue–specific emergency action plan for heat
30 acclimatization shall:
HOUSE BILL 935 3
1 (1) Include guidelines for student athletes consistent with the Model Policy
2 for Preseason–Practice Heat Acclimatization Guidelines for Student Athletes established
3 under § 7–434 of this subtitle;
4 (2) Ensure that each school is properly prepared and equipped to initiate
5 cold water immersion for the treatment of exertional heat stroke;
6 (3) Ensure that the cooling modality is readily available to student athletes
7 at all athletic practices and events on school property;
8 (4) Ensure that all members of the coaching staff are trained in cold water
9 immersion; and
10 (5) Ensure that a trained member of the coaching staff is present at all
11 athletic practices and events on school property.
12 (e) (1) Each venue–specific emergency action plan shall include a cardiac
13 emergency response plan.
14 (2) The cardiac emergency response plan shall:
15 (i) Identify the appropriate school personnel to serve as the cardiac
16 emergency response team who will be tasked with responding to incidents involving an
17 individual experiencing sudden cardiac arrest or a similar life–threatening emergency
18 while at an athletic facility;
19 (ii) Coordinate with first responders in the local emergency medical
20 services to integrate the cardiac emergency action plan into local emergency response
21 protocols; and
22 (iii) Include, at minimum, the following evidence–based core
23 elements recommended by an appropriate national organization, including the American
24 Heart Association:
25 1. Procedures for activating the cardiac emergency response
26 team during incidents involving an individual experiencing sudden cardiac arrest or a
27 similar life–threatening emergency while at an athletic facility; and
28 2. Requiring specified school personnel to have training in
29 first aid, cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR), and the operation and use of automated
30 external defibrillators, in accordance with § 7–425 of this subtitle, including cardiac
31 emergency response team members, athletic coaching and training staff, school nurses, and
32 any other individuals identified by the cardiac emergency response team.
33 (3) Each year, each middle school and high school shall review and update
34 the cardiac emergency response plan required under this subsection.
4 HOUSE BILL 935
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
2 1, 2025.
