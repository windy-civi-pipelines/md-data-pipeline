A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Election Law – Local Boards of Elections – Election Plan Requirements
3 FOR the purpose of requiring a local board of elections to submit an election plan to the
4 State Board of Elections for approval at least a certain number of months before each
5 statewide primary election; establishing requirements related to an election plan;
6 altering the requirements related to polling place plans, ballot drop box plans, and
7 early voting center plans; and generally relating to local boards of elections and
8 election plans.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
10 Article – Election Law
11 Section 2–303.1, 2–304, and 10–301.1(d) through (h)
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 BY repealing
15 Article – Election Law
16 Section 10–301.1(c)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
21 Article – Election Law
22 2–303.1.

10 (a) (1) EACH LOCAL BOARD SHALL DEVELOP A BALLOT DROP BOX PLAN
11 THAT INCLUDES THE PROPOSED LOCATION OF EACH BALLOT DROP BOX IN THE
12 COUNTY.
13 (2) A local board shall consider the following factors when determining the
14 location of a ballot drop box:
15 [(1)] (I) the accessibility of the ballot drop box to historically
16 disenfranchised communities, including voters with disabilities, cultural groups, ethnic
17 groups, and minority groups;
18 [(2)] (II) proximity of the ballot drop box to dense concentrations of voters;
19 [(3)] (III) accessibility of the ballot drop box by public transportation; AND
20 [(4)] (IV) equitable GEOGRAPHIC distribution of ballot drop boxes
21 throughout the county[; and
22 (5) maximizing voter participation, including through placement of ballot
23 drop boxes at community centers and public gathering places].
24 (b) Subject to subsection (c) of this section, a local board shall designate locations
25 in the county at which a ballot drop box will be placed in accordance with the factors set
26 forth in subsection (a) of this section.
27 (c) (1) Each local board shall submit the BALLOT DROP BOX PLAN WITH THE
28 proposed locations in the county for each ballot drop box to the State Administrator for
29 approval.
30 (2) The State Administrator shall approve a ballot drop box location that
31 meets the factors established under subsection (a) of this section.

1 (3) If a proposed ballot drop box [location] PLAN does not meet the factors
2 LISTED IN SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION, BEFORE THE LOCAL BOARD’S
3 ELECTION PLAN IS SUBMITTED TO THE STATE BOARD FOR APPROVAL UNDER §
4 2–303.1 OF THIS SUBTITLE, the State Administrator shall:
5 (i) require that the local board reconsider the proposed location for
6 one or more ballot drop boxes; and
7 (ii) provide to the local board a detailed explanation of why the
8 proposed location does not meet the factors.
9 (4) If the State Administrator finds that the proposed ballot drop box
10 locations submitted to the State Administrator after reconsideration by the local board
11 under paragraph (3) of this subsection do not meet the factors, the State Administrator may
12 add ballot drop box locations in the county.

14 [(c) (1) No later than 6 months before a primary election, the local board in
15 each county shall, subject to the approval of the State Board, designate each early voting
16 center in that county.
17 (2) A local board shall take into account the following factors when
18 determining the location of an early voting center:
19 (i) accessibility of the early voting center to historically
20 disenfranchised communities, including cultural groups, ethnic groups, and minority
21 groups;
22 (ii) proximity of the early voting center to dense concentrations of
23 voters;
24 (iii) accessibility of the early voting center by public transportation;
25 (iv) ensuring equitable distribution of early voting centers
26 throughout the county; and
27 (v) maximizing voter participation, including through the use of
28 community centers and public gathering places as locations for early voting centers.]
29 [(d)] (C) Each early voting center shall be open for voting as follows:
30 (1) beginning the second Thursday before a primary or general election
31 through the Thursday before the election; and
32 (2) during the hours between 7 a.m. and 8 p.m. each early voting day.
6 HOUSE BILL 274
1 [(e)] (D) (1) Each early voting center shall satisfy the requirements of §
2 10–101 of this title.
3 (2) Subject to paragraph (3) of this subsection, the public official
4 responsible for the use of a public building requested by a local board for an early voting
5 center shall make the public building available to the local board in accordance with §
6 10–101(a)(3) of this title.
7 (3) (i) The State Board shall adopt regulations that establish an
8 appeals process for public officials responsible for public buildings requested for use under
9 paragraph (2) of this subsection to contest the availability of the public building during the
10 period of time the building would be used as an early voting center.
11 (ii) The appeals process established under this paragraph shall
12 include:
13 1. an initial appeal to the local board that selected the public
14 building for use as an early voting center;
15 2. an opportunity for a public official to be heard publicly at
16 a meeting of the local board before the local board makes a decision on the appeal;
17 3. an appeal by a public official of an adverse decision of the
18 local board to the State Board;
19 4. an opportunity for a public official to be heard publicly at
20 a meeting of the State Board before the State Board makes a final decision on the appeal;
21 and
22 5. a final decision at least 8 months before the primary
23 election for which the local board seeks to use the public building as an early voting center.
24 (iii) The decision of the State Board regarding an appeal under this
25 paragraph is final.
26 [(f)] (E) Beginning 30 days prior to each early voting period the State Board and
27 each local board shall undertake steps to inform the public about early voting and the
28 location of early voting centers in each county, including:
29 (1) a series of public service media announcements;
30 (2) mailings to all registered voters in each county; and
31 (3) other measures as appropriate.

1 [(g)] (F) Except as expressly provided in this section, any provision of this article
2 that applies to voting on election day also applies to early voting.
3 [(h)] (G) The State Board shall adopt regulations and guidelines in accordance
4 with the requirements of this section for the conduct of early voting.
5 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
6 1, 2025.
