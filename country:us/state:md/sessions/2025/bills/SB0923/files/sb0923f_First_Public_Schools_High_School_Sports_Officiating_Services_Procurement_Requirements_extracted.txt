A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Schools – High School Sports Officiating Services – Procurement
3 Requirements
4 FOR the purpose of establishing requirements for local school systems that issue
5 invitations for bids for high school sports officiating services for boys’ and girls’ high
6 school sports; and generally relating to procurement requirements for high school
7 sports officiating services.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Education
10 Section 4–147
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Education
16 4–147.
17 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
18 INDICATED.
19 (2) “ASSIGNING FEES” MEANS THE FEES CHARGED BY AN ASSIGNOR.
20 (3) “ASSIGNOR” MEANS AN OFFICIATING ASSOCIATION OR
21 ORGANIZATION OR AN INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR THAT:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 SENATE BILL 923
1 (I) IS REGISTERED AND IN GOOD STANDING WITH THE STATE
2 DEPARTMENT OF ASSESSMENTS AND TAXATION; AND
3 (II) PROVIDES, ASSIGNS, AND SCHEDULES THE OFFICIALS THAT
4 PROVIDE THE HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS OFFICIATING SERVICES FOR A HIGH SCHOOL
5 SPORTING EVENT.
6 (4) “HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS OFFICIATING SERVICES” MEANS THE
7 OFFICIATING SERVICES PROVIDED BY AN OFFICIAL, AN UMPIRE, A REFEREE, OR A
8 JUDGE AT A HIGH SCHOOL SPORTING EVENT.
9 (5) “SIMILAR BOYS’ AND GIRLS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS” MEANS HIGH
10 SCHOOL SPORTS THAT:
11 (I) OCCUR IN THE SAME SEASON;
12 (II) HAVE COMPARABLE RULES;
13 (III) HAVE A SIMILAR NUMBER OF HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS
14 OFFICIALS; AND
15 (IV) HAVE A SIMILAR TIME OF COMPETITION.
16 (B) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL ISSUE INVITATIONS FOR BIDS OR
17 REQUESTS FOR PROPOSALS FOR SIMILAR BOYS’ AND GIRLS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS
18 OFFICIATING SERVICES SIMULTANEOUSLY.
19 (C) (1) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY ISSUE AN INVITATION FOR BIDS OR
20 A REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS THAT REQUIRES A PROSPECTIVE BIDDER OR OFFEROR
21 TO INCLUDE IN A BID OR AN OFFER BOTH BOYS’ AND GIRLS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS
22 OFFICIATING SERVICES.
23 (2) IF A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM ISSUES SEPARATE INVITATIONS FOR
24 BIDS OR REQUESTS FOR PROPOSALS FOR BOYS’ AND GIRLS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS
25 OFFICIATING SERVICES, THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM:
26 (I) MAY NOT SET SEPARATE DEADLINES FOR THE SUBMISSION
27 OF BIDS AND OFFERS FOR BOYS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS OFFICIATING SERVICES AND
28 GIRLS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS OFFICIATING SERVICES; AND
29 (II) MAY REQUIRE THAT PROSPECTIVE BIDDERS OR OFFERORS
30 PARTNER TO ENSURE THAT BIDS AND OFFERS FOR BOYS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS
SENATE BILL 923 3
1 OFFICIATING SERVICES ARE ACCOMPANIED BY BIDS AND OFFERS FOR GIRLS’ HIGH
2 SCHOOL SPORTS OFFICIATING SERVICES.
3 (D) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL REJECT A BID OR PROPOSAL IF
4 ENTERING INTO A PROCUREMENT CONTRACT BASED ON THE BID OR PROPOSAL
5 WOULD RESULT, FOR SIMILAR BOYS’ AND GIRLS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS, IN THE RATE
6 OF PAY OR ASSIGNING FEES RECEIVED BY INDIVIDUALS OFFICIATING GIRLS’ HIGH
7 SCHOOL SPORTS OR ASSIGNING GIRLS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS OFFICIALS BEING
8 DIFFERENT FROM THE RATE OF PAY OR ASSIGNING FEES FOR INDIVIDUALS
9 OFFICIATING BOYS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS OR ASSIGNING BOYS’ HIGH SCHOOL
10 SPORTS OFFICIALS.
11 (E) THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO PROHIBIT THE PAYMENT OF
12 ASSIGNING FEES AS A BONUS THAT IS PAID OUTSIDE A PROCUREMENT CONTRACT.
13 (F) THE DEPARTMENT MAY DEVELOP GUIDELINES TO ASSIST A LOCAL
14 SCHOOL SYSTEM IN THE IMPLEMENTATION OF THE PROVISIONS OF THIS SECTION,
15 INCLUDING ADDITIONAL CRITERIA FOR DETERMINING WHICH HIGH SCHOOL
16 SPORTS QUALIFY AS SIMILAR BOYS’ AND GIRLS’ HIGH SCHOOL SPORTS.
17 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
18 (a) A local school system that executed a procurement contract for high school
19 sports officiating services, as defined in § 4–147 of the Education Article, as enacted by
20 Section 1 of this Act, on or before July 1, 2025, may extend the term of the contract to align
21 the contract timelines for similar boys’ and girls’ high school sports.
22 (b) A local school system may not extend a contract under subsection (a) of this
23 section beyond July 1, 2029.
24 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, except for a contract for high
25 school sports officials extended by a local school system in accordance with Section 2 of this
26 Act, this Act shall be construed to apply only prospectively and may not be applied or
27 interpreted to have any effect on or application to any procurement contracts for high school
28 sports officiating services, as defined in § 4–147 of the Education Article, as enacted under
29 Section 1 of this Act, entered into before the effective date of this Act.
30 SECTION 4. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
31 1, 2025.
