CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Board of Dental Examiners – Expanded Function Dental Assistants –
3 Education and Reinstatement
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the State Board of Dental Examiners to approve a certain
5 course of study for expanded function dental assistant certification; requiring that a
6 course of study for initial dental assistant certification include an option for an a
7 certain number of hours of in–person and online course of study when appropriate
8 instruction; requiring the Board to reinstate an expired expanded function dental
9 assistant certification only under certain circumstances; requiring the Board to
10 adopt certain regulations relating to continuing education for expanded function
11 dental assistants; and generally relating to expanded function dental assistants.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Health Occupations
14 Section 4–302.1
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 BY adding to
18 Article – Health Occupations
19 Section 4–311.1
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0792*
2 SENATE BILL 792
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Health Occupations
4 4–302.1.
5 (a) To qualify for a certificate to be an expanded function dental assistant, an
6 applicant shall be an individual who meets the requirements of this section.
7 (b) The applicant must be of good moral character.
8 (c) The applicant must be at least 18 years old.
9 (d) The applicant must:
10 (1) Be a high school graduate or have attained a high school equivalency;
11 (2) Have successfully completed a course of study approved, subject to
12 subsection (e) of this section, by the Board related to the intraoral procedure for which the
13 applicant has applied for a certificate;
14 (3) Have the appropriate certification from the Dental Assisting National
15 Board;
16 (4) Have passed any examination provided for in a regulation adopted by
17 the Board; and
18 (5) Submit to a State criminal history records check.
19 (e) The Board may not approve a course of study under subsection (d)(2) of this
20 section unless the course of study is part of:
21 (1) A U.S. Department of Labor Job Corps dental assisting program;
22 (2) A dental assisting program located within an institution of
23 postsecondary education that is accredited by an accrediting institution recognized by the
24 U.S. Department of Education;
25 (3) A dental assisting program that is located within a high school and is
26 recognized by an accrediting body recognized by the U.S. Department of Education or the
27 high school accrediting body of a state; [or]
28 (4) A dental assisting program that is accredited by the Council on Dental
29 Accreditation, or a successor organization;
SENATE BILL 792 3
1 (5) A DENTAL ASSISTING COURSE OR PROGRAM OFFERED BY THE
2 DALE FOUNDATION, AN AFFILIATE OF THE DENTAL ASSISTING NATIONAL BOARD,
3 OR ANY SUCCESSOR ENTITY; OR
4 (6) AN ORTHODONTIC DENTAL ASSISTING COURSE OR PROGRAM
5 OFFERED BY THE TRAPEZIO ORTHODONTIC ASSISTANT TRAINING SOLUTIONS, OR
6 ANY SUCCESSOR ENTITY.
7 (F) (1) A COURSE OF STUDY FOR INITIAL CERTIFICATION UNDER THIS
8 SECTION SHALL INCLUDE, WHEN APPROPRIATE, AN OPTION FOR AN ONLINE COURSE
9 OF STUDY AT LEAST 35 HOURS OF INSTRUCTION CONSISTING OF BOTH ONLINE AND
10 IN–PERSON INSTRUCTION.
11 (2) THE BOARD SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS SPECIFYING THE
12 REQUIRED NUMBER OF HOURS FOR IN–PERSON INSTRUCTION, ONLINE
13 INSTRUCTION, AND CLINICAL HANDS–ON TRAINING.
14 4–311.1.
15 (A) THE BOARD SHALL REINSTATE AN EXPANDED FUNCTION DENTAL
16 ASSISTANT CERTIFICATE THAT IS EXPIRED ONLY IF THE HOLDER:
17 (1) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, HAS COMPLETED
18 THE CONTINUING EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS SET BY RULE AND REGULATION OF
19 THE BOARD;
20 (2) HAS SUBMITTED A RENEWAL APPLICATION TO THE BOARD
21 BEFORE THE DEADLINE SET BY THE BOARD; AND
22 (3) PAYS TO THE BOARD A RENEWAL FEE SET BY THE BOARD.
23 (B) THE BOARD SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS REQUIRING CONTINUING
24 EDUCATION, INCLUDING CLINICAL HANDS–ON TRAINING, FOR EXPANDED FUNCTION
25 DENTAL ASSISTANTS TO:
26 (1) INCLUDE AN OPTION FOR AN ONLINE COURSE OF STUDY; AND
27 (2) BE BE OFFERED BY:
28 (I) (1) DENTAL STATE–BASED DENTAL PROFESSIONAL SOCIETIES
29 AFFILIATED WITH NATIONAL DENTAL ORGANIZATIONS; AND
30 (II) (2) ENTITIES LISTED IN § 4–302.1(E) OF THIS SUBTITLE.
4 SENATE BILL 792
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
2 October 1, 2025.
Approved:
