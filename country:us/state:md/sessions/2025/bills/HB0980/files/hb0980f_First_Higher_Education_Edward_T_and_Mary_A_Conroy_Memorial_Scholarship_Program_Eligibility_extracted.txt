A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Higher Education – Edward T. and Mary A. Conroy Memorial Scholarship
3 Program – Eligibility
4 FOR the purpose of expanding the eligibility for the Edward T. and Mary A. Conroy
5 Memorial Scholarship Program to include certain individuals; authorizing certain
6 methods for verifying eligibility for the Program; and generally relating to the
7 Edward T. and Mary A. Conroy Memorial Scholarship Program.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
9 Article – Education
10 Section 18–601(c)(1)
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Education
15 Section 18–601(d)(2) and (f)(1)
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
20 Article – Education
21 18–601.
22 (c) (1) The program for military and public safety personnel and their eligible
23 dependents is the Edward T. and Mary A. Conroy Memorial Scholarship Program.

1 (d) (2) To apply for a scholarship under this section, the following persons are
2 required to be a resident of Maryland at the time of application or at the time of the
3 disabling or fatal event:
4 (i) A son, daughter, stepson, stepdaughter, or the surviving spouse
5 of a member of the armed forces who:
6 1. Died as a result of military service after December 7, 1941;
7 2. Suffered a service connected 100% permanent disability
8 after December 7, 1941; or
9 3. Was declared to be a prisoner of war or missing in action,
10 if that occurred on or after January 1, 1960, as a result of the Vietnam conflict, and if the
11 child was born prior to or while the parent was a prisoner of war or missing in action;

13 January 1, 1960, as a result of the Vietnam conflict and was a resident of this State at the
14 time the person was declared to be a prisoner of war or missing in action;
15 (iii) A veteran, as defined under § 9–901 of the State Government
16 Article, who:
17 1. Suffers a service connected disability of 25% or greater;

HOUSE BILL 980 3
1 (VII) A SON, DAUGHTER, OR STEPCHILD OR THE SURVIVING
2 SPOUSE OF A MEMBER OF THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED STATES WHO, AS A
3 RESULT OF THE VETERAN’S DISABILITY RATING, IS OR WAS ELIGIBLE, AT THE TIME
4 OF THE APPLICATION, FOR THE CHAPTER 35 SURVIVORS’ DEPENDENTS’
5 EDUCATIONAL ASSISTANCE BENEFIT, AS DETERMINED BY THE U.S. DEPARTMENT
6 OF VETERANS AFFAIRS.
7 (f) (1) (I) Each postsecondary institution shall determine the eligibility of
8 persons who apply to the institution for the Edward T. Conroy Memorial Scholarship
9 Program and the Jean B. Cryor Memorial Scholarship Program.
10 (II) TO DETERMINE THE ELIGIBILITY OF PERSONS WHO APPLY
11 TO A POSTSECONDARY INSTITUTION FOR THE EDWARD T. AND MARY A. CONROY
12 MEMORIAL SCHOLARSHIP PROGRAM, AN APPLICANT MAY SUBMIT A COPY OF THE
13 APPLICANT’S CERTIFICATE OF ELIGIBILITY FOR THE CHAPTER 35 SURVIVORS’ AND
14 DEPENDENTS’ EDUCATIONAL ASSISTANCE BENEFIT.
15 (III) IF AN APPLICANT IS UNABLE TO OBTAIN A CERTIFICATE OF
16 ELIGIBILITY UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS PARAGRAPH, A SCHOOL
17 CERTIFYING OFFICIAL OR AN INSTITUTIONAL LIAISON MAY VERIFY THAT THE
18 APPLICANT IS OR WAS ELIGIBLE FOR THE CHAPTER 35 SURVIVORS’ AND
19 DEPENDENTS’ EDUCATIONAL ASSISTANCE BENEFIT.
20 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
21 1, 2025.
