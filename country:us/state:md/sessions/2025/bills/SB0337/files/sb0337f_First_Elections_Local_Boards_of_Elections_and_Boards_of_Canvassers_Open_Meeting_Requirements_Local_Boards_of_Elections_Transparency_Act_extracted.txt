A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Elections – Local Boards of Elections and Boards of Canvassers – Open Meeting
3 Requirements
4 (Local Boards of Elections Transparency Act)
5 FOR the purpose of establishing enhanced open meeting requirements for each local board
6 of elections and each local board of canvassers; and generally relating to open
7 meeting requirements for local boards of elections and boards of canvassers.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Election Law
10 Section 2–208
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Election Law
15 Section 11–301, 11–302(a), and 11–303(a)
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
20 Article – Election Law
21 2–208.
22 (A) EACH LOCAL BOARD SHALL MAKE PUBLICLY AVAILABLE ON ITS
23 WEBSITE EACH OPEN MEETING AGENDA, TOGETHER WITH A SUMMARY OF ANY
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 SENATE BILL 337
1 FINALIZED DOCUMENTS, WRITTEN TESTIMONY FROM THE PUBLIC, AND OTHER
2 MATERIALS THAT THE LOCAL BOARD WILL VOTE ON AT THE OPEN MEETING:
3 (1) AT LEAST 48 HOURS IN ADVANCE OF EACH MEETING; OR
4 (2) IF THE MEETING IS BEING HELD DUE TO AN EMERGENCY, A
5 NATURAL DISASTER, OR ANY OTHER UNANTICIPATED SITUATION, AS FAR IN
6 ADVANCE OF THE MEETING AS PRACTICABLE.
7 (B) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION,
8 EACH LOCAL BOARD SHALL PROVIDE LIVE VIDEO STREAMING OF EACH OPEN
9 MEETING HELD BY THE LOCAL BOARD.
10 (2) THE LIVE STREAMING VIDEO REQUIREMENT UNDER PARAGRAPH
11 (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION DOES NOT APPLY WITH RESPECT TO:
12 (I) AN OPEN MEETING HELD OFF–SITE;
13 (II) A PROJECT SITE VISIT; OR
14 (III) AN INSPECTION CONDUCTED BY THE LOCAL BOARD.
15 (C) EACH LOCAL BOARD SHALL MAINTAIN A COMPLETE AND UNEDITED
16 ARCHIVED VIDEO RECORDING OF EACH OPEN MEETING FOR WHICH LIVE VIDEO
17 STREAMING WAS PROVIDED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION FOR A
18 MINIMUM OF 5 YEARS AFTER THE DATE OF THE MEETING.
19 11–301.
20 (a) A board of canvassers shall:
21 (1) convene at the designated counting center for that county on or before
22 the day of the election, but not later than 5 p.m. on that day, and be sworn in; and
23 (2) meet only in public session.
24 (B) A BOARD OF CANVASSERS AND THE STAFF OF A LOCAL BOARD SHALL:
25 (1) PROVIDE LIVE VIDEO STREAMING OF THE CANVASS; AND
26 (2) MAINTAIN A COMPLETE AND UNEDITED ARCHIVED VIDEO
27 RECORDING OF THE CANVASS FOR A MINIMUM OF 5 YEARS AFTER THE DATE OF THE
28 COMPLETION OF THE CANVASS.
SENATE BILL 337 3
1 [(a–1)] (C) (1) Subject to paragraph (3) of this subsection, a board of canvassers
2 and the staff of a local board may be observed IN PERSON as they complete each part of
3 the canvass by authorized observers designated under paragraph (2) of this subsection and
4 any other individuals who wish to be present.
5 (2) The following individuals or entities have the right to designate a
6 registered voter as an observer at each counting center:
7 (i) a candidate;
8 (ii) a political party; and
9 (iii) any other group of voters supporting or opposing a candidate,
10 principle, or proposition on the ballot.
11 (3) The State Board may adopt regulations prohibiting IN–PERSON public
12 observation of a part of the canvass only if prohibiting IN–PERSON public observation is
13 necessary to ensure:
14 (i) the integrity or accuracy of the canvass; or
15 (ii) that the canvass process is not impeded.
16 (4) The State Board shall ensure that the requirements of this subsection
17 are implemented uniformly and consistently by each local board.
18 [(b)] (D) Each board of canvassers shall elect by majority vote a chairman and
19 secretary from among its members.
20 [(c)] (E) Each member of the board of canvassers shall take an oath,
21 administered and recorded by the clerk of the circuit court, to canvass and declare the votes
22 cast truthfully and to perform other duties required by law.
23 [(d)] (F) (1) At the first meeting of the board of canvassers for the purpose of
24 canvassing, a majority of the whole board of canvassers shall be a quorum.
25 (2) If a quorum is not present, the members present shall adjourn until the
26 next day.
27 [(e)] (G) Except as otherwise provided in this article, all decisions shall be
28 reached by a majority vote of the members present.
29 [(f)] (H) In Montgomery County and Prince George’s County, if a member is not
30 present at the scheduled time for vote canvassing, a substitute member of the board of
31 canvassers may be sworn in.
4 SENATE BILL 337
1 [(g)] (I) At least one member of the board of canvassers present shall be a
2 registered voter of the principal minority party.
3 [(h)] (J) Once the board has satisfied the requirements of subsections (a)
4 through [(g)] (I) of this section, it may proceed with the canvass in accordance with the
5 regulations of the State Board.
6 11–302.
7 (a) (1) Each local board shall:
8 (I) meet at its designated counting center to canvass the absentee
9 ballots cast in that election in accordance with the regulations and guidelines established
10 by the State Board; AND
11 (II) COMPLY WITH THE LIVE VIDEO STREAMING AND ARCHIVAL
12 REQUIREMENTS OF § 11–301(B) OF THIS SUBTITLE.
13 (2) Promptly after receipt of an absentee ballot, a local board shall review
14 the ballot envelope or ballot/return envelope for the omission of the voter’s signature on the
15 oath.
16 (3) A local board shall record the receipt of an absentee ballot in the
17 statewide voter registration system and make the information available through the free
18 access system established under COMAR 33.11.06.03 as soon as practicable, but not later
19 than:
20 (i) 2 business days after receipt of an absentee ballot provided by
21 mail; or
22 (ii) 4 business days after receipt of an absentee ballot provided
23 through the Internet or by facsimile transmission.
24 11–303.
25 (a) Following an election, each local board shall:
26 (1) meet at its designated counting center to canvass the provisional ballots
27 cast in that election in accordance with the regulations and guidelines established by the
28 State Board; AND
29 (2) COMPLY WITH THE LIVE VIDEO STREAMING AND ARCHIVAL
30 REQUIREMENTS OF § 11–301(B) OF THIS SUBTITLE.
31 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That on or before December 1,
32 2025, the State Board of Elections shall adopt regulations to ensure that the live streaming
SENATE BILL 337 5
1 requirements under §§ 2–208 and 11–301 of the Election Law Article, as enacted by Section
2 1 of this Act, are implemented in a manner that:
3 (1) maximizes transparency of open meetings of local boards and the
4 canvassing of ballots; and
5 (2) as applicable, protects the anonymity of voters.
6 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
7 1, 2025.
