1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Insurance – Preventive Services – Enforcement Authority
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Insurance Commissioner to enforce certain
4 provisions of law related to preventive services consistent with federal rules and
5 guidance certain recommendations and guidelines set by certain federal agencies in
6 effect on a certain date; and generally relating to health insurance preventive
7 services.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Insurance
10 Section 15–1A–10
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2017 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Insurance
16 15–1A–10.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *hb0974*
2 HOUSE BILL 974
1 (a) [Except] SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION AND EXCEPT as
2 provided in subsections (b) and (c) of this section, a carrier shall provide coverage for and
3 may not impose any cost–sharing requirements, including copayments, coinsurance, or
4 deductibles for:
5 (1) evidence–based items or services that have in effect a rating of A or B
6 in the [current] MOST RECENT recommendations of the United States Preventive Services
7 Task Force with respect to the individual involved;
8 (2) immunizations for routine use in children, adolescents, and adults that
9 have in effect a recommendation from the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices
10 of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention with respect to the individual involved,
11 if the recommendation:
12 (i) has been adopted by the Director of the Centers for Disease
13 Control and Prevention; and
14 (ii) is listed on the Immunization Schedules of the Centers for
15 Disease Control and Prevention for routine use;
16 (3) with respect to infants, children, and adolescents, evidence–informed
17 preventive care and screenings provided for in comprehensive guidelines supported by the
18 Health Resources and Services Administration; and
19 (4) with respect to women:
20 (i) to the extent not provided in item (ii) of this item, preventive care
21 and screenings as provided for in comprehensive guidelines supported by the Health
22 Resources and Services Administration for purposes of § 2713(a)(4) of the federal Public
23 Health Service Act; and
24 (ii) subject to § 15–826(c) of this title, contraceptive coverage as
25 provided for in comprehensive guidelines supported by the Health Resources and Services
26 Administration for purposes of § 2713(a)(4) of the federal Public Health Service Act.
27 (b) To the extent that cost–sharing is otherwise allowed under federal or State
28 law, a health benefit plan that uses a network of providers may impose cost–sharing
29 requirements on the coverage described in subsection (a) of this section for items or services
30 delivered by an out–of–network provider.
31 (c) This section may not be construed to prohibit a carrier from providing
32 coverage for services in addition to those recommended by the United States Preventive
33 Services Task Force or to deny coverage for services that are not recommended by the Task
34 Force.
35 (D) (1) THE SUBJECT TO § 15–826(C) OF THIS TITLE:
HOUSE BILL 974 3
1 (1) THE COMMISSIONER SHALL ENFORCE THIS SECTION CONSISTENT
2 WITH FEDERAL RULES AND GUIDANCE THE RECOMMENDATIONS AND GUIDELINES
3 IN EFFECT ON DECEMBER 31, 2024., SET BY THE UNITED STATES PREVENTIVE
4 SERVICES TASK FORCE, THE ADVISORY COMMITTEE ON IMMUNIZATION
5 PRACTICES OF THE CENTERS FOR DISEASE CONTROL AND PREVENTION, OR THE
6 HEALTH RESOURCES AND SERVICES ADMINISTRATION, AND RELATED FEDERAL
7 RULES OR GUIDANCE; AND
8 (2) THE THE COMMISSIONER MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS:
9 (I) NECESSARY TO CARRY OUT THIS SECTION, CONSISTENT
10 WITH FEDERAL STATUTES, RULES, AND GUIDANCE IN EFFECT:
11 1. ON DECEMBER 31, 2024; OR
12 2. AT A LATER DATE THAT ENHANCE THE SCOPE OF
13 PREVENTIVE SERVICES TO THE BENEFIT OF CONSUMERS IN THE STATE; AND OR
14 (II) RELATED TO TO REQUIRE CARRIERS TO PROVIDE
15 COVERAGE WITHOUT IMPOSING COST–SHARING REQUIREMENTS, INCLUDING
16 COPAYMENTS, COINSURANCE, OR DEDUCTIBLES, FOR ANY FUTURE PREVENTIVE
17 SERVICES RECOMMENDATIONS AND GUIDELINES ISSUED AFTER DECEMBER 31,
18 2024, BY THE UNITED STATES PREVENTIVE SERVICES TASK FORCE, THE ADVISORY
19 COMMITTEE ON IMMUNIZATION PRACTICES OF THE CENTERS FOR DISEASE
20 CONTROL AND PREVENTION, OR THE HEALTH RESOURCES AND SERVICES
21 ADMINISTRATION, OR AND RELATED FEDERAL RULES OR GUIDANCE.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
23 1, 2025.
