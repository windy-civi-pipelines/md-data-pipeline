CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Lottery – Instant Ticket Lottery Machines – Veterans’ and Fraternal
3 Organizations
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the Director of the State Lottery and Gaming Control
5 Agency to issue certain fraternal organizations a license for not more than a certain
6 number of instant ticket lottery machines; altering the distribution of proceeds from
7 certain instant ticket lottery machine sales by veterans’ organizations; providing for
8 the distribution of proceeds from certain instant ticket lottery machine sales by
9 fraternal organizations; authorizing, instead of requiring, the Director to consider
10 certain information before issuing certain licenses; repealing a requirement that
11 certain veterans’ organizations purchase or lease certain instant ticket lottery
12 machines; prohibiting certain fraternal organizations from operating an instant
13 ticket lottery machine in certain locations; altering certain requirements concerning
14 the conduct of gaming through instant ticket lottery machines; and generally
15 relating to the operation of instant ticket lottery machines in the State.
16 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
17 Article – State Government
18 Section 9–112
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
21 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
22 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

9 ORGANIZATION THAT:
10 1. A. IS TAX EXEMPT AND ORGANIZED AS A
11 FRATERNAL OR SORORAL ORGANIZATION UNDER § 501(C)(7) OR § 501(C)(8) OF THE
12 INTERNAL REVENUE CODE;

14 C. HAS A BONA FIDE MEMBERSHIP OF AT LEAST 300

16 OF THIS SECTION APPLIES FOR AT LEAST 5 YEARS BEFORE THE ORGANIZATION
17 APPLIES FOR A LICENSE UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION; OR
18 2. IS INDEPENDENT AND THE MEMBERSHIP OF WHICH
19 COMPRISES SOLELY GRADUATE MEMBERS OF A NATIONWIDE FRATERNITY OR
20 SORORITY THAT IS TAX EXEMPT UNDER § 501(C)(7) OR § 501(C)(8) OF THE
21 INTERNAL REVENUE CODE.
22 (II) “FRATERNAL ORGANIZATION” DOES NOT INCLUDE:
23 1. ANY COLLEGE OR HIGH SCHOOL FRATERNITY OR
24 SORORITY; OR
25 2. ANY OTHER FRATERNITY OR SORORITY THE
26 MEMBERSHIP OF WHICH IS RESTRICTED IN WHOLE OR IN PART TO STUDENTS OR
27 GRADUATES OF AN EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTION OR A PROFESSIONAL SCHOOL.
28 (4) “VETERANS’ organization” means an organization that is tax exempt
29 and organized as a veterans’ organization under § 501(c)(19) or § 501(c)(4) of the Internal
30 Revenue Code.

32 regulations of the Agency and this subtitle, the Director shall issue licenses to the persons

9 (4) the volume of expected sales.

1 (5) (I) The Director may adopt regulations to implement the provisions
2 of this subsection that include restricting the location of instant ticket lottery machines in
3 areas of a veterans’ organization’s OR A FRATERNAL ORGANIZATION’S public meeting
4 hall that is accessible to the public.
5 (II) A FRATERNAL ORGANIZATION MAY NOT LOCATE OR
6 OPERATE ITS INSTANT TICKET LOTTERY MACHINES IN A MEETING HALL THAT IS:
7 1. LOCATED ON OR AFFILIATED WITH THE CAMPUS OF
8 AN INSTITUTION OF HIGHER EDUCATION; OR
9 2. LOCATED IN A BUILDING WHERE STUDENTS OF AN
10 INSTITUTION OF HIGHER EDUCATION RESIDE.
11 (6) The Agency shall ensure that the element of chance in the conduct of
12 the gaming through the instant ticket lottery machines established under this subsection
13 is consistent with the holding in the case of Chesapeake Amusements Inc. v. Riddle, 363
14 Md. 16 (2001), in that the element of chance must be CONTAINED wholly within the
15 pre–printed PREDETERMINED instant lottery ticket, and that player enhancements in an
16 instant ticket lottery machine may not affect the element of chance being wholly within the
17 pre–printed instant lottery ticket.
18 (e) The Director may not issue a license to:
19 (1) a person or governmental unit to engage in business primarily as a
20 licensed agent; or
21 (2) an individual who is under the age of 21 years.
22 (f) The Commission may hear and decide an appeal of a denial of a license.

24 1, 2025.
