CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Insurance Protections for Vehicle Service Contracts
3 Mechanical Repair Contracts – Technical Service Bulletins
4 FOR the purpose of establishing that a licensed vehicle dealer, an obligor, or an agent that
5 sells mechanical repair contracts for motor vehicles may not make deceptive or
6 misleading statements regarding the mechanical repair contract and shall ensure
7 that testimonials of any of their endorsers are truthful, accurate, and not deceptive
8 prohibiting a mechanical repair contract from containing a provision precluding
9 coverage for service to a motor vehicle for issues identified in a technical service
10 bulletin and prohibiting the denial of a service claim for such an issue by certain
11 persons; and generally relating to technical service bulletins and mechanical repair
12 contracts for motor vehicles.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
14 Article – Commercial Law
15 Section 14–404
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
19 Article – Transportation
20 Section 15–311.2(a) and (e) through (h)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

11 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
12 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
13 Article – Commercial Law
14 14–404.
15 (a) (1) A guarantor shall fulfill the guarantor’s guaranty according to its
16 terms:
17 (i) Within a reasonable time; and
18 (ii) For the stated period of the guaranty or, if no period is stated, for
19 a reasonable period of time.
20 (2) A provider shall fulfill the obligations under the service contract
21 according to its terms:
22 (i) At or within the period stated in the service contract, or if no
23 period is stated, within a reasonable time; and
24 (ii) For the stated duration of the service contract.
25 (b) (1) (i) A guaranty is extended automatically when a guarantor fails to
26 repair successfully a malfunctioning or defective product within the guaranty period.
27 (ii) The guaranty does not terminate until the consumer product
28 successfully performs its intended function for the remaining period of the guaranty plus a
29 period equal to the time of repair.
30 (2) (i) A service contract is extended automatically when the provider
31 fails to perform the services under the service contract.
32 (ii) The service contract does not terminate until the services are
33 provided in accordance with the terms of the service contract.
HOUSE BILL 1046 3
1 (c) If a guaranty fails to disclose the information required by § 14–403 of this
2 subtitle, the guarantor shall, without charge and within a reasonable period of time:
3 (1) Repair a malfunctioning or defective consumer product; or
4 (2) If repair is not commercially practicable or cannot be timely made,
5 replace the malfunctioning or defective consumer product.
6 (d) (1) If a guarantor is unable to fulfill the terms of the guaranty within 10
7 days of the tender or delivery of a consumer product to the guarantor, the guarantor shall
8 provide on request of the person guaranteed a brief written explanation of the reasons for
9 the delay.
10 (2) If a provider is unable to fulfill the terms of the service contract within
11 10 days after the date on which the provider is required to perform obligations under the
12 service contract, the provider shall provide on request of the person guaranteed a brief
13 written explanation of the reasons for the delay.
14 Article – Transportation
15 15–311.2.
16 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
17 (2) “Agent” means a business entity that is authorized by an obligor or a
18 licensed vehicle dealer to sell a mechanical repair contract.
19 (3) (i) “Mechanical repair contract” means any agreement or contract
20 sold by a licensed vehicle dealer, an obligor, or an agent under which the obligor agrees to
21 perform over a fixed period of time, for a specific duration, and for a specific identifiable
22 price, provided that the purchase of the contract is optional to the purchaser, any of the
23 following services:
24 1. The repair, replacement, or maintenance of a motor
25 vehicle, or the indemnification for the repair, replacement, or maintenance of a motor
26 vehicle, for the operational or structural failure of the motor vehicle due to a defect in
27 materials, workmanship, or normal wear and tear, with or without additional provisions
28 for incidental payment of indemnity for services including towing, rental and emergency
29 road service, and road hazard protection;
30 2. The repair, replacement, or maintenance of a motor
31 vehicle for the operational or structural failure of one or more parts or systems of the motor
32 vehicle brought about by the failure of an additive product to perform as represented;
4 HOUSE BILL 1046
1 3. The repair or replacement of tires or wheels on a motor
2 vehicle damaged as a result of coming into contact with road hazards, including potholes,
3 rocks, wood debris, metal parts, glass, plastic, curbs, or composite scraps;
4 4. The removal and repair of dents, dings, or creases on a
5 motor vehicle using the process of paintless dent removal;
6 5. The repair of chips or cracks in, or the replacement of,
7 motor vehicle windshields as a result of damage caused by road hazards;
8 6. The replacement of a motor vehicle key or key fob if the
9 key or key fob becomes inoperable or is lost or stolen; or
10 7. Other services or products that may be approved by the
11 Insurance Commissioner if consistent with the provisions of this section.
12 (ii) “Mechanical repair contract” includes extended warranties and
13 extended service contracts.
14 (iii) “Mechanical repair contract” does not include:
15 1. Warranties under the Magnuson–Moss Warranty Act, 15
16 U.S.C. § 2301, et seq.;
17 2. Contracts or agreements for regular maintenance only; or
18 3. An agreement between a motor club, as defined in §
19 26–101 of the Insurance Article, and a member or subscriber of the motor club.
20 (4) (i) “Obligor” means the person specified in a mechanical repair
21 contract that is contractually obligated to perform the services set forth in the mechanical
22 repair contract.
23 (ii) “Obligor” does not include an insurer that provides insurance
24 coverage in accordance with subsection (b) of this section.
25 (e) A mechanical repair contract shall be offered in addition to any express
26 warranty originally included as part of the contract for sale of a new motor vehicle.
27 (f) A mechanical repair contract shall clearly and conspicuously set forth the date
28 when the warranty begins.
29 (g) A mechanical repair contract shall clearly and conspicuously set forth the date
30 or the odometer reading at which the warranty expires and the name and address of the
31 insurer issuing the policy of insurance as described in subsection (b) of this section.
HOUSE BILL 1046 5
1 (h) The repair of a malfunction or defect covered under a mechanical repair
2 contract shall include the cost of the teardown and diagnosing the malfunction or defect.
3 (i) (1) The provisions of the Maryland Consumer Products Guaranty Act, Title
4 14, Subtitle 4 of the Commercial Law Article, apply to a mechanical repair contract sold in
5 the State.
6 (2) IN ADDITION TO THE REQUIREMENTS FOR LICENSED VEHICLE
7 DEALERS, OBLIGORS, AND AGENTS ESTABLISHED UNDER § 14–404 OF THE
8 COMMERCIAL LAW ARTICLE, A LICENSED VEHICLE DEALER, AN OBLIGOR, OR AN
9 AGENT:
10 (I) MAY NOT MAKE DECEPTIVE OR MISLEADING STATEMENTS
11 REGARDING A MECHANICAL REPAIR CONTRACT; AND
12 (II) SHALL ENSURE THAT TESTIMONIALS OF ANY OF THEIR
13 ENDORSERS ARE TRUTHFUL, ACCURATE, AND NOT DECEPTIVE.
14 14–404.1.
15 (A) A MECHANICAL REPAIR CONTRACT UNDER § 15–311.2 OF THE
16 TRANSPORTATION ARTICLE MAY NOT CONTAIN A PROVISION PRECLUDING
17 COVERAGE FOR AN ISSUE WITH A MOTOR VEHICLE IDENTIFIED IN A TECHNICAL
18 SERVICE BULLETIN.
19 (B) A GUARANTOR OR PROVIDER MAY NOT DENY A MECHANICAL REPAIR
20 CONTRACT CLAIM BECAUSE THE DIAGNOSED ISSUE WITH THE MOTOR VEHICLE FOR
21 WHICH THE CLAIM WAS MADE WAS IDENTIFIED IN A TECHNICAL SERVICE BULLETIN.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
23 October 1, 2025.
