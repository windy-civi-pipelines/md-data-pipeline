1 AN ACT concerning
2 Baltimore County Board of Education – Alterations of Elected Member Districts
3 and Establishment of Redistricting Process
4 FOR the purpose of requiring certain members of the Baltimore County Board of Education
5 to be elected in a certain manner from certain districts in certain elections;
6 establishing the process for how certain school board districts are reapportioned after
7 each decennial census of the United States; and generally relating to elected member
8 districts for the Baltimore County Board of Education.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
10 Article – Education
11 Section 3–2B–01
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 BY adding to
15 Article – Education
16 Section 3–2B–11
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

2 SENATE BILL 656
1 3–2B–01.
2 (a) The Baltimore County Board of Education consists of:
3 (1) Seven nonpartisan elected members;
4 (2) Four appointed members; and
5 (3) One student member.
6 (b) (1) Of the 11 elected and appointed members of the county board:
7 (i) 1. [One] BEFORE THE 2034 GENERAL ELECTION, ONE
8 member shall be elected from each of the seven councilmanic districts in the county,
9 established by the County Council of Baltimore County IN EXISTENCE ON OCTOBER 1,
10 2024, by the voters of that district; and
11 2. BEGINNING WITH THE 2034 GENERAL ELECTION, ONE
12 MEMBER SHALL BE ELECTED FROM EACH OF THE SEVEN SCHOOL BOARD DISTRICTS
13 ESTABLISHED BY THE REDISTRICTING PROCESS DESCRIBED IN § 3–2B–11 OF THIS
14 SUBTITLE, BY THE VOTERS OF THAT DISTRICT; AND
15 (ii) Four members shall be appointed by the Governor from the
16 county at large.
17 (2) (i) The seven elected members shall be elected at a general election
18 in gubernatorial election years in accordance with § 3–2B–02 of this subtitle.
19 (ii) The four appointed members shall be appointed by the Governor
20 in presidential election years from a list of nominees submitted by the Baltimore County
21 School Board Nominating Commission as provided in § 3–2B–03 of this subtitle:
22 1. On the expiration of the term of an incumbent appointed
23 member within 30 days after the general election; or
24 2. Within the 30–day period otherwise required under this
25 subtitle.
26 (3) (i) A member from a school board district shall be at least 21 years
27 old, a resident of that district for at least 2 years, and a registered voter of the county before
28 the election.
29 (ii) A member from a district who does not maintain residency in
30 that district may not continue as a member of the county board and the office shall be
31 deemed vacant.
SENATE BILL 656 3
1 (iii) If the boundary line of a district is changed, the term of an
2 incumbent member of the county board who no longer resides in the district because of the
3 change is not affected during this term.
4 (4) (i) A member may not be elected or appointed to serve on the county
5 board for more than three consecutive terms.
6 (ii) A member who has served three consecutive 4–year terms may
7 not be elected or appointed to the county board until at least 4 years have elapsed since the
8 end of the member’s last term on the county board.
9 (5) During an election year:
10 (i) The Governor may not appoint as a member of the county board
11 an individual who files a certificate of candidacy for election to the county board; and
12 (ii) An individual who files a certificate of candidacy for election to
13 the county board may not seek appointment to the county board by the Governor through
14 nomination by the Baltimore County School Board Nominating Commission.
15 3–2B–11.
16 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “SCHOOL BOARD DISTRICT” MEANS THE GEOGRAPHIC
17 AREA OF BALTIMORE COUNTY FROM WHICH AN ELECTED MEMBER OF THE
18 BALTIMORE COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION IS ELECTED.
19 (B) (1) BEGINNING AFTER THE 2030 U. S. CENSUS, THE SCHOOL BOARD
20 DISTRICTS SHALL BE:
21 1. REAPPORTIONED AFTER EACH DECENNIAL CENSUS OF THE
22 UNITED STATES; AND
23 2. EFFECTIVE FOR EACH GENERAL ELECTION WHERE A
24 MEMBER WILL BE ELECTED UNTIL THE FOLLOWING DECENNIAL CENSUS.
25 (2) THE REAPPORTIONED SCHOOL BOARD DISTRICTS UNDER THIS
26 SECTION SHALL BE SUBSTANTIALLY EQUAL IN POPULATION.
27 (C) (1) AFTER EACH DECENNIAL CENSUS, THE COUNTY BOARD SHALL
28 SUBMIT A PROPOSAL TO THE BALTIMORE COUNTY DELEGATION TO THE GENERAL
29 ASSEMBLY ON REAPPORTIONING THE SCHOOL BOARD DISTRICTS.
4 SENATE BILL 656
1 (2) THE COUNTY BOARD SHALL SUBMIT THE PROPOSAL IN
2 PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 1 OF THE
3 CALENDAR YEAR IMMEDIATELY FOLLOWING THE CENSUS.
4 (D) (1) THE BALTIMORE COUNTY DELEGATION TO THE GENERAL
5 ASSEMBLY SHALL:
6 (I) CONSIDER THE PROPOSAL SUBMITTED UNDER SUBSECTION
7 (C) OF THIS SECTION; AND
8 (II) INTRODUCE LEGISLATION TO REAPPORTION THE SCHOOL
9 BOARD DISTRICTS IN THE SESSION IMMEDIATELY FOLLOWING THE DATE THAT THE
10 PROPOSAL WAS SUBMITTED.
11 (2) LEGISLATION INTRODUCED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION MAY
12 DIFFER FROM THE PROPOSAL SUBMITTED UNDER SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS
13 SECTION.
14 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
15 1, 2025.
