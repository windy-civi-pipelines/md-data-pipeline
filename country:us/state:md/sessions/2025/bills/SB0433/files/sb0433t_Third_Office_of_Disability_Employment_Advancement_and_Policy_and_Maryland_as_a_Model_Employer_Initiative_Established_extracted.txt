CF HB 502
By: The President (By Request – Administration) and Senators Augustine,
Charles, Ellis, Henson, Hettleman, Kagan, King, and Lam Lam, and Hayes
Introduced and read first time: January 20, 2025
Assigned to: Finance
Committee Report: Favorable with amendments
Senate action: Adopted
Read second time: March 16, 2025
CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Office of Disability Employment Advancement and Policy and Maryland as a
3 Model Employer Initiative – Established
4 FOR the purpose of establishing the Office of Disability Employment Advancement and
5 Policy within the Department of Disabilities; establishing the Maryland as a Model
6 Employer Initiative within the Office of Disability Employment Advancement and
7 Policy to facilitate efforts that improve outcomes in the hiring, recruitment,
8 retention, and advancement of people with disabilities in the State government
9 workforce; and generally relating to the establishment of the Office of Disability
10 Employment Advancement and Policy and the Maryland as a Model Employer
11 Initiative.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Human Services
14 Section 7–114(b)
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 BY adding to
18 Article – Human Services
19 Section 7–1201 through 7–1205 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 12. Office of
20 Disability Employment Advancement and Policy”
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0433*
2 SENATE BILL 433
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Human Services
4 7–114.
5 (b) The Department shall oversee and administer the following programs and
6 units:
7 (1) constituent services and ombudsmen programs;
8 (2) the Assistive Technology Guaranteed Loan Program under Subtitle 6
9 of this title;
10 (3) the Office of Personal Assistance Services, including the Attendant
11 Care Program under Subtitle 4 of this title;
12 (4) Telecommunications Access of Maryland under Subtitle 8 of this title;
13 [and]
14 (5) Telecommunications Devices and Distribution of Accessible
15 Information for Disabled Individuals under Subtitle 9 of this title; AND
16 (6) THE OFFICE OF DISABILITY EMPLOYMENT ADVANCEMENT AND
17 POLICY.
18 SUBTITLE 12. OFFICE OF DISABILITY EMPLOYMENT ADVANCEMENT AND POLICY.
19 7–1201.
20 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
21 INDICATED.
22 (B) “DEPARTMENT” MEANS THE DEPARTMENT OF DISABILITIES.
23 (C) “INITIATIVE” MEANS THE MARYLAND AS A MODEL EMPLOYER
24 INITIATIVE.
25 (D) “OFFICE” MEANS THE OFFICE OF DISABILITY EMPLOYMENT
26 ADVANCEMENT AND POLICY.
27 (E) “SECRETARY” MEANS THE SECRETARY OF DISABILITIES.
28 7–1202.
SENATE BILL 433 3
1 (A) THERE IS AN OFFICE OF DISABILITY EMPLOYMENT ADVANCEMENT
2 AND POLICY WITHIN THE DEPARTMENT.
3 (B) THE PURPOSE OF THE OFFICE INCLUDES:
4 (1) THE DEVELOPMENT AND IMPLEMENTATION OF STRATEGIES IN
5 THE STATE DISABILITIES PLAN, ESTABLISHED UNDER § 7–132 OF THIS TITLE,
6 RELATING TO THE EMPLOYMENT, TRAINING, AND CAREER–READINESS OF
7 INDIVIDUALS WITH DISABILITIES;
8 (2) PERFORMING DUTIES UNDER § 7–114 OF THIS TITLE NECESSARY
9 TO SUPPORT STATEWIDE POLICIES AND STANDARDS RELATING TO THE
10 EMPLOYMENT, TRAINING, AND CAREER–READINESS OF INDIVIDUALS WITH
11 DISABILITIES;
12 (3) COLLECTING INFORMATION AND DATA FROM UNITS OF STATE
13 GOVERNMENT RELATING TO THE EMPLOYMENT, TRAINING, AND
14 CAREER–READINESS OF INDIVIDUALS WITH DISABILITIES, IN ACCORDANCE WITH §
15 7–115 OF THIS TITLE; AND
16 (4) IMPLEMENTING THE MARYLAND AS A MODEL EMPLOYER
17 INITIATIVE, ESTABLISHED UNDER § 7–1205 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
18 (C) BEGINNING IN FISCAL YEAR 2027, THE GOVERNOR MAY INCLUDE IN
19 THE ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION OF $250,000 FOR THE OFFICE.
20 7–1203.
21 THE SECRETARY MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS AS NECESSARY TO CARRY OUT
22 THE PROVISIONS OF THIS SUBTITLE.
23 7–1204.
24 THIS SUBTITLE MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO ESTABLISH AN ENTITLEMENT
25 PROGRAM.
26 7–1205.
27 (A) BEGINNING JULY 1, 2026, THE OFFICE SHALL BE RESPONSIBLE FOR
28 IMPLEMENTATION AND OVERSIGHT OF THE MARYLAND AS A MODEL EMPLOYER
29 INITIATIVE.
30 (B) THE PURPOSE OF THE INITIATIVE SHALL INCLUDE:
4 SENATE BILL 433
1 (1) PROMOTING THE RECRUITMENT, HIRING, RETENTION, AND
2 CAREER ADVANCEMENT OF PEOPLE WITH DISABILITIES ACROSS STATE
3 GOVERNMENT;
4 (2) CONDUCTING AND COORDINATING OUTREACH TO JOB SEEKERS
5 WITH DISABILITIES AND STATE GOVERNMENT TO ENCOURAGE PARTICIPATION IN
6 THE INITIATIVE;
7 (3) DEVELOPING AND DELIVERING TRAININGS TO STATE AGENCIES
8 AND UNITS ADDRESSING BEST PRACTICES, RESOURCES, AND RELEVANT FEDERAL
9 AND STATE LAWS PERTAINING TO THE EMPLOYMENT OF PEOPLE WITH
10 DISABILITIES;
11 (4) ASSISTING STATE GOVERNMENT AGENCIES AND UNITS TO
12 ATTRACT QUALIFIED JOB SEEKERS WITH DISABILITIES TO AVAILABLE POSITIONS;
13 (5) PROMOTING ACCESS TO AND ACQUISITION OF ASSISTIVE
14 TECHNOLOGY FOR NEW STATE EMPLOYEES WITH DISABILITIES;
15 (6) EVALUATING STATE EMPLOYMENT PRACTICES AND POLICIES AND
16 MAKING RECOMMENDATIONS REGARDING WAYS TO REDUCE BARRIERS FOR JOB
17 SEEKERS AND EMPLOYEES WITH DISABILITIES;
18 (7) COLLECTING FROM EACH UNIT OF STATE GOVERNMENT, AS PART
19 OF THE PLANS REQUIRED UNDER § 7–116 OF THIS TITLE, PERFORMANCE GOALS
20 RELATED TO HIRING AND RETENTION EFFORTS FOR EMPLOYEES WITH DISABILITIES
21 AND ANY DATA DEEMED NECESSARY BY THE SECRETARY TO DEMONSTRATE
22 PERFORMANCE OUTCOMES; AND
23 (8) REPORTING ON THE INITIATIVE’S OUTCOMES IN COMPLIANCE
24 WITH SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION.
25 (C) ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 1, 2026, AND EACH DECEMBER 1
26 THEREAFTER, THE OFFICE SHALL REPORT TO THE GOVERNOR AND THE GENERAL
27 ASSEMBLY, IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE,
28 ON:
29 (1) PROGRESS AND OUTCOMES OF THE INITIATIVE; AND
30 (2) RECOMMENDATIONS DEVELOPED BY THE INITIATIVE FOR
31 CONTINUED IMPROVEMENT OF STATE GOVERNMENT EMPLOYMENT PRACTICES FOR
32 PEOPLE WITH DISABILITIES.
SENATE BILL 433 5
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
2 1, 2025.
Approved:
