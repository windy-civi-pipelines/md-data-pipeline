CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Petitions for Emergency Evaluation
3 (Arnaud and Magruder Memorial Act)
4 FOR the purpose of specifying that a petition for emergency evaluation is effective for a
5 certain period of time; authorizing a court, on a certain motion and for good cause
6 shown, to extend a petition for a certain period of time; requiring a court to include
7 the date the petition expires on the petition; authorizing peace officers to use
8 reasonable and necessary force when executing a petition; and generally relating to
9 petitions for emergency evaluation.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
11 Article – Health – General
12 Section 10–623 and 10–624(a)
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – Health – General
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

Underlining indicates amendments to bill.

amendment. *hb0468*
2 HOUSE BILL 468
1 10–623.
2 (a) If the petitioner under Part IV of this subtitle is not a physician, psychologist,
3 clinical social worker, licensed clinical professional counselor, clinical nurse specialist in
4 psychiatric and mental health nursing, psychiatric nurse practitioner, licensed clinical
5 marriage and family therapist, health officer or designee of a health officer, or peace officer,
6 the petitioner shall present the petition to the court for immediate review.
7 (b) After review of the petition, the court shall endorse the petition if the court
8 finds probable cause to believe that the emergency evaluee has shown the symptoms of a
9 mental disorder and that the individual presents a danger to the life or safety of the
10 individual or of others.
11 (c) If the court does not find probable cause, the court shall indicate that fact on
12 the petition, and no further action may be taken under the petition.
13 (D) (1) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, THE PETITION
14 SHALL BE EFFECTIVE FOR 5 DAYS AFTER BEING ENDORSED BY THE COURT UNDER
15 SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
16 (2) (I) SUBJECT TO SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS PARAGRAPH, ON
17 A MOTION, THE COURT MAY EXTEND THE PETITION FOR GOOD CAUSE SHOWN BASED
18 ON THE PRESENTING BEHAVIOR OF THE INDIVIDUAL FOR UP TO 5 DAYS AT A TIME,
19 BUT NOT TO EXCEED A TOTAL OF 30 DAYS.
20 (II) 1. A MOTION UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS
21 PARAGRAPH MUST BE MADE BY THE ORIGINAL PETITIONER.
22 2. EACH EXTENSION SHALL BE BASED ON A SEPARATE
23 MOTION.
24 (3) A PETITION ENDORSED OR EXTENDED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION
25 SHALL BE SERVED EXPEDITIOUSLY.
26 (E) THE COURT SHALL INCLUDE ON THE PETITION THE DATE THE PETITION
27 EXPIRES.
28 10–624.
29 (a) (1) A peace officer shall take an emergency evaluee to the nearest
30 emergency facility if the peace officer has a petition under Part IV of this subtitle that:
31 (i) 1. Has been endorsed by a court [within the last 5 days]; AND
32 2. IS IN EFFECT; or
HOUSE BILL 468 3
1 (ii) Is signed and submitted by a physician, psychologist, clinical
2 social worker, licensed clinical professional counselor, clinical nurse specialist in
3 psychiatric and mental health nursing, psychiatric nurse practitioner, licensed clinical
4 marriage and family therapist, health officer or designee of a health officer, or peace officer.
5 (2) The petition required under paragraph (1) of this subsection may be:
6 (i) In the form of an electronic record; and
7 (ii) Transmitted and received electronically.
8 (3) A PEACE OFFICER MAY USE FORCE IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 3–524
9 OF THE PUBLIC SAFETY ARTICLE WHEN EXECUTING A PETITION.
10 [(3)] (4) To the extent practicable, a peace officer shall notify the
11 emergency facility in advance that the peace officer is bringing an emergency evaluee to
12 the emergency facility.
13 [(4)] (5) After a peace officer brings the emergency evaluee to an
14 emergency facility, the peace officer need not stay unless, because the emergency evaluee
15 is violent, emergency facility personnel ask the supervisor of the peace officer to have the
16 peace officer stay.
17 [(5)] (6) A peace officer shall stay until the supervisor responds to the
18 request for assistance. If the emergency evaluee is violent, the supervisor shall allow the
19 peace officer to stay.
20 [(6)] (7) If emergency facility personnel ask that a peace officer stay, a
21 physician shall examine the emergency evaluee as promptly as possible.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
23 October 1, 2025.
Approved:
