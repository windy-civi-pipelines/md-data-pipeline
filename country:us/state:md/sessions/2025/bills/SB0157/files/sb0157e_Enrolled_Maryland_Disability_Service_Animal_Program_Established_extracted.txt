6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
8 Article – Human Services
9 7–114.
10 (a) (1) The Department is the principal unit of State government responsible
11 for developing, maintaining, revising, and enforcing statewide disability policies and
12 standards throughout the units of State government.
13 (2) In this capacity, the Department shall:
14 (i) serve as the principal advisor to the Governor on the means and
15 methods available to:
16 1. implement and fund support to individuals with
17 disabilities in accordance with the State Disabilities Plan;
18 2. modify or consolidate support to individuals with
19 disabilities; and
20 3. collaborate with federal, regional, and local units of
21 government to enhance the effectiveness of the provision and funding of support to
22 individuals with disabilities;
23 (ii) annually recommend projects to the Department of Budget and
24 Management for inclusion in the capital budget to promote access to State–owned facilities
25 for individuals with disabilities;
26 (iii) assist units of State government to identify federal, State, local,
27 and private funds available to the State for programs and services for individuals with
28 disabilities; and
29 (iv) provide technical assistance to local jurisdictions in planning and
30 implementing collaborative strategies consistent with the State Disabilities Plan.
31 (b) The Department shall oversee and administer the following programs and
32 units:
33 (1) constituent services and ombudsmen programs;

1 (2) the Assistive Technology Guaranteed Loan Program under Subtitle 6
2 of this title;
3 (3) the Office of Personal Assistance Services, including the Attendant
4 Care Program under Subtitle 4 of this title;
5 (4) Telecommunications Access of Maryland under Subtitle 8 of this title;
6 [and]
7 (5) Telecommunications Devices and Distribution of Accessible
8 Information for Disabled Individuals under Subtitle 9 of this title; AND
9 (6) THE MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL PROGRAM UNDER
10 SUBTITLE 12 OF THIS TITLE.
11 SUBTITLE 12. MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL PROGRAM.
12 7–1201.
13 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
14 INDICATED.
15 (B) “DEPARTMENT” MEANS THE DEPARTMENT OF DISABILITIES.
16 (C) “ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUAL” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL A RESIDENT OF THE
17 STATE WITH A DISABILITY, AS DEFINED IN THE FEDERAL AMERICANS WITH
18 DISABILITIES ACT OF 1990, 42 U.S.C. § 12102.
19 (D) “FUND” MEANS THE MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL
20 PROGRAM FUND.
21 (E) “NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY” MEANS A CORPORATION, A
22 FOUNDATION, OR ANY OTHER LEGAL ENTITY THAT:
23 (1) IS TAX–EXEMPT UNDER § 501(C)(3) OF THE INTERNAL REVENUE
24 CODE;
25 (2) ENGAGES IN THE TRAINING OF SERVICE ANIMALS FOR USE BY
26 ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS; AND
27 (3) HAS BEEN SELECTED BY THE DEPARTMENT TO PROVIDE
28 SERVICES UNDER THIS SUBTITLE.
4 SENATE BILL 157
1 (F) “PROGRAM” MEANS THE MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL
2 PROGRAM ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SUBTITLE.
3 (G) “PROGRAM PARTICIPANT” MEANS AN ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUAL WHO
4 PARTICIPATES IN THE PROGRAM.
5 (H) (1) “SERVICE ANIMAL” MEANS AN ANIMAL THAT IS INDIVIDUALLY
6 TRAINED TO DO WORK OR PERFORM TASKS FOR THE BENEFIT OF AN INDIVIDUAL
7 WITH A DISABILITY.
8 (2) “SERVICE ANIMAL” DOES NOT INCLUDE AN ANIMAL THAT:
9 (I) AS A RESULT OF THE ANIMAL’S PRESENCE, IS MEANT TO
10 DETER CRIME; OR
11 (II) PROVIDES ONLY EMOTIONAL SUPPORT, WELL–BEING,
12 COMFORT, OR COMPANIONSHIP TO AN INDIVIDUAL.
13 (I) “SUCCESSFUL PROGRAM PARTICIPANT” MEANS A PROGRAM
14 PARTICIPANT WHO SUCCESSFULLY COMPLETES THE TRAINING PROTOCOL
15 SPECIFIED BY A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY.
16 7–1202.
17 THERE IS A MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL PROGRAM IN THE
18 DEPARTMENT.
19 7–1203.
20 THE PURPOSES OF THE PROGRAM ARE TO:
21 (1) REFER ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS WHO INQUIRE ABOUT
22 PARTICIPATION IN THE PROGRAM TO SELECT ONE OR MORE NONPROFIT TRAINING
23 ENTITIES TO PARTICIPATE IN THE PROGRAM; AND
24 (2) PROVIDE ADDITIONAL FUNDING MECHANISMS TO ASSIST
25 ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS IN THE PROGRAM; AND
26 (3) ENCOURAGE SUCCESSFUL PROGRAM PARTICIPANTS TO ASSIST IN
27 OUTREACH AND REFERRALS TO OTHER ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS WHO COULD
28 BENEFIT FROM PARTICIPATION IN THE PROGRAM.
29 7–1204.

1 THE DEPARTMENT SHALL:
2 (1) MANAGE, SUPERVISE, AND ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM;
3 (2) ADOPT REGULATIONS TO IMPLEMENT THE PROGRAM AND TO
4 ENSURE THAT FUND RESOURCES ARE UTILIZED TO CARRY OUT THE PURPOSES OF
5 THE PROGRAM, INCLUDING REGULATIONS ESTABLISHING PROCEDURES FOR THE
6 DEPARTMENT TO:
7 (I) PROMOTE THE PROGRAM TO ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS
8 THROUGH THE DEPARTMENT’S OUTREACH METHODS;
9 (II) REFER ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS TO SELECTED NONPROFIT
10 TRAINING ENTITIES;
11 (III) RECEIVE DONATIONS FOR THE FUND; AND
12 (IV) (II) USE REVENUE FROM THE FUND TO PAY SELECTED
13 NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITIES FOR SERVICES THAT ARE PROVIDED THROUGH THE
14 PROGRAM; AND
15 (3) SELECT AT LEAST ONE NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY TO:
16 (I) DEVELOP AND IMPLEMENT A TRAINING PROTOCOL THAT
17 WILL TEACH EACH PROGRAM PARTICIPANT METHODOLOGIES, STRATEGIES, AND
18 TECHNIQUES FOR PARTNERING WITH SERVICE ANIMALS;
19 (II) SELECT QUALIFIED PROGRAM PARTICIPANTS FROM THOSE
20 ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS REFERRED TO THE NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY UNDER
21 THE PROGRAM;
22 (III) SELECT AN APPROPRIATE SERVICE ANIMAL FOR EACH
23 PROGRAM PARTICIPANT;
24 (IV) FACILITATE EACH PROGRAM PARTICIPANT’S TRAINING
25 USING THE NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY’S TRAINING PROTOCOL; AND
26 (V) PARTNER EACH SUCCESSFUL PROGRAM PARTICIPANT WITH
27 THE SERVICE ANIMAL ON THE PROGRAM PARTICIPANT’S SUCCESSFUL COMPLETION
28 OF THE NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY’S TRAINING PROTOCOL.
29 7–1205.

1 (A) TO BE ELIGIBLE FOR SELECTION AS A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY
2 UNDER § 7–1204 OF THIS SUBTITLE, A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY SHALL:
3 (1) SERVE THE NEEDS OF ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS IN THE STATE; AND
4 (2) GENERATE ITS OWN REVENUE AND REINVEST THE PROCEEDS OF
5 THAT REVENUE IN THE GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT OF ITS PROGRAMS.
6 (B) A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY MAY DISQUALIFY A PROGRAM
7 PARTICIPANT FROM PARTICIPATION IN THE PROGRAM IF THE NONPROFIT
8 TRAINING ENTITY DETERMINES THAT THE PROGRAM PARTICIPANT’S INVOLVEMENT
9 IN THE PROGRAM:
10 (1) PRESENTS A DANGER TO THE PROGRAM PARTICIPANT’S MENTAL
11 OR PHYSICAL WELL–BEING;
12 (2) PRESENTS A DIRECT THREAT TO OTHERS, AS DEFINED BY THE
13 FEDERAL AMERICANS WITH DISABILITIES ACT;
14 (3) PRESENTS A DIRECT THREAT TO THE SERVICE ANIMAL’S MENTAL
15 OR PHYSICAL WELL–BEING; OR
16 (4) DOES NOT MEET THE TRAINING REQUIREMENTS OF THE
17 NONPROFIT.
18 (C) A PROGRAM PARTICIPANT MAY DISCONTINUE INVOLVEMENT IN THE
19 PROGRAM FOR ANY REASON.
20 7–1206.
21 (A) THERE IS A MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL PROGRAM FUND.
22 (B) THE PURPOSE OF THE FUND IS TO:
23 (1) PAY A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY; AND
24 (2) COVER THE COSTS OF ADMINISTERING THE PROGRAM.
25 (C) THE SECRETARY SHALL ADMINISTER THE FUND.
26 (D) (1) THE FUND IS A SPECIAL, NONLAPSING FUND THAT IS NOT
27 SUBJECT TO § 7–302 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.
SENATE BILL 157 7
1 (2) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL HOLD THE FUND, AND THE
2 COMPTROLLER SHALL ACCOUNT FOR THE FUND.
3 (E) (1) THE FUND CONSISTS OF:
4 (I) REVENUE COLLECTED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN THE FORM
5 OF DONATIONS TO THE PROGRAM;
6 (II) MONEY APPROPRIATED IN THE STATE BUDGET TO THE
7 FUND; AND
8 (III) ANY OTHER MONEY FROM ANY OTHER SOURCE ACCEPTED
9 FOR THE BENEFIT OF THE FUND.
10 (2) FOR EACH FISCAL YEAR, THE GOVERNOR SHALL INCLUDE IN THE
11 ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION OF $25,000 TO THE FUND.
12 (2) (3) EXPENDITURES FROM THE FUND MAY BE MADE ONLY IN
13 ACCORDANCE WITH THE STATE BUDGET.
14 (3) (4) MONEY EXPENDED FROM THE FUND IS SUPPLEMENTAL TO
15 AND IS NOT INTENDED TO TAKE THE PLACE OF FUNDING THAT OTHERWISE WOULD
16 BE APPROPRIATED FOR THE PROGRAM.
17 (F) THE FUND MAY BE USED ONLY TO PAY:
18 (1) A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY; AND
19 (2) ADMINISTRATIVE COSTS OF THE PROGRAM.
20 (G) (1) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL INVEST AND REINVEST THE
21 MONEY OF THE FUND IN THE SAME MANNER AS OTHER STATE MONEY MAY BE
22 INVESTED.
23 (2) ANY INVESTMENT EARNINGS OF THE FUND SHALL BE CREDITED
24 TO THE GENERAL FUND OF THE STATE.
25 (H) FOR THE PURPOSE OF IMPLEMENTING THIS SECTION, THE
26 DEPARTMENT MAY ACCEPT GIFTS OR GRANTS FOR DONATION TO THE FUND.
27 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
28 October 1, 2025:
29 (a) This Act is contingent on the Department of Disabilities certifying that the
30 Department has sufficient staff to administer the Maryland Disability Service Animal
8 SENATE BILL 157
1 Program under Title 7, Subtitle 12 of the Human Services Article, as enacted by Section 1 of
2 this Act.
3 (b) On or before October 1, 2026, the Department of Disabilities shall notify the
4 Department of Legislative Services whether the Department has certified that the
5 Department has sufficient staff to administer the Maryland Disability Service Animal
6 Program.
7 (c) (1) If the Department of Legislative Services receives notification that the
8 Department of Disabilities has sufficient staff to administer the Maryland Disability Service
9 Animal Program on or before October 1, 2026, this Act shall take effect on the date the notice
10 is received by the Department of Legislative Services in accordance with subsection (b) of
11 this section.
12 (2) If the Department of Legislative Services receives notice that the
13 Department of Disabilities lacks sufficient staff to administer the Maryland Disability
14 Service Animal Program on or before October 1, 2026, this Act, with no further action
15 required by the General Assembly, shall be null and void.
