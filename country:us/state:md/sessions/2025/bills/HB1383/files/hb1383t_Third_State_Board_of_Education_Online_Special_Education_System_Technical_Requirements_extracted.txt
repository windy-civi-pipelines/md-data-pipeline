1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Board of Education – Online Special Education System – Technical
3 Requirements
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the State Board of Education to adopt regulations concerning
5 minimum functional and technical requirements to ensure optimal performance of
6 the State’s online special education system, including the Maryland Online
7 Individualized Education Program and the Maryland Online Individualized Family
8 Service Plan; directing the State Department of Education to study minimum
9 functional and technical requirements to ensure optimal performance of the State’s
10 online special education system and report the results of the study to the State Board
11 on or before a certain date; requiring the State Board, on or before a certain date, to
12 report to the Governor and the General Assembly on the results of the study and the
13 status of certain proposed regulations; and generally relating to the State’s online
14 special education system.
15 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
16 Article – Education
17 Section 8–404
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

1 Article – Education
2 8–404.
3 (a) The State Board shall adopt, as regulations, standards for the identification,
4 evaluation, educational placement, and the provision of a free appropriate public education
5 of each child in this State who has begun the school year under the age of 21 and is found
6 to need special education and related services, whether or not the child is receiving
7 nonduplicative services from another governmental agency.
8 (b) Before these standards are adopted, the State Board shall consult with public
9 and private agencies and persons who are concerned with and knowledgeable about the
10 educational needs of children with disabilities who require special education and related
11 services.
12 (c) The standards for the education of a child with a disability who is enrolled in
13 a school that is operated by an agency other than a local school system may not be lower
14 than the standards for the education of a child with a disability who is enrolled in a school
15 that is operated by a local school system. The standards for the approval of schools located
16 in jurisdictions outside Maryland shall be the same as standards applicable to the approval
17 of schools within Maryland. However, no school located in a jurisdiction outside Maryland
18 shall be approved if the charges for pupils approved by the Department are higher than
19 charges for pupils from any other state for the same services, unless waived by the State
20 Superintendent.
21 (d) The standards shall include:
22 (1) The required qualifications for teachers, administrators, and other
23 professionals serving a child with a disability;
24 (2) Procedures for the identification, evaluation, educational placement,
25 and provision of a free appropriate public education for a child with a disability who
26 requires the provision of special education and related services;
27 (3) Guidelines for curricula, instructional materials, equipment, and the
28 organization, administration, and supervision of the program, including accounting,
29 auditing, and reporting procedures;
30 (4) MINIMUM FUNCTIONAL AND TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS TO
31 ENSURE OPTIMAL PERFORMANCE OF THE STATE’S ONLINE SPECIAL EDUCATION
32 SYSTEM, INCLUDING THE MARYLAND ONLINE INDIVIDUALIZED EDUCATION
33 PROGRAM AND THE MARYLAND ONLINE INDIVIDUALIZED FAMILY SERVICE PLAN;
34 [(4)] (5) Provisions for local, regional, and State day and residential
35 centers for children with disabilities who cannot be served appropriately in the public
36 schools;
HOUSE BILL 1383 3
1 [(5)] (6) Coordination of these special education services with services
2 given by any other government agency; and
3 [(6)] (7) Guidelines for approval of placement in nonpublic schools or
4 facilities if appropriate public services are not available.
5 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
6 (a) The State Department of Education shall study minimum functional and
7 technical requirements to ensure optimal performance of the State’s online special
8 education system, including the Maryland Online Individualized Education Program and
9 the Maryland Online Individualized Family Service Plan, to implement the State Board of
10 Education’s regulatory requirements under § 8–404(d)(4) of the Education Article, as
11 enacted by Section 1 of this Act.
12 (b) In conducting the study required under subsection (a) of this section, the State
13 Department of Education shall:
14 (1) ensure that the State’s online special education system is:
15 (i) a secure, user–friendly, and web–based platform that serves as a
16 dynamic tool to allow real–time monitoring of student demographic and disability
17 information for use by family, teachers, staff, and administrators to help students thrive;
18 (ii) capable of tracking important events and deadlines to notify all
19 stakeholders of important information and developments related to a student;
20 (iii) able to store, allow access to, and allow for the processing of
21 documents related to a student;
22 (iv) adaptable to short–term and long–term updates and changes
23 necessary to address the system’s growing and evolving needs; and
24 (v) compliant with all relevant federal and State laws and
25 regulations, including federal and State privacy laws and regulations;
26 (2) perform a technical review of the State’s online special education
27 system; and
28 (3) compare the results of the technical review under item (2) of this
29 subsection with other systems currently available.
30 (c) On or before October 1, 2025, the State Department of Education shall report
31 the results of the study to the State Board of Education.
4 HOUSE BILL 1383
1 (d) On or before December 1, 2025, the State Board of Education shall report to
2 the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, the
3 General Assembly on:
4 (1) the results of the study; and
5 (2) the status of proposed regulations to implement the standards required
6 under § 8–404(d)(4) of the Education Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act.
7 (e) Nothing in this section shall be construed to alter the duties of the State Board
8 of Education under the Administrative Procedure Act.
9 SECTION 3. 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
10 July 1, 2025.
Approved:
