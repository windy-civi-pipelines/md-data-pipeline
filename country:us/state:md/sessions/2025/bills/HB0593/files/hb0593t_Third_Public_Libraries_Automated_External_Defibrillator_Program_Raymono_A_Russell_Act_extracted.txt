CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Libraries – Automated External Defibrillator Program
3 (Raymono A. Russell Act)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring operators of certain libraries, beginning on a certain date, to
5 place an automated external defibrillator in a certain area, maintain the
6 functionality of the automated external defibrillator, and ensure a trained individual
7 is present under certain circumstances comply with the Public Access Automated
8 External Defibrillator Program; granting certain immunities to libraries and
9 individuals for certain acts or omissions under certain circumstances; altering the
10 definition of “facility” for the purpose of excluding certain libraries from the Public
11 Access Automated External Defibrillator Program; requiring the Maryland
12 Department of Health and the Maryland Institute for Emergency Medical Services
13 Systems jointly to adopt certain regulations; and generally relating to an automated
14 external defibrillator program for libraries.
15 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
16 Article – Education
17 Section 13–517(a)
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 BY adding to
21 Article – Education
22 Section 23–102.2
23 Annotated Code of Maryland

6 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
7 (2) “Automated external defibrillator (AED)” means a medical heart
8 monitor and defibrillator device that:
9 (i) Is cleared for market by the federal Food and Drug
10 Administration;
11 (ii) Recognizes the presence or absence of ventricular fibrillation or
12 rapid ventricular tachycardia;
13 (iii) Determines, without intervention by an operator, whether
14 defibrillation should be performed;
15 (iv) On determining that defibrillation should be performed,
16 automatically charges; and
17 (v) 1. Requires operator intervention to deliver the electrical
18 impulse; or
19 2. Automatically continues with delivery of electrical
20 impulse.
21 (3) “Certificate” means a certificate issued by the EMS Board to a
22 registered facility.
23 (4) (i) “Facility” means an agency, an association, a corporation, a firm,
24 a partnership, or any other entity.
25 (ii) “Facility” does not include [a]:
26 1. A grocery store or restaurant that is subject to §
27 21–330.3 of the Health – General Article; OR
28 2. A LIBRARY THAT IS SUBJECT TO § 23–102.2 OF THIS
29 ARTICLE.

4 government and State and federal emergency medical services programs.
5 (6) “Program” means the Public Access Automated External Defibrillator
6 Program.
7 (7) “Regional administrator” means the individual employed by the
8 Institute as regional administrator in each EMS region.
9 (8) “Regional council” means an EMS advisory body as created by the Code
10 of Maryland Regulations 30.05.
11 (9) “Regional council AED committee” means a committee appointed by the
12 regional council consisting of:
13 (i) The regional medical director;
14 (ii) The regional administrator; and
15 (iii) Three or more individuals with knowledge of and expertise in
16 AEDs.
17 (10) “Registered facility” means an organization, a business association, an
18 agency, or any other entity that meets the requirements of the EMS Board for registering
19 with the Program.

1 (2) COMPLY WITH § 13–517 OF THIS ARTICLE; AND
2 (2) (3) MAINTAIN THE FUNCTIONALITY OF THE AUTOMATED
3 EXTERNAL DEFIBRILLATOR; AND
4 (3) ENSURE THAT AN INDIVIDUAL TRAINED IN THE OPERATION OF
5 THE AUTOMATED EXTERNAL DEFIBRILLATOR IS PRESENT AT ANY TIME DURING
6 WHICH THE LIBRARY IS OPEN TO USE.
7 (C) IN ADDITION TO ANY OTHER IMMUNITIES AVAILABLE UNDER
8 STATUTORY OR COMMON LAW:
9 (1) A LIBRARY IS NOT CIVILLY LIABLE FOR ANY ACT OR OMISSION IN
10 THE PROVISION OF AUTOMATED EXTERNAL DEFIBRILLATION IF THE LIBRARY HAS
11 SATISFIED THE REQUIREMENTS UNDER THIS SECTION;
12 (2) AN INDIVIDUAL IS NOT CIVILLY LIABLE FOR ANY ACT OR
13 OMISSION IF:
14 (I) THE INDIVIDUAL IS ACTING IN GOOD FAITH WHILE

16 WHO IS THE VICTIM OR IS REASONABLY BELIEVED BY THE INDIVIDUAL TO BE A
17 VICTIM OF A SUDDEN CARDIAC ARREST;

19 PRUDENT MANNER; AND
20 (III) THE AUTOMATED EXTERNAL DEFIBRILLATION IS PROVIDED

24 VICTIM OR IS REASONABLY BELIEVED TO BE A VICTIM OF A SUDDEN CARDIAC
25 ARREST.

27 INSTITUTE FOR EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES SYSTEMS JOINTLY SHALL ADOPT
28 REGULATIONS THAT:
29 (1) ESTABLISH GUIDELINES FOR PERIODIC INSPECTIONS AND
30 ANNUAL MAINTENANCE OF THE AUTOMATED EXTERNAL DEFIBRILLATORS;
31 (2) ASSIST THE OPERATOR OF A LIBRARY IN CARRYING OUT THE
32 PROVISIONS OF THIS SECTION; AND
6 HOUSE BILL 593
1 (3) REQUIRE EACH LIBRARY SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION TO REGISTER
2 WITH THE MARYLAND INSTITUTE FOR EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES SYSTEMS
3 IN ORDER TO:
4 (I) MAKE ITS LOCATION PUBLICLY AVAILABLE TO EMERGENCY
5 DISPATCHERS; AND
6 (II) RECEIVE MAINTENANCE AND RECALL NOTICES FROM THE
7 MARYLAND INSTITUTE FOR EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES SYSTEMS.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before December 1,
9 2026, the Maryland Institute for Emergency Medical Services Systems shall report to the
10 Senate Committee on Education, Energy, and the Environment and the House Economic
11 Matters Ways and Means Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government
12 Article, on:
13 (1) the libraries that registered automated external defibrillators in
14 accordance with regulations adopted under § 23–102.2 of the Education Article, as enacted
15 by Section 1 of this Act; and
16 (2) the number of adverse cardiac events that required the use of an
17 automated external defibrillator experienced in the libraries that registered the automated
18 external defibrillators.
19 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
20 October 1, 2025.
