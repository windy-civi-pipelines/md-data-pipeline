1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – False Advertising – Digital Goods
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from advertising or offering for sale a digital good
4 using certain terminology or alongside an option for a time–limited rental unless a
5 certain acknowledgments are acknowledgment is received from the purchaser and
6 or a certain statements are statement is provided by the seller; establishing that a
7 person is guilty of a misdemeanor and subject to a certain penalties penalty for a
8 violation of this Act; and generally relating to the advertising and offering of digital
9 goods for sale.

2 HOUSE BILL 208
1 Article – Commercial Law
2 14–2901.
3 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
4 (b) “Advertise” means:
5 (1) To publish, circulate, disseminate, or place before the public in any way
6 or through any medium for the purpose of selling merchandise; and
7 (2) Advertising by:
8 (i) Exterior or interior signs, including neon or other electrical signs;
9 (ii) Radio, telephone, or television; and
10 (iii) Newspaper, magazine, book, notice, or any other method or
11 material.
12 (C) “CLEAR AND CONSPICUOUS” MEANS IN A MANNER THAT CLEARLY
13 CALLS ATTENTION TO THE LANGUAGE, INCLUDING TEXT THAT IS:
14 (1) IN LARGER TYPE THAN THE SURROUNDING TEXT;
15 (2) IN A CONTRASTING TYPE, FONT, OR COLOR TO THE SURROUNDING
16 TEXT; OR
17 (3) SET OFF FROM THE SURROUNDING TEXT BY SYMBOLS OR OTHER
18 MARKS.
19 (D) “DIGITAL APPLICATION OR GAME” MEANS ANY APPLICATION OR GAME
20 THAT A PERSON ACCESSES AND MANIPULATES USING A SPECIALIZED ELECTRONIC
21 GAMING DEVICE, COMPUTER, MOBILE DEVICE, TABLET, OR OTHER DEVICE WITH A
22 DISPLAY SCREEN, INCLUDING ANY ADD–ONS OR ADDITIONAL CONTENT FOR THE
23 APPLICATION OR GAME.
24 (E) “DIGITAL AUDIO WORK” MEANS A WORK THAT RESULTS FROM THE
25 FIXATION OF A SERIES OF MUSICAL, SPOKEN, OR OTHER SOUNDS THAT ARE
26 TRANSFERRED ELECTRONICALLY, INCLUDING:
27 (1) PRERECORDED OR LIVE SONGS;
28 (2) MUSIC;
HOUSE BILL 208 3
1 (3) READINGS OF BOOKS OR OTHER WRITTEN MATERIALS;
2 (4) SPEECHES;
3 (5) RINGTONES; OR
4 (6) OTHER SOUND RECORDINGS.
5 (F) (1) “DIGITAL AUDIOVISUAL WORK” MEANS A SERIES OF RELATED
6 IMAGES THAT, WHEN SHOWN IN SUCCESSION, IMPART AN IMPRESSION OF MOTION,
7 TOGETHER WITH ACCOMPANYING SOUNDS.
8 (2) “DIGITAL AUDIOVISUAL WORK” INCLUDES MOTION PICTURES,
9 MUSICALS, VIDEOS, NEWS AND ENTERTAINMENT PROGRAMS, AND LIVE EVENTS.
10 (G) “DIGITAL BOOK” MEANS A WORK THAT IS GENERALLY RECOGNIZED AS
11 A BOOK AND IS TRANSFERRED ELECTRONICALLY, INCLUDING A WORK OF FICTION
12 OR NONFICTION.
13 (H) (1) “DIGITAL CODE” MEANS A CODE THAT GRANTS THE PERSON
14 HOLDING THE CODE A RIGHT TO OBTAIN AN ADDITIONAL DIGITAL GOOD THAT MAY
15 BE OBTAINED BY ANY MEANS, INCLUDING TANGIBLE FORMS AND ELECTRONIC MAIL,
16 REGARDLESS OF WHETHER THE CODE IS DESIGNATED AS A SONG CODE, VIDEO
17 CODE, OR BOOK CODE.
18 (2) “DIGITAL CODE” INCLUDES:
19 (I) A A CODE USED TO ACCESS OR OBTAIN ANY SPECIFIED
20 DIGITAL GOODS, OR ANY ADDITIONAL DIGITAL GOODS THAT HAVE BEEN
21 PREVIOUSLY PURCHASED; AND
22 (II) PROMOTION CARDS OR CODES THAT ARE PURCHASED BY A
23 RETAILER OR OTHER BUSINESS ENTITY FOR USE BY THE RETAILER’S OR ENTITY’S
24 CUSTOMERS.
25 (I) (1) “DIGITAL GOOD” MEANS A DIGITAL APPLICATION OR GAME,
26 DIGITAL AUDIO WORK, DIGITAL AUDIOVISUAL WORK, DIGITAL BOOK, OR DIGITAL
27 CODE, WHETHER ELECTRONICALLY OR DIGITALLY DELIVERED OR ACCESSED.
28 (2) “DIGITAL GOOD” DOES NOT INCLUDE A CABLE TELEVISION
29 SERVICE, SATELLITE RELAY TELEVISION SERVICE, OR ANY OTHER DISTRIBUTION OF
30 TELEVISION, VIDEO, OR RADIO SERVICE.

1 [(c)] (J) “Person” includes an association, firm, partnership, corporation, or an
2 agent or employee of any of these entities.
3 [(d)] (K) “Property”, as used in § 14–2902(a) through (c) of this subtitle, includes:
4 (1) Merchandise;
5 (2) Real estate;
6 (3) Securities;
7 (4) Employment;
8 (5) A loan made at interest;
9 (6) Any contract relating to real estate, securities, service, employment, or
10 the making of loans at interest; or
11 (7) Anything else of value.
12 14–2904.
13 (A) A PERSON MAY NOT ADVERTISE OR OFFER FOR SALE A DIGITAL GOOD
14 USING TERMINOLOGY THAT A REASONABLE PERSON WOULD UNDERSTAND TO
15 CONFER AN UNRESTRICTED OWNERSHIP INTEREST IN THE DIGITAL GOOD, UNLESS,
16 AT THE TIME OF SALE:
17 (1) THE SELLER RECEIVES AN AFFIRMATIVE ACKNOWLEDGMENT
18 FROM THE PURCHASER OF THE FOLLOWING:
19 (I) THAT THE PURCHASER IS RECEIVING A LICENSE TO ACCESS
20 A DIGITAL GOOD;
21 (II) A COMPLETE LIST OF RESTRICTIONS AND CONDITIONS OF
22 THE LICENSE; AND
23 (III) THAT ACCESS TO THE DIGITAL GOOD MAY BE
24 UNILATERALLY REVOKED BY THE SELLER IF THE PURCHASER NO LONGER HOLDS A
25 RIGHT TO THE DIGITAL GOOD; AND OR
26 (2) THE SELLER PROVIDES TO THE PURCHASER A CLEAR AND
27 CONSPICUOUS STATEMENT THAT:
28 (I) STATES IN PLAIN LANGUAGE THAT THE PURCHASER IS
29 BUYING A LICENSE FOR THE DIGITAL GOOD; AND
HOUSE BILL 208 5
1 (II) INCLUDES A DIGITAL METHOD TO ACCESS THE LICENSE
2 DETAILS, TERMS, AND CONDITIONS.
3 (B) A PERSON MAY NOT ADVERTISE OR OFFER FOR SALE A DIGITAL GOOD
4 ALONGSIDE AN OPTION FOR A TIME–LIMITED RENTAL, UNLESS, AT THE TIME OF
5 SALE:
6 (1) THE SELLER RECEIVES AN AFFIRMATIVE ACKNOWLEDGMENT
7 FROM THE PURCHASER OF THE FOLLOWING:
8 (I) THAT THE PURCHASER IS RECEIVING A LICENSE TO ACCESS
9 A DIGITAL GOOD;
10 (II) A COMPLETE LIST OF RESTRICTIONS AND CONDITIONS OF
11 THE LICENSE; AND
12 (III) THAT ACCESS TO THE DIGITAL GOOD MAY BE
13 UNILATERALLY REVOKED BY THE SELLER IF THE PURCHASER NO LONGER HOLDS A
14 RIGHT TO THE DIGITAL GOOD; AND
15 (2) THE SELLER PROVIDES TO THE PURCHASER A CLEAR AND
16 CONSPICUOUS STATEMENT THAT:
17 (I) STATES IN PLAIN LANGUAGE THAT THE PURCHASER IS
18 BUYING A LICENSE FOR THE DIGITAL GOOD; AND
19 (II) INCLUDES A DIGITAL METHOD TO ACCESS THE LICENSE
20 DETAILS, TERMS, AND CONDITIONS.
21 (C) (B) THE AFFIRMATIVE ACKNOWLEDGMENTS ACKNOWLEDGMENT
22 REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTIONS SUBSECTION (A)(1) AND (B)(1) OF THIS SECTION
23 SHALL BE DISTINCT AND SEPARATE FROM ANY OTHER STATEMENT THAT THE
24 PURCHASER ACKNOWLEDGES.
25 (D) (C) THE CLEAR AND CONSPICUOUS STATEMENTS STATEMENT
26 REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTIONS SUBSECTION (A)(2) AND (B)(2) OF THIS SECTION
27 SHALL BE DISTINCT AND SEPARATE FROM ANY OTHER STATEMENTS, DETAILS,
28 TERMS, OR CONDITIONS.
29 (E) (D) THIS SECTION DOES NOT:
30 (1) REQUIRE A PERSON TO DOWNLOAD A DIGITAL GOOD; OR
6 HOUSE BILL 208
1 (2) PROHIBIT A PERSON FROM STORING A DIGITAL GOOD ON A
2 SERVER THAT CAN BE ACCESSED THROUGH THE INTERNET.
3 (F) (E) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO A PERSON WHO ADVERTISES
4 OR OFFERS FOR SALE:
5 (1) A SUBSCRIPTION–BASED SERVICE THAT PROVIDES ACCESS TO A
6 DIGITAL GOOD ONLY FOR THE DURATION OF THE SUBSCRIPTION;
7 (2) ACCESS TO A DIGITAL GOOD WITHOUT THE REQUIREMENT FOR
8 MONETARY CONSIDERATION; OR
9 (3) A DIGITAL GOOD FOR WHICH THE SELLER IS UNABLE TO REVOKE
10 ACCESS AFTER COMPLETION OF THE SALE, INCLUDING A DIGITAL GOOD THAT IS, AT
11 THE TIME OF PURCHASE, AVAILABLE FOR PERMANENT OFFLINE DOWNLOAD TO AN
12 EXTERNAL STORAGE SOURCE TO BE USED WITHOUT A CONNECTION TO THE
13 INTERNET.
14 (G) (F) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS GUILTY OF A
15 MISDEMEANOR AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $1,000
16 OR IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 1 YEAR OR BOTH.
17 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
18 October 1, 2025.
