A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Real Estate Commission – Continuing Education Requirements – Alterations
3 FOR the purpose of altering certain requirements for continuing education required for the
4 renewal of a license for real estate brokers, associate real estate brokers, and real
5 estate salespersons, including the number of clock hours, course topics, course
6 instructors, and course application fees; and generally relating to the Real Estate
7 Commission and continuing education requirements.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
9 Article – Business Occupations and Professions
10 Section 17–101(a), (d), and (k)
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2018 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Business Occupations and Professions
15 Section 17–315
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2018 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
20 Article – Business Occupations and Professions
21 17–101.
22 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
23 (d) “Commission” means the State Real Estate Commission.

1 (k) “Licensee” means a licensed real estate broker, a licensed associate real estate
2 broker, or a licensed real estate salesperson.
3 17–315.
4 (a) (1) To qualify for renewal of a license under this subtitle, a licensee shall
5 complete at least 15 clock hours of continuing education instruction, as provided in
6 subsection (b) of this section, during the preceding 2–year term.
7 (2) For a licensee who provides real estate brokerage services solely in
8 connection with nonresidential real estate, of the clock hours required under paragraph (1)
9 of this subsection, [1.5] 2 clock hours [may] SHALL be satisfied by a course regarding [fair
10 housing laws and regulations or] the federal Americans with Disabilities Act.
11 (3) A licensee holding a license from another state must complete at least
12 the number of clock hours of continuing education instruction required under paragraph
13 (1) of this subsection during each 2–year license term and may substitute clock hours of
14 continuing education instruction earned in another state, if those clock hours:
15 (i) are approved as real estate continuing education in that state;
16 and
17 (ii) meet the distribution requirements of subsection (b)(2) of this
18 section.
19 (4) The Commission shall grant the substitution of clock hours in
20 paragraph (3) of this subsection only if the other state permits the substitution of clock
21 hours of continuing education instruction approved by the Commission for a licensee of this
22 State.
23 (b) (1) The Commission shall approve the form, substance, and, as provided
24 under paragraphs (2) and (3) of this subsection, subject matter of all continuing education
25 courses.
26 (2) For a licensee who has been issued a renewal certificate under § 17–314
27 of this subtitle, the subject matter approved by the Commission shall:
28 (i) relate to real estate or to a subject matter intended to assist a
29 licensee in providing real estate brokerage services to the public in a more efficient and
30 effective manner, provided that the subject matter is related to helping the public buy or
31 sell real estate;
32 (ii) every 2 years, include at least one 3 clock hour course that
33 outlines relevant changes that have occurred in federal, State, or local laws and
34 regulations, court cases and industry trends that have an impact on those laws and
35 regulations, or any combination of those laws, regulations, court cases, and industry trends;

1 (iii) every 2 years, include at least one [1.5] 2 clock hour course that
2 outlines federal, State, and local fair housing laws and regulations, including fair housing
3 advertising;
4 (iv) every 2 years, include at least one 3 clock hour ethics course that
5 includes a discussion of:
6 1. the Maryland Code of Ethics;
7 2. [the practice of flipping;
8 3.] fraudulent real estate practices; and
9 [4.] 3. professionalism as it relates to the Maryland Code
10 of Ethics, including a discussion relating to conflict resolution and a licensee’s duty to
11 respect the public, peers, and property;
12 (v) every 2 years, include at least one 3 clock hour course that
13 includes the principles of real estate brokerage relationships and disclosures; and
14 (vi) every 2 years for the renewal of a real estate broker license and
15 the renewal of the license of an individual designated as a branch office manager or a team
16 leader, include at least one [3] 1.5 clock hour course that includes the requirements of
17 broker supervision.
18 (3) For a licensee who was issued an initial REAL ESTATE SALESPERSON
19 license under § 17–309 of this subtitle, the subject matter approved by the Commission
20 shall include:
21 (i) at least one 3 clock hour course for each of the following topics:
22 1. outlining contract writing, standard contract provisions,
23 key regional contract requirements, and standard addenda and contingencies;
24 2. the principles of real estate brokerage relationships and
25 disclosures [and disclosure requirements for sellers];
26 3. the principles of real estate practice in relation to:
27 A. advertising requirements for licensees;
28 B. the handling and [disclosure] DISBURSEMENT of escrow
29 funds; and
30 C. common ownership communities; and
4 HOUSE BILL 972
1 4. property management and landlord and tenant laws; and
2 (ii) at least one 1.5 clock hour course for each of the following topics:
3 1. professionalism as it relates to the Maryland Code of
4 Ethics, including a discussion relating to conflict resolution and a licensee’s duty of respect
5 for the public, peers, and property; and
6 2. real estate financing.
7 (4) The requirement of paragraph (2)(iii) of this subsection does not apply
8 to a licensee who provides real estate brokerage services solely in connection with
9 nonresidential real estate.
10 (5) To be acceptable for credit as a continuing education course under this
11 section, the course shall cover 1 or more topics approved by the Commission.
12 (c) (1) Continuing education courses may be conducted by:
13 (i) the Maryland Association of Realtors or its member boards;
14 (ii) the Real Estate Brokers of Baltimore, Inc.;
15 (iii) any similar professional association; or
16 (iv) an educational institution approved by the Commission.
17 (2) Continuing education courses shall be taught by a qualified instructor
18 who is experienced in the real estate industry AND SELECTED BY THE COURSE
19 PROVIDER.
20 (3) The Commission shall adopt regulations that provide for the conduct of
21 continuing education instruction courses by virtual or remote means.
22 (4) To verify the identity of an individual enrolled in a continuing education
23 course, the entity that conducts the continuing education course shall require, as a
24 condition of participating in the course, that the individual provide:
25 (i) for an in–person course, a photo identification; and
26 (ii) for a virtual or other remote course, a signed affidavit attesting
27 to the identity of the individual.
28 (d) If feasible, continuing education courses shall be offered at reasonable
29 intervals in each county and in each major geographic area of the larger counties.
HOUSE BILL 972 5
1 (e) Within 14 days after completion of a continuing education course, the entity
2 that conducted the course may submit the course completion information directly to the
3 Commission and the licensee by electronic means.
4 (f) The Commission may waive the requirements of this section for a licensee if
5 the licensee shows good cause for being unable to meet the requirements.
6 (g) The Commission shall require each course provider to pay a continuing
7 education course application fee [of $25] SET BY THE COMMISSION.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
9 October 1, 2025.
