HB 403/20 – JUD
By: Delegates Wilkins, Solomon, Allen, Alston, Bagnall, Barnes, Boafo, Boyce,
Charkoudian, Crutchfield, Davis, Ebersole, Fair, Feldmark, Foley,
Fraser–Hidalgo, Guyton, Harris, Healey, Hill, Kaufman, Korman, R. Lewis,
J. Long, Lopez, Martinez, McCaskill, Mireku–North, Palakovich Carr,
Pasteur, Pena–Melnyk, Phillips, Ruff, Ruth, Simmons, Spiegel, Stein,
Stewart, Taveras, Taylor, Terrasa, Valderrama, Vogel, Wells,
White Holland, Williams, Wims, Woods, Woorman, and Young
Introduced and read first time: January 31, 2025
Assigned to: Judiciary
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Immigration Enforcement – Sensitive Locations – Guidelines and Policies
3 (Protecting Sensitive Locations Act)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Attorney General to develop guidelines relating to
5 immigration enforcement at certain sensitive locations; requiring State agencies that
6 operate at certain sensitive locations to adopt certain policies or provide certain
7 written notice of a decision not to adopt certain policies; and generally relating to
8 immigration enforcement.
9 BY adding to
10 Article – State Government
11 Section 6–111
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
16 Article – State Government
17 6–111.
18 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “SENSITIVE LOCATION” MEANS:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb1006*
2 HOUSE BILL 1006
1 (1) A SCHOOL, INCLUDING A PRESCHOOL, PRIMARY OR SECONDARY
2 SCHOOL, VOCATIONAL OR TRADE SCHOOL, OR COLLEGE OR UNIVERSITY;
3 (2) A MEDICAL OR MENTAL HEALTH CARE FACILITY, INCLUDING A
4 HOSPITAL, A DOCTOR’S OFFICE, A HEALTH CLINIC, A VACCINATION OR TESTING
5 SITE, AN URGENT CARE CENTER, A SITE THAT SERVES PREGNANT INDIVIDUALS, OR
6 A COMMUNITY HEALTH CENTER;
7 (3) A PLACE OF WORSHIP OR RELIGIOUS STUDY, WHETHER IN A
8 STRUCTURE DEDICATED TO ACTIVITIES OF FAITH OR A TEMPORARY FACILITY OR
9 LOCATION WHERE ACTIVITIES OF FAITH ARE TAKING PLACE;
10 (4) A PLACE WHERE CHILDREN GATHER, INCLUDING A PLAYGROUND,
11 RECREATION CENTER, CHILDCARE CENTER, BEFORE– OR AFTER–SCHOOL CARE
12 CENTER, FOSTER CARE FACILITY, GROUP HOME FOR CHILDREN, OR SCHOOL BUS
13 STOP;
14 (5) A SOCIAL SERVICES ESTABLISHMENT, INCLUDING A CRISIS
15 CENTER, DOMESTIC VIOLENCE SHELTER, VICTIMS SERVICES CENTER, CHILD
16 ADVOCACY CENTER, SUPERVISED VISITATION CENTER, FAMILY JUSTICE CENTER,
17 COMMUNITY–BASED ORGANIZATION, FACILITY THAT SERVES DISABLED PERSONS,
18 HOMELESS SHELTER, DRUG OR ALCOHOL COUNSELING AND TREATMENT FACILITY,
19 OR FOOD BANK OR PANTRY OR OTHER ESTABLISHMENT DISTRIBUTING FOOD OR
20 OTHER ESSENTIALS OF LIFE TO PEOPLE IN NEED;
21 (6) A PLACE WHERE DISASTER OR EMERGENCY RESPONSE AND
22 RELIEF IS BEING PROVIDED, INCLUDING ALONG EVACUATION ROUTES, WHERE
23 SHELTER OR EMERGENCY SUPPLIES, FOOD, OR WATER ARE BEING DISTRIBUTED, OR
24 REGISTRATION FOR DISASTER–RELATED ASSISTANCE OR FAMILY REUNIFICATION IS
25 UNDERWAY;
26 (7) A PLACE WHERE A FUNERAL, GRAVESIDE CEREMONY, ROSARY,
27 WEDDING, OR OTHER RELIGIOUS OR CIVIL CEREMONY OR OBSERVANCE OCCURS;
28 (8) A PLACE WHERE THERE IS AN ONGOING PARADE,
29 DEMONSTRATION, OR RALLY;
30 (9) A COURTHOUSE; AND
31 (10) ANY OTHER LOCATION DEEMED APPROPRIATE BY THE ATTORNEY
32 GENERAL.
HOUSE BILL 1006 3
1 (B) (1) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL, IN CONSULTATION WITH
2 APPROPRIATE STAKEHOLDERS, SHALL DEVELOP GUIDELINES FOR ENTITIES
3 OPERATING AT SENSITIVE LOCATIONS THAT LIMIT IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT AT
4 SENSITIVE LOCATIONS TO THE FULLEST EXTENT POSSIBLE CONSISTENT WITH
5 FEDERAL AND STATE LAW.
6 (2) A STATE AGENCY THAT OPERATES AT A SENSITIVE LOCATION
7 SHALL:
8 (I) ADOPT POLICIES CONSISTENT WITH THE GUIDELINES
9 DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION; OR
10 (II) PROVIDE WRITTEN NOTICE TO THE ATTORNEY GENERAL OF
11 ITS DECISION NOT TO ADOPT POLICIES CONSISTENT WITH THE GUIDELINES
12 DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION, INCLUDING:
13 1. AN EXPLANATION OF THE REASONS FOR NOT
14 ADOPTING POLICIES; AND
15 2. COPIES OF ANY EXISTING POLICIES RELATING TO
16 IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT AT THE SENSITIVE LOCATION.
17 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act is an emergency
18 measure, is necessary for the immediate preservation of the public health or safety, has
19 been passed by a yea and nay vote supported by three–fifths of all the members elected to
20 each of the two Houses of the General Assembly, and shall take effect from the date it is
21 enacted.
