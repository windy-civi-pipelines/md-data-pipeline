A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Procedure – U Nonimmigrant Status Petitions
3 FOR the purpose of altering the requirements for certification of a U Nonimmigrant Status
4 Petition; altering the individuals eligible to request a certain official to certify victim
5 helpfulness on a certain form relating to certification of a U Nonimmigrant Status
6 Petition; authorizing disclosure of certain information under certain circumstances;
7 and generally relating to U Nonimmigrant Status petitions.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Criminal Procedure
10 Section 11–930 and 11–931
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2018 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 BY adding to
14 Article – Criminal Procedure
15 Section 11–932
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2018 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
20 Article – Criminal Procedure
21 11–930.
22 (a) In this part the following words have the meanings indicated.
23 (b) “Certifying entity” means:

2 HOUSE BILL 579
1 (1) a State or local law enforcement agency;
2 (2) a State’s Attorney or deputy or assistant State’s Attorney;
3 (3) any other authority that has responsibility for the detection,
4 investigation, or prosecution of a qualifying crime or criminal activity; or
5 (4) an agency that has criminal, CIVIL, FAMILY, OR ADMINISTRATIVE
6 detection [or], investigative, OR PROSECUTORIAL jurisdiction in the agency’s respective
7 areas of expertise, including child protective services, ADULT PROTECTIVE SERVICES, the
8 Commission on Civil Rights, and the Maryland Department of Labor.
9 (c) “Certifying official” means:
10 (1) the head of a certifying entity;
11 (2) an individual in a supervisory role who has been specifically designated
12 by the head of a certifying entity to provide U Nonimmigrant Status certifications on behalf
13 of that entity; or
14 (3) any other certifying official defined under Title 8, § 214.14(a)(3)(i) of the
15 Code of Federal Regulations.
16 (d) “FEDERAL U VISA GUIDELINES” MEANS FEDERAL STATUTES,
17 REGULATIONS, POLICIES, PUBLICATIONS, GUIDANCE, AND INSTRUCTIONS
18 RELATING TO U NONIMMIGRANT STATUS, INCLUDING 8 U.S.C. § 1101(A)(15)(U), 8
19 U.S.C. § 1184(O), 8 U.S.C. § 1367, 72 FED. REG. 53014 (SEPT. 17, 2007), 8 C.F.R. §
20 214.14, AND 8 U.S.C. § 1255(M)(1).
21 (E) “Qualifying crime” includes a criminal offense for which the nature and
22 elements of the offense are substantially similar to the criminal activity described in
23 subsection [(e)] (F) of this section and the attempt, conspiracy, or solicitation to commit the
24 offense.
25 [(e)] (F) “Qualifying criminal activity” means criminal activity under §
26 1101(a)(15)(U)(iii) of the United States Code OR THAT IS DESCRIBED IN FEDERAL U VISA
27 GUIDELINES.
28 11–931.
29 (a) (1) For purposes of filing a petition with the United States Citizenship and
30 Immigration Services for U Nonimmigrant Status, a victim or the victim’s parent,
31 guardian, [or] next friend, ATTORNEY, VICTIM ADVOCATE, OR OTHER
32 REPRESENTATIVE may request a certifying official of a certifying entity to certify victim
33 helpfulness on a Form I–918, Supplement B certification if the victim:
HOUSE BILL 579 3
1 [(1)] (I) was a victim of a qualifying criminal activity and has been
2 helpful, IS BEING HELPFUL, OR IS LIKELY TO BE HELPFUL [to the certifying entity] in
3 the detection, investigation, or prosecution, INCLUDING SENTENCING, of that qualifying
4 criminal activity;
5 [(2)] (II) was under the age of 16 years on the date that an act that
6 constitutes an element of qualifying criminal activity first occurred and the victim’s parent,
7 guardian, or next friend has been helpful to the certifying entity in the detection,
8 investigation, or prosecution, INCLUDING SENTENCING, of that qualifying criminal
9 activity; or
10 [(3)] (III) is incapacitated or incompetent and the victim’s parent,
11 guardian, or next friend has been helpful to the certifying entity in the detection,
12 investigation, or prosecution, INCLUDING SENTENCING, of that qualifying criminal
13 activity.
14 (2) (I) A VICTIM OF A QUALIFYING CRIME OR A QUALIFYING
15 CRIMINAL ACTIVITY IS ELIGIBLE FOR CERTIFICATION.
16 (II) IF A VICTIM IS APPLYING FOR CERTIFICATION AS A RESULT
17 OF BEING A VICTIM OF MORE THAN ONE QUALIFYING CRIME OR QUALIFYING
18 CRIMINAL ACTIVITY, EACH QUALIFYING CRIME AND QUALIFYING CRIMINAL
19 ACTIVITY SHALL BE LISTED ON THE CERTIFICATION.
20 (b) FOR PURPOSES OF DETERMINING HELPFULNESS FOR A REQUEST FOR
21 CERTIFICATION UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION, AN INDIVIDUAL SHALL
22 BE CONSIDERED HELPFUL IF, SINCE THE INITIATION OF HELPFULNESS, THE
23 INDIVIDUAL HAS NOT UNREASONABLY REFUSED TO COOPERATE OR FAILED TO
24 PROVIDE INFORMATION AND ASSISTANCE REASONABLY REQUESTED BY A
25 CERTIFYING ENTITY.
26 (C) For purposes of determining helpfulness under subsection (a) of this section,
27 if the victim or the victim’s parent, guardian, or next friend is assisting, has assisted, or is
28 likely to assist law enforcement authorities in the detection, investigation, or prosecution
29 of qualifying criminal activity, the victim or the victim’s parent, guardian, or next friend
30 shall be considered to be helpful, to have been helpful, or likely to be helpful.
31 [(c)] (D) If the victim or the victim’s parent, guardian, or next friend satisfies
32 the criteria specified under subsection (a) of this section, the certifying official shall fully
33 complete and sign the Form I–918, Supplement B certification and, with respect to victim
34 helpfulness, include:
35 (1) specific details about the nature of the crime DETECTED, investigated
36 [or], prosecuted, OR SENTENCED;

1 (2) a detailed description of the victim’s PAST OR PRESENT helpfulness or
2 likely helpfulness to the detection, investigation, or prosecution, INCLUDING
3 SENTENCING, of the criminal activity; and
4 (3) copies of any documents in the possession of the certifying official that
5 evince the harm endured by the victim due to the criminal activity.
6 [(d)] (E) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, the
7 certifying entity shall certify or decline certification of the Form I–918, Supplement B
8 certification within 90 days after receiving a request under subsection (a) of this section.
9 (2) If a noncitizen victim is the subject of removal, exclusion, or deportation
10 proceedings or subject to a final order of removal, exclusion, or deportation, the certifying
11 entity shall certify or decline certification of the Form I–918, Supplement B certification
12 within 14 days after receiving a request under subsection (a) of this section.
13 [(e)] (F) A current investigation, the filing of charges, a prosecution, or a
14 conviction is not required for a victim or the victim’s parent, guardian, [or] next friend,
15 ATTORNEY, VICTIM ADVOCATE, OR OTHER REPRESENTATIVE to request and obtain the
16 Form I–918, Supplement B certification under this section.
17 (G) THERE IS NO STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS FOR WHEN A QUALIFYING
18 CRIMINAL ACTIVITY OCCURRED RELATIVE TO THE REQUEST FOR CERTIFICATION OF
19 VICTIM HELPFULNESS UNDER THIS SECTION.
20 (H) IF THE CERTIFYING OFFICIAL CANNOT DETERMINE WHETHER THE
21 APPLICANT IS A VICTIM OF A QUALIFYING CRIMINAL ACTIVITY OR DETERMINES
22 THAT THE APPLICANT DOES NOT QUALIFY FOR CERTIFICATION, THE CERTIFYING
23 OFFICIAL SHALL PROVIDE A WRITTEN EXPLANATION TO THE VICTIM OR THE
24 VICTIM’S PARENT, GUARDIAN, NEXT FRIEND, ATTORNEY, VICTIM ADVOCATE, OR
25 OTHER REPRESENTATIVE SETTING FORTH REASONS WHY THE AVAILABLE EVIDENCE
26 DOES NOT SUPPORT ISSUANCE OF THE CERTIFICATION.
27 [(f)] (I) A certifying official may DENY OR withdraw the certification provided
28 under this section only [on refusal to provide information and assistance when reasonably
29 requested of:
30 (1) the victim; or

32 age of 16 years on the date that an act that constitutes an element of qualifying criminal
33 activity first occurred or if the victim is incapacitated or incompetent] IF, AFTER

35 THE HELPFULNESS UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION UNREASONABLY

1 REFUSED TO COOPERATE OR FAILED TO PROVIDE INFORMATION OR ASSISTANCE
2 REASONABLY REQUESTED BY A CERTIFYING OFFICIAL.
3 (J) (1) NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER PROVISION OF THIS SECTION, A
4 CERTIFYING OFFICIAL’S COMPLETION OF A CERTIFICATION FORM MAY NOT BE
5 CONSIDERED SUFFICIENT EVIDENCE THAT AN APPLICANT FOR A U OR A T VISA HAS
6 MET ALL ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS FOR THAT VISA, AND COMPLETION OF A
7 CERTIFICATION FORM BY A CERTIFYING OFFICIAL MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO
8 GUARANTEE THAT THE VICTIM WILL RECEIVE IMMIGRATION RELIEF UNDER
9 FEDERAL LAW.
10 (2) COMPLETION OF A CERTIFICATION FORM BY A CERTIFYING
11 OFFICIAL ONLY SERVES AS VERIFICATION OF THE FACTUAL INFORMATION
12 RELEVANT FOR A FEDERAL IMMIGRATION OFFICIAL TO DETERMINE ELIGIBILITY
13 FOR A U OR A T VISA.
14 (3) BY COMPLETING A CERTIFICATION FORM, THE CERTIFYING
15 OFFICIAL ATTESTS THAT THE INFORMATION IS TRUE AND CORRECT TO THE BEST OF
16 THE CERTIFYING OFFICIAL’S KNOWLEDGE.
17 (4) THIS SECTION DOES NOT LIMIT THE MANNER IN WHICH A
18 CERTIFYING OFFICIAL OR CERTIFYING AGENCY MAY:
19 (I) DESCRIBE WHETHER THE PERSON HAS BEEN HELPFUL TO
20 THE CERTIFYING ENTITY; OR
21 (II) PROVIDE ANY ADDITIONAL INFORMATION THE CERTIFYING
22 OFFICIAL OR CERTIFYING ENTITY BELIEVES MIGHT BE RELEVANT TO THE
23 ADJUDICATION OF A U OR A T VISA APPLICATION.
24 [(g) A certifying entity may disclose information relating to a victim who is seeking
25 or has obtained U Nonimmigrant Status only:
26 (1) in order to comply with federal law, court order, or a discovery
27 obligation in the prosecution of a criminal offense; or
28 (2) after adult petitioners for U Nonimmigrant Status or adult U
29 Nonimmigrant Status holders have provided written consent for the disclosure of the
30 information.
31 (h) (1) Except in cases of willful or wanton misconduct, a certifying entity or
32 certifying official who acts or fails to act in good faith in compliance with this section has
33 the immunity from liability described under § 5–643 of the Courts Article.

5 (A) A CERTIFYING ENTITY OR A CERTIFYING OFFICIAL MAY DISCLOSE
6 INFORMATION RELATING TO A VICTIM WHO IS SEEKING CERTIFICATION, OR WHO IS
7 SEEKING OR HAS OBTAINED U NONIMMIGRANT STATUS ONLY:
8 (1) TO COMPLY WITH FEDERAL LAW, A COURT ORDER, OR A
9 DISCOVERY OBLIGATION IN THE PROSECUTION OF A CRIMINAL OFFENSE; OR
10 (2) IF APPLICABLE, AFTER THE VICTIM HAS WAIVED

12 UNDER 34 U.S.C. § 12291(B)(2).
13 (B) THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO ALTER OR DIMINISH THE
14 DUTIES AND REQUIREMENTS OF A LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER, A STATE’S
15 ATTORNEY, OR THE ATTORNEY GENERAL FROM DISCLOSING EXCULPATORY
16 INFORMATION TO A DEFENDANT IN A CRIMINAL CASE.
17 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
18 October 1, 2025.
