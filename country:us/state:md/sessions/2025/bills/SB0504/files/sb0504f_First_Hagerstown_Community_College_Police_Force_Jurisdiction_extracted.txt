A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Hagerstown Community College Police Force – Jurisdiction
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing a Hagerstown Community College police officer to exercise
4 powers granted to a peace and police officer within Washington County under certain
5 circumstances; and generally relating to the Hagerstown Community College Police
6 Force.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – Education
9 Section 16–407.1
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Education
15 16–407.1.
16 (a) There is a Hagerstown Community College Police Force.
17 (b) (1) A Hagerstown Community College police officer has the powers granted
18 to a peace and police officer.
19 (2) (i) [A] EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS
20 PARAGRAPH, A Hagerstown Community College police officer may exercise these powers
21 only on property that is owned, leased, operated by, or under the control of Hagerstown
22 Community College.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0504*
2 SENATE BILL 504
1 (ii) The police officer may [not] exercise these powers on any other
2 property [unless] WITHIN WASHINGTON COUNTY IF:
3 1. Engaged in fresh pursuit of a suspected offender;
4 2. Necessary to facilitate the orderly flow of traffic to and
5 from property owned, leased, operated by, or under the control of Hagerstown Community
6 College; or
7 3. Ordered to do so by the Governor.
8 (c) (1) In consultation with the Secretary of State Police and the Maryland
9 Police Training and Standards Commission, the Hagerstown Community College Board of
10 Trustees shall adopt standards, qualifications, and prerequisites of character, training,
11 education, human and public relations, and experience for Hagerstown Community College
12 police officers, including standards for the performance of their duties.
13 (2) To the extent practicable, the Board shall adopt standards that are
14 similar to the standards adopted for the Department of State Police.
15 (d) The Hagerstown Community College Board of Trustees shall adopt
16 regulations governing the operation and conduct of the Hagerstown Community College
17 Police Force and of Hagerstown Community College police officers.
18 (E) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION MAY BE CONSTRUED TO:
19 (1) SUPERSEDE THE AUTHORITY OR JURISDICTION OF:
20 (I) THE WASHINGTON COUNTY SHERRIFF’S OFFICE; OR
21 (II) ANY OTHER LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCY, AS DEFINED IN §
22 1–101(C) OF THE PUBLIC SAFETY ARTICLE; OR
23 (2) LIMIT THE AUTHORITY OR JURISDICTION OF THE HAGERSTOWN
24 COMMUNITY COLLEGE POLICE FORCE AUTHORIZED UNDER § 5–802 OF THE
25 CRIMINAL LAW ARTICLE.
26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
27 1, 2025.
