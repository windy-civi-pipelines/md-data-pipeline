A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Pesticide and Pest Control – Revisions to Pesticide Applicator’s Law and Repeal
3 of Obsolete Provisions
4 FOR the purpose of establishing requirements for the commercial application of general
5 use pesticides, the commercial application of restricted use pesticides, and the
6 private application of restricted use pesticides; requiring a certified private
7 applicator to ensure that certain individuals working under the certified applicator’s
8 supervision meet certain requirements; specifying the responsibility and liability of
9 certified applicators for the application of certain pesticides; repealing provisions of
10 law concerning the sale and use of cyclodiene termiticide and antifouling paint
11 containing tributyltin compound; and generally relating to pesticide and pest control.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
13 Article – Agriculture
14 Section 5–201(a), (c), (i), (j), (o), (p), (r), and (s)
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 BY repealing
18 Article – Agriculture
19 Section 5–209 and 5–210.5; and 5–901 through 5–906 and the subtitle “Subtitle 9.
20 Antifouling Paints”
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
23 BY adding to
24 Article – Agriculture
25 Section 5–209
26 Annotated Code of Maryland
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

4 Article – Agriculture
5 5–201.
6 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
7 (c) “Certified applicator” means a person who is certified by the Secretary under
8 this subtitle.
9 (i) “Pest control” means engaging in, or offering to engage in, recommending,
10 advertising, soliciting the use of, supervising the use of, or using, a pesticide or a device for
11 the identification, control, eradication, mitigation, detection, inspection, or prevention of a
12 pest in, on, or around any house, building, water, air, land, plant, structure, or animal.
13 (j) (1) “Pest control applicator” means a person engaged in the business of pest
14 control.
15 (2) Except as provided by the Secretary, “pest control applicator” includes
16 a person who applies pesticides on any premises where the public is commonly invited for
17 the sale of goods or services.
18 (o) “Private applicator” means a person who uses a restricted use pesticide for the
19 purpose of producing any agricultural commodity on property owned or rented by the
20 applicator or applicator’s employer.
21 (p) “Public agency applicator” means a person employed by a unit of federal,
22 State, county or local government or any training institution which is engaged in pest
23 control.
24 (r) “Restricted use pesticide” means a pesticide so classified by the provisions in
25 this title or by the federal government or the Secretary of Agriculture, State of Maryland.
26 (s) “Supervision” means, unless otherwise provided by federal or State labeling,
27 the act or process whereby the application of a pesticide is made by a competent person
28 acting under the instructions and control of a certified applicator who is:
29 (1) Responsible for actions of that person; and
30 (2) Available when needed, though the certified applicator may not be
31 physically present at the time and place the pesticide is applied.

26 APPLICATOR WHO IS CERTIFIED IN THE SPECIFIC CATEGORY OF APPLICATION

17 termites.
18 (b) (1) On and after October 1, 1987, each cyclodiene termiticide distributed,
19 sold, or offered for sale in this State shall be classified as a restricted use pesticide.
20 (2) A person may distribute, sell, or offer for sale a cyclodiene termiticide
21 only if:
22 (i) The cyclodiene termiticide is designated as a restricted use
23 pesticide in a manner determined by the Department; and

26 2. Registered employee who has completed a course in
27 termiticide application that is approved by the Department.

29 (i) A certified pesticide applicator; or
30 (ii) A registered employee who has completed a course in termiticide
31 application that is approved by the Department and is making the application under the
32 direct supervision of a certified pesticide applicator.

4 [5–901.
5 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
6 (b) “Acceptable release rate” means a measured release rate equal to or less than
7 5.0 micrograms per square centimeter per day at steady state conditions determined in
8 accordance with the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency testing procedure, as outlined
9 in the Agency’s call–in notice of July 29, 1986 on tributyltin in antifouling paints under the
10 Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act.
11 (c) “Antifouling paint” means a compound, coating, paint, or treatment applied or
12 used for the purpose of controlling freshwater or marine fouling organisms on vessels.
13 (d) “Commercial boatyard” means:
14 (1) A facility that engages for hire in the construction, storage,
15 maintenance, repair, or refurbishing of vessels; or
16 (2) An independent marine maintenance contractor who engages in any of
17 the activities under paragraph (1) of this subsection.
18 (e) “Tributyltin compound” means any organotin compound that has 3 normal
19 butyl groups attached to a tin atom and with or without an anion, such as chloride, fluoride,
20 or oxide.
21 (f) (1) “Vessel” means a watercraft or other contrivance used as a means of
22 transportation on water, whether self–propelled or otherwise.
23 (2) “Vessel” includes barges and tugs.]
24 [5–902.
25 (a) (1) Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, a person may not
26 distribute, possess, sell, offer for sale, use, or offer for use any antifouling paint containing

28 (2) A person may not distribute, possess, sell, offer for sale, use, or offer for
29 use any substance that contains a tributyltin compound in concentrated form and that is
30 labeled for mixing with paint by the user to produce an antifouling paint for use on a vessel.
6 SENATE BILL 249
1 (b) (1) A person may distribute or sell an antifouling paint containing a
2 tributyltin compound with an acceptable release rate to the owner or agent of a commercial

5 purchase for application an antifouling paint containing tributyltin with an acceptable
6 release rate, if the antifouling paint:
7 (i) Is applied only within a commercial boatyard; and
8 (ii) Is applied only to vessels exceeding 25 meters in length or that

11 antifouling paint containing a tributyltin compound, if the antifouling paint:
12 (1) Is in a spray can of 16 ounces or less;
13 (2) Is commonly referred to as an outboard or lower drive unit paint; and
14 (3) Has an acceptable release rate.]
15 [5–903.

18 release rate may travel and dock in State waters.]
