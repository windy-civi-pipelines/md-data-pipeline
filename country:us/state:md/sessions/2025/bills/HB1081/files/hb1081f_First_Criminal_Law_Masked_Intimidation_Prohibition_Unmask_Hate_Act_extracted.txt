A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Masked Intimidation – Prohibition
3 (Unmask Hate Act)
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from intentionally harassing, intimidating, or
5 threatening another person while hiding or concealing their face; and generally
6 relating to masked intimidation.
7 BY adding to
8 Article – Criminal Law
9 Section 3–810
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Criminal Law
15 3–810.
16 (A) A PERSON MAY NOT INTENTIONALLY HARASS, INTIMIDATE, OR
17 THREATEN A PERSON:
18 (1) WHILE HIDING OR CONCEALING THEIR FACE WITH A MASK OR ANY
19 OTHER ARTICLE OR DEVICE WITH THE PURPOSE OF CONCEALING THE PERSON’S
20 IDENTITY; AND
21 (2) WITH THE INTENT TO PLACE ANOTHER PERSON IN REASONABLE
22 FEAR OF THEIR PHYSICAL SAFETY.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb1081*
2 HOUSE BILL 1081
1 (B) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR
2 AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO:
3 (1) FOR A FIRST OFFENSE, IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 90 DAYS
4 OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $500 OR BOTH; AND
5 (2) FOR A SECOND OR SUBSEQUENT OFFENSE, IMPRISONMENT NOT
6 EXCEEDING 180 DAYS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $1,000 OR BOTH.
7 (C) NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER LAW, A CONVICTION UNDER THIS
8 SECTION MAY NOT MERGE WITH A CONVICTION FOR ANY OTHER CRIME BASED ON
9 THE ACT ESTABLISHING THE VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION.
10 (D) A SENTENCE IMPOSED UNDER THIS SECTION MAY BE SEPARATE FROM
11 AND CONSECUTIVE TO OR CONCURRENT WITH A SENTENCE FOR ANY CRIME BASED
12 ON THE ACT ESTABLISHING THE VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION.
13 (E) IT IS AN AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE IN A PROCEEDING UNDER THIS
14 SECTION THAT THE PERSON WAS:
15 (1) WEARING A MASK OR FACE COVERING IN CELEBRATION OF A
16 HOLIDAY, CELEBRATION, OR OTHER EVENT WHERE MASKS OR FACE COVERINGS ARE
17 TRADITIONALLY WORN;
18 (2) WEARING A MASK, HOOD, ARTICLE, OR OTHER DEVICE AS
19 PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT AS PART OF THE PERSON’S OCCUPATION,
20 TRADE, OR PROFESSION;
21 (3) WEARING A MASK, HOOD, ARTICLE, OR OTHER DEVICE FOR THE
22 PURPOSE OF PROTECTION FROM WEATHER ELEMENTS OR WHILE PARTICIPATING IN
23 A WINTER SPORT;
24 (4) WEARING A MASK, HOOD, ARTICLE, OR OTHER DEVICE IN AN
25 ARTISTIC OR THEATRICAL PRODUCTION OR CELEBRATION;
26 (5) WEARING A GAS MASK OR OTHER PROTECTIVE FACIAL COVERING
27 FOR THE PURPOSES OF PROTECTION DURING OR RELATING TO AN EMERGENCY
28 SITUATION OR DURING AN EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT DRILL;
29 (6) WEARING A MASK TO LIMIT THE SPREAD OF AIRBORNE ILLNESSES;
30 OR
31 (7) WEARING ANY GARMENT FOR RELIGIOUS PURPOSES.
HOUSE BILL 1081 3
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
2 October 1, 2025.
