HB 1000/24 – ENT (PRE–FILED)
By: Delegate Ruff
Requested: October 29, 2024
Introduced and read first time: January 8, 2025
Assigned to: Environment and Transportation
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Real Property – Residential Contracts of Sale – Buyer Privacy Rights
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a contract of sale for single–family residential real property
4 from containing the name of an individual buyer prior to acceptance of the contract
5 of sale by the seller; and generally relating to residential contracts of sale.
6 BY adding to
7 Article – Real Property
8 Section 10–715
9 Annotated Code of Maryland
10 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
11 Preamble
12 WHEREAS, Under § 20–702 of the State Government Article, it is the policy of the
13 State to provide for fair housing throughout the State to all, regardless of race, color,
14 religion, sex, familial status, national origin, marital status, sexual orientation, gender
15 identity, disability, or source of income; and
16 WHEREAS, The name of a buyer of real property, when the buyer is a natural
17 person, can hold or be used to access information about certain of these protected
18 classifications; and
19 WHEREAS, Advances in modern technology have increased access to personal
20 information that may be indicative of a person’s membership in a protected class; and
21 WHEREAS, Fair housing guidelines and protections alone are inadequate to protect
22 citizens against the intentional or unintentional misuse of available information about a
23 buyer of real property; and
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb0013*
2 HOUSE BILL 13
1 WHEREAS, The act of removing the buyer’s name from the contract of sale, prior to
2 contract acceptance, will help eliminate bias, intentional or otherwise, in housing decisions;
3 now, therefore,
4 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
5 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
6 Article – Real Property
7 10–715.
8 (A) (1) THIS SECTION APPLIES ONLY TO A CONTRACT OF SALE FOR
9 SINGLE–FAMILY RESIDENTIAL REAL PROPERTY THAT AN INDIVIDUAL BUYER
10 EXECUTES WITH THE SERVICES OF A REAL ESTATE BROKER.
11 (2) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO A PURCHASE BY:
12 (I) A CORPORATE OR OTHER BUSINESS ENTITY; OR
13 (II) A FIDUCIARY ACTING FOR THE BENEFIT OF A THIRD PARTY.
14 (B) IT IS THE INTENT OF THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY TO ELIMINATE BIAS,
15 INTENTIONAL OR OTHERWISE, IN HOUSING DECISIONS CONCERNING THE SALE OF
16 SINGLE–FAMILY RESIDENTIAL REAL PROPERTY TO AN INDIVIDUAL BUYER BY
17 REMOVING THE NAME OF THE BUYER FROM THE CONTRACT OF SALE PRIOR TO
18 ACCEPTANCE OF THE CONTRACT BY THE SELLER.
19 (C) A RESIDENTIAL CONTRACT OF SALE MAY NOT CONTAIN THE NAME OF AN
20 INDIVIDUAL BUYER PRIOR TO ACCEPTANCE OF THE CONTRACT BY THE SELLER.
21 (D) BEFORE OR DURING THE PREPARATION OF A RESIDENTIAL CONTRACT
22 OF SALE, THE INDIVIDUAL BUYER SHALL EXECUTE WITH THE BUYER’S REAL ESTATE
23 BROKER:
24 (1) A BUYER–BROKER AGREEMENT; OR
25 (2) AN UNREPRESENTED BUYER AGREEMENT THAT:
26 (I) IDENTIFIES THE BUYER AS AN INDIVIDUAL; AND
27 (II) STATES THAT THE BUYER:
28 1. HAS NOT MISREPRESENTED THE BUYER’S STATUS AS
29 AN INDIVIDUAL;
HOUSE BILL 13 3
1 2. HAS NOT BEEN CONVICTED OF FRAUD FOR A
2 MISREPRESENTATION MADE IN CONNECTION WITH THE PURCHASE OF RESIDENTIAL
3 REAL PROPERTY; AND
4 3. INTENDS TO OCCUPY THE RESIDENTIAL REAL
5 PROPERTY AS THE INDIVIDUAL BUYER’S PRINCIPAL PLACE OF RESIDENCE.
6 (E) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION MAY BE INTERPRETED TO AFFECT
7 REQUIREMENTS REGARDING THE IDENTIFICATION OF AN INDIVIDUAL BUYER:
8 (1) IN A DEED OR OTHER INSTRUMENT OFFERED FOR RECORDATION
9 IN THE LAND RECORDS UNDER TITLE 3 OF THIS ARTICLE;
10 (2) IN A MORTGAGE APPLICATION, MORTGAGE, OR DEED OF TRUST
11 SECURED BY RESIDENTIAL REAL PROPERTY;
12 (3) MARKING THE INDIVIDUAL BUYER’S INITIALS ON THE
13 RESIDENTIAL CONTRACT OF SALE AS VALID AND BINDING ACCORDING TO THE
14 STATUTE OF FRAUDS; OR
15 (4) ON THE RESIDENTIAL CONTRACT OF SALE AT THE TIME OF
16 CONTRACT ACCEPTANCE BY THE SELLER.
17 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall be construed to
18 apply only prospectively and may not be applied or interpreted to have any effect on or
19 application to any residential contract of sale before the effective date of this Act.
20 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
21 October 1, 2026.
