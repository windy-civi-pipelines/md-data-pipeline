CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Agriculture – Roosters Crimes Relating to Animals – Restrictions on Keeping
3 Roosters
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting, unless authorized by the Department of Agriculture
5 beginning on a certain date, a certain person from keeping a
6 rooster movement–constrained through the use of a certain enclosure or tether
7 certain number of roosters on certain property beginning on a certain date, subject
8 to certain exceptions; authorizing the Department to work in cooperation with a local
9 animal control or law enforcement officer to enforce this Act; and generally relating
10 to restrictions on keeping roosters in the State.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Agriculture Criminal Law
13 Section 3–105.2 10–608.1
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2016 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
16 Preamble
17 WHEREAS, Despite its illegality in Maryland, ongoing issues persist with
18 cockfighting; and
19 WHEREAS, Breeding roosters for cockfighting is directly linked to the spread of
20 Avian Flu; now, therefore,
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0375*
2 SENATE BILL 375
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Agriculture
4 3–105.2.
5 Article – Criminal Law
6 10–608.1.
7 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
8 INDICATED.
9 (2) “ENCLOSURE” MEANS A STRUCTURE USED FOR CONFINEMENT,
10 INCLUDING A WIRE CAGE OR AN ITEM TRADITIONALLY USED FOR ANOTHER
11 PURPOSE SUCH AS A WATER TANK OR PLASTIC BARREL, THAT:
12 (I) IS USED TO PROVIDE LONG–TERM HOUSING FOR ONE ADULT
13 ROOSTER; AND
14 (II) PREVENTS INTERACTION WITH OTHER ROOSTERS.
15 (2) (3) “PROPERTY” MEANS:
16 (I) A A PARCEL OF LAND; OR
17 (II) A A COMBINATION OF PARCELS OF LAND OPERATING AS A
18 SINGLE UNIT.
19 (3) (4) “ROOSTER” MEANS A MALE CHICKEN THAT:
20 (I) IS IS AT LEAST 6 MONTHS OLD;
21 (II) HAS HAS FULLY DEVELOPED AN ADULT PLUMAGE; OR
22 (III) IS IS CAPABLE OF CROWING.
23 (B) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO:
24 (1) COMMERCIAL COMMERCIAL POULTRY PRODUCERS, AS DEFINED
25 IN § 8–704.2 OF THIS ARTICLE THE AGRICULTURE ARTICLE;
26 (2) A A PUBLIC SCHOOL OR NONPUBLIC SCHOOL THAT RECEIVES
27 STATE FUNDS;
SENATE BILL 375 3
1 (3) A A GOVERNMENT–OPERATED ANIMAL SHELTER;
2 (4) AN AN ANIMAL WELFARE ORGANIZATION, AS DEFINED IN § 19–701
3 OF THE BUSINESS REGULATION ARTICLE; OR
4 (5) A A MEMBER OF A 4–H OR FUTURE FARMERS OF AMERICA
5 PROGRAM IF, ON REQUEST OF AN ANIMAL CONTROL OFFICER, AN OFFICER OF A
6 COUNTY HUMANE SOCIETY, OR A POLICE OFFICER, THE MEMBER PROVIDES IN
7 WRITING PROOF OF:
8 (I) MEMBERSHIP MEMBERSHIP IN A 4–H OR FUTURE
9 FARMERS OF AMERICA PROGRAM; AND
10 (II) AUTHORIZATION AUTHORIZATION FROM THE COUNTY IN
11 WHICH THE ROOSTERS ARE LOCATED THAT SPECIFIES:
12 1. THE THE REASON FOR KEEPING MORE THAN 5
13 ROOSTERS PER ACRE OR MORE THAN 25 ROOSTERS ON A PROPERTY;
14 2. THE THE NUMBER OF ROOSTERS THAT MAY BE KEPT;
15 3. THE THE BREED OF EACH ROOSTER THAT MAY BE
16 KEPT;
17 4. THE THE DURATION OF TIME EACH ROOSTER MAY BE
18 KEPT; AND
19 5. THE THE ADDRESS OF THE PROPERTY WHERE EACH
20 ROOSTER MAY BE KEPT; OR
21 (6) A PERSON IF, ON REQUEST OF AN ANIMAL CONTROL OFFICER, AN
22 OFFICER OF A COUNTY HUMANE SOCIETY, OR A POLICE OFFICER, THE PERSON
23 PROVIDES IN WRITING PROOF THAT THE PERSON KEPT MORE ADULT HENS THAN
24 ROOSTERS WITHIN THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING 6–MONTH PERIOD.
25 (C) BEGINNING JANUARY 1, 2027, UNLESS AUTHORIZED BY THE
26 DEPARTMENT, A PERSON WHO KEEPS ROOSTERS THAT ARE MAINTAINED
27 INDIVIDUALLY AND KEPT MAY NOT KEEP A ROOSTER MOVEMENT–CONSTRAINED
28 THROUGH THE USE OF AN ENCLOSURE OR TETHER MAY NOT:
29 (1) KEEP MORE THAN 5 ROOSTERS PER ACRE ON A SINGLE
30 PROPERTY; OR
4 SENATE BILL 375
1 (2) KEEP MORE THAN A TOTAL OF 25 ROOSTERS ON A SINGLE
2 PROPERTY.
3 (D) (1) THIS SECTION DOES NOT AFFECT THE AUTHORITY OF A COUNTY,
4 MUNICIPALITY, OR UNIT OF LOCAL GOVERNMENT TO ENACT AND ENFORCE
5 STANDARDS OR REQUIREMENTS RELATED TO ROOSTERS THAT ARE MORE
6 STRINGENT THAN THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SECTION.
7 (2) THIS SECTION DOES NOT LIMIT OR SUPERSEDE ANY OTHER
8 COUNTY, MUNICIPAL, OR STATE LAW, RULE, OR REGULATION THAT PROVIDES MORE
9 STRINGENT REQUIREMENTS REGARDING THE KEEPING OF ROOSTERS.
10 (E) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS:
11 (1) GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO
12 A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $1,000; OR
13 (2) SUBJECT TO ENFORCEMENT UNDER § 3–119 OF THIS SUBTITLE
14 (1) A VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION IS A CIVIL OFFENSE.
15 (2) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS SUBJECT TO:
16 (I) FOR A FIRST OFFENSE, A WARNING;
17 (II) FOR A SECOND OFFENSE, A CIVIL PENALTY OF UP TO $500
18 PER ROOSTER; AND
19 (III) FOR A THIRD OR SUBSEQUENT OFFENSE, A CIVIL PENALTY
20 OF UP TO $1,000 PER ROOSTER.
21 (3) THIS SECTION MAY BE ENFORCED BY:
22 (I) ANY STATE OR LOCAL LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER; OR
23 (II) A LOCAL ANIMAL CONTROL AUTHORITY FOR THE
24 JURISDICTION WHERE THE VIOLATION OCCURS.
25 (F) THE DEPARTMENT MAY WORK IN COOPERATION WITH A LOCAL ANIMAL
26 CONTROL OR LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER TO ENFORCE THIS SECTION.
27 (G) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION SHALL BE CONSTRUED TO REQUIRE THE
28 DEPARTMENT TO CONDUCT ADDITIONAL INSPECTIONS BEYOND THOSE
29 INSPECTIONS REQUIRED BY LAW.
SENATE BILL 375 5
1 (H) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT THIS
2 SECTION.
3 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
4 October 1, 2025.
Approved:
