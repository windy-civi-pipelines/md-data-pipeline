1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Libraries – Automated External Defibrillator Program
3 (Raymono A. Russell Act)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring operators of certain libraries, beginning on a certain date, to
5 place an automated external defibrillator in a certain area, maintain the
6 functionality of the automated external defibrillator, and ensure a trained individual
7 is present under certain circumstances comply with the Public Access Automated
8 External Defibrillator Program; granting certain immunities to libraries and
9 individuals for certain acts or omissions under certain circumstances; altering the
10 definition of “facility” for the purpose of excluding certain libraries from the Public
11 Access Automated External Defibrillator Program; requiring the Maryland
12 Department of Health and the Maryland Institute for Emergency Medical Services
13 Systems jointly to adopt certain regulations; and generally relating to an automated
14 external defibrillator program for libraries.

16 (2) “Automated external defibrillator (AED)” means a medical heart
17 monitor and defibrillator device that:
18 (i) Is cleared for market by the federal Food and Drug
19 Administration;
20 (ii) Recognizes the presence or absence of ventricular fibrillation or
21 rapid ventricular tachycardia;
22 (iii) Determines, without intervention by an operator, whether
23 defibrillation should be performed;
24 (iv) On determining that defibrillation should be performed,
25 automatically charges; and
26 (v) 1. Requires operator intervention to deliver the electrical
27 impulse; or
28 2. Automatically continues with delivery of electrical
29 impulse.
30 (3) “Certificate” means a certificate issued by the EMS Board to a
31 registered facility.

6 ARREST.
7 (D) THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND THE MARYLAND
8 INSTITUTE FOR EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES SYSTEMS JOINTLY SHALL ADOPT
9 REGULATIONS THAT:
10 (1) ESTABLISH GUIDELINES FOR PERIODIC INSPECTIONS AND
11 ANNUAL MAINTENANCE OF THE AUTOMATED EXTERNAL DEFIBRILLATORS;
12 (2) ASSIST THE OPERATOR OF A LIBRARY IN CARRYING OUT THE
13 PROVISIONS OF THIS SECTION; AND
14 (3) REQUIRE EACH LIBRARY SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION TO REGISTER
15 WITH THE MARYLAND INSTITUTE FOR EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES SYSTEMS
16 IN ORDER TO:
17 (I) MAKE ITS LOCATION PUBLICLY AVAILABLE TO EMERGENCY
18 DISPATCHERS; AND
19 (II) RECEIVE MAINTENANCE AND RECALL NOTICES FROM THE
20 MARYLAND INSTITUTE FOR EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES SYSTEMS.
21 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before December 1,
22 2026, the Maryland Institute for Emergency Medical Services Systems shall report to the
23 Senate Committee on Education, Energy, and the Environment and the House Economic
24 Matters Ways and Means Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government
25 Article, on:
26 (1) the libraries that registered automated external defibrillators in
27 accordance with regulations adopted under § 23–102.2 of the Education Article, as enacted
28 by Section 1 of this Act; and
29 (2) the number of adverse cardiac events that required the use of an
30 automated external defibrillator experienced in the libraries that registered the automated
31 external defibrillators.
32 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
33 October 1, 2025.
