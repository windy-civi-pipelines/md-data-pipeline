F1 5lr2731
By: Delegates Patterson, Hill, Kaufman, McComas, Schindler, and Turner
Introduced and read first time: February 6, 2025
Assigned to: Ways and Means
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public and Nonpublic Schools – Student Elopement – Notice and Reporting
3 Requirements
4 FOR the purpose of requiring certain public and nonpublic schools to notify a parent or
5 guardian if a student attempts or accomplishes an elopement from the school campus
6 except under certain circumstances; establishing certain reporting requirements for
7 certain public and nonpublic schools and the State Department of Education
8 regarding incidents of student elopement from school; requiring the Department to
9 develop a certain accountability system; and generally relating to student elopement
10 and public and nonpublic schools.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Education
13 Section 7–11A–01 through 7–11A–07 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 11A.
14 Student Elopement From School”
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Education
20 SUBTITLE 11A. STUDENT ELOPEMENT FROM SCHOOL.
21 7–11A–01.
22 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
23 INDICATED.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1204
1 (B) “BEHAVIOR INTERVENTION PLAN” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN §
2 7–1101 OF THIS TITLE.
3 (C) “ELOPEMENT” MEANS THE ACT OF A STUDENT LEAVING THE SCHOOL
4 CAMPUS WITHOUT THE PERMISSION OF A SCHOOL STAFF MEMBER WHO HAS
5 RESPONSIBILITY FOR THE STUDENT.
6 (D) “IEP” MEANS AN INDIVIDUALIZED EDUCATION PROGRAM AS DEFINED
7 UNDER THE FEDERAL INDIVIDUALS WITH DISABILITIES EDUCATION ACT.
8 (E) “NONPUBLIC SCHOOL” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 7–1101 OF THIS
9 TITLE.
10 (F) “SCHOOL” MEANS:
11 (1) A PUBLIC ELEMENTARY, MIDDLE, OR HIGH SCHOOL;
12 (2) THE MARYLAND SCHOOL FOR THE BLIND;
13 (3) THE MARYLAND SCHOOL FOR THE DEAF; AND
14 (4) A NONPUBLIC SCHOOL.
15 (G) “SCHOOL CAMPUS” MEANS THE BUILDINGS AND GROUNDS OF A
16 SCHOOL, INCLUDING A PLAYGROUND.
17 (H) “WANDERING” MEANS THE ACT OF A STUDENT LEAVING A CLASSROOM
18 OR AREA ON THE SCHOOL CAMPUS, BUT NOT LEAVING THE SCHOOL CAMPUS,
19 WITHOUT THE PERMISSION OF A SCHOOL STAFF MEMBER WHO HAS RESPONSIBILITY
20 FOR THE STUDENT.
21 7–11A–02.
22 (A) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, EACH TIME
23 A STUDENT ATTEMPTS OR ACCOMPLISHES AN ELOPEMENT, THE SCHOOL SHALL
24 NOTIFY THE STUDENT’S PARENT OR GUARDIAN WITHIN 24 HOURS OF THE
25 ELOPEMENT INCIDENT.
26 (B) (1) A SCHOOL IS NOT REQUIRED TO PROVIDE THE NOTICE UNDER
27 SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION TO THE PARENT OR GUARDIAN OF A STUDENT
28 WHO HAS AN IEP OR A BEHAVIOR INTERVENTION PLAN THAT INCLUDES MEASURES
29 FOR RESPONDING TO AN INCIDENT OF WANDERING OR ELOPEMENT BEHAVIOR BY
30 THE STUDENT.
HOUSE BILL 1204 3
1 (2) AT LEAST ONE TIME EACH YEAR, A SCHOOL OR A STUDENT’S IEP
2 TEAM SHALL REVIEW AND ADDRESS WITH RELEVANT STAKEHOLDERS A STUDENT’S
3 WANDERING OR ELOPEMENT BEHAVIOR IN UPDATING THE STUDENT’S IEP OR
4 BEHAVIOR INTERVENTION PLAN.
5 7–11A–03.
6 (A) ON OR BEFORE AUGUST 1, 2026, AND EACH AUGUST 1 THEREAFTER,
7 EACH PUBLIC SCHOOL SHALL SUBMIT TO THE COUNTY BOARD A REPORT FOR THE
8 PRIOR SCHOOL YEAR ON:
9 (1) THE NUMBER OF ELOPEMENT INCIDENTS, DISAGGREGATED BY
10 JURISDICTION, DISABILITY, RACE, GENDER, AGE, AND TYPE OF PLACEMENT;
11 (2) THE NUMBER OF ELOPEMENT INCIDENTS FOR EACH STUDENT
12 WHO HAD AT LEAST ONE ELOPEMENT INCIDENT, DISAGGREGATED BY
13 JURISDICTION, DISABILITY, RACE, GENDER, AGE, AND TYPE OF PLACEMENT; AND
14 (3) THE STEPS TAKEN BY THE SCHOOL TO REDUCE THE NUMBER OF
15 ELOPEMENT INCIDENTS AND TO ENCOURAGE POSITIVE BEHAVIORAL
16 INTERVENTIONS, INCLUDING A DESCRIPTION OF:
17 (I) THE PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT PROVIDED TO
18 DESIGNATED SCHOOL PERSONNEL ON IDENTIFICATION OF WANDERING OR
19 ELOPEMENT BEHAVIOR IN STUDENTS, INCLUDING ANTECEDENTS TO WANDERING
20 OR ELOPEMENT BEHAVIOR, AND POSITIVE BEHAVIORAL INTERVENTIONS USED TO
21 ADDRESS WANDERING AND ELOPEMENT BEHAVIOR; AND
22 (II) THE POLICY CHANGES MADE OR NEW PROFESSIONAL
23 DEVELOPMENT OPPORTUNITIES OFFERED THAT WERE DESIGNED TO PROMOTE
24 POSITIVE BEHAVIORAL INTERVENTIONS AND REDUCE INCIDENTS OF WANDERING
25 AND ELOPEMENT BEHAVIORS IN THE UPCOMING SCHOOL YEAR.
26 (B) (1) A COUNTY BOARD SHALL VERIFY THE ACCURACY OF A REPORT
27 FROM A SCHOOL THAT REPORTS ZERO ELOPEMENT INCIDENTS UNDER SUBSECTION
28 (A) OF THIS SECTION.
29 (2) IF A COUNTY BOARD IS UNABLE TO VERIFY THE ACCURACY OF A
30 REPORT SUBMITTED BY A SCHOOL UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION, THE
31 COUNTY BOARD SHALL MAKE RECOMMENDATIONS TO THE SCHOOL FOR
32 IMPROVEMENTS IN DATA COLLECTION AND POSITIVE BEHAVIORAL INTERVENTIONS
33 AT THE SCHOOL.
