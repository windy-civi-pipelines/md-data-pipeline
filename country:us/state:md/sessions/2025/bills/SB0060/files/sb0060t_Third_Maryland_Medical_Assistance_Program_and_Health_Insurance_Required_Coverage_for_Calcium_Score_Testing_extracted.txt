CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Medical Assistance Program and Health Insurance – Required
3 Coverage for Calcium Score Testing
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Medical Assistance Program and certain
5 insurers, nonprofit health service plans, health maintenance organizations, and
6 managed care organizations to provide coverage for calcium score testing for
7 individuals who have certain risk factors in accordance with certain guidelines; and
8 generally relating to the coverage of calcium score testing by the Maryland Medical
9 Assistance Program and health insurance carriers.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Health – General
12 Section 15–102.3(m) and 15–103(a)(2)(xxv)
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
16 Article – Health – General
17 Section 15–103(a)(1)
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
21 Article – Health – General

9 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
10 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
11 Article – Health – General
12 15–102.3.
13 (M) THE PROVISIONS OF § 15–861 OF THE INSURANCE ARTICLE APPLY TO
14 MANAGED CARE ORGANIZATIONS IN THE SAME MANNER THEY APPLY TO CARRIERS.
15 15–103.
16 (a) (1) The Secretary shall administer the Maryland Medical Assistance
17 Program.
18 (2) The Program:
19 (xxiii) Beginning on July 1, 2025, shall provide, subject to the
20 limitations of the State budget, and as permitted by federal law, coverage for biomarker
21 testing in accordance with § 15–859 of the Insurance Article; [and]
22 (xxiv) Beginning on January 1, 2025, shall provide coverage for
23 prostheses in accordance with § 15–844 of the Insurance Article; AND
24 (XXV) BEGINNING ON JANUARY 1, 2026, SHALL PROVIDE
25 CALCIUM SCORE TESTING IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 15–861 OF THE INSURANCE
26 ARTICLE.
27 Article – Insurance
28 15–861.
29 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO:
30 (1) INSURERS AND NONPROFIT HEALTH SERVICE PLANS THAT
31 PROVIDE HOSPITAL, MEDICAL, OR SURGICAL BENEFITS TO INDIVIDUALS OR GROUPS
SENATE BILL 60 3
1 ON AN EXPENSE–INCURRED BASIS UNDER HEALTH INSURANCE POLICIES OR
2 CONTRACTS THAT ARE ISSUED OR DELIVERED IN THE STATE; AND
3 (2) HEALTH MAINTENANCE ORGANIZATIONS THAT PROVIDE
4 HOSPITAL, MEDICAL, OR SURGICAL BENEFITS TO INDIVIDUALS OR GROUPS UNDER
5 CONTRACTS THAT ARE ISSUED OR DELIVERED IN THE STATE.
6 (B) AN ENTITY SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION SHALL PROVIDE COVERAGE FOR
7 CALCIUM SCORE TESTING IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE MOST RECENT GUIDELINES
8 ISSUED BY THE AMERICAN COLLEGE OF CARDIOLOGY THAT EXPAND THE SCOPE OF
9 PREVENTIVE CARE SERVICES FOR THE BENEFIT OF CONSUMERS FOR INDIVIDUALS
10 WHO HAVE AT LEAST THREE OF THE FOLLOWING RISK FACTORS:
11 (1) DIABETES;
12 (2) HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE;
13 (3) HIGH CHOLESTEROL; OR
14 (4) A FAMILY HISTORY OF PREMATURE CORONARY ARTERY DISEASE.
15 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall apply to all
16 policies, contracts, and health benefit plans issued, delivered, or renewed in the State on or
17 after January 1, 2026.
18 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
19 January 1, 2026.
