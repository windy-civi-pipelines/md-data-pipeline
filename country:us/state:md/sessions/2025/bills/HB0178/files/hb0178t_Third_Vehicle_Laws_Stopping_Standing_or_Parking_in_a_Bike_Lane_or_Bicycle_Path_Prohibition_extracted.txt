CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Vehicle Laws – Stopping, Standing, or Parking in a Bike Lane or Bicycle Path –
3 Prohibition
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from stopping, standing, or parking a vehicle in a
5 bike lane or bicycle path, subject to certain exceptions and a certain civil penalty;
6 and generally relating to stopping, standing, or parking a vehicle in a bike lane or
7 bicycle path.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
9 Article – Transportation
10 Section 21–101(a), (c), and (e) and 21–1003(a)
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Transportation
15 Section 21–1003(ff)
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 BY adding to
19 Article – Transportation
20 Section 21–1003(gg)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland

Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *hb0178*
2 HOUSE BILL 178
1 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
2 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
3 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
4 Article – Transportation
5 21–101.
6 (a) In this title and Title 25 of this article the following words have the meanings
7 indicated.
8 (c) “Bicycle path” means any travelway designed and designated by signing or
9 signing and marking for bicycle use, located within its own right–of–way or in a shared
10 right–of–way, and physically separated from motor vehicle traffic by berm, shoulder, curb,
11 or other similar device.
12 (e) “Bike lane” means any portion of a roadway or shoulder designated for single
13 directional bicycle flow.
14 21–1003.
15 (a) The provisions of this section apply except as necessary to avoid conflict with
16 other traffic or in compliance with law or the directions of a police officer or traffic control
17 device.
18 (ff) (1) A person convicted of a violation of subsection (j) of this section while
19 operating a commercial motor vehicle in Anne Arundel County is subject to:
20 (i) For a first offense, a fine of $100;
21 (ii) For a second offense, a fine of $250; and
22 (iii) For a third or subsequent offense, a fine of $500.
23 (2) A person convicted of a violation of subsection (u) or (dd) of this section
24 is subject to a fine of $25.
25 (3) A DRIVER OF A MOTOR VEHICLE IS SUBJECT TO A CIVIL PENALTY
26 NOT EXCEEDING $500 FOR A VIOLATION OF SUBSECTION (GG) OF THIS SECTION.
27 (GG) (1) THIS SUBSECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO:
28 (I) AN AUTHORIZED EMERGENCY VEHICLE;
29 (II) A TRANSIT VEHICLE;
HOUSE BILL 178 3
1 (III) A VEHICLE PICKING UP OR DISCHARGING INDIVIDUALS
2 WITH DISABILITIES;
3 (IV) A VEHICLE THAT HAS BECOME UNINTENTIONALLY SO
4 DISABLED WHILE ON THE HIGHWAY THAT THE DRIVER CANNOT AVOID STOPPING
5 AND TEMPORARILY STANDING OR PARKING THE VEHICLE; OR
6 (V) A VEHICLE WHILE LOADING OR UNLOADING GOODS FOR
7 PICK–UP OR DELIVERY:
8 1. A. UNLESS A SHORTER PERIOD OF TIME IS
9 IMPOSED UNDER A LOCAL LAW, FOR A PERIOD OF TIME NOT EXCEEDING 30
10 MINUTES; AND
11 B. THAT IS DISPLAYING FLASHING HAZARD WARNING
12 LIGHTS; OR
13 2. UNDER A LOCAL CURBSIDE PARKING PERMIT.
14 (2) A PERSON MAY NOT STOP, STAND, OR PARK A VEHICLE IN A BIKE
15 LANE OR BICYCLE PATH.
16 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
17 October 1, 2025.
Approved:
