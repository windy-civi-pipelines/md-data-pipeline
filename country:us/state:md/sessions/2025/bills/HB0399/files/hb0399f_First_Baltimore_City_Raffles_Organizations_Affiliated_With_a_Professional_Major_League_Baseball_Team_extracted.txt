A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Baltimore City – Raffles – Organizations Affiliated With a Professional Major
3 League Baseball Team
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing certain organizations that are affiliated with a professional
5 major league baseball team that plays its home games in Baltimore City to conduct
6 certain raffles; requiring the organization to obtain a permit to conduct raffles from
7 an agency designated by the Baltimore City government; authorizing a raffle ticket
8 to be sold and received on an electronic device located within the geographical
9 boundaries of Baltimore City; and generally relating to raffles in Baltimore City.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
11 Article – Criminal Law
12 Section 13–501, 13–503, 13–505, and 13–510
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 BY adding to
16 Article – Criminal Law
17 Section 13–505.1
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
22 Article – Criminal Law
23 13–501.
24 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.

1 (b) “Commissioner” means the Baltimore City Police Commissioner.
2 (C) “DESIGNATED CITY AGENCY” MEANS AN AGENCY DESIGNATED BY THE
3 BALTIMORE CITY GOVERNMENT.
4 (D) (1) “ELECTRONIC DEVICE” MEANS A DEVICE THAT IS CAPABLE OF
5 TRANSMITTING, RECEIVING, OR RECORDING MESSAGES, IMAGES, SOUNDS, DATA, OR
6 OTHER INFORMATION BY ELECTRONIC MEANS OR THAT, IN APPEARANCE,
7 PURPORTS TO BE A CELL PHONE, COMPUTER, OR OTHER SUCH DEVICE.
8 (2) “ELECTRONIC DEVICE” INCLUDES A CELL PHONE OR COMPUTER.
9 (3) “ELECTRONIC DEVICE” DOES NOT INCLUDE A VIDEO LOTTERY
10 TERMINAL AS DEFINED UNDER § 9–1A–01 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE.
11 [(c)] (E) “Gaming event” means a carnival, bazaar, or raffle.
12 (F) (1) “GEOFENCE” MEANS TECHNOLOGY THAT ESTABLISHES A
13 VIRTUAL GEOGRAPHICAL BOUNDARY.
14 (2) “GEOFENCE” INCLUDES BOUNDARIES THAT ARE ESTABLISHED
15 OR MONITORED THROUGH THE USE OF:
16 (I) GLOBAL POSITIONING TECHNOLOGY;
17 (II) CELL TOWER CONNECTIVITY;
18 (III) CELLULAR DATA;
19 (IV) RADIO FREQUENCY IDENTIFICATION;
20 (V) WIRELESS FIDELITY TECHNOLOGY; OR
21 (VI) ANY OTHER FORM OF LOCATION DETERMINATION
22 TECHNOLOGY.
23 [(d)] (G) “Raffle” means one or more drawings from a single series of chances
24 sold [from chance books].

26 (a) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO A RAFFLE AUTHORIZED UNDER §
27 13–505.1 OF THIS SUBTITLE.

1 (B) Before an organization listed in subsection [(b)] (C) of this section may operate
2 a gaming event, the organization shall obtain a permit from the commissioner.
3 [(b)] (C) (1) An organization that meets the conditions of paragraph (2) of this
4 subsection may conduct a gaming event for the benefit of any of the following listed
5 organizations if the organization is a bona fide:
6 (i) religious organization;
7 (ii) fraternal organization;
8 (iii) civic organization;
9 (iv) veterans’ hospital;
10 (v) amateur athletic organization in which all playing members are

16 for:

19 3. purposes related to a veterans’ hospital;

21 5. charitable purposes.

23 shall review the character of the organization applying for the permit to ascertain that the
24 organization meets the requirements of §§ 13–503 through 13–505 of this subtitle.

26 action taken by the commissioner on that application a matter of public record.
27 [(d)] (E) (1) The permit shall state that the gaming event shall be managed
28 and operated personally only by members of the organization obtaining the permit.

3 allow an individual or group of individuals to:

5 (2) receive any of the proceeds of the gaming event for personal use or
6 benefit.
7 13–505.
8 (a) A permit holder may award prizes in merchandise and money in a raffle in
9 any amount.
10 (b) [A] EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN § 13–505.1(D)(1)(II) OF THIS SUBTITLE, A
11 permit holder may not conduct more than 12 raffles in a calendar year.
12 13–505.1.
13 (A) A RAFFLE MAY BE CONDUCTED BY AN ORGANIZATION THAT:
14 (1) IS LOCATED IN BALTIMORE CITY;
15 (2) SPENDS A MAJORITY OF THE ORGANIZATION’S FUNDS IN
16 BALTIMORE CITY FOR:
17 (I) FRATERNAL PURPOSES;
18 (II) CIVIC PURPOSES;
19 (III) PURPOSES RELATED TO A VETERANS’ HOSPITAL;
20 (IV) PURPOSES RELATED TO AMATEUR ATHLETICS; OR
21 (V) CHARITABLE PURPOSES; AND
22 (3) IS AFFILIATED WITH A PROFESSIONAL MAJOR LEAGUE BASEBALL
23 TEAM THAT PLAYS ITS HOME GAMES IN BALTIMORE CITY.
24 (B) (1) BEFORE CONDUCTING A RAFFLE, AN ORGANIZATION SHALL
25 OBTAIN A WRITTEN PERMIT FROM THE DESIGNATED CITY AGENCY.
HOUSE BILL 399 5
1 (2) BEFORE ISSUING A PERMIT, THE DESIGNATED CITY AGENCY
2 SHALL ASCERTAIN THE CHARACTER OF THE ORGANIZATION TO DETERMINE IF THE
3 PERMIT SHOULD BE ISSUED.
4 (C) (1) A PERMIT ISSUED TO AN ORGANIZATION UNDER THIS SECTION
5 MAY NOT BE TRANSFERRED.
6 (2) THE PERMIT SHALL STATE THAT THE RAFFLE IS REQUIRED TO BE
7 MANAGED AND OPERATED ONLY BY MEMBERS OF THE ORGANIZATION OBTAINING
8 THE PERMIT.
9 (D) (1) (I) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, THE
10 DESIGNATED CITY AGENCY MAY ISSUE A SINGLE PERMIT TO AN ORGANIZATION TO
11 HOLD RAFFLES AT MULTIPLE PROFESSIONAL MAJOR LEAGUE BASEBALL GAMES
12 PLAYED IN BALTIMORE CITY.
13 (II) THERE IS NO LIMIT ON THE NUMBER OF RAFFLES THAT AN
14 ORGANIZATION MAY CONDUCT IN CONJUNCTION WITH PROFESSIONAL MAJOR
15 LEAGUE BASEBALL GAMES PLAYED IN BALTIMORE CITY.
16 (2) A PERMIT TO HOLD RAFFLES AT MULTIPLE PROFESSIONAL
17 MAJOR LEAGUE BASEBALL GAMES PLAYED IN BALTIMORE CITY EXPIRES AT THE
18 END OF THE CALENDAR YEAR IN WHICH THE PERMIT IS ISSUED.
19 (E) AN ORGANIZATION CONDUCTING A RAFFLE MAY:
20 (1) SET THE PRICE OF RAFFLE TICKETS; AND
21 (2) AWARD PRIZES IN MERCHANDISE AND MONEY IN ANY AMOUNT.
22 (F) AN ORGANIZATION CONDUCTING A RAFFLE MAY NOT:
23 (1) BENEFIT FINANCIALLY FROM THE RAFFLE; OR
24 (2) RECEIVE ANY OF THE PROCEEDS OF THE RAFFLE FOR PERSONAL
25 USE OR BENEFIT.
26 (G) (1) A RAFFLE TICKET SOLD BY AN ORGANIZATION IN CONJUNCTION
27 WITH A PROFESSIONAL MAJOR LEAGUE BASEBALL GAME PLAYED IN BALTIMORE
28 CITY:
29 (I) MAY BE SOLD AND RECEIVED ON AN ELECTRONIC DEVICE
30 ONLY WITHIN THE GEOGRAPHICAL BOUNDARIES OF BALTIMORE CITY; AND
6 HOUSE BILL 399
1 (II) SHALL BE SOLD AND RECEIVED NOT EARLIER THAN 1 HOUR
2 BEFORE THE OFFICIAL START OF THE BASEBALL GAME AND NOT LATER THAN THE
3 SEVENTH INNING OF THAT BASEBALL GAME.
4 (2) AN ORGANIZATION CONDUCTING A RAFFLE UNDER THIS SECTION
5 SHALL USE A GEOFENCE TO ENSURE THAT RAFFLE TICKETS SOLD AND RECEIVED ON
6 AN ELECTRONIC DEVICE ARE SOLD AND RECEIVED WITHIN THE GEOGRAPHICAL
7 BOUNDARIES OF BALTIMORE CITY.
8 13–510.
9 (a) A person may not knowingly conduct or attempt to conduct a bazaar or raffle
10 in violation of §§ 13–503 through 13–505 AND 13–505.1 of this subtitle.
11 (b) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and on conviction
12 is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 1 year or a fine not exceeding $1,000 or both.
13 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
14 1, 2025.
