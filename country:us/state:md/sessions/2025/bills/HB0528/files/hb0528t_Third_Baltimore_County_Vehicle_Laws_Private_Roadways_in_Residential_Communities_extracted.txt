CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Baltimore County – Vehicle Laws – Private Roadways in Residential
3 Subdivisions and Communities
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting, for a certain period of time, a person from driving a motor
5 vehicle in violation of certain provisions of the Maryland Vehicle Law on private
6 roadways in certain residential areas a certain residential community in Baltimore
7 County; authorizing, for a certain period of time, a police officer to enforce the
8 Maryland Vehicle Law on private roadways in certain residential areas a certain
9 residential community in Baltimore County; and generally relating to complying
10 with and enforcing the Maryland Vehicle Law on private roadways in Baltimore
11 County.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Transportation
14 Section 11–205(c) and 21–101.1(b)(1)
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Transportation
20 11–205.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *hb0528*
2 HOUSE BILL 528
1 (c) (1) In BALTIMORE COUNTY AND Calvert County, a police officer may
2 enforce the provisions of the Maryland Vehicle Law on a private roadway in [Calvert
3 County] THE COUNTY that is located within a residential subdivision or community and
4 used for vehicular travel by residents of the subdivision or community.
5 (2) IN BALTIMORE COUNTY, BEGINNING OCTOBER 1, 2025,
6 THROUGH THE END OF JUNE 30, 2028, A POLICE OFFICER MAY ENFORCE THE
7 PROVISIONS OF THE MARYLAND VEHICLE LAW ON A PRIVATE ROADWAY WITHIN
8 THE QUEEN ANNE VILLAGE COMMUNITY IN OWINGS MILLS THAT IS USED FOR
9 VEHICULAR TRAFFIC BY RESIDENTS OF THE COMMUNITY.
10 21–101.1.
11 (b) (1) A person may not drive a motor vehicle in violation of any provision of
12 this title on any:
13 (I) ANY private property that is used by the public in general,; or,
14 in BALTIMORE COUNTY AND Calvert County, on any
15 (II) ANY private [road] ROADWAY located within:
16 1. IN CALVERT COUNTY, a residential subdivision or
17 community; OR
18 2. IN BALTIMORE COUNTY, BEGINNING OCTOBER 1,
19 2025, THROUGH THE END OF JUNE 30, 2028, THE QUEEN ANNE VILLAGE
20 COMMUNITY IN OWINGS MILLS.
21 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before September 1,
22 2028, the Baltimore County Police Department shall report to the members of the
23 Baltimore County Delegation to the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the
24 State Government Article, on the implementation of this Act and whether it has improved
25 compliance with the Maryland Vehicle Law in the Queen Anne Village community in
26 Owings Mills.
27 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
28 October 1, 2025. It shall remain effective for a period of 3 years and, at the end of September
29 30, 2028, this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be
30 abrogated and of no further force and effect.
