14 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – Agriculture
18 10–2001.

2 SENATE BILL 226
1 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
2 (b) “FMNP” means the federal Farmers Market Nutrition Program.
3 (c) “Fund” means the Maryland Farms and Families Fund.
4 (d) “Program” means the Maryland Farms and Families Program.
5 (e) “SNAP” means the federal Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program.
6 (f) “WIC” means the federal Special Supplemental Food Program for Women,
7 Infants, and Children.
8 10–2002.
9 (a) There is a Maryland Farms and Families Program in the Department.
10 (b) The purpose of the Program is to double the purchasing power of food–insecure
11 Maryland residents with limited access to fresh fruits and vegetables and to increase
12 revenue for farmers through redemption of federal nutrition benefits at Maryland farmers
13 markets.
14 10–2003.
15 (a) There is a Maryland Farms and Families Fund.
16 (b) The purpose of the Fund is [to provide grants] to:
17 (1) [Nonprofit organizations that match] MATCH purchases made with
18 FMNP, SNAP, and WIC benefits at participating farmers markets and farm stands;
19 (2) [Nonprofit] SUPPORT NONPROFIT farmers markets to implement the
20 Program [at the farmers markets] BY PROVIDING LOGISTICAL AND MARKETING
21 SUPPORT TO INCREASE THE USE OF FEDERAL NUTRITION PROGRAMS AT THESE
22 FARMERS MARKETS; and
23 (3) [Local] SUPPORT nonprofit organizations AND OTHER RESPONSIBLE
24 PERSONS to implement the Program in partnership with one or more participating local
25 farmers markets.
26 (c) The Secretary shall administer the Fund.
27 (d) (1) The Fund is a special, nonlapsing fund that is not subject to § 7–302 of
28 the State Finance and Procurement Article.
SENATE BILL 226 3
1 (2) The State Treasurer shall hold the Fund separately, and the
2 Comptroller shall account for the Fund.
3 (e) The Fund consists of:
4 (1) Money appropriated in the State budget to the Fund; and
5 (2) Any other money from any other source accepted for the benefit of the
6 Fund.
7 (f) (1) In accordance with this subsection, THE DEPARTMENT MAY USE the
8 Fund [shall be used to provide grants] FOR EITHER ONE OR BOTH OF THE FOLLOWING
9 PURPOSES:
10 (i) [Nonprofit organizations that:] TO IMPLEMENT, IN WHOLE OR
11 IN PART, THE PROGRAM WITHIN THE DEPARTMENT; AND
12 [1. Meet the qualifications established in § 10–2004 of this
13 subtitle; and
14 2. Distribute the grant money to farmers markets and local
15 nonprofit organizations in accordance with this subtitle;]
16 (ii) [Nonprofit farmers markets to implement the Program at the
17 farmers markets; and] TO PROVIDE FUNDS TO A QUALIFIED NONPROFIT
18 ORGANIZATION OR OTHER RESPONSIBLE PERSON TO IMPLEMENT, IN WHOLE OR IN
19 PART, THE PROGRAM.
20 [(iii) Local nonprofit organizations to implement the Program in
21 partnership with one or more local farmers markets.]
22 (2) A qualified nonprofit organization OR OTHER PERSON that receives [a
23 grant] FUNDS under this section:
24 (i) Shall distribute at least 60% of the [grant] money it receives
25 directly to participating farmers markets as funding for local market access coordinators
26 and matching dollars for purchases made with FMNP, SNAP, and WIC benefits; and
27 (ii) May not use more than 40% of the [grant] money it receives for
28 Program development, promotion and outreach, farmers market training and capacity
29 building, technical assistance, program data collection, evaluation, administration, and
30 reporting.
31 (g) (1) Subject to paragraph (2) of this subsection, the Governor shall include
32 in the annual budget bill an appropriation to the Fund as follows:
4 SENATE BILL 226
1 (i) For fiscal years 2021 through 2023, $100,000; and
2 (ii) For fiscal year 2024 and each fiscal year thereafter, $300,000.
3 (2) The appropriation required under paragraph (1) of this subsection shall
4 be in addition to, and may not supplant, any funding appropriated to the [Marketing and
5 Promotion Division] AGRICULTURE AND SEAFOOD MARKETING PROGRAM in the
6 Department.
7 10–2004.
8 (a) (1) A nonprofit organization OR OTHER PERSON is qualified to receive [a
9 grant] FUNDS in accordance with this subtitle if the Department determines that the
10 [nonprofit] organization OR PERSON has a demonstrated record of:
11 (i) Designing and implementing successful healthy food incentive
12 programs that connect federal food benefits recipients with local producers;
13 (ii) Implementing funds distributing and reporting processes;
14 (iii) Providing training and technical assistance to farmers markets;
15 (iv) Conducting community outreach and data collection, including
16 customer surveys; and
17 (v) Providing a full accounting and administration of funds
18 distributed to farmers markets.
19 (2) In addition to the requirements under paragraph (1) of this subsection,
20 in awarding [a grant] FUNDS in accordance with this subtitle, the Department may
21 consider whether the [nonprofit] organization OR PERSON has a demonstrated record of
22 providing services in healthy food priority areas.
23 (b) Within 90 days after the end of a [grant cycle] CALENDAR YEAR, [a qualified
24 nonprofit] AN organization OR PERSON that received [a grant] FUNDS in accordance with
25 this subtitle shall submit a report to the Department that includes the following
26 information:
27 (1) The names and locations of Maryland farmers markets that received
28 funds under the Program;
29 (2) The dollar amount of funds awarded to each participating farmers
30 market;
SENATE BILL 226 5
1 (3) The dollar amount of FMNP, SNAP, and WIC benefits, and funds
2 provided under the Program that were spent at participating farmers markets, as well as
3 any unspent funds;
4 (4) The number of FMNP, SNAP, and WIC transactions carried out at
5 participating farmers markets; and
6 (5) The impact of the Program on increasing the quantity of fresh fruits
7 and vegetables consumed by FMNP, SNAP, and WIC families, as determined by customer
8 surveys.
9 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
10 October 1, 2025.
