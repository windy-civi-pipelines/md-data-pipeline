CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Prince George’s County – Chesapeake and Atlantic Coastal Bays Critical Area
3 Protection Program – Cutting or Clearing Trees
4 MC/PG 113–25
5 FOR the purpose of providing that, in Prince George’s County, a certain person may not be
6 held liable for the cutting or clearing of trees in the Chesapeake and Atlantic Coastal
7 Bays Critical Area under certain circumstances; requiring the Critical Area
8 Commission for the Chesapeake and Atlantic Coastal Bays to adopt regulations
9 authorizing a certain person to appeal a certain action to enforce a certain violation
10 involving the clearing or cutting of trees in the Chesapeake and Atlantic Coastal
11 Bays Critical Area, the Prince George’s County Department of Permitting,
12 Inspections, and Enforcement must record a lien on property that is the subject of
13 the violation in a certain manner; establishing certain limits, prohibitions,
14 requirements, and authorizations on actions certain local jurisdictions may take if a
15 lien is not recorded as required by this Act; requiring the Prince George’s County
16 government and the Maryland–National Capital Park and Planning Commission to
17 vacate certain administrative enforcement actions related to a certain violation
18 occurring on or after a certain date; requiring the Prince George’s County
19 government to reimburse a current property owner for certain costs and fees
20 associated with certain administrative enforcement actions; applying this Act
21 retroactively to certain violations occurring on or before a certain date; and generally
22 relating to the cutting or clearing of trees in the Chesapeake and Atlantic Coastal
23 Bays Critical Area in Prince George’s County.

2 HOUSE BILL 1470
1 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
2 Article – Natural Resources
3 Section 8–1815.1
4 Annotated Code of Maryland
5 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
8 Article – Natural Resources
9 8–1815.1.
10 (a) (1) The provisions of this section are in addition to any other sanction,
11 remedy, or penalty provided by law.
12 (2) This section does not apply to any cutting or clearing of trees that is
13 allowed under regulations adopted by the Commission under this subtitle.
14 (b) [If] SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (F) OF THIS SECTION, IF a person cuts or
15 clears or plans to cut or clear trees within the Chesapeake Bay Critical Area or Atlantic
16 Coastal Bays Critical Area in violation of an approved local critical area program or of
17 regulations adopted by the Commission, the chair may bring an action, or the local
18 jurisdiction may bring an action or request that the chair of the Commission refer the
19 matter to the Attorney General to bring an action:
20 (1) To require the person to replant trees where the cutting or clearing
21 occurred in accordance with a plan prepared by the State Forester, a registered professional
22 forester, or a registered landscape architect;
23 (2) To restrain the planned violation; or
24 (3) For damages:
25 (i) To be assessed by a circuit court in an amount equal to the
26 estimated cost of replanting trees; and
27 (ii) To be paid to the Department by the person found to have
28 violated the provisions of this subsection.
29 (c) If the chair of the Commission has reason to believe that the local jurisdiction
30 is failing to enforce the requirements of subsection (b) of this section, the chair shall refer
31 the matter to the Attorney General as provided under § 8–1815(b) of this subtitle.
32 (d) On the chair of the Commission’s referral of an alleged violation under
33 subsection (c) of this section to the Attorney General, the Attorney General may invoke the
34 remedies available to the local jurisdiction under subsection (b) of this section in any court
HOUSE BILL 1470 3
1 of competent jurisdiction in which the local jurisdiction would be authorized to prosecute
2 or sue.
3 (e) On the request of a local jurisdiction or the chair of the Commission, the State
4 Forester, a registered professional forester, or a registered landscape architect may
5 prepare, oversee, and approve the final implementation of a plan to:
6 (1) Replant trees in any part of the Chesapeake Bay Critical Area where
7 trees in the Chesapeake Bay Critical Area are cut or cleared in violation of subsection (b)
8 of this section; and
9 (2) Replant trees in any part of the Atlantic Coastal Bays Critical Area
10 where trees in the Atlantic Coastal Bays Critical Area are cut or cleared in violation of
11 subsection (b) of this section.
12 (F) (1) THIS SUBSECTION APPLIES ONLY IN:
13 (I) IN PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY; AND
14 (II) TO REAL PROPERTY CONSISTING OF A PARCEL OF LAND OF
15 LESS THAN 5 ACRES THAT IS NOT ZONED FOR COMMERCIAL USE.
16 (2) A PERSON WHO IN GOOD FAITH PURCHASES PROPERTY IN THE
17 CRITICAL AREA MAY NOT BE HELD LIABLE FOR THE CUTTING OR CLEARING OF
18 TREES BY PREVIOUS OWNERS OR USERS OF THE PROPERTY IN VIOLATION OF
19 SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
20 (G) THE COMMISSION SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS AUTHORIZING A
21 PERSON TO APPEAL AN ACTION BROUGHT UNDER THIS SECTION.
22 (2) (I) IN THE ENFORCEMENT OF SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS
23 SECTION FOR A VIOLATION INVOLVING THE CLEARING OR CUTTING OF TREES IN THE
24 CRITICAL AREA, THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY DEPARTMENT OF PERMITTING,
25 INSPECTIONS, AND ENFORCEMENT SHALL RECORD A LIEN ON THE PROPERTY THAT
26 IS THE SUBJECT OF THE VIOLATION WITHIN 90 DAYS AFTER ISSUING NOTICE OF THE
27 VIOLATION TO THE PROPERTY OWNER.
28 (II) A PROPERLY RECORDED LIEN AS DESCRIBED IN
29 SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH IS REQUIRED TO ENFORCE AND HOLD A
30 CURRENT PROPERTY OWNER LIABLE FOR THE CUTTING OR CLEARING OF TREES BY
31 A PREVIOUS PROPERTY OWNER OR USER WHO PERFORMED THE ACT THAT CAUSED
32 THE VIOLATION.
33 (III) IF A LIEN IS NOT RECORDED AS REQUIRED, THE LOCAL
34 JURISDICTION:
4 HOUSE BILL 1470
1 1. MAY PURSUE AN ENFORCEMENT ACTION AGAINST
2 ONLY THE PREVIOUS OWNER OR USER THAT PERFORMED THE ACT THAT CAUSED
3 THE VIOLATION;
4 2. MAY NOT SEEK THE REMEDIES DESCRIBED IN
5 SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION AGAINST THE CURRENT PROPERTY OWNER; AND
6 3. A. SHALL PERFORM ANY RESTORATION OR
7 MITIGATION THAT MAY BE REQUIRED UNDER THIS SECTION OR § 8–1815 OF THIS
8 SUBTITLE RESULTING FROM THE VIOLATION THAT IS NOT PERFORMED BY THE
9 PREVIOUS OWNER OR USER; AND
10 B. MAY ENTER THE PROPERTY THAT IS THE SUBJECT OF
11 THE VIOLATION FOR RESTORATION OR MITIGATION PURPOSES IN ACCORDANCE
12 WITH § 8–1815 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
13 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
14 (a) This Act shall be construed to apply retroactively and shall be applied to and
15 interpreted to affect a violation of § 8–1815(b) of the Natural Resources Article, as enacted
16 by Section 1 of this Act, in Prince George’s County occurring on or before November 7, 2018,
17 but only with respect to a violation imposed on a current property owner who in good faith
18 purchases the property that is the subject of the violation after a previous property owner
19 or user committed the act that caused the violation and was cited for the violation.
20 (b) The Prince George’s County government and the Maryland–National Capital
21 Park and Planning Commission shall vacate any administrative enforcement actions taken
22 for a violation described in subsection (a) of this section, including the release of a lien
23 recorded on the property that is the subject of the violation.
24 (c) The Prince George’s County government shall reimburse a current property
25 owner for all costs and fees associated with any administrative enforcement actions taken
26 for a violation described in subsection (a) of this section, including civil penalties, bonds,
27 reforestation costs, fees for attorneys, engineers, foresters, and landscape architects, and
28 any other costs or fees.
29 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
30 October 1, 2025.
