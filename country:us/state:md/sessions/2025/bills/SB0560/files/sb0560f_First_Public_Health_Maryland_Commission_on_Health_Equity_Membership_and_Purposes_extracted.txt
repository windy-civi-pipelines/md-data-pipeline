A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Health – Maryland Commission on Health Equity – Membership and
3 Purposes
4 FOR the purpose of altering the membership of the Maryland Commission on Health
5 Equity; altering the purposes of the Commission to include employing a health equity
6 framework to examine the impact of access to transportation, transit programs, and
7 transit services and proximity to health care facilities and primary care providers on
8 the health of residents of the State; and generally relating to the Maryland
9 Commission on Health Equity.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
11 Article – Health – General
12 Section 13–4302
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
16 Article – Health – General
17 Section 13–4303(a) and 13–4305(a)
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
22 Article – Health – General
23 13–4302.
24 There is a Maryland Commission on Health Equity.

6 (a) The [purpose] PURPOSES of the Commission [is] ARE to:
7 (1) Employ a health equity framework to:
8 (i) Develop a statewide health equity plan; and
9 (ii) Examine:
10 1. The health of residents of the State to the extent necessary
11 to carry out the requirements of this section;
12 2. Ways for units of State and local government to
13 collaborate to implement policies that will positively impact the health of residents of the
14 State; and
15 3. The impact of the following factors on the health of
16 residents of the State:
17 A. Access to safe and affordable housing;
18 B. Educational attainment;
19 C. Opportunities for employment;
20 D. Economic stability;
21 E. ACCESS TO TRANSPORTATION, TRANSIT PROGRAMS,
22 AND TRANSIT SERVICES, AS DEFINED IN § 7–101 OF THE TRANSPORTATION
23 ARTICLE;
24 F. PROXIMITY TO HEALTH CARE FACILITIES, AS
25 DEFINED IN § 19–114 OF THIS ARTICLE, AND PRIMARY CARE PROVIDERS;
26 [E.] G. Environmental factors;
27 [F.] H. Public safety, including the impact of crime, citizen
28 unrest, the criminal justice system, and governmental policies that affect individuals who
29 are in prison or released from prison; and
SENATE BILL 560 5
1 [G.] I. Food insecurity;
2 (2) Provide direct advice to the Secretary and the State’s independent
3 health regulatory commissions, and indirect advice to the Department’s senior
4 administrators and planners through the Secretary, regarding issues of racial, ethnic,
5 cultural, or socioeconomic health disparities;
6 (3) Facilitate coordination of the expertise and experience of the State’s
7 health and human services, housing, transportation, education, environment, community
8 development, and labor systems in developing a comprehensive health equity plan
9 addressing the social determinants of health; and
10 (4) Set goals for health equity and prepare a plan for the State to achieve
11 health equity in alignment with any other statewide planning activities.
12 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
13 1, 2025.
