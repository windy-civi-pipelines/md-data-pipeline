Section 1:
D3 5lr2267
By: Delegates Lopez, Allen, and Kaufman
Introduced and read first time: January 24, 2025
Assigned to: Judiciary
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Civil Actions – Unsolicited Obscene Material
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from knowingly sending by electronic means to
4 another person an image or a video depicting obscene material that the sender knows
5 or reasonably should know is unsolicited; authorizing a person to bring an action
6 against a person who violates this Act; exempting certain entities and individuals
7 from this Act; and generally relating to civil actions against a person who sends
8 unsolicited obscene material to another person.
9 BY adding to
10 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
11 Section 3–2601 through 3–2604 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 26. Civil
12 Actions for Sending Unsolicited Obscene Material”
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
18 SUBTITLE 26. CIVIL ACTIONS FOR SENDING UNSOLICITED OBSCENE MATERIAL.
19 3–2601.
20 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
21 INDICATED.
22 (B) (1) “OBSCENE MATERIAL” MEANS MATERIAL THAT, TAKEN AS A
23 WHOLE:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb0670*
2 HOUSE BILL 670
1 (I) AN AVERAGE ADULT, APPLYING CONTEMPORARY
2 COMMUNITY STANDARDS, WOULD FIND APPEALS TO THE PRURIENT INTEREST;
3 (II) DEPICTS OR DESCRIBES SEXUAL CONDUCT IN A WAY THAT
4 IS PATENTLY OFFENSIVE TO PREVAILING STANDARDS IN THE ADULT COMMUNITY;
5 AND
6 (III) LACKS SERIOUS ARTISTIC, EDUCATIONAL, LITERARY,
7 POLITICAL, OR SCIENTIFIC VALUE.
8 (2) “OBSCENE MATERIAL” INCLUDES IMAGES OR VIDEO DEPICTING A
9 PERSON ENGAGING IN AN ACT OF SEXUAL INTERCOURSE, SODOMY, ORAL
10 COPULATION, SEXUAL PENETRATION, OR MASTURBATION, OR DEPICTING THE
11 EXPOSED GENITALS OR ANUS OF ANY PERSON.
12 (C) “UNSOLICITED” MEANS THE RECIPIENT HAS NOT CONSENTED TO OR
13 HAS EXPRESSLY FORBIDDEN THE RECEIPT OF THE IMAGE OR VIDEO.
14 3–2602.
15 THIS SUBTITLE DOES NOT APPLY TO:
16 (1) AN INTERNET SERVICE PROVIDER, A MOBILE DATA PROVIDER, OR
17 AN OPERATOR OF AN ONLINE OR MOBILE APPLICATION, TO THE EXTENT THAT THE
18 ENTITY IS TRANSMITTING, ROUTING, OR PROVIDING CONNECTIONS FOR
19 ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATIONS INITIATED BY OR AT THE DIRECTION OF ANOTHER
20 PERSON;
21 (2) ANY SERVICE THAT TRANSMITS IMAGES OR AUDIOVISUAL WORKS,
22 INCLUDING AN ON–DEMAND, SUBSCRIPTION, OR ADVERTISING–SUPPORTED
23 SERVICE; OR
24 (3) A HEALTH CARE PROVIDER TRANSMITTING AN IMAGE FOR A
25 LEGITIMATE MEDICAL PURPOSE.
26 3–2603.
27 A PERSON MAY NOT KNOWINGLY SEND BY ELECTRONIC MEANS TO ANOTHER
28 PERSON AN IMAGE OR A VIDEO CONTAINING OBSCENE MATERIAL THAT THE SENDER
29 KNOWS OR REASONABLY SHOULD KNOW IS UNSOLICITED.
30 3–2604.
HOUSE BILL 670 3
1 (A) A PERSON WHO SUFFERS HARM AS A RESULT OF RECEIVING AN IMAGE
2 OR A VIDEO IN VIOLATION OF § 3–2603 OF THIS SUBTITLE MAY BRING AN ACTION
3 FOR ECONOMIC AND NONECONOMIC DAMAGES PROXIMATELY CAUSED BY THE
4 RECEIPT OF THE IMAGE OR VIDEO, INCLUDING DAMAGES FOR EMOTIONAL
5 DISTRESS.
6 (B) IF THE RECEIPT OF THE IMAGE OR VIDEO IN VIOLATION OF § 3–2603 OF
7 THIS SUBTITLE WAS EXPRESSLY FORBIDDEN BY THE PERSON BRINGING AN ACTION
8 UNDER THIS SUBTITLE, THE PERSON MAY ADDITIONALLY SEEK AND BE AWARDED,
9 ON REQUEST AND BEFORE THE FINAL JUDGMENT IS RENDERED:
10 (1) STATUTORY DAMAGES NOT EXCEEDING $30,000 AND NOT LESS
11 THAN $1,500; AND
12 (2) PUNITIVE DAMAGES.
13 (C) A PERSON BRINGING AN ACTION UNDER THIS THIS SUBTITLE MAY ALSO
14 SEEK AND BE AWARDED:
15 (1) REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES;
16 (2) COURT COSTS;
17 (3) INJUNCTIVE RELIEF; AND
18 (4) ANY OTHER AVAILABLE RELIEF.
19 (D) THE REMEDIES PROVIDED IN THIS SECTION ARE CUMULATIVE AND MAY
20 NOT BE CONSTRUED AS RESTRICTING ANY OTHER REMEDY THAT IS AVAILABLE
21 UNDER LAW.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
23 October 1, 2025.
