A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Board of Dental Examiners – Expanded Function Dental Assistants –
3 Education and Reinstatement
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the State Board of Dental Examiners to approve a certain
5 course of study for expanded function dental assistant certification; requiring that a
6 course of study for initial dental assistant certification include an option for an online
7 course of study when appropriate; requiring the Board to reinstate an expired
8 expanded function dental assistant certification only under certain circumstances;
9 requiring the Board to adopt certain regulations relating to continuing education for
10 expanded function dental assistants; and generally relating to expanded function
11 dental assistants.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Health Occupations
14 Section 4–302.1
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 BY adding to
18 Article – Health Occupations
19 Section 4–311.1
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
22 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
23 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
24 Article – Health Occupations
25 4–302.1.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb0573*
2 HOUSE BILL 573
1 (a) To qualify for a certificate to be an expanded function dental assistant, an
2 applicant shall be an individual who meets the requirements of this section.
3 (b) The applicant must be of good moral character.
4 (c) The applicant must be at least 18 years old.
5 (d) The applicant must:
6 (1) Be a high school graduate or have attained a high school equivalency;
7 (2) Have successfully completed a course of study approved, subject to
8 subsection (e) of this section, by the Board related to the intraoral procedure for which the
9 applicant has applied for a certificate;
10 (3) Have the appropriate certification from the Dental Assisting National
11 Board;
12 (4) Have passed any examination provided for in a regulation adopted by
13 the Board; and
14 (5) Submit to a State criminal history records check.
15 (e) The Board may not approve a course of study under subsection (d)(2) of this
16 section unless the course of study is part of:
17 (1) A U.S. Department of Labor Job Corps dental assisting program;
18 (2) A dental assisting program located within an institution of
19 postsecondary education that is accredited by an accrediting institution recognized by the
20 U.S. Department of Education;
21 (3) A dental assisting program that is located within a high school and is
22 recognized by an accrediting body recognized by the U.S. Department of Education or the
23 high school accrediting body of a state; [or]
24 (4) A dental assisting program that is accredited by the Council on Dental
25 Accreditation, or a successor organization;
26 (5) A DENTAL ASSISTING COURSE OR PROGRAM OFFERED BY THE
27 DALE FOUNDATION, AN AFFILIATE OF THE DENTAL ASSISTING NATIONAL BOARD,
28 OR ANY SUCCESSOR ENTITY; OR
29 (6) AN ORTHODONTIC DENTAL ASSISTING COURSE OR PROGRAM
30 OFFERED BY THE TRAPEZIO ORTHODONTIC ASSISTANT TRAINING SOLUTIONS, OR
31 ANY SUCCESSOR ENTITY.
HOUSE BILL 573 3
1 (F) A COURSE OF STUDY FOR INITIAL CERTIFICATION UNDER THIS SECTION
2 SHALL INCLUDE, WHEN APPROPRIATE, AN OPTION FOR AN ONLINE COURSE OF
3 STUDY.
4 4–311.1.
5 (A) THE BOARD SHALL REINSTATE AN EXPANDED FUNCTION DENTAL
6 ASSISTANT CERTIFICATE THAT IS EXPIRED ONLY IF THE HOLDER:
7 (1) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, HAS COMPLETED
8 THE CONTINUING EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS SET BY RULE AND REGULATION OF
9 THE BOARD;
10 (2) HAS SUBMITTED A RENEWAL APPLICATION TO THE BOARD
11 BEFORE THE DEADLINE SET BY THE BOARD; AND
12 (3) PAYS TO THE BOARD A RENEWAL FEE SET BY THE BOARD.
13 (B) THE BOARD SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS REQUIRING CONTINUING
14 EDUCATION FOR EXPANDED FUNCTION DENTAL ASSISTANTS TO:
15 (1) INCLUDE AN OPTION FOR AN ONLINE COURSE OF STUDY; AND
16 (2) BE OFFERED BY:
17 (I) DENTAL PROFESSIONAL SOCIETIES AFFILIATED WITH
18 NATIONAL DENTAL ORGANIZATIONS; AND
19 (II) ENTITIES LISTED IN § 4–302.1(E) OF THIS SUBTITLE.
20 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
21 October 1, 2025.
