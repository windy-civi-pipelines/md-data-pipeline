CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Vehicle Laws – Obscured or Modified, Modified, or Blocked Registration Plates
3 and Registration Plate Covers
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from obscuring or modifying any vehicle
5 registration plate in a manner that may prevent identification and enforcing the
6 prohibition as a secondary offense; prohibiting a person from advertising for or
7 promoting the sale of certain registration plate covers in a certain manner and
8 establishing that a violation is an unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practice subject
9 to certain enforcement and penalties; and generally relating to obscured or modified,
10 modified, or blocked vehicle registration plates and registration plate covers.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
12 Article – Commercial Law
13 Section 13–301(14)(xlii)
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
16 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
17 Article – Commercial Law
18 Section 13–301(14)(xliii)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
21 BY adding to
22 Article – Commercial Law

14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
16 Article – Commercial Law
17 13–301.
18 Unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practices include any:
19 (14) Violation of a provision of:
20 (xlii) Section 12–6C–09.1 of the Health Occupations Article; [or]
21 (xliii) Title 14, Subtitle 48 of this article; or
22 (XLIV) SECTION 13–411.1(C)(2) OF THE TRANSPORTATION
23 ARTICLE; OR
24 Article – Transportation
25 13–411.
26 (c) (1) At all times, each registration plate shall be:
27 (i) Maintained free from foreign materials, including registration
28 plate covers as defined in § 13–411.1 of this subtitle, and in a condition to be clearly legible;
29 and
30 (ii) Securely fastened to the vehicle for which it is issued:
31 1. In a horizontal position;
SENATE BILL 322 3
1 2. In a manner that prevents the plate from swinging; and
2 3. In a place and position to be clearly visible.
3 (2) For a violation involving the placement of an object framing or
4 bordering the edges of a registration plate, a police officer may enforce this subsection only
5 as a secondary action when the police officer detains a driver of a motor vehicle for a
6 suspected violation of another provision of the Code.
7 13–411.1.
8 (a) In this section, “registration plate cover” means any tinted, colored, painted,
9 marked, [clear,] or illuminated object that is designed to:
10 (1) [Cover] OBSCURE OR BLOCK any of the characters of a vehicle’s
11 registration plate; or
12 (2) Distort a recorded image of any of the characters of a vehicle’s
13 registration plate recorded by a traffic control signal monitoring system under § 21–202.1
14 of this article.
15 (b) A person may not sell or offer for sale a registration plate cover.
16 (c) (1) A person may not advertise for the purpose of promoting the sale of
17 registration plate covers.
18 (2) A PERSON MAY NOT ADVERTISE FOR OR PROMOTE THE SALE OF
19 AN UNLAWFUL REGISTRATION PLATE COVER BY FALSELY CLAIMING THAT THE
20 REGISTRATION PLATE COVER IS LAWFUL IN THE STATE.
21 (D) A VIOLATION OF SUBSECTION (C)(2) OF THIS SECTION IS:
22 (1) AN UNFAIR, ABUSIVE, OR DECEPTIVE TRADE PRACTICE WITHIN
23 THE MEANING OF TITLE 13 OF THE COMMERCIAL LAW ARTICLE; AND
24 (2) IN ADDITION TO THE PENALTY UNDER § 27–101 OF THIS ARTICLE,
25 SUBJECT TO THE ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTY PROVISIONS CONTAINED IN TITLE
26 13 OF THE COMMERCIAL LAW ARTICLE.
27 21–1112.1.
28 (a) A person may not obscure or modify any vehicle registration plate [with intent
29 to avoid] IN A MANNER THAT MAY PREVENT identification.
4 SENATE BILL 322
1 (b) A violation of this section is a moving violation for the purpose of the
2 assessment of points under § 16–402 of this article.
3 (C) A POLICE OFFICER MAY ENFORCE THIS SECTION ONLY AS A SECONDARY
4 ACTION WHEN THE POLICE OFFICER DETAINS A DRIVER OF A MOTOR VEHICLE FOR
5 A SUSPECTED VIOLATION OF ANOTHER PROVISION OF THE CODE.
6 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
7 October 1, 2025.
