CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Local Comprehensive Planning and State Economic Growth, Resource
3 Protection, and Planning Policy – Planning Principles
4 FOR the purpose of providing that certain planning principles are intended to accomplish
5 certain goals and that certain goals are to be accomplished with certain public
6 participation; altering the planning visions for local comprehensive planning to
7 consist of certain planning principles; altering the State Economic Growth, Resource
8 Protection, and Planning Policy to consist of certain planning principles; requiring
9 the publisher of the Annotated Code of Maryland, in consultation with the
10 Department of Legislative Services, to correct cross–references and terminology in
11 the Code that are rendered incorrect by this Act; and generally relating to local
12 comprehensive planning and the State Economic Growth, Resource Protection, and
13 Planning Policy.
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – Land Use
16 Section 1–201

18 (2012 Volume and 2024 Supplement)

1 Annotated Code of Maryland
2 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
3 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
4 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
5 Article – Land Use
6 1–201.
7 (A) (1) THE PLANNING PRINCIPLES UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS
8 SECTION ARE COLLECTIVELY INTENDED TO CREATE SUSTAINABLE COMMUNITIES
9 AND TO PROTECT THE ENVIRONMENT IN ORDER TO FOSTER A HIGH QUALITY OF LIFE
10 FOR ALL RESIDENTS OF THE STATE.
11 (2) TO ACCOMPLISH THE GOALS UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
12 SUBSECTION, RESIDENTS SHOULD BE ACTIVE PARTNERS IN THE PLANNING AND
13 IMPLEMENTATION OF COMMUNITY INITIATIVES AND SENSITIVE TO THEIR
14 RESPONSIBILITIES AS STEWARDS IN BALANCING AND ACHIEVING COMMUNITY
15 GOALS.
16 (B) In addition to the requirements of § 3–201(a) and (b) of this article, a planning
17 commission shall implement the following [visions] PLANNING PRINCIPLES through the
18 comprehensive plan described in Title 3 of this article:
19 [(1) quality of life and sustainability: a high quality of life is achieved
20 through universal stewardship of the land, water, and air resulting in sustainable
21 communities and protection of the environment;
22 (2) public participation: citizens are active partners in the planning and
23 implementation of community initiatives and are sensitive to their responsibilities in
24 achieving community goals;
25 (3) growth areas: growth is concentrated in existing population and
26 business centers, growth areas adjacent to these centers, or strategically selected new
27 centers;
28 (4) community design: compact, mixed–use, walkable design consistent
29 with existing community character and located near available or planned transit options is
30 encouraged to ensure efficient use of land and transportation resources and preservation
31 and enhancement of natural systems, open spaces, recreational areas, and historical,
32 cultural, and archaeological resources;
33 (5) infrastructure: growth areas have the water resources and
34 infrastructure to accommodate population and business expansion in an orderly, efficient,
35 and environmentally sustainable manner;
HOUSE BILL 286 3
1 (6) transportation: a well–maintained, multimodal transportation system
2 facilitates the safe, convenient, affordable, and efficient movement of people, goods, and
3 services within and between population and business centers;
4 (7) housing: a range of housing densities, types, and sizes provides
5 residential options for citizens of all ages and incomes;
6 (8) economic development: economic development and natural
7 resource–based businesses that promote employment opportunities for all income levels
8 within the capacity of the State’s natural resources, public services, and public facilities are
9 encouraged;
10 (9) environmental protection: land and water resources, including the
11 Chesapeake and coastal bays, are carefully managed to restore and maintain healthy air
12 and water, natural systems, and living resources;
13 (10) resource conservation: waterways, forests, agricultural areas, open
14 space, natural systems, and scenic areas are conserved;
15 (11) stewardship: government, business entities, and residents are
16 responsible for the creation of sustainable communities by collaborating to balance efficient
17 growth with resource protection; and
18 (12) implementation: strategies, policies, programs, and funding for growth
19 and development, resource conservation, infrastructure, and transportation are integrated
20 across the local, regional, State, and interstate levels to achieve these visions.]
21 (1) LAND: OPTIMIZE LAND PRODUCTIVITY OF WORKING LANDSCAPES,
22 INCLUDING FARMS AND FORESTS, AND FISHERIES, AND PRIORITIZE DEVELOPMENT
23 WITHIN POPULATION CENTERS AND THAT ARE IN PROXIMITY TO EXISTING
24 INFRASTRUCTURE AND FACILITIES;
25 (2) TRANSPORTATION: PRIORITIZE TRANSPORTATION NETWORKS
26 THAT CREATE ENERGY EFFICIENT, AFFORDABLE, AND RELIABLE ACCESS TO JOBS,
27 HOUSING, AND SERVICES;
28 (3) HOUSING: ENABLE A MIX OF QUALITY HOUSING TYPES AND
29 AFFORDABILITY OPTIONS TO ACCOMMODATE ALL WHO WANT TO LIVE IN THE STATE;
30 (4) ECONOMY: ALLOW FOR ADAPTIVE REUSE, MIXED–USE, AND
31 CONTEXT APPROPRIATE NEW DEVELOPMENT THAT RESPONDS TO CHANGING
32 MARKETS AND INNOVATIONS;
4 HOUSE BILL 286

2 DEVELOPMENT TO ENSURE DIVERSE VOICES ARE HEARD AND THE NEEDS OF
3 UNDERSERVED POPULATIONS ARE PRIORITIZED;
4 (6) RESILIENCE: INTEGRATE RESILIENCY MEASURES THAT WILL
5 MINIMIZE THE IMPACTS OF RAPID AND UNEXPECTED NATURAL– AND
6 HUMAN–CAUSED THREATS ON COMMUNITIES;
7 (7) PLACE: PROVIDE FOR PUBLIC SPACES THAT ENCOURAGE SOCIAL

11 AGRICULTURAL AREAS, AND WATERWAYS.

15 (ii) serves to connect priority funding areas, if:
16 1. the Department of Transportation and the Department of
17 Planning determine that adequate access control or other measures are in place to:
18 A. prevent development that is inconsistent with §
19 [5–7A–01(1), (2), and (3)] 5–7A–01 of this title; and
20 B. maintain the viability of the project while concomitantly
21 constraining development which potentially detracts from main street business areas; and
22 2. the Department of Transportation and the Department of
23 Planning have first determined whether alternative transportation modes, such as mass
24 transit and transportation demand management, provide a reasonable alternative to the
25 project and that no reasonable alternative exists;
26 (iii) has the sole purpose of providing control of access by the
27 Department of Transportation along an existing highway corridor; or
28 (iv) due to its operational or physical characteristics, must be located
29 away from other development.
