CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Business Regulation – Home Improvement Guaranty Fund – Alterations
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing the Maryland Home Improvement Commission to award a
4 claimant a certain amount for attorney’s fees from the Home Improvement Guaranty
5 Fund; altering the content in a final court judgment or final award in arbitration
6 that a claimant must provide to the Commission to claim payment from the Fund
7 under a certain provision of law; and generally relating to recovery awards from the
8 Home Improvement Guaranty Fund.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
10 Article – Business Regulation
11 Section 8–201, 8–401, 8–403(a), and 8–405(a) and (b)
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2024 Replacement Volume)
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – Business Regulation
16 Section 8–405(e) and 8–409(a)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2024 Replacement Volume)
19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
21 Article – Business Regulation
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0578*
2 SENATE BILL 578
1 8–201.
2 There is a Maryland Home Improvement Commission in the Department.
3 8–401.
4 In this subtitle, “actual loss” means the costs of restoration, repair, replacement, or
5 completion that arise from an unworkmanlike, inadequate, or incomplete home
6 improvement.
7 8–403.
8 (a) The Commission shall:
9 (1) establish a Home Improvement Guaranty Fund;
10 (2) keep the Fund at a level of at least $1,000,000; and
11 (3) submit a report detailing actions being taken to restore the balance of
12 the Fund to a sustainable level to the Senate Committee on Education, Energy, and the
13 Environment and the House Economic Matters Committee within 30 days of projecting that
14 the Fund balance will be less than $1,000,000.
15 8–405.
16 (a) Subject to this subtitle, an owner may recover compensation from the Fund
17 for an actual loss that results from an act or omission by a licensed contractor or a violation
18 of § 8–607(4) of this title as found by the Commission or a court of competent jurisdiction.
19 (b) For purposes of recovery from the Fund, the act or omission of a licensed
20 contractor includes the act or omission of a subcontractor, salesperson, or employee of the
21 licensed contractor, whether or not an express agency relationship exists.
22 (e) The Commission may not award from the Fund:
23 (1) more than $30,000 to one claimant for acts or omissions of one
24 contractor;
25 (2) more than $250,000 to all claimants for acts or omissions of one
26 contractor unless, after the Commission has paid out $250,000 on account of acts or
27 omissions of the contractor, the contractor reimburses $250,000 to the Fund;
28 (3) an amount for [attorney fees,] consequential damages, court costs,
29 interest, personal injury damages, or punitive damages;
SENATE BILL 578 3
1 (4) AN AMOUNT FOR ATTORNEY’S FEES GREATER THAN THE AMOUNT
2 AWARDED FOR THE ACTUAL LOSS RESULTING FROM THE ACTS OR OMISSIONS OF
3 THE CONTRACTOR, PROVIDED THAT THE TOTAL AMOUNT AWARDED TO A CLAIMANT
4 DOES NOT EXCEED $30,000;
5 (5) an amount as a result of a default judgment in court; or
6 [(5)] (6) an amount in excess of the amount paid by or on behalf of the
7 claimant to the contractor against whom the claim is filed.
8 8–409.
9 (a) The Commission may order payment of a claim against the Fund only if:
10 (1) the decision or order of the Commission is final in accordance with Title
11 10, Subtitle 2 of the State Government Article and all rights of appeal are exhausted; or
12 (2) the claimant provides the Commission with a certified copy of a final
13 judgment of a court of competent jurisdiction or a final award in arbitration, with all rights
14 of appeal exhausted, in which the court or arbitrator:
15 (i) [expressly] has found on the merits that the claimant is entitled
16 to recover under COMPENSATION IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 8–405(a) of this subtitle; and
17 (ii) has found the value of the actual loss.
18 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
19 October 1, 2025.
