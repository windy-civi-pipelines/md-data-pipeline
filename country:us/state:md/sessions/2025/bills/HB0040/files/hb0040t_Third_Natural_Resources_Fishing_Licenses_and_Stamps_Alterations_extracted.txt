1 AN ACT concerning
2 Natural Resources – Fishing Licenses and Stamps – Alterations
3 FOR the purpose of altering the fees for resident consolidated senior sport fishing licenses,
4 angler’s licenses, and trout stamps; repealing the exemption from the requirement
5 to obtain a trout stamp under certain circumstances for a holder of a resident
6 consolidated senior sport fishing license; and generally relating to fishing licenses
7 and stamps.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Natural Resources
10 Section 4–216, 4–604, and 4–614
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Natural Resources
16 4–216.

2 HOUSE BILL 40
1 (a) The Department shall establish a resident consolidated senior sport fishing
2 license, to be issued to residents of Maryland beginning in the calendar year in which they
3 attain the age of 65.
4 (b) (1) The resident consolidated senior sport fishing license may be obtained
5 from the Department or from any authorized agent of the Department.
6 (2) The annual fee for the license is [$5] $12.
7 (3) As compensation, an agent shall retain 50 cents for each license issued.
8 (c) A resident consolidated senior sport fishing license shall be valid for 1 year
9 following the date of issuance.
10 (d) A holder of a resident consolidated senior sport fishing license may:
11 (1) Engage in angling on the nontidal waters of the State without holding
12 an angler’s license under § 4–604 of this title; AND
13 (2) [Engage in angling for trout on the nontidal waters of the State without
14 holding a trout stamp under § 4–614 of this title; and
15 (3)] Fish for finfish in the Chesapeake Bay and its tributaries or in the State
16 waters of the Atlantic Ocean and the Atlantic coastal bays and their tributaries without
17 holding a Chesapeake Bay and coastal sport fishing license under § 4–745 of this title.
18 4–604.
19 (a) This section provides a fund to pay the expense of protecting and managing
20 game and freshwater fish and preventing unauthorized persons from fishing or attempting
21 to fish for any game and freshwater fish without first procuring an angler’s license.
22 (b) Any person 16 years old or older shall secure an angler’s license to fish in the
23 nontidal waters of the State. An angler’s license entitles the holder to fish in the nontidal
24 waters of the State only during the open season.
25 (c) An angler’s license is not required of the following:
26 (1) The owner or tenant of land bordering on nontidal water, his spouse
27 and children, or the spouse of any child who resides on the land with the owner or tenant
28 when he fishes in nontidal water adjoining his land;
29 (2) Any resident serving in the armed forces of the United States, while on
30 leave in the State, during the resident’s leave period, if, while angling, the resident
31 possesses a copy of the resident’s official leave order;
HOUSE BILL 40 3
1 (3) A person who fishes in nontidal waters of the State on free fishing days
2 designated by the Secretary;
3 (4) A person holding a current resident consolidated senior sport fishing
4 license issued under § 4–216 of this title;
5 (5) A person fishing in a free fishing area established under § 4–214(b)(2)
6 of this title; or
7 (6) A person authorized by a disability exemption issued under § 4–217 of
8 this title.
9 (d) A person who wishes to obtain an angler’s license shall complete and submit
10 an application to the Department or to any person designated by the Department to issue
11 an angler’s license.
12 (e) (1) An applicant for a license issued under this section:
13 (i) Shall provide all the information requested by the Department;
14 and
15 (ii) May not willfully make a false statement or representation to the
16 Department on the application.
17 (2) The Department shall require an applicant for a license under this
18 section to provide the last four digits of the applicant’s Social Security number, if the
19 applicant has a Social Security number.
20 (f) (1) Subject to paragraph (3) of this subsection, the following annual license
21 fees shall apply:
22 (i) Resident ………………………………………………… [$20.50] $32
23 (ii) Subject to paragraph (2)(ii) of this subsection, short–term license
24 valid for 7 consecutive days from date of issuance ………………………………….. [$7.50] $16
25 (iii) Resident and nonresident blind persons …………………… No fee
26 (iv) Complimentary license ……………………………………….. No fee
27 (2) For a nonresident:
28 (i) The fee for an annual angler’s license is the greater of:
29 1. [$30.50] $55; or
4 HOUSE BILL 40
1 2. A fee equal to the fee charged a Maryland resident by the
2 nonresident’s home state for a similar license; and
3 (ii) The fee for a short–term license valid for 7 consecutive days from
4 the date of issuance is the greater of:
5 1. [$7.50] $45; or
6 2. A fee equal to the fee charged a Maryland resident by the
7 nonresident’s home state for a license that permits an equal number of days of fishing or
8 the next higher number of days as permitted by the Maryland license.
9 (iii) The fee for a short–term license valid for 3 consecutive days from
10 the date of issuance is the greater of:
11 1. [$5] $35; or
12 2. A fee equal to the fee charged a Maryland resident by the
13 nonresident’s home state for a license that permits an equal number of days of fishing or
14 the next higher number of days as permitted by the Maryland license.
15 (3) The Department shall implement a program to provide discounted
16 angler’s licenses and trout stamps to Maryland residents who are recipients of the Purple
17 Heart Award.
18 (4) (i) All fees collected by the Department under this section may only
19 be used in accordance with § 4–208 of this title.
20 (ii) The Department shall publicly report annually the amounts
21 collected and the expenditures under this section.
22 (g) A person designated to sell an angler’s license shall issue the license and
23 collect the fee prescribed in subsection (f) of this section. The collected money shall be
24 transmitted to the Department as prescribed by the Department. As compensation, the
25 agent shall retain $1 for each license issued.
26 (h) (1) Every angler’s license shall be valid for 1 year following the date of
27 issuance.
28 (2) The license may not be transferred to another person, nor used or
29 presented by any person other than the person to whom it was issued.
30 (i) Every person licensed under this section shall submit any report the
31 Department requires in accordance with Department regulations.
32 4–614.

1 (a) (1) The Department shall adopt regulations to establish fishing areas
2 where a trout stamp is required.
3 (2) Except as provided in paragraph (3) of this subsection, a person may
4 not fish or possess trout in an area established in paragraph (1) of this subsection unless
5 the person first obtains a trout stamp in addition to an angler’s license.
6 (3) A trout stamp is not required of the following:
7 (i) [A holder of a current resident consolidated senior sport fishing
8 license issued under § 4–216 of this title;
9 (ii)] A holder of a lifetime complimentary angler’s license issued
10 under § 4–607(a)(2) or (3) of this subtitle;
11 [(iii)] (II) A person authorized by a disability exemption issued
12 under § 4–217 of this title;
13 [(iv)] (III) Any resident serving in the armed forces of the United
14 States while on leave in the State, during the resident’s leave period, if, while angling, the
15 resident possesses a copy of the resident’s official leave order; or
16 [(v)] (IV) A person under 16 years of age.
17 (4) The stamp may be obtained from the Department or an authorized
18 agent of the Department.
19 (b) A person is not required to have a trout stamp to possess trout taken from:
20 (1) Any privately owned lake or pond that is stocked with trout artificially
21 propagated by commercial hatcheries or purchased from persons licensed to sell fish; or
22 (2) Any fee fishing lake or pond operated under § 4–11A–20 of this title.
23 (c) The following annual trout stamp fees shall apply:
24 (1) Resident………………………………………………………………[$5] $20
25 (2) Nonresident……………………………………………………..….[$10] $30
26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
27 1, 2025.
