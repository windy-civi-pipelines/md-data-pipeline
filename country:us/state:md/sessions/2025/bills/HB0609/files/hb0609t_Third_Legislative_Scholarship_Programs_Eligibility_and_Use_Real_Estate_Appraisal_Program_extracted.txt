1 AN ACT concerning
2 Legislative Scholarship Programs – Eligibility and Use – Real Estate Appraisal
3 Program
4 FOR the purpose of exempting certain applicants for a senatorial scholarship from certain
5 examination requirements; authorizing certain uses of senatorial and Delegate
6 scholarships for applicants who plan to complete the Real Property Appraiser
7 Qualification Criteria of the Practical Applications of Real Estate Appraisal
8 Program; and generally relating to the legislative scholarship programs.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
10 Article – Education
11 Section 18–402, 18–405, 18–501, 18–503, and 18–506
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
16 Article – Education
17 18–402.

2 HOUSE BILL 609
1 (a) (1) Except as provided in paragraphs (2) [and], (3), AND (4) of this
2 subsection, each applicant for a senatorial scholarship shall:
3 (i) Take a competitive examination administered by the Office; and
4 (ii) 1. Be accepted for admission in the regular undergraduate,
5 graduate, or professional program at an eligible institution; or
6 2. Be enrolled in a 2–year terminal certificate program in
7 which the course work is acceptable for transfer credit for an accredited baccalaureate
8 program in an eligible institution.
9 (2) An applicant is exempt from the examination if the applicant:
10 (i) Is attending an eligible institution and has completed at least 1
11 year in good academic standing at the institution;
12 (ii) Graduated from high school at least 5 years before application
13 for a senatorial scholarship;
14 (iii) Is accepted for admission to a private career institution, if the
15 institution’s curriculum is approved by the Commission, and the institution is accredited
16 by a national accrediting association approved by the United States Department of
17 Education; or
18 (iv) Is planning to attend or is attending a Maryland community
19 college.
20 (3) An applicant is exempt from the requirements of paragraph (1) of this
21 subsection if the applicant is or was enrolled in a certificate or license program, course, or
22 sequence of courses at a community college that leads to certification or licensure.
23 (4) AN APPLICANT IS EXEMPT FROM THE REQUIREMENTS OF
24 PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION IF THE APPLICANT IS REGISTERED FOR OR IS
25 PLANNING TO COMPLETE THE REAL PROPERTY APPRAISER QUALIFICATION
26 CRITERIA OF THE PRACTICAL APPLICATIONS OF REAL ESTATE APPRAISAL
27 PROGRAM ADOPTED BY THE APPRAISAL FOUNDATION APPRAISER
28 QUALIFICATIONS BOARD AUTHORIZED UNDER THE FEDERAL FINANCIAL
29 INSTITUTIONS REFORM, RECOVERY, AND ENFORCEMENT ACT OF 1989.
30 (b) Each applicant shall:
31 (1) Be eligible for in–State tuition under this article; and
32 (2) At the time of the applicant’s initial application, be domiciled in the
33 legislative district from which the applicant seeks an award.
HOUSE BILL 609 3
1 (c) (1) Each applicant shall demonstrate to the Office a definite financial need,
2 and each Senator shall consider the financial need of each applicant.
3 (2) Each Senator is the final judge of the financial need of each applicant
4 to whom the Senator awards a scholarship, and the Office may not negate the Senator’s
5 judgment or impose requirements of time or procedure.
6 (d) Each applicant shall accept any other conditions attached to the award.
7 18–405.
8 (a) Except as provided in [subsection] SUBSECTIONS (B)(3) AND (d) of this
9 section, a senatorial scholarship may be used only at any community college or
10 undergraduate, graduate, or professional school in the State.
11 (b) A senatorial scholarship may be used:
12 (1) If the curriculum is approved by the Commission, at a degree granting
13 institution of higher education, a hospital diploma school for training registered nurses, or
14 to attend a private postsecondary proprietary institution that is accredited by a national
15 accrediting association approved by the United States Department of Education as a
16 full–time student; [and]
17 (2) For educational expenses as defined by the Office, including:
18 (i) Tuition and mandatory fees; and
19 (ii) Room and board; AND
20 (3) FOR EXPENSES TO REGISTER FOR AND COMPLETE THE REAL
21 PROPERTY APPRAISER QUALIFICATION CRITERIA OF THE PRACTICAL
22 APPLICATIONS OF REAL ESTATE APPRAISAL PROGRAM ADOPTED BY THE
23 APPRAISAL FOUNDATION APPRAISER QUALIFICATIONS BOARD AUTHORIZED
24 UNDER THE FEDERAL FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS REFORM, RECOVERY, AND
25 ENFORCEMENT ACT OF 1989.
26 (c) If on–campus housing is available, a senatorial scholarship may not be used
27 to pay for off–campus housing.
28 (d) Each Senator may award not more than 10% of the funds available under this
29 subtitle to applicants planning to attend accredited undergraduate, graduate, or
30 professional institutions outside the State, provided the applicant:
4 HOUSE BILL 609
1 (1) Will be studying in an academic area that is not offered in a program
2 that is accredited by a national accrediting association approved by the United States
3 Department of Education in any institution in the State;
4 (2) Is a hearing impaired person who will be studying at an institution
5 outside the State that makes special provisions for hearing impaired students, and
6 comparable special provisions are not available to the applicant at an institution in the
7 State; or
8 (3) Is an individual who is on active duty with the United States military
9 who is domiciled in this State.
10 18–501.
11 (a) There is a program of Delegate Scholarships in this State that are awarded
12 under this subtitle.
13 (b) A scholarship awarded under this subtitle may be used at:
14 (1) An eligible institution for a program of undergraduate, graduate, or
15 professional studies;
16 (2) An accredited undergraduate, graduate, or professional institution
17 outside the State, if the applicant:
18 (i) Will be studying in an academic area that is not offered in a
19 program that is accredited by a national accrediting association approved by the United
20 States Department of Education in any institution in this State;
21 (ii) Is disabled and will be studying at an institution outside the
22 State that makes special provisions for disabled students that are not available to the
23 applicant at an institution in the State; or
24 (iii) Is an individual who is on active duty with the United States
25 military who is domiciled in this State; [and]
26 (3) A private career school within the State that is approved by the
27 Maryland Higher Education Commission under § 11–202 of this article and that is
28 accredited by a national accrediting association that is approved by the United States
29 Department of Education; AND
30 (4) A PRACTICAL APPLICATIONS OF REAL ESTATE APPRAISAL
31 PROGRAM ADOPTED BY THE APPRAISAL FOUNDATION APPRAISER
32 QUALIFICATIONS BOARD AUTHORIZED UNDER THE FEDERAL FINANCIAL
33 INSTITUTIONS REFORM, RECOVERY, AND ENFORCEMENT ACT OF 1989.

1 (c) Money appropriated to the Commission for scholarships awarded under this
2 section that is not used by the end of the fiscal year shall be retained by the Commission
3 for use by the awarding Delegate in the Delegate Scholarship Program during subsequent
4 fiscal years.

6 (a) During each term in office, each Delegate may award the equivalent of four
7 4–year full–time scholarships, which may be awarded for either 1–, 2–, 3–, or 4–year

10 institution OR EXPENSES TO REGISTER FOR AND COMPLETE A PRACTICAL

14 ENFORCEMENT ACT OF 1989.

16 any graduate or professional program may not exceed the equivalent annual tuition and
17 mandatory fees of an undergraduate program at the 4–year public institution of higher
18 education within the University System of Maryland, other than the University of
19 Maryland Global Campus and University of Maryland, Baltimore Campus, with the
20 highest annual expenses for a full–time resident undergraduate.
21 18–506.
22 (a) As an alternative to the scholarship awards authorized by §§ 18–503 through
23 18–505 of this subtitle, and subject to the provisions of subsection (b) of this section, during
24 each year in office each Delegate may award scholarships in a total amount equal to four
25 times the tuition and mandatory fees for a full–time undergraduate student enrolled at the
26 University of Maryland, College Park Campus for the academic year commencing in that
27 year.
28 (b) A scholarship award under subsection (a) of this section:
29 (1) May not be for an amount less than $200 or more than one–half of the
30 total amount of scholarships authorized by subsection (a) of this section for that year;
