Requested: July 1, 2024
Introduced and read first time: January 8, 2025
Assigned to: Ways and Means
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Primary and Secondary Education – Comprehensive Health Education
3 Framework – Established
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the State Department of Education, in collaboration with the
5 Maryland Department of Health, to develop a comprehensive health education
6 framework; requiring each county board of education to create an age–appropriate
7 curriculum that is consistent with the comprehensive health education framework;
8 requiring each county board to establish policies, guidelines, and procedures for a
9 parent or guardian to opt out of a certain topic for the parent’s or guardian’s student,
10 subject to a certain prohibition and requirement; and generally relating to the
11 establishment of a comprehensive health education framework in public schools.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Education
14 Section 7–401
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Education
20 7–401.
21 (a) With the assistance of the county health department, each county board shall
22 provide:
23 (1) Adequate school health services;

1 (2) Instruction in health education, including the importance of physical
2 activity in maintaining good health; and
3 (3) A healthful school environment.
4 (b) The Department and the Maryland Department of Health jointly shall:
5 (1) Develop public standards and guidelines for school health programs;
6 and
7 (2) Offer assistance to the county boards and county health departments
8 in their implementation.
9 (c) (1) THE DEPARTMENT, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE MARYLAND
10 DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH, SHALL DEVELOP A COMPREHENSIVE HEALTH
11 EDUCATION FRAMEWORK.
12 (2) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL UPDATE THE COMPREHENSIVE
13 HEALTH EDUCATION FRAMEWORK IN THE MANNER AND AT THE TIME THE STATE
14 BOARD DETERMINES IS NECESSARY.
15 (3) THE COMPREHENSIVE HEALTH EDUCATION FRAMEWORK SHALL,
16 AT A MINIMUM, INCLUDE THE FOLLOWING TOPICS:
17 (I) HEALTH PROMOTION;
18 (II) MENTAL AND EMOTIONAL HEALTH;
19 (III) SUBSTANCE ABUSE PREVENTION;
20 (IV) FAMILY LIFE AND HUMAN SEXUALITY;
21 (V) GENDER IDENTITY AND SEXUAL ORIENTATION;
22 (VI) SAFETY AND VIOLENCE PREVENTION;
23 (VII) SAFE AND APPROPRIATE SOCIAL MEDIA AND INTERNET
24 USE;
25 (VIII) HEALTHY EATING; AND
26 (IX) DISEASE PREVENTION AND CONTROL.
HOUSE BILL 161 3
1 (4) (I) EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL CREATE AN
2 AGE–APPROPRIATE CURRICULUM THAT IS CONSISTENT WITH THE COMPREHENSIVE
3 HEALTH EDUCATION FRAMEWORK.
4 (II) IN DEVELOPING A CURRICULUM UNDER THIS PARAGRAPH,
5 EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL ESTABLISH A COMMITTEE COMPOSED OF EDUCATORS,
6 HEALTH EXPERTS, AND MEMBERS OF THE LOCAL COMMUNITY TO REVIEW AND
7 COMMENT ON WHETHER CURRICULUM MATERIALS ARE CONSISTENT WITH THE
8 COMPREHENSIVE HEALTH EDUCATION FRAMEWORK.
9 (5) (I) SUBJECT TO SUBPARAGRAPHS (II) AND (III) OF THIS
10 PARAGRAPH, EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL ESTABLISH POLICIES, GUIDELINES, AND
11 PROCEDURES FOR A PARENT OR GUARDIAN TO OPT OUT OF THE FAMILY LIFE AND
12 HUMAN SEXUALITY TOPIC FOR THE PARENT’S OR GUARDIAN’S STUDENT IN EACH
13 GRADE IN WHICH THE TOPIC IS TAUGHT.
14 (II) THE COUNTY BOARD MAY NOT AUTHORIZE A PARENT OR
15 GUARDIAN TO OPT THE PARENT’S OR GUARDIAN’S STUDENT OUT OF EDUCATION
16 RELATING TO HIV AND AIDS PREVENTION.
17 (III) EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL IDENTIFY APPROPRIATE
18 ALTERNATIVE LEARNING OBJECTIVES AND MEASURABLE GOALS THAT MEET STATE
19 AND LOCAL HEALTH EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS FOR THE TOPIC THE STUDENT’S
20 PARENT OR GUARDIAN HAS ELECTED TO OPT THE STUDENT OUT OF UNDER
21 SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH.
22 (6) ON OR BEFORE JUNE 15, 2026, AND EACH JUNE 15 THEREAFTER,
23 EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL SUBMIT A REPORT TO THE DEPARTMENT DESCRIBING
24 THE ACTIONS TAKEN BY THE COUNTY BOARD TO COMPLY WITH THE REQUIREMENTS
25 IN PARAGRAPHS (4) AND (5) OF THIS SUBSECTION.
26 (D) (1) (i) Each county board shall designate a school health services
27 program coordinator.
28 (ii) A county board may authorize the county health department to
29 designate the school health services program coordinator.
30 (2) The school health services program coordinator shall:
31 (i) Implement State and local health policies in the public schools;
32 (ii) Ensure that public schools adhere to local health services
33 guidelines; and
4 HOUSE BILL 161
1 (iii) Communicate State and local health policies to the parents and
2 guardians of public school students.
3 (3) (i) Except as provided in subparagraph (ii) of this paragraph, the
4 county board shall grant the school health services program coordinator the authority to
5 carry out the provisions of this subsection.
6 (ii) The county board shall consult with a county superintendent
7 before any change in the hiring or termination of personnel in connection with a school
8 health services program.
9 (4) The Department shall conduct at least two meetings annually with all
10 school health services program coordinators in the State.
11 [(d)] (E) On or before December 1, 2015, and every 5 years thereafter, the
12 Department shall report to the Governor and, subject to § 2–1257 of the State Government
13 Article, to the General Assembly a summary of the information reported to the State
14 Superintendent during the COMAR certification process.
15 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
16 1, 2025.
