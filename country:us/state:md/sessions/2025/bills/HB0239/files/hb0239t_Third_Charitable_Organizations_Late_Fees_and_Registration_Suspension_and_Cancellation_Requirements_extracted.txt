5 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
6 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
7 Article – Business Regulation
8 6–407.
9 (a) A charitable organization that collects less than $25,000 in charitable
10 contributions from the public in a year need not pay an annual fee, except that, if the
11 charitable organization uses a professional solicitor, it shall pay an annual fee of $50.
12 (b) (1) Each charitable organization that submits a separate registration
13 statement and collects at least $25,000 in charitable contributions from the public in a year
14 shall pay an annual fee based on the charitable contributions collected.
15 (2) The annual fee shall be:
16 (i) $50, if charitable contributions from the public are at least
17 $25,000 but less than $50,001;
18 (ii) $75, if charitable contributions from the public are at least
19 $50,001 but less than $75,001;
20 (iii) $100, if charitable contributions from the public are at least
21 $75,001 but less than $100,001;
22 (iv) $200, if charitable contributions from the public are at least
23 $100,001 but less than $500,001; and
24 (v) $300, if charitable contributions from the public are at least
25 $500,001.
26 (c) (1) [An] A CHARITABLE organization failing to file an annual report
27 either by the end of the 6–month period after the end of the charitable organization’s fiscal
28 year or within any period of extension granted shall pay, in addition to the annual fee, $25
29 for each month or part thereof after the date on which the annual report was due to be filed
30 or after the period of extension granted for such filing.
31 (2) The late fee shall be assessed 60 days after:
32 (i) the end of the 6th month after the end of the fiscal year; or
HOUSE BILL 239 3
1 (ii) the period of extension.
2 (3) Failure to remit an assessed late fee is a violation of this title.
3 (4) THE SECRETARY OF STATE MAY SUSPEND OR WAIVE LATE FEES
4 IN ACCORDANCE WITH:
5 (I) REGULATIONS ADOPTED UNDER SUBSECTION (F) OF THIS
6 SECTION; OR
7 (II) A SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT MADE UNDER § 6–205(B) AND
8 (C) OF THIS TITLE.
9 (d) The following revenues shall be distributed to the Charitable Enforcement
10 Fund under Subtitle 2A of this title, to be used only to support the actions of the Secretary
11 of State and the Attorney General in carrying out the duties of the Secretary of State and
12 the Attorney General under this title and Title 6.5 of this article:
13 (1) $100 of the annual fee paid by each charitable organization under
14 subsection (b)(2)(v) of this section; and
15 (2) the late fees collected under subsection (c) of this section.
16 (E) A LATE FEE ASSESSED UNDER THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE CONSIDERED
17 A DELINQUENT ACCOUNT OR DEBT REQUIRING REFERRAL TO THE CENTRAL
18 COLLECTION UNIT FOR COLLECTION PURPOSES UNDER § 3–302 OF THE STATE
19 FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.
20 (F) THE SECRETARY OF STATE MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS REGARDING THE
21 SUSPENSION OR WAIVER OF THE IMPOSITION OR COLLECTION OF LATE FEES FOR
22 CHARITABLE ORGANIZATIONS THAT FAIL TO FILE AN ANNUAL REPORT UNDER
23 SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION.
24 6–417.
25 A charitable organization that intends to end soliciting shall submit to the Secretary
26 of State:
27 (1) a statement of its intent; and
28 (2) a final annual report within 6 months after the end of its fiscal year.
29 6–418.
30 (A) THE SECRETARY OF STATE MAY CANCEL THE REGISTRATION OF A
31 CHARITABLE ORGANIZATION IF:
4 HOUSE BILL 239
1 (1) THE ORGANIZATION FAILED TO SUBMIT:
2 (I) A STATEMENT OF INTENT AND FINAL ANNUAL REPORT
3 UNDER § 6–417 OF THIS SUBTITLE WITHIN 3 YEARS AFTER THE DATE THE
4 STATEMENT OF INTENT AND FINAL ANNUAL REPORT WERE DUE; OR
5 (II) ANY ANNUAL REPORT OR UPDATES REQUIRED UNDER §
6 6–408 OF THIS SUBTITLE FOR A 3–YEAR PERIOD; AND
7 (2) THE SECRETARY OF STATE SENDS A NOTICE OF CANCELLATION
8 TO THE LAST KNOWN ADDRESS AND E–MAIL ADDRESS OF THE ORGANIZATION.
9 (B) A CHARITABLE ORGANIZATION MAY REQUEST THAT THE SECRETARY OF
10 STATE REINSTATE A REGISTRATION CANCELED UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS
11 SECTION IF THE ORGANIZATION:
12 (1) SUBMITS TO THE SECRETARY OF STATE ANY OUTSTANDING OR
13 MISSING ANNUAL REPORTS, SUPPORTING MATERIALS, AND ANNUAL FEES
14 REQUIRED UNDER THIS TITLE;
15 (2) (I) REMITS ALL UNPAID LATE FEES; OR
16 (II) HAD LATE FEES SUSPENDED OR WAIVED IN ACCORDANCE
17 WITH § 6–407(C) OF THIS SUBTITLE;
18 (3) IS IN GOOD STANDING WITH THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF
19 ASSESSMENTS AND TAXATION, IF APPLICABLE;
20 (4) HAS A CURRENT TAX–EXEMPT STATUS UNDER § 501(C)(3) OF THE
21 INTERNAL REVENUE CODE, IF APPLICABLE; AND
22 (5) PROVIDES ANY OTHER INFORMATION THAT THE SECRETARY OF
23 STATE REQUIRES.
24 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
25 1, 2025.
