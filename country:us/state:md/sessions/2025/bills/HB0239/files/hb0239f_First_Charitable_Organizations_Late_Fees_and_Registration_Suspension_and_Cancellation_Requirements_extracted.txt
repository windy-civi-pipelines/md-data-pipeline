2 Charitable Organizations – Late Fees and Registration – Suspension and
3 Cancellation Requirements
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the Secretary of State to suspend payment of certain late
5 fees assessed to certain charitable organizations under certain circumstances;
6 authorizing the Secretary of State to cancel a charitable organization’s registration
7 or reinstate a charitable organization’s canceled registration under certain
8 circumstances; and generally relating to the regulation of charitable organizations
9 by the Secretary of State.

2 HOUSE BILL 239
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Business Regulation
4 6–407.
5 (a) A charitable organization that collects less than $25,000 in charitable
6 contributions from the public in a year need not pay an annual fee, except that, if the
7 charitable organization uses a professional solicitor, it shall pay an annual fee of $50.
8 (b) (1) Each charitable organization that submits a separate registration
9 statement and collects at least $25,000 in charitable contributions from the public in a year
10 shall pay an annual fee based on the charitable contributions collected.
11 (2) The annual fee shall be:
12 (i) $50, if charitable contributions from the public are at least
13 $25,000 but less than $50,001;
14 (ii) $75, if charitable contributions from the public are at least
15 $50,001 but less than $75,001;
16 (iii) $100, if charitable contributions from the public are at least
17 $75,001 but less than $100,001;
18 (iv) $200, if charitable contributions from the public are at least
19 $100,001 but less than $500,001; and
20 (v) $300, if charitable contributions from the public are at least
21 $500,001.
22 (c) (1) [An] A CHARITABLE organization failing to file an annual report
23 either by the end of the 6–month period after the end of the charitable organization’s fiscal
24 year or within any period of extension granted shall pay, in addition to the annual fee, $25
25 for each month or part thereof after the date on which the annual report was due to be filed
26 or after the period of extension granted for such filing.
27 (2) The late fee shall be assessed 60 days after:
28 (i) the end of the 6th month after the end of the fiscal year; or
29 (ii) the period of extension.
30 (3) Failure to remit an assessed late fee is a violation of this title.
HOUSE BILL 239 3
1 (4) THE SECRETARY OF STATE MAY SUSPEND OR WAIVE LATE FEES
2 IN ACCORDANCE WITH:
3 (I) REGULATIONS ADOPTED UNDER SUBSECTION (F) OF THIS
4 SECTION; OR
5 (II) A SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT MADE UNDER § 6–205(B) AND
6 (C) OF THIS TITLE.
7 (d) The following revenues shall be distributed to the Charitable Enforcement
8 Fund under Subtitle 2A of this title, to be used only to support the actions of the Secretary
9 of State and the Attorney General in carrying out the duties of the Secretary of State and
10 the Attorney General under this title and Title 6.5 of this article:
11 (1) $100 of the annual fee paid by each charitable organization under
12 subsection (b)(2)(v) of this section; and
13 (2) the late fees collected under subsection (c) of this section.
14 (E) A LATE FEE ASSESSED UNDER THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE CONSIDERED
15 A DELINQUENT ACCOUNT OR DEBT REQUIRING REFERRAL TO THE CENTRAL
16 COLLECTION UNIT FOR COLLECTION PURPOSES UNDER § 3–302 OF THE STATE
17 FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.
18 (F) THE SECRETARY OF STATE MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS REGARDING THE
19 SUSPENSION OR WAIVER OF THE IMPOSITION OR COLLECTION OF LATE FEES FOR
20 CHARITABLE ORGANIZATIONS THAT FAIL TO FILE AN ANNUAL REPORT UNDER
21 SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION.
22 6–417.
23 A charitable organization that intends to end soliciting shall submit to the Secretary
24 of State:
25 (1) a statement of its intent; and
26 (2) a final annual report within 6 months after the end of its fiscal year.
27 6–418.
28 (A) THE SECRETARY OF STATE MAY CANCEL THE REGISTRATION OF A
29 CHARITABLE ORGANIZATION IF:
30 (1) THE ORGANIZATION FAILED TO SUBMIT:
4 HOUSE BILL 239
1 (I) A STATEMENT OF INTENT AND FINAL ANNUAL REPORT
2 UNDER § 6–417 OF THIS SUBTITLE WITHIN 3 YEARS AFTER THE DATE THE
3 STATEMENT OF INTENT AND FINAL ANNUAL REPORT WERE DUE; OR
4 (II) ANY ANNUAL REPORT OR UPDATES REQUIRED UNDER §
5 6–408 OF THIS SUBTITLE FOR A 3–YEAR PERIOD; AND
6 (2) THE SECRETARY OF STATE SENDS A NOTICE OF CANCELLATION
7 TO THE LAST KNOWN ADDRESS AND E–MAIL ADDRESS OF THE ORGANIZATION.
8 (B) A CHARITABLE ORGANIZATION MAY REQUEST THAT THE SECRETARY OF
9 STATE REINSTATE A REGISTRATION CANCELED UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS
10 SECTION IF THE ORGANIZATION:
11 (1) SUBMITS TO THE SECRETARY OF STATE ANY OUTSTANDING OR
12 MISSING ANNUAL REPORTS, SUPPORTING MATERIALS, AND ANNUAL FEES
13 REQUIRED UNDER THIS TITLE;
14 (2) (I) REMITS ALL UNPAID LATE FEES; OR
15 (II) HAD LATE FEES SUSPENDED OR WAIVED IN ACCORDANCE
16 WITH § 6–407(C) OF THIS SUBTITLE;
17 (3) IS IN GOOD STANDING WITH THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF
18 ASSESSMENTS AND TAXATION, IF APPLICABLE;
19 (4) HAS A CURRENT TAX–EXEMPT STATUS UNDER § 501(C)(3) OF THE
20 INTERNAL REVENUE CODE, IF APPLICABLE; AND
21 (5) PROVIDES ANY OTHER INFORMATION THAT THE SECRETARY OF
22 STATE REQUIRES.
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
24 1, 2025.
