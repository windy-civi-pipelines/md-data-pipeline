J1 5lr2656
HB 411/24 – HGO CF 5lr3219
By: Delegate Kipke
Introduced and read first time: January 23, 2025
Assigned to: Health and Government Operations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Health – Opioid Overdose Reversal Drugs
3 FOR the purpose of requiring a licensed health care provider, when issuing a standing order
4 for an opioid overdose reversal drug, to allow an individual to choose any formulation
5 of any opioid overdose reversal drug approved by the federal Food and Drug
6 Administration; prohibiting certain private and public entities that prescribe and
7 dispense opioid overdose reversal drugs from being required to keep in stock or
8 dispense all opioid overdose reversal drugs approved by the federal Food and Drug
9 Administration; and generally relating to opioid overdose reversal drugs.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
11 Article – Health – General
12 Section 13–3101(a), (c), and (e) and 13–3104
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
16 Article – Health – General
17 Section 13–3106
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 BY adding to
21 Article – Health – General
22 Section 13–3110
23 Annotated Code of Maryland
24 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
25 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
26 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

4 (c) “Private or public entity” means a health care provider, local health
5 department, community–based organization, substance abuse treatment organization, or

16 (a) A licensed health care provider with prescribing authority may prescribe and
17 dispense an opioid overdose reversal drug approved by the federal Food and Drug
18 Administration to an individual who:
19 (1) Is believed by the licensed health care provider to be at risk of
20 experiencing an opioid overdose; or
21 (2) Is in a position to assist an individual at risk of experiencing an opioid
22 overdose.
23 (b) (1) A licensed health care provider with prescribing authority may
24 prescribe and dispense opioid overdose reversal drugs approved by the federal Food and
25 Drug Administration by issuing a standing order if the licensed health care provider:
26 (i) Is employed by the Department or a local health department; or
27 (ii) Has a written agreement with an authorized private or public
28 entity under § 13–3104 of this subtitle.
29 (2) WHEN ISSUING A STANDING ORDER FOR AN OPIOID OVERDOSE
30 REVERSAL DRUG UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, A LICENSED HEALTH
31 CARE PROVIDER SHALL ALLOW THE INDIVIDUAL TO CHOOSE ANY FORMULATION OF
32 ANY OPIOID OVERDOSE REVERSAL DRUG APPROVED BY THE FEDERAL FOOD AND
33 DRUG ADMINISTRATION.
HOUSE BILL 572 3
1 [(2)] (3) A licensed health care provider with prescribing authority who
2 issues a standing order under paragraph (1) of this subsection may delegate the dispensing
3 of opioid overdose reversal drugs approved by the federal Food and Drug Administration to
4 an employee or a volunteer of an authorized private or public entity in accordance with a
5 written agreement under § 13–3104 of this subtitle.
6 [(3)] (4) Any licensed health care provider who has dispensing authority
7 also may dispense an opioid overdose reversal drug approved by the federal Food and Drug
8 Administration to any individual in accordance with a standing order issued by a licensed
9 health care provider with prescribing authority in accordance with this subsection.
10 (c) A pharmacist may dispense opioid overdose reversal drugs approved by the
11 federal Food and Drug Administration in accordance with a therapy management contract
12 under Title 12, Subtitle 6A of the Health Occupations Article.
13 13–3110.
14 AN AUTHORIZED PRIVATE OR PUBLIC ENTITY THAT PRESCRIBES AND
15 DISPENSES OPIOID OVERDOSE REVERSAL DRUGS MAY NOT BE REQUIRED TO KEEP
16 IN STOCK OR DISPENSE ALL OPIOID OVERDOSE REVERSAL DRUGS APPROVED BY THE
17 FEDERAL FOOD AND DRUG ADMINISTRATION.
18 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
19 October 1, 2025.
