1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Health – Pediatric Hospital Overstay Patients and Workgroup on
3 Children in Unlicensed Settings and Pediatric Overstays
4 FOR the purpose of specifying that the scope of the Maryland Mental Health and Substance
5 Use Disorder Registry and Referral System includes both private and State inpatient
6 and outpatient mental health and substance use services; requiring the Maryland
7 Department of Health, in coordination with and the Department of Human Services,
8 under certain circumstances, to ensure pediatric hospital overstay patients are
9 placed in the least restrictive setting when clinically indicated and when possible;
10 authorizing a hospital to concurrently explore in–State and out–of–state placements
11 for pediatric hospital overstay patients; establishing the requiring the Maryland
12 Department of Health and the Department of Human Services to establish a
13 Pediatric Hospital Overstay Coordinator within the Governor’s Office for Children;
14 requiring the Maryland Department of Health to conduct a certain study and review
15 of residential treatment center and respite facility rates; each department;
16 establishing the Workgroup on Children in Unlicensed Settings and Pediatric
17 Overstays in the State; and generally relating to pediatric hospital overstay patients
18 and children in unlicensed settings.

20 Article – Health – General
21 Section 7.5–802(a) and (d)

18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Health – General
20 7.5–802.
21 (a) (1) There is a Maryland Mental Health and Substance Use Disorder
22 Registry and Referral System in the Department.
23 (2) The purpose of the Registry and Referral System is to provide a
24 statewide system through which health care providers can identify and access available
25 PRIVATE AND STATE inpatient and outpatient mental health and substance use services
26 for patients in a seamless manner.
27 (3) Subject to the availability of funds, the Department shall develop and
28 implement the Registry and Referral System, in collaboration with the State–designated
29 Health Information Exchange.
30 (4) The Registry and Referral System shall include:
31 (i) A searchable inventory of any PRIVATE OR STATE provider of
32 mental health and substance use disorder services, including inpatient, crisis, and
33 outpatient services;
34 (ii) The capability to allow a provider of mental health and substance
35 use disorder services to update registry information including the real–time availability of
36 services; and
SENATE BILL 696 3
1 (iii) An electronic referral system that is available to any health care
2 provider in the State to facilitate electronic referrals to mental health and substance use
3 disorder providers.
4 (d) Each PRIVATE AND STATE hospital shall ensure the availability of staff to
5 identify appropriate and available services for patients in the hospital who are in need of
6 mental health or substance use disorder services and to assist the patient in accessing the
7 services.

11 19–388.
12 (A) IN THIS PART, “PEDIATRIC HOSPITAL THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE
13 THE MEANINGS INDICATED.

19 DEPARTMENT OF A HOSPITAL FOR MORE THAN 24 48 HOURS AFTER BEING
20 MEDICALLY CLEARED FOR DISCHARGE OR TRANSFER.

22 (A) (1) THE DEPARTMENT, IN COORDINATION WITH THE DEPARTMENT
23 OF HUMAN SERVICES, EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS
24 SUBSECTION, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ENSURE THAT A PEDIATRIC HOSPITAL
25 OVERSTAY PATIENT IS TRANSFERRED TO AND TREATED IN THE LEAST RESTRICTIVE
26 SETTING WHEN CLINICALLY INDICATED AND WHEN POSSIBLE.
27 (2) THE DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES, IN COORDINATION
28 WITH THE DEPARTMENT, SHALL ENSURE THAT A PEDIATRIC HOSPITAL OVERSTAY
29 PATIENT WHO IS A CHILD COMMITTED TO THE CARE AND CUSTODY OF THE
30 DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES IS TRANSFERRED TO AND TREATED IN THE
31 LEAST RESTRICTIVE SETTING WHEN CLINICALLY INDICATED AND WHEN POSSIBLE.
