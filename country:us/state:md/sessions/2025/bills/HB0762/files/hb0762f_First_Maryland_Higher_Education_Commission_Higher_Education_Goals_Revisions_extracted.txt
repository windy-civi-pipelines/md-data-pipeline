By: Chair, Appropriations Committee (By Request – Departmental – Higher
Education Commission)
Introduced and read first time: January 29, 2025
Assigned to: Appropriations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Higher Education Commission – Higher Education Goals – Revisions
3 FOR the purpose of repealing certain provisions of law establishing certain goals of the
4 State regarding postsecondary degree attainment; requiring the Maryland Higher
5 Education Commission to set certain goals for the State for certain higher education
6 policy areas; and generally relating to the Maryland Higher Education Commission.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – Education
9 Section 10–205
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Education
15 10–205.
16 [(a) It is the goal of the State that at least 55% of Maryland’s adults age 25 to 64
17 will hold at least an associate’s degree by the year 2025.
18 (b) It is the goal of the State that all degree–seeking students enrolled in a public
19 community college earn an associate’s degree before leaving the community college or
20 transferring to a public senior higher education institution.]
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb0762*
2 HOUSE BILL 762
1 [(c)] (A) Institutions of higher education should utilize educational resources to
2 provide the greatest possible benefit to the citizens of the State and to foster economic
3 development.
4 [(d)] (B) In each region of the State, institutions of higher education should
5 cooperate to ensure an effective and efficient education system.
6 [(e)] (C) In developing missions and programs, the Maryland Higher Education
7 Commission and each governing board and its constituent institutions shall consider the
8 role, mission, and function of other public senior higher education institutions, particularly
9 those institutions offering unique programs and services in the same geographical region.
10 (D) THE COMMISSION SHALL SET GOALS FOR THE STATE FOR:
11 (1) MARYLAND’S ADULTS AGE 25 TO 64 HOLDING A POSTSECONDARY
12 DEGREE OR CERTIFICATE;
13 (2) MARYLAND STUDENTS COMPLETING A POSTSECONDARY
14 CREDENTIAL OF VALUE EACH YEAR;
15 (3) INSTITUTIONS OF HIGHER EDUCATION’S GOALS FOR RESEARCH
16 AND INNOVATION ALIGNED WITH MARYLAND’S ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT AND
17 WORKFORCE SECTORS; AND
18 (4) ANY OTHER POLICY THE COMMISSION DETERMINES IS
19 NECESSARY TO ENSURE THAT INSTITUTIONS OF HIGHER EDUCATION IN THE STATE
20 PROVIDE THE GREATEST POSSIBLE BENEFIT TO THE CITIZENS OF THE STATE AND
21 TO FOSTER ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
23 October 1, 2025.
