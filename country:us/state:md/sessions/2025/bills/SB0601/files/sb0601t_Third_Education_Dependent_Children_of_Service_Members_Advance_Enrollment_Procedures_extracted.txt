CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Dependent Children of Service Members – Advance Enrollment
3 Procedures
4 FOR the purpose of requiring a county superintendent of schools to allow a certain
5 dependent child of a service member to advance enroll in a school in the county in a
6 certain manner; requiring a local school system to coordinate with the parents or
7 guardians of a certain child with a disability and the school district in a certain
8 sending state to prevent a delay in transferring the child; requiring the Secretary of
9 Veterans and Military Families, in consultation with certain entities, to publish and
10 maintain certain information on school enrollment and related services for
11 dependent children of service members; and generally relating to school enrollment
12 procedures for dependent children of service members.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Education
15 Section 7–115.1
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
19 Article – Education
20 Section 8–401(a)(1), (2), and (3)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0601*
2 SENATE BILL 601
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Education
4 7–115.1.
5 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
6 (2) “CHILD WITH A DISABILITY” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN §
7 8–401 OF THIS ARTICLE.
8 (3) “Dependent child” means an individual of school age who is a natural
9 child, a stepchild, an adopted child, or a financially dependent child of a service member.
10 [(3)] (4) “Enrollment” includes course registration and charter school
11 lotteries.
12 (5) “FREE APPROPRIATE PUBLIC EDUCATION” HAS THE MEANING
13 STATED IN § 8–401 OF THIS ARTICLE.
14 (6) “SENDING STATE” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 7–1303 OF THIS
15 TITLE.
16 [(4)] (7) (i) “Service member” means an active duty member of the
17 United States armed forces UNIFORMED SERVICES, AS DEFINED IN 37 U.S.C. § 101.
18 (ii) “Service member” includes a member of the National Guard OR
19 A RESERVE COMPONENT OF THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED STATES, AS
20 DEFINED IN 37 U.S.C. § 101, on active duty orders.
21 (b) (1) [A] SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION AND
22 SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION, A county superintendent shall allow a dependent
23 child of a service member who is relocating to the State on military orders and is not
24 domiciled in that county during the enrollment period to apply for ADVANCE enrollment in
25 a public school in the county[, in the same manner and at the same time as individuals
26 domiciled in the county].
27 (2) TO ADVANCE ENROLL UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
28 SUBSECTION, A SERVICE MEMBER SHALL PROVIDE TO A COUNTY SUPERINTENDENT
29 MILITARY ORDERS EVIDENCING THAT THE SERVICE MEMBER WILL BE STATIONED IN
30 THE STATE DURING THE CURRENT OR IMMEDIATELY FOLLOWING SCHOOL YEAR.
SENATE BILL 601 3
1 (3) (I) A COUNTY SUPERINTENDENT SHALL ESTABLISH A REMOTE
2 REGISTRATION PROCESS FOR ADVANCE ENROLLMENT UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF
3 THIS SUBSECTION.
4 (II) A REMOTE REGISTRATION PROCESS ESTABLISHED UNDER
5 THIS PARAGRAPH MAY NOT:
6 1. CHARGE A FEE; OR
7 2. REQUIRE THE SERVICE MEMBER OR OTHER PARENT
8 OR GUARDIAN OR THE DEPENDENT CHILD TO PHYSICALLY APPEAR AT A LOCATION
9 WITHIN THE COUNTY TO COMPLETE ANY PART OF THE ADVANCE ENROLLMENT
10 PROCESS.
11 (4) ON RECEIPT OF A VALID ADVANCE ENROLLMENT UNDER THIS
12 SECTION, IF THE DEPENDENT CHILD OF THE SERVICE MEMBER IS A CHILD WITH A
13 DISABILITY WHO IS RECEIVING SPECIAL EDUCATION AND SERVICES IN THE SENDING
14 STATE EQUIVALENT TO A FREE APPROPRIATE PUBLIC EDUCATION, THE LOCAL
15 SCHOOL SYSTEM ENROLLING THE CHILD PROMPTLY SHALL COORDINATE WITH THE
16 CHILD’S PARENTS OR GUARDIANS AND THE SCHOOL DISTRICT IN THE SENDING
17 STATE TO PREVENT UNDUE DELAY IN TRANSFERRING THE CHILD TO THE STATE.
18 (c) (1) [Within] TO FINALIZE ADVANCE ENROLLMENT UNDER THIS
19 SECTION, WITHIN 10 days of the published arrival date on the service member’s military
20 orders, the service member shall provide the school with:
21 (i) Satisfactory evidence of the dependent child’s status as a
22 dependent child of the service member;
23 (ii) A copy of the service member’s military orders to relocate; and
24 (iii) Proof of residence in the county.
25 (2) The service member may use the address of any of the following as proof
26 of residence:
27 (i) A temporary on–base lodging facility;
28 (ii) A purchased or leased home or apartment; or
29 (iii) Any federal government housing unit or off–base military
30 housing unit.
31 (D) (1) (I) A DEPENDENT CHILD OF A SERVICE MEMBER WHO ADVANCE
32 ENROLLS IN A SCHOOL UNDER THIS SECTION MAY REMAIN ENROLLED IN SCHOOL
4 SENATE BILL 601
1 UNTIL COMPLETION OF THE HIGHEST GRADE LEVEL AVAILABLE AT THE SCHOOL
2 SUBJECT TO A CASE–BY–CASE REVIEW OF THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM.
3 (II) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL PRIORITIZE A DEPENDENT
4 CHILD’S CONTINUITY IN EDUCATION WHEN MAKING DETERMINATIONS UNDER
5 SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH.
6 (2) NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER PROVISION OF LAW, A
7 DEPENDENT CHILD OF A SERVICE MEMBER SHALL HAVE ACCESS TO ALL ACADEMIC
8 COURSES AND PROGRAMS FOR WHICH THE DEPENDENT CHILD QUALIFIES,
9 INCLUDING EXTRACURRICULAR ACTIVITIES AND ATHLETIC AND SOCIAL PROGRAMS,
10 WHEN USING ADVANCE ENROLLMENT.
11 (E) THE SECRETARY OF VETERANS AND MILITARY FAMILIES, IN
12 CONSULTATION WITH THE MILITARY FAMILY EDUCATION LIAISON DESIGNATED
13 UNDER § 7–1303 OF THIS TITLE AND THE OFFICE OF MILITARY AND FEDERAL
14 AFFAIRS AND THE MARYLAND MILITARY INSTALLATION COUNCIL IN THE
15 DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE, SHALL PUBLISH AND MAINTAIN INFORMATION ON:
16 (1) THE ADVANCE ENROLLMENT PROCEDURES ESTABLISHED UNDER
17 THIS SECTION; AND
18 (2) ANY ASSISTANCE AND SERVICES AVAILABLE TO SERVICE
19 MEMBERS REGARDING THE ADVANCE ENROLLMENT OF A DEPENDENT CHILD,
20 INCLUDING A CHILD WITH A DISABILITY ENTITLED TO RECEIVE A FREE
21 APPROPRIATE PUBLIC EDUCATION IN THE STATE.
22 8–401.
23 (a) (1) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
24 (2) “Child with a disability” means a child who has been determined
25 through appropriate assessment as having autism, deaf–blindness, hearing impairment,
26 including deafness, emotional disability, intellectual disability, multiple disabilities,
27 orthopedic impairment, other health impairment, specific learning disability, speech or
28 language impairment, traumatic brain injury, visual impairment, including blindness, and
29 who because of that impairment needs special education and related services.
30 (3) “Free appropriate public education” means special education and
31 related services that:
32 (i) Are provided at public expense, under public supervision and
33 direction, at no cost to the parents;
SENATE BILL 601 5
1 (ii) Meet the standards of the State Board regulations and the
2 Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (20 U.S.C. 1400 et seq.);
3 (iii) Includes preschool, elementary, and secondary education; and
4 (iv) Are provided in conformance with the requirements of the child’s
5 individualized education program.
6 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
7 1, 2025.
Approved:
