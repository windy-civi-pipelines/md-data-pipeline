CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Department of Health – Forensic Review Board and Community
3 Forensic Aftercare Program – Established
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Health to establish a forensic
5 review board at facilities that have charge of persons who have been committed to
6 the facility as not criminally responsible; authorizing a forensic review board to make
7 recommendations relating to the release or rehabilitation of a committed person;
8 requiring each forensic review board to review the eligibility for release of committed
9 persons on a certain basis and determine whether to recommend to the court that
10 the committed person is eligible for discharge or conditional release; establishing the
11 Community Forensic Aftercare Program in the Department to monitor committed
12 persons on conditional release; and generally relating to forensic review boards and
13 the Community Forensic Aftercare Program.
14 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
15 Article – Criminal Procedure
16 Section 3–101(a), (b), and (d) and 3–121(b)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2018 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
19 BY adding to
20 Article – Criminal Procedure
21 Section 3–120.1
22 Annotated Code of Maryland

10 Article – Criminal Procedure
11 3–101.
12 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
13 (b) “Committed person” means a person committed to the Health Department as
14 not criminally responsible under the test for criminal responsibility.
15 (d) “Health Department” means the Maryland Department of Health.
16 3–120.1.
17 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “AUTHORIZED AGENT” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL WHO IS
18 NOT COUNSEL FOR THE COMMITTED PERSON AND WHO IS SELECTED BY THE
19 COMMITTED PERSON TO REPRESENT THE INTERESTS OF THE COMMITTED PERSON
20 IN ALL FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD MATTERS.
21 (B) (1) THE HEALTH DEPARTMENT SHALL ESTABLISH A FORENSIC
22 REVIEW BOARD AT EACH FACILITY THAT HAS CHARGE OF A COMMITTED PERSON
23 UNDER THIS SUBTITLE.
24 (2) A FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD MAY MAKE RECOMMENDATIONS
25 RELATING TO THE RELEASE OR REHABILITATION OF A COMMITTED PERSON UNDER
26 THIS SUBTITLE.
27 (B) (C) EACH MEMBER OF A FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD SHALL BE A
28 HEALTH CARE PROVIDER LICENSED UNDER THE HEALTH OCCUPATIONS ARTICLE.
29 (C) (D) (1) EACH AT LEAST ONCE EACH YEAR, EACH FORENSIC REVIEW
30 BOARD SHALL REVIEW ANNUALLY EACH COMMITTED PERSON’S ELIGIBILITY FOR
31 RELEASE UNDER § 3–114 OF THIS SUBTITLE.

1 (2) THE FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD SHALL, BY A MAJORITY VOTE OF
2 THE MEMBERS, DETERMINE WHETHER TO RECOMMEND TO THE COURT THAT A
3 COMMITTED PERSON IS ELIGIBLE FOR DISCHARGE OR CONDITIONAL RELEASE, WITH
4 OR WITHOUT PROPOSED CONDITIONS.
5 (3) (I) EACH FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD SHALL MAINTAIN A
6 WRITTEN RECORD CONTAINING ITS FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS.
7 (II) THE WRITTEN RECORD SHALL BE INCLUDED IN THE
8 MEDICAL RECORD OF THE COMMITTED PERSON.
9 (III) THE FINDINGS SHALL INCLUDE THE REASONS FOR THE
10 FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD’S RECOMMENDATION AND A PLAN FOR OVERCOMING ANY
11 BARRIERS TO RELEASE.
12 (4) WHEN MAKING A RECOMMENDATION UNDER THIS SECTION, A
13 FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD SHALL CONSIDER A WRITTEN STATEMENT OFFERED BY
14 THE COMMITTED PERSON TO THE FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD.
15 (5) THE FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD SHALL PROVIDE NOTICE TO A
16 COMMITTED PERSON AND THE COUNSEL OF RECORD FOR THE COMMITTED PERSON:
17 (I) AT LEAST 10 DAYS BEFORE THE FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD
18 HOLDS A MEETING CONCERNING THE COMMITTED PERSON; AND
19 (II) WITHIN 10 DAYS AFTER THE FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD
20 ISSUES A RECOMMENDATION TO THE COURT.
21 (D) (E) (1) EACH FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD SHALL HAVE AN ODD
22 NUMBER OF MEMBERS.
23 (2) EACH MEMBER OF THE FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD SHALL HAVE
24 ONE VOTE.
25 (3) (I) A FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD MAY NOT CONDUCT A REVIEW
26 UNDER SUBSECTION (C) (D) OF THIS SECTION UNLESS A QUORUM IS PRESENT.
27 (II) A MAJORITY OF THE MEMBERS OF THE FORENSIC REVIEW
28 BOARD IS A QUORUM.
29 (F) (1) A COMMITTED PERSON MAY APPOINT AN AUTHORIZED AGENT TO
30 ATTEND THE PORTION OF A FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD MEETING CONCERNING THE
31 COMMITTED PERSON.

7 (E) (G) (1) IF THE COMMITTED PERSON’S TREATMENT TEAM

17 THAT INCLUDES THE CLINICAL REASONS UNDERLYING THE DETERMINATION THAT

19 (4) A COPY OF THE NOTIFICATION MADE UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF
20 THIS SUBSECTION AND THE WRITTEN STATEMENT REQUIRED UNDER PARAGRAPH
21 (3) OF THIS SUBSECTION SHALL BE INCLUDED IN THE MEDICAL RECORD OF THE
22 COMMITTED PERSON.

4 TESTIFY AS TO THE HEALTH DEPARTMENT’S POSITION AT A RELEASE HEARING.
5 (2) IF A FORENSIC REVIEW BOARD TAKES A POSITION CONTRARY TO
6 THE POSITION OF THE COMMITTED PERSON’S TREATMENT TEAM, THE
7 REPRESENTATIVE APPOINTED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION MAY NOT BE A MEMBER OF
8 THE COMMITTED PERSON’S TREATMENT TEAM.
9 (K) THE HEALTH DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY
10 OUT THIS SECTION.

12 (b) (1) If a court receives a report that alleges that a committed person has
13 violated a condition of a conditional release, the court promptly shall:
14 (i) notify:
15 1. the Health Department;
16 2. counsel of record for the committed person; and
17 3. the State’s Attorney; and
18 (ii) provide the State’s Attorney with the name, address, and
19 telephone number of the person who reported the violation and a copy of the order for
20 conditional release.
21 (2) If the Health Department receives a report that alleges that a
22 committed person has violated conditional release, the Department shall:
23 (i) notify:
24 1. the court;
25 2. counsel of record for the committed person; and
26 3. the State’s Attorney; and
27 (ii) provide the State’s Attorney with the name, address, and
28 telephone number of the person who reported the violation and a copy of the order for
29 conditional release.

4 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS

7 CRIMINAL PROCEDURE ARTICLE.
8 (C) “PROGRAM” MEANS THE COMMUNITY FORENSIC AFTERCARE
9 PROGRAM.
10 13–5502.
11 (A) THERE IS A COMMUNITY FORENSIC AFTERCARE PROGRAM IN THE
12 DEPARTMENT.
13 (B) THE PURPOSE OF THE PROGRAM IS TO MONITOR COMMITTED PERSONS
14 ON CONDITIONAL RELEASE.
15 (C) (1) THE PROGRAM SHALL MONITOR COMMITTED PERSONS ON
16 CONDITIONAL RELEASE IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE RECOMMENDATIONS OF THE
17 COMMITTED PERSON’S MENTAL HEALTH TEAM.
18 (2) THE PROGRAM MAY NOT MAKE CLINICAL DECISIONS REGARDING
19 THE TREATMENT OF COMMITTED PERSONS.

22 (I) EACH COMMITTED PERSON MONITORED BY THE PROGRAM;
23 AND
24 (II) THE MENTAL HEALTH TEAM OF THE COMMITTED PERSON.

26 A REPRESENTATIVE FROM THE OFFICE OF THE PUBLIC DEFENDER, TO ATTEND A
27 MEETING HELD UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION.
28 (E) IF THE PROGRAM RECEIVES A REPORT ALLEGING THAT A COMMITTED
29 PERSON HAS VIOLATED CONDITIONAL RELEASE, THE PROGRAM SHALL:
SENATE BILL 43 7
1 (1) MAKE THE NOTIFICATIONS REQUIRED UNDER § 3–121(B)(2)(I) OF
2 THE CRIMINAL PROCEDURE ARTICLE; AND
3 (2) PROVIDE THE STATE’S ATTORNEY WITH THE INFORMATION
4 REQUIRED UNDER § 3–121(B)(2)(II) OF THE CRIMINAL PROCEDURE ARTICLE.
5 (F) IF THE PROGRAM RECEIVES A REQUEST FOR RECORDS, THE PROGRAM
6 SHALL PROVIDE THE RECORDS WITHIN 48 HOURS AFTER THE REQUEST IS RECEIVED
7 UNLESS THE PROGRAM IS PROHIBITED UNDER APPLICABLE FEDERAL OR STATE
8 LAW FROM DISCLOSING THE RECORDS TO THE REQUESTING PERSON.
9 (G) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO IMPLEMENT THE
10 PROGRAM.

12 October 1, 2025.
