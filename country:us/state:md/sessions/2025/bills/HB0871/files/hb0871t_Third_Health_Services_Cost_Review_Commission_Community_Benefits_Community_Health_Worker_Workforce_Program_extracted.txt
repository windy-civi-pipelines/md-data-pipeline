CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Services Cost Review Commission – Community Benefits – Community
3 Health Worker Workforce Program
4 FOR the purpose of altering the definition of “community benefit” to include a certain
5 community health worker workforce program; authorizing a nonprofit hospital and
6 a community–based organization to establish through a memorandum of
7 understanding a community health worker workforce program to provide a
8 community benefit; establishing requirements for the memorandum of
9 understanding; requiring authorizing a partnership that establishes a community
10 health worker workforce program to provide health insurance to certain individuals
11 under certain circumstances; and generally relating to community health worker
12 workforce programs.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
14 Article – Health – General
15 Section 13–3701(a) and (c)
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
19 Article – Health – General
20 Section 19–303(a)

2 HOUSE BILL 871
1 Annotated Code of Maryland
2 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
3 BY adding to
4 Article – Health – General
5 Section 19–303.1
6 Annotated Code of Maryland
7 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
8 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
9 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
10 Article – Health – General
11 13–3701.
12 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
13 (c) “Community health worker” means a frontline public health worker who:
14 (1) Is a trusted member of, or has an unusually close understanding of, the
15 community being served;
16 (2) Serves as a liaison to, link to, or intermediary between health and social
17 services and the community to:
18 (i) Facilitate access to services; and
19 (ii) Improve the quality and cultural competence of service delivery;
20 and
21 (3) Builds individual and community capacity by increasing health
22 knowledge and self–sufficiency through a range of activities, including:
23 (i) Outreach;
24 (ii) Community education;
25 (iii) The provision of information to support individuals in the
26 community;
27 (iv) Social support; and
28 (v) Advocacy.
29 19–303.
HOUSE BILL 871 3
1 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
2 (2) “Commission” means the Health Services Cost Review Commission.
3 (3) (i) “Community benefit” means a planned, organized, and measured
4 activity that is intended to meet identified community health needs within a service area.
5 (ii) “Community benefit” may include:
6 1. A community health service;
7 2. Health professional education;
8 3. Research;
9 4. A financial contribution;
10 5. A community–building activity, including partnerships
11 with community–based organizations;
12 6. Charity care;
13 7. An activity described under subparagraph (i) of this
14 paragraph that is funded by a foundation;
15 8. A mission–driven health service;
16 9. An operation related to an activity described under
17 subparagraph (i) of this paragraph; [and]
18 10. Financial or in–kind support of the Maryland Behavioral
19 Health Crisis Response System; AND
20 11. A COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKER WORKFORCE
21 PROGRAM ESTABLISHED IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 19–303.1 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
22 (4) “Community Benefit Reporting Workgroup” means the Community
23 Benefit Reporting Workgroup established in accordance with subsection (b) of this section.
24 (5) “Community health needs assessment” means the process required by
25 the Affordable Care Act by which unmet community health care needs and priorities are
26 identified by a nonprofit hospital in accordance with § 501(r)(3) of the Internal Revenue
27 Code.
28 19–303.1.
4 HOUSE BILL 871
1 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKER” HAS THE MEANING
2 STATED IN § 13–3701 OF THIS ARTICLE.
3 (B) (1) A COMMUNITY BENEFIT UNDER § 19–303 OF THIS SUBTITLE MAY
4 INCLUDE A PARTNERSHIP BETWEEN A NONPROFIT HOSPITAL AND A
5 COMMUNITY–BASED ORGANIZATION TO ESTABLISH A COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKER
6 WORKFORCE PROGRAM.
7 (2) THE PRIMARY PURPOSE OF A COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKER
8 WORKFORCE PROGRAM SHALL BE TO PROVIDE SERVICES TO PATIENTS OF A
9 NONPROFIT HOSPITAL THAT ARE INTENDED TO IMPROVE HEALTH OUTCOMES AND
10 ADDRESS SOCIAL DETERMINANTS OF HEALTH.
11 (C) (1) A PARTNERSHIP ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL BE
12 EXECUTED THROUGH A MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING BETWEEN A
13 NONPROFIT HOSPITAL AND A COMMUNITY–BASED ORGANIZATION.
14 (2) THE MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING SHALL INCLUDE:
15 (I) THE PROGRAM DESIGN;
16 (II) GOALS FOR PATIENT HEALTH OUTCOMES;
17 (III) IMPLEMENTATION PROCEDURES;
18 (IV) CRITERIA FOR EVALUATION AND IMPACT MEASUREMENT;
19 (V) DATA COLLECTION, MANAGEMENT, AND REPORTING
20 STANDARDS AND PROTOCOLS THAT ARE IN ACCORDANCE WITH APPLICABLE
21 FEDERAL AND STATE LAWS AND REGULATIONS, INCLUDING THE FEDERAL HEALTH
22 INSURANCE PORTABILITY AND ACCOUNTABILITY ACT; AND
23 (VI) AN AGREEMENT BETWEEN THE NONPROFIT HOSPITAL AND
24 THE COMMUNITY–BASED ORGANIZATION IDENTIFYING WHICH PARTY WILL BE
25 RESPONSIBLE FOR:
26 1. RECRUITING AND EMPLOYING THE COMMUNITY
27 HEALTH WORKER WORKFORCE;
28 2. SUPERVISING THE WORKFORCE IN THE SETTINGS IN
29 WHICH THE COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKERS WILL PROVIDE SERVICES TO PATIENTS;
30 3. TRAINING THE WORKFORCE; AND
HOUSE BILL 871 5
1 4. PROVIDING EMPLOYER–SPONSORED HEALTH
2 INSURANCE COVERAGE TO EACH COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKER.
3 (D) A PARTNERSHIP ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL MAY
4 PROVIDE HEALTH INSURANCE COVERAGE TO EACH COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKER
5 FOR THE DURATION OF THE PROGRAM IF THE INDIVIDUAL DOES NOT HAVE HEALTH
6 INSURANCE COVERAGE AND REQUESTS COVERAGE.
7 (E) A NONPROFIT HOSPITAL AND A COMMUNITY–BASED ORGANIZATION
8 MAY COLLABORATE TO IDENTIFY FINANCIAL RESOURCES TO ADDRESS COSTS
9 ASSOCIATED WITH INCREASING ADMINISTRATIVE CAPACITY WITHIN THE
10 COMMUNITY–BASED ORGANIZATION NECESSARY TO PARTICIPATE IN THE
11 PARTNERSHIP.
12 (F) SUBJECT TO OTHER PROVISIONS OF LAW GOVERNING COMMUNITY
13 BENEFITS AND ON THE AGREEMENT OF THE PARTIES, A NONPROFIT HOSPITAL MAY
14 PROVIDE FINANCIAL SUPPORT TO A COMMUNITY–BASED ORGANIZATION TO COVER
15 THE COSTS ASSOCIATED WITH INCREASING ADMINISTRATIVE CAPACITY WITHIN THE
16 COMMUNITY–BASED ORGANIZATION NECESSARY TO PARTICIPATE IN THE
17 PARTNERSHIP.
18 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
19 October 1, 2025.
Approved:
