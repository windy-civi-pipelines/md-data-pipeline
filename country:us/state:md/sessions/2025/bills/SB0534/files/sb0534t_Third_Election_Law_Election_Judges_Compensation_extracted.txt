1 AN ACT concerning
2 Election Law – Election Judges – Compensation
3 FOR the purpose of requiring a local board of elections to pay an election judge
4 compensation for completing a certain course of instruction only after the election
5 judge actually serves an election day or early voting day; authorizing a public school
6 student in the State to both earn hours toward the service–learning graduation
7 requirement and or receive election judge compensation for service as an election
8 judge; and generally relating to election judges.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
10 Article – Election Law
11 Section 10–205
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
16 Article – Election Law
17 10–205.
18 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0534*
2 SENATE BILL 534
1 (2) “Compensation” includes stipends for transportation, training, and
2 bonus payments.
3 (3) “Returning election judge” means an election judge who has served a
4 term of office for the immediately preceding statewide election.
5 (b) (1) (i) The county governing body shall provide sufficient funding to
6 meet the minimum compensation levels required under this section.
7 (ii) Within the limits authorized by the county governing body, a
8 local board may fix the compensation of election judges above the minimum compensation
9 levels required under this section.
10 (2) A local board shall pay an election judge for:
11 (i) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (4)(II) OF THIS SUBSECTION, each
12 course of instruction required under § 10–206(g)(1) of this subtitle that the election judge
13 completes; and
14 (ii) each election day and each early voting day that the election
15 judge actually serves.
16 (3) (i) For each election judge who is not a returning election judge, the
17 compensation for each election day and each early voting day actually served shall be at
18 least $250 per day.
19 (ii) For each returning election judge, the compensation for each
20 election day and each early voting day actually served shall be at least $100 more per day
21 than the compensation provided to an election judge who is not a returning election judge.
22 (4) (I) The compensation for each completed course of instruction
23 required under § 10–206(g)(1) of this subtitle shall be at least $50.
24 (II) A LOCAL BOARD SHALL PAY AN ELECTION JUDGE THE
25 COMPENSATION REQUIRED UNDER PARAGRAPH (2)(I) OF THIS SUBSECTION ONLY
26 AFTER THE ELECTION JUDGE ACTUALLY SERVES AN ELECTION DAY OR EARLY
27 VOTING DAY.
28 (c) The State Board shall reimburse each local board for $50 of the additional
29 compensation required under subsection (b)(3)(ii) of this section that is paid to each
30 returning election judge.
31 (D) FOR SERVICE AS AN ELECTION JUDGE, A PUBLIC SCHOOL STUDENT IN
32 THE STATE MAY BOTH:
SENATE BILL 534 3
1 (1) EARN HOURS TOWARD THE SERVICE–LEARNING REQUIREMENT
2 FOR GRADUATION UNDER COMAR 13A.03.02.05; AND OR
3 (2) RECEIVE THE ELECTION JUDGE COMPENSATION UNDER THIS
4 SECTION.
5 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
6 October 1, 2025.
Approved:

Governor.
