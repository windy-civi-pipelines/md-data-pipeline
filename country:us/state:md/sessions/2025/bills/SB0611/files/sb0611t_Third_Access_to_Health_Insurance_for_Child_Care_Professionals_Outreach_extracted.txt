CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Access to Health Insurance for Child Care Professionals – Outreach and
3 Qualifying Nonprofit Satellite Organizations
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the State Department of Education, in collaboration with the
5 Maryland Health Benefit Exchange, to promote access to health insurance for child
6 care professionals; authorizing a child care provider that receives a certain amount
7 of funding from a unit of State, local, or federal government to participate in the
8 State Employee and Retiree Health and Welfare Benefits Program as a satellite
9 organization; requiring the Exchange to conduct a certain survey; and generally
10 relating to access to health insurance and health benefits for child care professionals.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
12 Article – Education
13 Section 1–101(a) and (f) and 7–1A–01(a) and (i)
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
16 BY adding to
17 Article – Education
18 Section 2–107
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
21 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
22 Article – State Personnel and Pensions

6 (II) (III) CHILD CHILD CARE ASSOCIATIONS; AND
7 (III) (IV) RELEVANT RELEVANT NONPROFIT ORGANIZATIONS;
8 (2) HELPING HELPING CHILD CARE PROFESSIONALS OBTAIN HEALTH
9 INSURANCE THROUGH THE MARYLAND HEALTH BENEFIT EXCHANGE OR
10 MEDICAID;
11 (3) ASSISTING ASSISTING CHILD CARE PROFESSIONALS WHO LOSE
12 MEDICAID COVERAGE WITH FINDING ALTERNATIVE HEALTH INSURANCE OPTIONS;
13 (4) HELPING HELPING ENSURE HEALTH PLAN OPTIONS MEET THE
14 BUDGETS AND HEALTH INSURANCE COVERAGE NEEDS OF CHILD CARE
15 PROFESSIONALS AND CHILD CARE EMPLOYERS; AND
16 (5) PROVIDING PROVIDING INFORMATIONAL RESOURCES OR
17 REFERRALS FOR ADDITIONAL SUPPORT, INCLUDING FLYERS, WEBINARS,
18 IN–PERSON EVENTS, AND OTHER OUTREACH MATERIALS TARGETED TO THE CHILD
19 CARE PROVIDER COMMUNITY; AND
20 (6) ASSESSING CURRENT LEVELS OF HEALTH CARE ACCESS ACROSS
21 THE CHILD CARE COMMUNITY BY CONDUCTING A SURVEY:
22 (I) DEVELOPED IN CONSULTATION WITH THE STATE
23 DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION AND RELEVANT NONPROFIT ORGANIZATIONS; AND
24 (II) WITH A PARTICULAR EMPHASIS ON PROVIDERS WHO
25 ACCEPT CHILD CARE SCHOLARSHIP VOUCHERS.

30 § 7–1A–09 of this subtitle.
4 SENATE BILL 611
1 Article – State Personnel and Pensions
2 2–501.
3 (a) In this subtitle the following terms have the meanings indicated.
4 (b) (1) “County board” means the board of education of a county.
5 (2) “County board” includes the Baltimore City Board of School
6 Commissioners.
7 (c) “Program” means the State Employee and Retiree Health and Welfare
8 Benefits Program.
9 (d) “Satellite organization” means any organization or entity whose employees
10 are eligible to participate in the State Employee and Retiree Health and Welfare Benefits
11 Program as a separate account.
12 (e) “Wellness program” means a program that is designed to:
13 (1) promote health or prevent or detect disease or illness;
14 (2) improve clinical outcomes;
15 (3) prevent or reduce acute admissions and readmissions to health care
16 facilities;
17 (4) improve treatment compliance for chronic conditions;
18 (5) promote healthy behaviors; or
19 (6) prevent or control injury.
20 2–512.
21 (a) In this section, “qualifying nonprofit organization” means an organization
22 that:
23 (1) (i) 1. receives State funds from the Maryland Department of
24 Health that cover more than one–third of the organization’s operating expenses; OR
25 2. IS A CHILD CARE PROVIDER THAT RECEIVES FUNDS
26 FROM A UNIT OF STATE, LOCAL, OR FEDERAL GOVERNMENT THAT COVER MORE
27 THAN TWO–THIRDS OF THE ORGANIZATION’S OPERATING EXPENSES; and
28 (ii) is:
SENATE BILL 611 5
1 1. described in § 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code; and
2 2. exempt from income tax under § 501(a) of the Internal
3 Revenue Code;
4 (2) is the Legal Aid Bureau, Inc.;
5 (3) is a corporation, a limited liability company, or any other entity that is
6 wholly owned by the Legal Aid Bureau, Inc.; or
7 (4) is the Maryland Crime Victims’ Resource Center.
8 (b) The Secretary shall adopt regulations for the enrollment and participation of
9 employees of a qualifying nonprofit organization to participate in the Program as a satellite
10 organization.
11 (c) A qualifying nonprofit organization that participates in the Program as a
12 satellite organization shall:
13 (1) pay to the State:
14 (i) a premium in the amount determined by the Secretary; and
15 (ii) any costs, as determined by the Secretary, for the administration
16 of this Program; and
17 (2) determine the extent to which the organization will subsidize
18 participation by its employees in the Program.
19 (d) The participation of a satellite organization in the Program may not impede,
20 undermine, or conflict with the Program’s federal compliance obligations or governmental
21 and cafeteria plan status, as defined in 26 U.S.C. § 125.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
23 October 1, 2025.
