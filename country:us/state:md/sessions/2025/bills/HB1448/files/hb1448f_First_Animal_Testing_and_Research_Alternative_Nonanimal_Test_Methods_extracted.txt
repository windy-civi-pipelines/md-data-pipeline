J1 5lr2984
By: Delegate Cullison
Introduced and read first time: February 7, 2025
Assigned to: Health and Government Operations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Animal Testing and Research – Alternative Nonanimal Test Methods
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting certain research facilities in the State from using a
4 traditional animal test method if an alternative nonanimal test method has received
5 certain approval or the requirement to use a traditional animal test method is
6 waived; requiring certain research facilities to conduct testing using traditional
7 animal test methods in a certain manner; requiring certain research facilities to
8 annually submit a report to the Attorney General regarding the test methods used;
9 requiring the Attorney General to make the reports publicly available; requiring the
10 Attorney General to enforce this Act; and generally relating to the use of alternative
11 nonanimal test methods.
12 BY adding to
13 Article – Health – General
14 Section 24–23A–01 through 24–23A–05 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 23A.
15 Alternative Nonanimal Test Methods”
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
20 Article – Health – General
21 SUBTITLE 23A. ALTERNATIVE NONANIMAL TEST METHODS.
22 24–23A–01.
23 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
24 INDICATED.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb1448*
2 HOUSE BILL 1448
1 (B) (1) “ALTERNATIVE NONANIMAL TEST METHOD” MEANS A TEST
2 METHOD THAT:
3 (I) DOES NOT USE A LIVE VERTEBRATE ANIMAL; AND
4 (II) PROVIDES INFORMATION OF EQUIVALENT OR BETTER
5 SCIENTIFIC QUALITY AND RELEVANCE COMPARED TO A TRADITIONAL ANIMAL TEST
6 METHOD.
7 (2) “ALTERNATIVE NONANIMAL TEST METHOD” INCLUDES:
8 (I) COMPUTATIONAL TOXICOLOGY AND BIOINFORMATICS;
9 (II) HIGH–THROUGHPUT SCREENING METHODS;
10 (III) TESTING OF CATEGORIES OF CHEMICAL SUBSTANCES;
11 (IV) TIERED TESTING METHODS;
12 (V) IN VITRO STUDIES; AND
13 (VI) MICROPHYSIOLOGICAL SYSTEMS.
14 (C) “TRADITIONAL ANIMAL TEST METHOD” MEANS A PROCESS,
15 PROCEDURE, OR EXPERIMENT USING LIVE VERTEBRATE ANIMALS TO:
16 (1) OBTAIN INFORMATION ON THE CHARACTERISTICS OF A PRODUCT,
17 INCLUDING:
18 (I) A CHEMICAL;
19 (II) AN INGREDIENT;
20 (III) A DRUG;
21 (IV) A VACCINE; OR
22 (V) A PRODUCT FORMULATION; AND
23 (2) GENERATE INFORMATION REGARDING THE ABILITY OF THE
24 PRODUCT TO PRODUCE A SPECIFIC BIOLOGICAL EFFECT UNDER SPECIFIED
25 CONDITIONS.
HOUSE BILL 1448 3
1 24–23A–02.
2 THIS SUBTITLE APPLIES ONLY TO A RESEARCH FACILITY THAT IS LOCATED IN
3 THE STATE AND REQUIRED TO SUBMIT AN ANIMAL AND PLANT HEALTH
4 INSPECTION SERVICE FORM 7023, AS DEFINED IN § 24–2301 OF THIS TITLE.
5 24–23A–03.
6 (A) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY WITH RESPECT TO:
7 (1) ANIMAL RESEARCH CONDUCTED FOR THE PURPOSES OF
8 BIOMEDICAL RESEARCH TO INVESTIGATE:
9 (I) THE BIOLOGICAL PROCESS;
10 (II) CAUSES OF DISEASE; OR
11 (III) HOW PROCESSES IN LIVING ORGANISMS DEVELOP AND
12 FUNCTION; OR
13 (2) A TRADITIONAL ANIMAL TEST METHOD USED TO COMPLY WITH:
14 (I) THE REQUIREMENTS OF A FEDERAL OR STATE AGENCY; OR
15 (II) A WRITTEN REQUEST FROM A FEDERAL OR STATE AGENCY
16 WHEN THE AGENCY HAS EXPRESSLY CONCLUDED THAT A TRADITIONAL ANIMAL
17 TEST METHOD IS NEEDED TO FULLY ASSESS THE IMPACT ON THE HEALTH OR SAFETY
18 OF CONSUMERS.
19 (B) A RESEARCH FACILITY MAY NOT USE A TRADITIONAL ANIMAL TEST
20 METHOD IF:
21 (1) AN ALTERNATIVE NONANIMAL TEST METHOD HAS BEEN
22 APPROVED BY THE FEDERAL OR STATE AGENCY RESPONSIBLE FOR REGULATING
23 THE SPECIFIC PRODUCT OR ACTIVITY BEING TESTED; OR
24 (2) THE FEDERAL OR STATE AGENCY RESPONSIBLE FOR
25 REGULATING THE SPECIFIC PRODUCT OR ACTIVITY BEING TESTED HAS MADE
26 AVAILABLE OR GRANTED A WAIVER FROM USING A TRADITIONAL ANIMAL TEST
27 METHOD.
4 HOUSE BILL 1448
1 (C) IF ALLOWED TO USE A TRADITIONAL ANIMAL TEST METHOD, A
2 RESEARCH FACILITY SHALL:
3 (1) USE THE FEWEST NUMBER OF ANIMALS POSSIBLE; AND
4 (2) REDUCE THE LEVEL OF PAIN, SUFFERING, AND STRESS OF THE
5 ANIMALS USED FOR TESTING.
6 24–23A–04.
7 (A) (1) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 20 EACH YEAR, BEGINNING IN 2027,
8 EACH RESEARCH FACILITY SHALL SUBMIT A REPORT TO THE ATTORNEY GENERAL
9 WITH INFORMATION RELATED TO THE TRADITIONAL ANIMAL TEST METHODS AND
10 ALTERNATIVE NONANIMAL TEST METHODS USED BY THE RESEARCH FACILITY IN
11 THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING CALENDAR YEAR.
12 (2) THE REPORT REQUIRED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
13 SUBSECTION SHALL INCLUDE:
14 (I) IF THE RESEARCH FACILITY USED TRADITIONAL ANIMAL
15 TEST METHODS DURING THE REPORTING PERIOD:
16 1. A DESCRIPTION OF EACH TRADITIONAL ANIMAL TEST
17 METHOD USED AND THE REASON THE TEST METHOD WAS REQUIRED; AND
18 2. THE NUMBER AND SPECIES OF ANIMAL USED; AND
19 (II) IF THE RESEARCH FACILITY USED ALTERNATIVE
20 NONANIMAL TEST METHODS DURING THE REPORTING PERIOD:
21 1. THE TYPES AND NUMBER OF ALTERNATIVE
22 NONANIMAL TEST METHODS USED; AND
23 2. THE NUMBER OF WAIVERS RECEIVED BY THE
24 RESEARCH FACILITY UNDER § 24–23A–03 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
25 (B) ON OR BEFORE APRIL 20 EACH YEAR, BEGINNING IN 2027, THE
26 ATTORNEY GENERAL SHALL MAKE THE INFORMATION SUBMITTED UNDER
27 SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION PUBLICLY AVAILABLE.
28 24–23A–05.
29 (A) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL SHALL ENFORCE THIS SUBTITLE.
HOUSE BILL 1448 5
1 (B) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY PETITION THE CIRCUIT COURT TO:
2 (1) ISSUE AN ORDER RESTRAINING OR ENJOINING A VIOLATION OF
3 THIS SUBTITLE; AND
4 (2) GRANT ANY OTHER RELIEF THAT THE COURT DETERMINES IS
5 NECESSARY.
6 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
7 January 1, 2026.
