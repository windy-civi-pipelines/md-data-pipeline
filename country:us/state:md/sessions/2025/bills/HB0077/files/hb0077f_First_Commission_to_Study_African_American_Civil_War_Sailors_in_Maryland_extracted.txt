(PRE–FILED) CF SB 13
By: Delegates Bouchat, Pasteur, and Wims
Requested: July 29, 2024
Introduced and read first time: January 8, 2025
Assigned to: Health and Government Operations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Commission to Study African American Civil War Sailors in Maryland
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Commission to Study African American Civil War
4 Sailors in Maryland to study the history of the African American sailors who fought
5 for the Union Navy and make certain recommendations regarding a memorial
6 dedicated to African American Civil War sailors; and generally relating to the
7 Commission to Study African American Civil War Sailors in Maryland.
8 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
9 That:
10 (a) There is a Commission to Study African American Civil War Sailors in
11 Maryland.
12 (b) The Commission consists of the following members:
13 (1) one member of the Senate of Maryland, appointed by the President of
14 the Senate;
15 (2) one member of the House of Delegates, appointed by the Speaker of the
16 House; and
17 (3) the following members, appointed by the Governor:
18 (i) one representative from the State Archives;
19 (ii) one representative from the Department of Planning;
20 (iii) one representative from the Governor’s Commission on
21 Maryland Military Monuments;

2 HOUSE BILL 77
1 (iv) one representative from the Commission on African American
2 History and Culture;
3 (v) one representative from Preservation Maryland, Inc.; and
4 (vi) one representative from the Heart of the Civil War Heritage
5 Area, Inc.
6 (c) The Governor shall designate the chair of the Commission.
7 (d) The State Archives shall provide staff for the Commission.
8 (e) A member of the Commission:
9 (1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Commission; but
10 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
11 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.
12 (f) The Commission shall:
13 (1) study the history of African American Civil War sailors who fought for
14 the Union Navy in Maryland, including muster rolls, locations of service, and all
15 distinguished honors and recognitions awarded; and
16 (2) make findings and recommendations regarding the funding, design,
17 construction, and location of an appropriate memorial dedicated to the African American
18 Civil War sailors in Maryland.
19 (g) On or before December 1, 2025, the Commission shall report its findings and
20 recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
21 Government Article, the General Assembly.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
23 1, 2025. It shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and, at the end of June 30, 2026, this
24 Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no
25 further force and effect.
