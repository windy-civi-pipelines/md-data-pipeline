1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Correctional Training Commission – Membership
3 FOR the purpose of altering the membership of the Maryland Correctional Training
4 Commission to include the Director and an employee of the Division of Parole and
5 Probation within the Department of Public Safety and Correctional Services and a
6 certain parole and probation employee; and generally relating to the membership of
7 the Maryland Correctional Training Commission.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Correctional Services
10 Section 8–204(b) and (d)
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2017 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

2 HOUSE BILL 123
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Correctional Services
4 8–204.
5 (b) The Commission consists of the following members:
6 (1) the Secretary of Public Safety and Correctional Services;
7 (2) the Secretary of Juvenile Services;
8 (3) a representative of the Department of Public Safety and Correctional
9 Services, designated by the Secretary of Public Safety and Correctional Services;
10 (4) a representative of the Department of Juvenile Services, designated by
11 the Secretary of Juvenile Services;
12 (5) a Deputy Secretary of Public Safety and Correctional Services;
13 (6) the president of the Maryland Correctional Administrators Association;
14 (7) the president of the Maryland Sheriffs Association;
15 (8) the president of the Maryland Criminal Justice Association;
16 (9) the Attorney General of the State;
17 (10) the president of a university or college in the State with a correctional
18 education curriculum, appointed by the Maryland Higher Education Commission;
19 (11) one correctional officer of the State recommended by the exclusive
20 representative for the officers covered under Title 10, Subtitle 9 of this article and
21 appointed by the Governor; [and]
22 (12) five correctional officers or officials appointed under subsection (c) of
23 this section; AND
24 (13) THE DIRECTOR OF THE DIVISION OF PAROLE AND PROBATION
25 WITHIN THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC SAFETY AND CORRECTIONAL SERVICES; AND
26 (14) ONE PAROLE AND PROBATION EMPLOYEE OF THE STATE
27 RECOMMENDED BY THE EXCLUSIVE REPRESENTATIVE FOR PAROLE AND
28 PROBATION EMPLOYEES, AS SPECIFIED AND APPOINTED BY THE GOVERNOR.
HOUSE BILL 123 3
1 (14) ONE EMPLOYEE OF THE DIVISION OF PAROLE AND PROBATION
2 RECOMMENDED BY THE EXCLUSIVE REPRESENTATIVE FOR THE EMPLOYEES OF THE
3 DIVISION OF PAROLE AND PROBATION AND APPOINTED BY THE GOVERNOR.
4 (d) Except for the five members appointed by the Governor under subsection (c) of
5 this section AND THE MEMBER APPOINTED UNDER SUBSECTION (B)(14) OF THIS
6 SECTION, a member of the Commission may serve personally at a Commission meeting or
7 designate a representative from the member’s unit or association who may act at any meeting
8 to the same effect as if the member were personally present.
9 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
10 October 1, 2025.
Approved:
________________________________________________________________________________
Governor.
________________________________________________________________________________
Speaker of the House of Delegates.
________________________________________________________________________________
President of the Senate.
