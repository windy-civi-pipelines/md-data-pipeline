CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Montgomery County – Alcoholic Beverages Licenses – Health and Safety
3 Regulations
4 MC 21–25
5 FOR the purpose of requiring the Board of License Commissioners for Montgomery County
6 to adopt regulations to gather certain information about certain retail
7 establishments with certain alcoholic beverages licenses and to promote the public
8 health and safety of the community based on the density of the retail establishments;
9 authorizing the Board to consider certain public testimony consider, before deciding
10 whether to approve an application and issue a new license permitting the sale of
11 alcoholic beverages for off–premises consumption, the impact of the density of
12 existing retail establishments licensed in the county to sell alcoholic beverages for
13 off–premises consumption on the public health and safety of the community; and
14 generally relating to alcoholic beverages licenses in Montgomery County.
15 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
16 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
17 Section 4–210(a) and 25–102
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2024 Replacement Volume)
20 BY adding to
21 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
22 Section 25–207

13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
16 4–210.
17 (a) Before deciding whether to approve an application and issue a license, a local
18 licensing board shall consider:
19 (1) the public need and desire for the license;
20 (2) the number and location of existing license holders;
21 (3) the potential effect on existing license holders of the license for which
22 application is made;
23 (4) the potential commonality or uniqueness of the services and products
24 to be offered by the business of the applicant;
25 (5) the impact of the license for which application is made on the health,
26 safety, and welfare of the community, including issues relating to crime, traffic, parking,
27 or convenience; and
28 (6) any other factor that the local licensing board considers necessary.
29 25–102.
30 This title applies only in Montgomery County.
31 25–207.
HOUSE BILL 1229 3
1 (A) THE BOARD SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO:
2 (1) GATHER INFORMATION ABOUT THE NUMBER AND LOCATION OF
3 RETAIL ESTABLISHMENTS LICENSED TO SELL ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES FOR
4 OFF–PREMISES CONSUMPTION IN THE COUNTY; AND
5 (2) PROMOTE THE PUBLIC HEALTH AND SAFETY OF THE COMMUNITY
6 BASED ON THE IMPACT OF THE DENSITY OF RETAIL ESTABLISHMENTS LICENSED TO
7 SELL ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES FOR OFF–PREMISES CONSUMPTION IN THE COUNTY.
8 (B) THE INFORMATION REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS
9 SECTION SHALL BE:
10 (1) COLLECTED EACH TIME A NEW LICENSE IS ISSUED OR AN
11 EXISTING LICENSE IS RENEWED FOR A RETAIL ESTABLISHMENT; AND
12 (2) CONSIDERED WHEN DETERMINING WHETHER TO AUTHORIZE NEW
13 LICENSES OR RENEW EXISTING LICENSES FOR RETAIL ESTABLISHMENTS.
14 (C) FOR EACH LICENSE BEING CONSIDERED, THE BOARD MAY HEAR
15 TESTIMONY RELATED TO THE IMPACT ON THE HEALTH AND SAFETY OF THE PUBLIC
16 BASED ON THE NUMBER OF LICENSES ISSUED FOR OFF–PREMISES CONSUMPTION,
17 IN ADDITION TO ANY PUBLICLY OFFERED CONCERNS.
18 25–1501.
19 (a) The following sections of Title 4, Subtitle 2 (“Issuance or Denial of Local
20 Licenses”) of Division I of this article apply in the county without exception or variation:
21 (1) § 4–205 (“Chain store, supermarket, or discount house”);
22 (2) § 4–206 (“Limitations on retail sales floor space”);
23 (3) § 4–207 (“Licenses issued to minors”);
24 (4) § 4–209 (“Hearing”);
25 (5) [§ 4–210 (“Approval or denial of license application”);
26 (6)] § 4–211 (“License forms; effective date; expiration”);
27 [(7)] (6) § 4–212 (“License not property”); and
28 [(8)] (7) § 4–213 (“Replacement licenses”).
4 HOUSE BILL 1229
1 (b) Section 4–214 (“Waiting periods after denial of license applications”) of
2 Division I of this article does not apply in the county.
3 (c) The following sections of Title 4, Subtitle 2 (“Issuance or Denial of Local
4 Licenses”) of Division I of this article apply in the county:
5 (1) § 4–202 (“Authority of local licensing boards”), subject to § 25–1502 of
6 this subtitle;
7 (2) § 4–203 (“Prohibition against issuing multiple licenses to individual or
8 for use of entity”), subject to § 25–1503 of this subtitle and Subtitle 13, Part III and Subtitle
9 16, Part II of this title;
10 (3) § 4–204 (“Prohibition against issuing multiple licenses for same
11 premises”), subject to § 25–1503 of this subtitle and Subtitle 13, Part III of this title; [and]
12 (4) § 4–208 (“Notice of license application required”), subject to § 25–1506
13 of this subtitle; AND
14 (5) § 4–210 (“APPROVAL OR DENIAL OF LICENSE APPLICATION”),
15 SUBJECT TO § 25–207 § 25–1507 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
16 25–1507.
17 BEFORE DECIDING WHETHER TO APPROVE AN APPLICATION AND ISSUE A NEW
18 LICENSE PERMITTING THE SALE OF ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES FOR OFF–PREMISES
19 CONSUMPTION, THE BOARD SHALL CONSIDER THE IMPACT OF THE DENSITY OF
20 EXISTING RETAIL ESTABLISHMENTS LICENSED IN THE COUNTY TO SELL ALCOHOLIC
21 BEVERAGES FOR OFF–PREMISES CONSUMPTION ON THE PUBLIC HEALTH AND
22 SAFETY OF THE COMMUNITY.
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
24 1, 2025.
