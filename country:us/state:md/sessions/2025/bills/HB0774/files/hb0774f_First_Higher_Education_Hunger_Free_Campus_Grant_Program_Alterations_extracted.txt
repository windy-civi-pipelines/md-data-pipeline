A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Higher Education – Hunger–Free Campus Grant Program – Alterations
3 FOR the purpose of altering the requirement of the Governor, beginning in a certain fiscal
4 year, to include in the annual budget bill a certain appropriation for the
5 Hunger–Free Campus Grant Program; altering eligibility requirements for the
6 Program; requiring the Maryland Higher Education Commission to allocate certain
7 grant funding to certain public institutions of higher education or regional higher
8 education centers for certain purposes; authorizing the Commission to maintain a
9 certain percentage of each grant to administer the Program; and generally relating
10 to alterations to the Hunger–Free Campus Grant Program.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
12 Article – Education
13 Section 11–1701 through 11–1703
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
16 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
17 Article – Education
18 Section 11–1704 through 11–1706
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
21 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
22 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
23 Article – Education
24 11–1701.
25 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.

1 (b) “Emergency assistance” means immediate direct assistance provided by a
2 hunger–free campus to any student facing an unanticipated financial crisis or hardship.
3 (c) “Hunger–free campus” means a campus of any 4–year public institution of
4 higher education or regional higher education center that meets the requirements of §
5 11–1703(a) of this subtitle or any 2–year public institution of higher education that meets
6 the requirements of § 11–1703(b) of this subtitle.
7 (d) “Program” means the Hunger–Free Campus Grant Program.
8 11–1702.
9 (a) There is a Hunger–Free Campus Grant Program.
10 (b) The purpose of the Program is to:
11 (1) Address student hunger;
12 (2) Leverage more sustainable solutions to address basic food needs on
13 campus;
14 (3) Raise awareness of services currently offered on campus that address
15 basic food needs; and
16 (4) Build strategic partnerships at the local, State, and national levels to
17 address food insecurity among students.
18 (c) (1) The Commission shall administer the Program.
19 (2) The Commission shall develop the form to be used for the annual
20 student survey required under § 11–1703 of this subtitle.
21 11–1703.
22 (a) The Commission shall designate as a hunger–free campus any campus of a
23 4–year public institution of higher education or regional higher education center that:
24 (1) Has established a hunger task force that:
25 (i) Meets at least three times per academic year; and
26 (ii) Sets at least two goals with action plans;
27 (2) Has designated a staff member responsible for:
HOUSE BILL 774 3
1 (i) Assisting students in enrolling in the Supplemental Nutrition
2 Assistance Program; or
3 (ii) Connecting students with available Supplemental Nutrition
4 Assistance Program outreach partners that can assist students in enrolling in the
5 Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program;
6 (3) Has designated a staff member responsible for informing students
7 participating in federal work–study programs that they are eligible for the Supplemental
8 Nutrition Assistance Program;
9 (4) (i) Provides options for students to utilize Supplemental Nutrition
10 Assistance Program benefits at campus retailers; or
11 (ii) Provides students with information on the names and locations
12 of off–campus retailers that accept Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program benefits;
13 (5) Participates in an awareness day campaign activity and plans at least
14 one campus awareness event during the National Hunger and Homelessness Awareness
15 Week;
16 (6) Provides at least one food pantry on campus, or enables students in
17 need to receive food through a separate, stigma–free arrangement;
18 (7) Develops and maintains a meal–sharing program that allows students
19 to donate their unused meal plan credits to be distributed to students in need for use in
20 campus dining halls or at an on–campus food pantry, if applicable;
21 (8) Conducts a standardized annual student survey on hunger and submits
22 the results to the Commission; and
23 (9) Submits an annual report detailing its efforts to address student
24 hunger to the Commission.
25 (b) The Commission shall designate as a hunger–free campus any campus of a
26 2–year public institution of higher education that meets all of the requirements for 4–year
27 public institutions of higher education or regional higher education centers identified in
28 subsection (a) of this section, with the exception of the requirements in subsection (a)(4)
29 and (7) of this section.
30 (c) (1) Each 4–year public institution of higher education may develop its own
31 procedures for a meal–sharing program.
32 (2) The 4–year public institutions of higher education shall make
33 information about the meal–sharing program publicly available, including:
34 (i) How a student may donate unused meal plan credits;
4 HOUSE BILL 774
1 (ii) How a student in need may apply to receive donated meal plan
2 credits; and
3 (iii) The methodology that a 4–year public institution of higher
4 education uses to ensure that unused donated meal plan credits are used to purchase food
5 to support an on–campus food pantry, if applicable.
6 11–1704.
7 (A) For fiscal [year 2023 and each fiscal year thereafter] YEARS 2023 THROUGH
8 2026, the Governor shall include in the annual budget bill an appropriation of $150,000
9 for the Program.
10 (B) FOR FISCAL YEAR 2027 AND EACH FISCAL YEAR THEREAFTER, THE
11 GOVERNOR SHALL INCLUDE IN THE ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION OF
12 $225,000 FOR THE PROGRAM.
13 11–1705.
14 (a) The Commission shall allocate grant funding to any 4–year or 2–year public
15 institution of higher education or regional higher education center that:
16 (1) Pledges a matching contribution to be used to implement the goals of
17 the Program, INCLUDING AT LEAST $25,000 IN CASH OR IN–KIND CONTRIBUTION; and
18 (2) Is designated as a hunger–free campus.
19 (b) (1) The Commission shall determine the amount of each grant.
20 (2) THE COMMISSION SHALL ALLOCATE TO EACH FIRST–TIME
21 RECIPIENT AT LEAST $25,000 AND NOT MORE THAN $150,000 FOR PROGRAMMATIC
22 AND ORGANIZATION–BUILDING ACTIVITIES THAT:
23 (I) INCREASE THE CAPACITY OF INSTITUTIONS AND CENTERS
24 TO IMPLEMENT THE PROGRAM; AND
25 (II) PROVIDE SEED MONEY FOR INDIRECT, UP–FRONT COSTS
26 ASSOCIATED WITH PROGRAM IMPLEMENTATION.
27 (3) FOR EACH GRANT, THE COMMISSION MAY RETAIN 15% FOR THE
28 COST OF ADMINISTERING THE PROGRAM.
29 (c) The Commission shall incorporate into the 2026–2030 State Plan for Higher
30 Education a plan for addressing any basic needs insecurity of higher education students.
HOUSE BILL 774 5
1 (d) (1) The Commission shall adopt regulations necessary to carry out the
2 requirements of this subtitle.
3 (2) The regulations adopted under this subsection shall allow grant
4 recipients to use grant funds flexibly to implement the goals of the Program, including
5 giving recipients the ability to:
6 (i) Support emergency assistance;
7 (ii) Hire staff to manage initiatives related to the Program; and
8 (iii) Use grant funds for operational activities related to the Program.
9 11–1706.
10 (a) Not later than 2 years after establishing the Program, the Commission shall
11 report to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article,
12 the General Assembly on the implementation of this subtitle.
13 (b) The report shall include:
14 (1) The number and amounts of grants awarded, INCLUDING
15 DEMOGRAPHIC DATA OF STUDENTS BENEFITING FROM THE PROGRAM;
16 (2) The impact of the Program on establishing hunger–free campuses at
17 public institutions of higher education and regional higher education centers;
18 (3) The impact of awarded grants on reducing the number of students
19 experiencing food insecurity; [and]
20 (4) Recommendations on expanding the Program; AND
21 (5) INFORMATION ON BEST PRACTICES AMONG OTHER
22 HUNGER–FREE CAMPUS PROGRAMS.
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
24 1, 2025.
