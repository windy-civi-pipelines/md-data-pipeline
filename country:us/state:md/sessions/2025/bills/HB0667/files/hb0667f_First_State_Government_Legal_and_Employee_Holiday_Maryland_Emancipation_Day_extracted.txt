A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Government – Legal and Employee Holiday – Maryland Emancipation Day
3 FOR the purpose of establishing Maryland Emancipation Day as a State legal holiday and
4 State employee holiday; repealing the requirement that the Governor declare a
5 certain day as Maryland Emancipation Day; and generally relating to Maryland
6 Emancipation Day.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – General Provisions
9 Section 1–111
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
12 BY repealing
13 Article – General Provisions
14 Section 7–420
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
18 Article – State Personnel and Pensions
19 Section 9–201
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
22 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
23 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

2 HOUSE BILL 667
1 Article – General Provisions
2 1–111.
3 (a) In this Code and any regulation or directive adopted under it, “legal holiday”
4 means:
5 (1) January 1, for New Year’s Day;
6 (2) (i) January 15, for Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.’s birthday; or
7 (ii) if the United States Congress designates another day for
8 observance of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.’s birthday, the day designated by the United
9 States Congress;
10 (3) February 12, for Lincoln’s birthday;
11 (4) the third Monday in February, for Washington’s birthday;
12 (5) March 25, for Maryland Day;
13 (6) Good Friday;
14 (7) (i) May 30, for Memorial Day; or
15 (ii) if the United States Congress designates another day for
16 observance of Memorial Day, the day designated by the United States Congress;
17 (8) June 19, for Juneteenth National Independence Day;
18 (9) July 4, for Independence Day;
19 (10) the first Monday in September, for Labor Day;
20 (11) September 12, for Defenders’ Day;
21 (12) (i) October 12, for Columbus Day; or
22 (ii) if the United States Congress designates another day for
23 observance of Columbus Day, the day designated by the United States Congress;
24 (13) NOVEMBER 1, FOR MARYLAND EMANCIPATION DAY;
25 [(13)] (14) November 11, for Veterans’ Day;
26 [(14)] (15) the fourth Thursday in November, for Thanksgiving Day;
