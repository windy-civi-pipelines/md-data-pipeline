1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Maryland Civic Excellence Program – Established
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland Civic Excellence Program as a voluntary
4 program in which local school systems may participate to recognize students and
5 public schools that promote the civic readiness and civic engagement of students in
6 public schools in the State; and generally relating to the establishment of the
7 Maryland Civic Excellence Program.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Education
10 Section 4–147
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Education
16 4–147.

2 SENATE BILL 8
1 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “PROGRAM” MEANS THE MARYLAND CIVIC
2 EXCELLENCE PROGRAM ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SECTION.
3 (B) (1) THERE IS A MARYLAND CIVIC EXCELLENCE PROGRAM.
4 (2) THE PURPOSE OF THE PROGRAM IS TO RECOGNIZE STUDENTS
5 AND PUBLIC SCHOOLS THAT PROMOTE THE CIVIC READINESS AND CIVIC
6 ENGAGEMENT OF STUDENTS IN PUBLIC SCHOOLS IN THE STATE.
7 (C) THE PROVISIONS OF THIS SECTION APPLY ONLY TO A LOCAL SCHOOL
8 SYSTEM THAT CHOOSES TO PARTICIPATE IN AND ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM.
9 (D) BEGINNING WITH THE 2026–2027 SCHOOL YEAR, EACH PARTICIPATING
10 LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL:
11 (1) AWARD A STUDENT A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE IF THE
12 STUDENT APPLIES AND MEETS THE CRITERIA ESTABLISHED BY THE LOCAL SCHOOL
13 SYSTEM UNDER SUBSECTION (E) OF THIS SECTION; AND
14 (2) DESIGNATE A PUBLIC SCHOOL AS A CENTER OF CIVIC
15 EXCELLENCE IF THE SCHOOL APPLIES AND MEETS THE CRITERIA ADOPTED BY THE
16 LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM UNDER SUBSECTION (G) OF THIS SECTION.
17 (E) AT MINIMUM, A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM’S CRITERIA
18 FOR A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE SHALL REQUIRE A STUDENT TO:
19 (1) (I) TAKE THE MARYLAND COMPREHENSIVE ASSESSMENT FOR
20 GOVERNMENT; AND
21 (II) MEET THE ASSESSMENT REQUIREMENTS FOR
22 GRADUATION;
23 (2) EARN A GRADE OF A OR B IN EACH OF THE FOLLOWING HIGH
24 SCHOOL SOCIAL STUDIES COURSES:
25 (I) UNITED STATES HISTORY;
26 (II) MODERN WORLD HISTORY; AND
27 (III) AMERICAN GOVERNMENT; AND
28 (3) COMPLETE AND DEMONSTRATE EVIDENCE OF AT LEAST 75 HOURS
29 OF STUDENT SERVICE, OF WHICH AT LEAST 30 HOURS SHALL BE DEDICATED TO A
SENATE BILL 8 3
1 SERVICE LEARNING PROJECT WITH A FOCUS ON COMMUNITY AND CIVIC
2 ENGAGEMENT.
3 (F) A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL ESTABLISH
4 APPLICATION CRITERIA TO ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM.
5 (G) AT MINIMUM, A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM’S CRITERIA
6 FOR A CENTER OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE DESIGNATION SHALL REQUIRE A PUBLIC
7 SCHOOL TO HAVE AT LEAST 50% OF THE STUDENTS IN ITS GRADUATING CLASS WHO
8 HAVE EARNED A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE.
9 (H) (1) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM THAT DETERMINES A STUDENT HAS
10 FULFILLED THE CRITERIA UNDER THE PROGRAM SHALL:
11 (I) AWARD THE STUDENT A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE;
12 (II) RECORD THE AWARD ON THE STUDENT’S OFFICIAL HIGH
13 SCHOOL TRANSCRIPT; AND
14 (III) RECOGNIZE THE AWARD BY AWARDING A CORD OR MEDAL
15 OR BY ATTACHING OTHER INSIGNIA ON THE STUDENT’S HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA.
16 (2) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM THAT DETERMINES THAT A PUBLIC
17 SCHOOL HAS FULFILLED THE CRITERIA UNDER THE PROGRAM SHALL:
18 (I) DESIGNATE THE SCHOOL AS A CENTER OF CIVIC
19 EXCELLENCE; AND
20 (II) RECOGNIZE THE DESIGNATION BY PROVIDING ANY OF THE
21 FOLLOWING:
22 1. A BANNER;
23 2. A GOVERNOR’S CITATION; OR
24 3. A CITATION FROM THE DEPARTMENT.
25 (I) ON OR BEFORE JULY 1, 2027, AND EACH JULY 1 THEREAFTER, EACH
26 PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL SUBMIT TO THE DEPARTMENT A
27 REPORT ON THE PROGRAM BASED ON THE MOST RECENTLY COMPLETED SCHOOL
28 YEAR.
29 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
30 1, 2025.
