CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Disability Service Animal Program – Established
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland Disability Service Animal Program in the
4 Department of Disabilities; requiring the Department to select a nonprofit training
5 entity for participation in the Program; establishing the Maryland Disability Service
6 Animal Program Fund as a special, nonlapsing fund; and generally relating to the
7 Maryland Disability Service Animal Program.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Human Services
10 Section 7–114
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 BY adding to
14 Article – Human Services
15 Section 7–1201 through 7–1206 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 12. Maryland
16 Disability Service Animal Program”
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

2 HOUSE BILL 384
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Human Services
4 7–114.
5 (a) (1) The Department is the principal unit of State government responsible
6 for developing, maintaining, revising, and enforcing statewide disability policies and
7 standards throughout the units of State government.
8 (2) In this capacity, the Department shall:
9 (i) serve as the principal advisor to the Governor on the means and
10 methods available to:
11 1. implement and fund support to individuals with
12 disabilities in accordance with the State Disabilities Plan;
13 2. modify or consolidate support to individuals with
14 disabilities; and
15 3. collaborate with federal, regional, and local units of
16 government to enhance the effectiveness of the provision and funding of support to
17 individuals with disabilities;
18 (ii) annually recommend projects to the Department of Budget and
19 Management for inclusion in the capital budget to promote access to State–owned facilities
20 for individuals with disabilities;
21 (iii) assist units of State government to identify federal, State, local,
22 and private funds available to the State for programs and services for individuals with
23 disabilities; and
24 (iv) provide technical assistance to local jurisdictions in planning and
25 implementing collaborative strategies consistent with the State Disabilities Plan.
26 (b) The Department shall oversee and administer the following programs and
27 units:
28 (1) constituent services and ombudsmen programs;
29 (2) the Assistive Technology Guaranteed Loan Program under Subtitle 6
30 of this title;

1 (3) the Office of Personal Assistance Services, including the Attendant
2 Care Program under Subtitle 4 of this title;
3 (4) Telecommunications Access of Maryland under Subtitle 8 of this title;
4 [and]
5 (5) Telecommunications Devices and Distribution of Accessible
6 Information for Disabled Individuals under Subtitle 9 of this title; AND
7 (6) THE MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL PROGRAM UNDER
8 SUBTITLE 12 OF THIS TITLE.
9 SUBTITLE 12. MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL PROGRAM.
10 7–1201.
11 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
12 INDICATED.
13 (B) “DEPARTMENT” MEANS THE DEPARTMENT OF DISABILITIES.
14 (C) “ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUAL” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL A RESIDENT OF THE
15 STATE WITH A DISABILITY, AS DEFINED IN THE FEDERAL AMERICANS WITH
16 DISABILITIES ACT OF 1990, 42 U.S.C. § 12102.
17 (D) “FUND” MEANS THE MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL
18 PROGRAM FUND.
19 (E) “NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY” MEANS A CORPORATION, A
20 FOUNDATION, OR ANY OTHER LEGAL ENTITY THAT:
21 (1) IS TAX–EXEMPT UNDER § 501(C)(3) OF THE INTERNAL REVENUE
22 CODE;
23 (2) ENGAGES IN THE TRAINING OF SERVICE ANIMALS FOR USE BY
24 ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS; AND
25 (3) HAS BEEN SELECTED BY THE DEPARTMENT TO PROVIDE
26 SERVICES UNDER THIS SUBTITLE.
27 (F) “PROGRAM” MEANS THE MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL
28 PROGRAM ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SUBTITLE.
29 (G) “PROGRAM PARTICIPANT” MEANS AN ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUAL WHO
30 PARTICIPATES IN THE PROGRAM.
4 HOUSE BILL 384
1 (H) (1) “SERVICE ANIMAL” MEANS AN ANIMAL THAT IS INDIVIDUALLY
2 TRAINED TO DO WORK OR PERFORM TASKS FOR THE BENEFIT OF AN INDIVIDUAL
3 WITH A DISABILITY.
4 (2) “SERVICE ANIMAL” DOES NOT INCLUDE AN ANIMAL THAT:
5 (I) AS A RESULT OF THE ANIMAL’S PRESENCE, IS MEANT TO
6 DETER CRIME; OR
7 (II) PROVIDES ONLY EMOTIONAL SUPPORT, WELL–BEING,
8 COMFORT, OR COMPANIONSHIP TO AN INDIVIDUAL.
9 (I) “SUCCESSFUL PROGRAM PARTICIPANT” MEANS A PROGRAM
10 PARTICIPANT WHO SUCCESSFULLY COMPLETES THE TRAINING PROTOCOL
11 SPECIFIED BY A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY.
12 7–1202.
13 THERE IS A MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL PROGRAM IN THE
14 DEPARTMENT.
15 7–1203.
16 THE PURPOSES OF THE PROGRAM ARE TO:
17 (1) REFER ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS WHO INQUIRE ABOUT
18 PARTICIPATION IN THE PROGRAM TO SELECT ONE OR MORE NONPROFIT TRAINING
19 ENTITIES TO PARTICIPATE IN THE PROGRAM; AND
20 (2) PROVIDE ADDITIONAL FUNDING MECHANISMS TO ASSIST
21 ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS IN THE PROGRAM; AND
22 (3) ENCOURAGE SUCCESSFUL PROGRAM PARTICIPANTS TO ASSIST IN
23 OUTREACH AND REFERRALS TO OTHER ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS WHO COULD
24 BENEFIT FROM PARTICIPATION IN THE PROGRAM.
25 7–1204.
26 THE DEPARTMENT SHALL:
27 (1) MANAGE, SUPERVISE, AND ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM;
28 (2) ADOPT REGULATIONS TO IMPLEMENT THE PROGRAM AND TO
29 ENSURE THAT FUND RESOURCES ARE UTILIZED TO CARRY OUT THE PURPOSES OF
HOUSE BILL 384 5
1 THE PROGRAM, INCLUDING REGULATIONS ESTABLISHING PROCEDURES FOR THE
2 DEPARTMENT TO:
3 (I) PROMOTE THE PROGRAM TO ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS
4 THROUGH THE DEPARTMENT’S OUTREACH METHODS;
5 (II) REFER ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS TO SELECTED NONPROFIT
6 TRAINING ENTITIES;
7 (III) RECEIVE DONATIONS FOR THE FUND; AND
8 (IV) (II) USE REVENUE FROM THE FUND TO PAY SELECTED
9 NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITIES FOR SERVICES THAT ARE PROVIDED THROUGH THE
10 PROGRAM; AND
11 (3) SELECT AT LEAST ONE NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY TO:
12 (I) DEVELOP AND IMPLEMENT A TRAINING PROTOCOL THAT
13 WILL TEACH EACH PROGRAM PARTICIPANT METHODOLOGIES, STRATEGIES, AND
14 TECHNIQUES FOR PARTNERING WITH SERVICE ANIMALS;
15 (II) SELECT QUALIFIED PROGRAM PARTICIPANTS FROM THOSE
16 ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS REFERRED TO THE NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY UNDER
17 THE PROGRAM;
18 (III) SELECT AN APPROPRIATE SERVICE ANIMAL FOR EACH
19 PROGRAM PARTICIPANT;
20 (IV) FACILITATE EACH PROGRAM PARTICIPANT’S TRAINING
21 USING THE NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY’S TRAINING PROTOCOL; AND
22 (V) PARTNER EACH SUCCESSFUL PROGRAM PARTICIPANT WITH
23 THE SERVICE ANIMAL ON THE PROGRAM PARTICIPANT’S SUCCESSFUL COMPLETION
24 OF THE NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY’S TRAINING PROTOCOL.
25 7–1205.
26 (A) TO BE ELIGIBLE FOR SELECTION AS A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY
27 UNDER § 7–1204 OF THIS SUBTITLE, A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY SHALL:
28 (1) SERVE THE NEEDS OF ELIGIBLE INDIVIDUALS IN THE STATE; AND
29 (2) GENERATE ITS OWN REVENUE AND REINVEST THE PROCEEDS OF
30 THAT REVENUE IN THE GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT OF ITS PROGRAMS.
6 HOUSE BILL 384
1 (B) A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY MAY DISQUALIFY A PROGRAM
2 PARTICIPANT FROM PARTICIPATION IN THE PROGRAM IF THE NONPROFIT
3 TRAINING ENTITY DETERMINES THAT THE PROGRAM PARTICIPANT’S INVOLVEMENT
4 IN THE PROGRAM:
5 (1) PRESENTS A DANGER TO THE PROGRAM PARTICIPANT’S MENTAL
6 OR PHYSICAL WELL–BEING;
7 (2) PRESENTS A DIRECT THREAT TO OTHERS, AS DEFINED BY THE
8 FEDERAL AMERICANS WITH DISABILITIES ACT;
9 (3) PRESENTS A DIRECT THREAT TO THE SERVICE ANIMAL’S MENTAL
10 OR PHYSICAL WELL–BEING; OR
11 (4) DOES NOT MEET THE TRAINING REQUIREMENTS OF THE
12 NONPROFIT.
13 (C) A PROGRAM PARTICIPANT MAY DISCONTINUE INVOLVEMENT IN THE
14 PROGRAM FOR ANY REASON.
15 7–1206.
16 (A) THERE IS A MARYLAND DISABILITY SERVICE ANIMAL PROGRAM FUND.
17 (B) THE PURPOSE OF THE FUND IS TO:
18 (1) PAY A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY; AND
19 (2) COVER THE COSTS OF ADMINISTERING THE PROGRAM.
20 (C) THE SECRETARY SHALL ADMINISTER THE FUND.
21 (D) (1) THE FUND IS A SPECIAL, NONLAPSING FUND THAT IS NOT
22 SUBJECT TO § 7–302 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.
23 (2) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL HOLD THE FUND, AND THE
24 COMPTROLLER SHALL ACCOUNT FOR THE FUND.
25 (E) (1) THE FUND CONSISTS OF:
26 (I) REVENUE COLLECTED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN THE FORM
27 OF DONATIONS TO THE PROGRAM;
HOUSE BILL 384 7
1 (II) MONEY APPROPRIATED IN THE STATE BUDGET TO THE
2 FUND; AND
3 (III) ANY OTHER MONEY FROM ANY OTHER SOURCE ACCEPTED
4 FOR THE BENEFIT OF THE FUND.
5 (2) FOR EACH FISCAL YEAR, THE GOVERNOR SHALL INCLUDE IN THE
6 ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION OF $25,000 TO THE FUND.
7 (2) (3) EXPENDITURES FROM THE FUND MAY BE MADE ONLY IN
8 ACCORDANCE WITH THE STATE BUDGET.
9 (3) (4) MONEY EXPENDED FROM THE FUND IS SUPPLEMENTAL TO
10 AND IS NOT INTENDED TO TAKE THE PLACE OF FUNDING THAT OTHERWISE WOULD
11 BE APPROPRIATED FOR THE PROGRAM.
12 (F) THE FUND MAY BE USED ONLY TO PAY:
13 (1) A NONPROFIT TRAINING ENTITY; AND
14 (2) ADMINISTRATIVE COSTS OF THE PROGRAM.
15 (G) (1) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL INVEST AND REINVEST THE
16 MONEY OF THE FUND IN THE SAME MANNER AS OTHER STATE MONEY MAY BE
17 INVESTED.
18 (2) ANY INVESTMENT EARNINGS OF THE FUND SHALL BE CREDITED
19 TO THE GENERAL FUND OF THE STATE.
20 (H) FOR THE PURPOSE OF IMPLEMENTING THIS SECTION, THE
21 DEPARTMENT MAY ACCEPT GIFTS OR GRANTS FOR DONATION TO THE FUND.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
23 October 1, 2025:
24 (a) This Act is contingent on the Department of Disabilities certifying that the
25 Department has sufficient staff to administer the Maryland Disability Service Animal
26 Program under Title 7, Subtitle 12 of the Human Services Article, as enacted by Section 1
27 of this Act.
28 (b) On or before October 1, 2026, the Department of Disabilities shall notify the
29 Department of Legislative Services whether the Department has certified that the
30 Department has sufficient staff to administer the Maryland Disability Service Animal
31 Program.
8 HOUSE BILL 384
1 (c) (1) If the Department of Legislative Services receives notification that the
2 Department of Disabilities has sufficient staff to administer the Maryland Disability
3 Service Animal Program on or before October 1, 2026, this Act shall take effect on the date
4 the notice is received by the Department of Legislative Services in accordance with
5 subsection (b) of this section.
6 (2) If the Department of Legislative Services receives notice that the
7 Department of Disabilities lacks sufficient staff to administer the Maryland Disability
8 Service Animal Program on or before October 1, 2026, this Act, with no further action
9 required by the General Assembly, shall be null and void.
