CF SB 477
By: Delegates Reilly, Arentz, Ghrist, Griffith, and Jacobs
Introduced and read first time: February 6, 2025
Assigned to: Environment and Transportation
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Port of Deposit State Historical Park – Size and Scope
3 FOR the purpose of altering the size and scope of the Port of Deposit State Historical Park;
4 extending the deadline for the Bainbridge Development Corporation to transfer
5 certain property to the Department of Natural Resources; altering the purpose for
6 which a certain appropriation may be used; and generally relating to the Port of
7 Deposit State Historical Park.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Natural Resources
10 Section 5–1012(g)
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Chapter 39 of the Acts of the General Assembly of 2022, as amended by Chapter 602
15 of the Acts of the General Assembly of 2023
16 Section 5
17 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
18 Chapter 602 of the Acts of the General Assembly of 2023
19 Section 2
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
22 Article – Natural Resources
23 5–1012.

2 HOUSE BILL 1184
1 (g) (1) (i) The Department shall establish the Port of Deposit State
2 Historical Park as a partnership park that seeks to educate the public about and preserve
3 and interpret the lives and experiences of Black Americans both before and after the
4 abolition of slavery.
5 (ii) Port of Deposit State Historical Park shall be held by the State
6 as a State park under the protection and administration of the Department.
7 (2) Port of Deposit State Historical Park shall consist of a portion of the
8 Bainbridge Naval Training Center site, measuring not less than [120] 12 acres and not
9 more than [150] 19 acres, that the Bainbridge Development Corporation transfers to the
10 Department, including:
11 (i) [The historic Tome School;
12 (ii)] The Snow Hill archaeological site; and
13 [(iii)] (II) The adjacent wooded conservation area.
14 (3) (i) The Department shall establish a stakeholder advisory
15 committee to advise the Department on the scope and management of Port of Deposit State
16 Historical Park.
17 (ii) The stakeholder advisory committee shall consist of the following
18 members:
19 1. One member designated by the Mayor and Town Council
20 of Port Deposit;
21 2. Two members of the House of Delegates and one member
22 of the Senate of Maryland representing Cecil County, designated by the Cecil County
23 Delegation of the General Assembly;
24 3. The County Executive of Cecil County, or the County
25 Executive’s designee;
26 4. The President of the Cecil County Council, or the
27 President’s designee;
28 5. The President of the Cecil County branch of the NAACP,
29 or the President’s designee;
30 6. The President of the Historical Society of Cecil County, or
31 the President’s designee;
32 7. The Director of the Cecil County Department of Parks and
33 Recreation;
HOUSE BILL 1184 3
1 8. The Chair of the Bainbridge Development Corporation, or
2 the Chair’s designee;
3 9. The Chief Archaeologist of the Maryland Department of
4 Transportation, State Highway Administration, or the Chief Archaeologist’s designee; and
5 10. The following nonvoting ex officio members:
6 A. The Secretary, or the Secretary’s designee;
7 B. The Secretary of Commerce, or the Secretary’s designee;
8 C. The Secretary of the Environment, or the Secretary’s
9 designee;
10 D. The Secretary of Planning, or the Secretary’s designee;
11 E. The Director of the Maryland Historical Trust, or the
12 Director’s designee;
13 F. The President of the Lower Susquehanna Heritage
14 Greenway, or the President’s designee;
15 G. The Superintendent of Cecil County Public Schools;
16 H. The Executive Director of the Cecil County Public Library;
17 and
18 I. The President of Cecil College.
19 (iii) To the extent practicable, the stakeholder advisory committee
20 shall reflect the diversity of Cecil County.
21 (4) On or before September 1, 2024, the Department shall submit a report
22 to the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article on:
23 (i) Property that is suitable for inclusion in Port of Deposit State
24 Historical Park;
25 (ii) The funding needed to establish and promote Port of Deposit
26 State Historical Park; and
27 (iii) The appropriate manner of preserving property that is not
28 identified for inclusion in Port of Deposit State Historical Park in perpetuity for its
29 archaeological, historical, and cultural significance.
4 HOUSE BILL 1184
1 Chapter 39 of the Acts of 2022, as amended by Chapter 602 of the Acts of 2023
2 SECTION 5. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
3 (a) On or before June 1, [2025] 2026, the Bainbridge Development Corporation
4 shall transfer to the Department of Natural Resources a portion of the Bainbridge Naval
5 Training Center site consisting of not less than [120] 12 acres and not more than [150] 19
6 acres, including:
7 (1) [the historic Tome School;
8 (2)] the Snow Hill archaeological site; and
9 [(3)] (2) the adjacent wooded conservation area.
10 (b) Any property transferred to the Department of Natural Resources under this
11 section that is not included in Port of Deposit State Historical Park shall be preserved in
12 perpetuity for its archaeological, historical, and cultural significance, in accordance with
13 the recommendations of the stakeholder advisory committee established under § 5–1012(g)
14 of the Natural Resources Article.
15 Chapter 602 of the Acts of 2023
16 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That for fiscal year 2025, the
17 Governor may include in the annual State budget an appropriation of at least $400,000 to
18 the Department of Natural Resources to conduct a Phase 1 Archaeological Survey and Soil
19 Study for the [Tome School and] Snow Hill [sites] SITE.
20 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act is an emergency
21 measure, is necessary for the immediate preservation of the public health or safety, has
22 been passed by a yea and nay vote supported by three–fifths of all the members elected to
23 each of the two Houses of the General Assembly, and shall take effect from the date it is
24 enacted.
