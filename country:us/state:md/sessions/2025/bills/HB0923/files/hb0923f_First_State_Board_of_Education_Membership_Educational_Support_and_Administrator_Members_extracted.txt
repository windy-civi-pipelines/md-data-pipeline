A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Board of Education – Membership – Educational Support and
3 Administrator Members
4 FOR the purpose of altering the membership of the State Board of Education to include an
5 educational support member and an administrator member; providing for the
6 qualifications, terms, powers, and elections of the new members; and generally
7 relating to the membership of the State Board of Education.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Education
10 Section 2–202
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Education
16 2–202.
17 (a) The State Board consists of 13 regular members, and 1 student member,
18 appointed by the Governor with the advice and consent of the Senate.
19 (b) (1) In making appointments to the State Board, the Governor shall
20 consider representation from:
21 (i) All parts of this State; and

1 (ii) Areas of this State with concentrations of population or unique
2 needs.
3 (2) Except as provided in paragraphs (4) through [(6)] (8) of this
4 subsection, the members of the Board shall be appointed from the general public.
5 (3) The following individuals may not be appointed to the Board:
6 (i) Except for the teacher member, EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT
7 MEMBER, ADMINISTRATOR MEMBER, and student member, any individual who is subject
8 to the authority of the Board;
9 (ii) The Governor; and
10 (iii) The State Superintendent.
11 (4) (i) Of the 13 regular members of the State Board, one regular
12 member shall be a certified teacher who is actively teaching.
13 (ii) The Governor shall appoint the teacher member, with the advice
14 and consent of the Senate, who received the highest number of votes after an election by
15 teachers in the State.
16 (iii) The Department shall provide notice of a teacher member
17 vacancy on the State Board to:
18 1. All certified teachers who are actively teaching in the
19 State; and
20 2. All teachers’ organizations representing teachers in the
21 State for purposes of collective bargaining.
22 (iv) 1. The election shall be conducted under regulations that the
23 Department adopts.
24 2. The Department may consult with the State Retirement
25 Agency of the Maryland State Retirement and Pension System to conduct the election
26 required under this subparagraph.
27 (v) The teacher member may attend and participate in an executive
28 session of the State Board.
29 (vi) The teacher member may not vote on any matter that relates to
30 appeals to the State Board under § 6–202 of this article.
31 (5) (I) OF THE 13 REGULAR MEMBERS OF THE STATE BOARD, ONE
32 REGULAR MEMBER SHALL BE AN EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT PROFESSIONAL WHO IS
HOUSE BILL 923 3
1 ACTIVELY WORKING IN A PUBLIC SCHOOL AS A NONCERTIFICATED,
2 NONSUPERVISORY PUBLIC SCHOOL EMPLOYEE DESIGNATED AS PART OF A
3 BARGAINING UNIT UNDER TITLE 6, SUBTITLE 5 OF THIS ARTICLE.
4 (II) THE GOVERNOR SHALL APPOINT THE EDUCATIONAL
5 SUPPORT MEMBER, WITH THE ADVICE AND CONSENT OF THE SENATE, WHO
6 RECEIVED THE HIGHEST NUMBER OF VOTES AFTER AN ELECTION BY EDUCATIONAL
7 SUPPORT PROFESSIONALS IN THE STATE.
8 (III) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE NOTICE OF AN
9 EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT MEMBER VACANCY ON THE STATE BOARD TO:
10 1. ALL EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT PROFESSIONALS WHO
11 ARE ACTIVELY WORKING IN A PUBLIC SCHOOL AS A NONCERTIFICATED,
12 NONSUPERVISORY PUBLIC SCHOOL EMPLOYEE DESIGNATED AS PART OF A
13 BARGAINING UNIT UNDER TITLE 6, SUBTITLE 5 OF THIS ARTICLE IN THE STATE; AND
14 2. ALL EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT PROFESSIONALS’
15 ORGANIZATIONS REPRESENTING EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT PROFESSIONALS IN THE
16 STATE FOR PURPOSES OF COLLECTIVE BARGAINING.
17 (IV) THE ELECTION SHALL BE CONDUCTED UNDER
18 REGULATIONS THAT THE DEPARTMENT ADOPTS.
19 (V) THE EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT MEMBER MAY ATTEND AND
20 PARTICIPATE IN AN EXECUTIVE SESSION OF THE STATE BOARD.
21 (VI) THE EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT MEMBER MAY NOT VOTE ON
22 ANY MATTER THAT RELATES TO APPEALS TO THE STATE BOARD UNDER § 6–202 OF
23 THIS ARTICLE.
24 (6) (I) IN THIS PARAGRAPH, “ADMINISTRATOR” MEANS A
25 PRINCIPAL, AN ASSISTANT PRINCIPAL, OR ANY OTHER ADMINISTRATIVE LEVEL
26 PROFESSIONAL WHO IS ACTIVELY WORKING IN A PUBLIC SCHOOL.
27 (II) OF THE 13 REGULAR MEMBERS OF THE STATE BOARD, ONE
28 REGULAR MEMBER SHALL BE AN ADMINISTRATOR.
29 (III) THE GOVERNOR SHALL APPOINT THE ADMINISTRATOR
30 MEMBER, WITH THE ADVICE AND CONSENT OF THE SENATE, WHO RECEIVED THE
31 HIGHEST NUMBER OF VOTES AFTER AN ELECTION BY ADMINISTRATORS IN THE
32 STATE.

1 (IV) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE NOTICE OF AN
2 ADMINISTRATOR MEMBER VACANCY ON THE STATE BOARD TO:
3 1. ALL ADMINISTRATORS WHO ARE ACTIVELY WORKING
4 IN A PUBLIC SCHOOL; AND
5 2. ALL ADMINISTRATORS’ ORGANIZATIONS

7 BARGAINING.

9 REGULATIONS THAT THE DEPARTMENT ADOPTS.

15 [(5)] (7) (i) Of the 13 regular members of the State Board, one regular
16 member shall be the parent of a student enrolled in a public school in the State.
17 (ii) The Governor shall appoint the parent member, with the advice
18 and consent of the Senate, from a list of three qualified individuals submitted to the
19 Governor by the Maryland PTA.
20 (iii) The Department shall provide notice of the parent member
21 vacancy on the State Board to the Maryland PTA.
22 (iv) The parent member may attend and participate in an executive
23 session of the State Board.
24 [(6)] (8) The student member shall be selected by the Governor from a list
25 of 2 persons nominated by the Maryland Association of Student Councils.
26 (c) (1) The student member shall be:
27 (i) A regularly enrolled student; and
28 (ii) In good standing in a public high school in the State.
29 (2) The student member may attend and participate in an executive
30 session of the Board.

1 (3) The student member may not vote on any matter that relates to:
2 (i) The dismissal of or other disciplinary action involving personnel;
3 or
4 (ii) Appeals to the State Board under § 2–205 of this subtitle or §
5 4–205 or § 6–202 of this article.
6 (d) (1) Each regular member serves for a term of 4 years and until a successor
7 is appointed and qualifies. These terms are staggered as required by the terms of the
8 members serving on the State Board as of July 1, 1989.
9 (2) The Governor shall appoint a new member to fill any vacancy on the
10 Board for the remainder of that term and until a successor is appointed and qualifies.
11 (3) A member is eligible for reappointment but may not serve for more than
12 two full 4–year terms.
13 (4) The student member shall serve for a term of 1 year. A student member
14 is eligible for reappointment but may not serve more than two full 1–year terms.
15 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
16 1, 2025.
