A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Service Commission – Transmission Line Siting – Limitations
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting the Public Service Commission from authorizing, and a
4 person from undertaking, the construction of an overhead transmission line under
5 certain circumstances; and generally relating to overhead transmission lines.
6 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
7 Article – Public Utilities
8 Section 7–207(b)(1)(i)
9 Annotated Code of Maryland
10 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Public Utilities
13 Section 7–207(h)
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
16 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
17 Article – Public Utilities
18 Section 7–207(h)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
21 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
22 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
23 Article – Public Utilities
24 7–207.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 SENATE BILL 853
1 (b) (1) (i) Unless a certificate of public convenience and necessity for the
2 construction is first obtained from the Commission, a person may not begin construction in
3 the State of:
4 1. a generating station; or
5 2. a qualified generator lead line.
6 (H) (1) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, THE
7 COMMISSION MAY NOT AUTHORIZE, AND A PERSON MAY NOT UNDERTAKE, THE
8 CONSTRUCTION OF AN OVERHEAD TRANSMISSION LINE THAT DOES NOT UTILIZE AN
9 EXISTING TRANSMISSION LINE RIGHT–OF–WAY UNLESS THE APPLICANT
10 ADEQUATELY DEMONSTRATES TO THE COMMISSION THAT EXISTING TRANSMISSION
11 LINE RIGHTS–OF–WAY ARE NOT SUFFICIENT FOR THE PROPOSED OVERHEAD
12 TRANSMISSION LINE.
13 (2) IF THE COMMISSION DETERMINES THAT AN EXISTING
14 TRANSMISSION LINE RIGHT–OF–WAY IS NOT SUFFICIENT FOR A PROPOSED
15 OVERHEAD TRANSMISSION LINE, THE COMMISSION MAY AUTHORIZE
16 CONSTRUCTION OF A NEW OVERHEAD TRANSMISSION LINE WITHIN 0.25 MILES OF
17 AN EXISTING OVERHEAD TRANSMISSION LINE.
18 [(h)] (I) (1) A county or municipal corporation has the authority to approve
19 or deny any local permit required under a certificate of public convenience and necessity
20 issued under this section.
21 (2) A county or municipal corporation shall approve or deny any local
22 permits required under a certificate of public convenience and necessity issued under this
23 section:
24 (i) within a reasonable time; and
25 (ii) to the extent local laws are not preempted by State law, in
26 accordance with local laws.
27 (3) A county or municipal corporation may not condition the approval of a
28 local permit required under a certificate of public convenience and necessity issued under
29 this section on receipt of any of the following approvals for any aspect of a generating
30 station, an overhead transmission line, or a qualified lead line proposed to be constructed
31 under the certificate:
32 (i) a conditional use approval;
33 (ii) a special exception approval; or
34 (iii) a floating zone approval.
SENATE BILL 853 3
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
2 1, 2025.
