Section 1:
J1, O1, P1 5lr3309
By: Delegates Grammer, Arikan, Beauchamp, Chisholm, Fisher, M. Morgan,
Nawrocki, and Szeliga
Introduced and read first time: February 7, 2025
Assigned to: Appropriations and Health and Government Operations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Department of Health and Department of Human Services – Public
3 Benefits – Prevention of Waste, Fraud, and Abuse
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Health and the Department of
5 Human Services to enter into certain data–sharing agreements with certain agencies
6 to conduct certain reviews related to eligibility for and use of certain public benefits;
7 requiring the Maryland Department of Health to seek approval for a certain waiver
8 to impose a certain lockout period on certain individuals who fail to report a change
9 in eligibility for the Maryland Medical Assistance Program; requiring the
10 Department of Human Services, in consultation with law enforcement agencies, to
11 conduct annual inspections of certain retail facilities; and generally relating to the
12 review of public assistance programs.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
14 Article – Health – General
15 Section 15–101(a), (h), and (i)
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 BY adding to
19 Article – Health – General
20 Section 15–123.1
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
23 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
24 Article – Human Services
25 Section 5–501
26 Annotated Code of Maryland
27 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Health – General
4 15–101.
5 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
6 (h) “Program” means the Maryland Medical Assistance Program.
7 (i) “Program recipient” means an individual who receives benefits under the
8 Program.
9 15–123.1.
10 (A) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ESTABLISH MEMORANDUMS OF
11 UNDERSTANDING WITH FEDERAL AND STATE AGENCIES TO FACILITATE
12 DATA–SHARING IN ORDER TO CONDUCT:
13 (1) MONTHLY DEATH RECORD REVIEWS, USING THE VITAL
14 STATISTICS ADMINISTRATION DATABASE TO IDENTIFY DECEASED INDIVIDUALS
15 WHO ARE PROGRAM RECIPIENTS;
16 (2) QUARTERLY REVIEWS, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE MARYLAND
17 DEPARTMENT OF LABOR, TO DETERMINE THE EFFECT OF EMPLOYMENT OR WAGE
18 CHANGES ON THE CONTINUING ELIGIBILITY OF PROGRAM RECIPIENTS;
19 (3) QUARTERLY REVIEWS OF TAX FILINGS FROM THE COMPTROLLER
20 TO VERIFY INCOME AND RESIDENCY CHANGES OF PROGRAM RECIPIENTS;
21 (4) NOTWITHSTANDING § 15–113 OF THIS SUBTITLE, MONTHLY
22 REVIEWS OF INCARCERATED INDIVIDUALS, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE
23 DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC SAFETY AND CORRECTIONAL SERVICES, TO ENSURE
24 THAT INCARCERATED INDIVIDUALS ARE NOT RECEIVING BENEFITS UNDER THE
25 PROGRAM; AND
26 (5) TWICE MONTHLY REVIEWS OF UNEMPLOYMENT INSURANCE
27 BENEFITS RECORDS, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF
28 LABOR, TO VERIFY CHANGES IN EMPLOYMENT OR INCOME THAT MIGHT AFFECT THE
29 CONTINUING ELIGIBILITY OF PROGRAM RECIPIENTS USING:
30 (I) SOCIAL SECURITY ADMINISTRATION EARNINGS AND DEATH
31 RECORDS;

1 (II) U.S. DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
2 NATIONAL DIRECTORY OF NEW HIRES;
3 (III) U.S. DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES
4 CHILD SUPPORT ENFORCEMENT RECORDS;
5 (IV) U.S. DEPARTMENT OF HOUSING AND URBAN
6 DEVELOPMENT PAYMENT AND EARNINGS DATA; AND
7 (V) FEDERAL BUREAU OF INVESTIGATION NATIONAL INSTANT
8 CRIMINAL BACKGROUND CHECK SYSTEM RECORDS OF FUGITIVES FROM JUSTICE.
9 (B) WHEN DETERMINING PROGRAM ELIGIBILITY, THE DEPARTMENT MAY
10 NOT ACCEPT SELF–ATTESTATION OF INCOME, RESIDENCY, HOUSEHOLD
11 COMPOSITION, OR OTHER ELIGIBILITY FACTORS, UNLESS REQUIRED BY FEDERAL
12 LAW.
13 (C) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL SEEK APPROVAL FROM THE CENTERS FOR
14 MEDICARE AND MEDICAID SERVICES FOR A § 1115 WAIVER TO IMPOSE A 6–MONTH
15 LOCKOUT PERIOD FOR A PROGRAM RECIPIENT WHO:
16 (1) IS AT LEAST 19 YEARS OLD AND UNDER THE AGE OF 65 YEARS;
17 (2) FAILS TO REPORT A CHANGE IN ELIGIBILITY; AND
18 (3) IS NOT:
19 (I) DESIGNATED AS DISABLED BY A GOVERNMENT ENTITY; OR
20 (II) PREGNANT.
21 (D) THE DEPARTMENT AND THE STATE LOTTERY AND GAMING CONTROL
22 AGENCY SHALL ENTER INTO AN AGREEMENT TO IDENTIFY THE NAMES OF EACH
23 PROGRAM RECIPIENT WHO HAS WON A PRIZE OF $3,000 OR MORE.
24 (E) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PUBLISH ON ITS WEBSITE QUARTERLY
25 REPORTS ON INVESTIGATIONS AND COMPLIANCE FINDINGS RELATING TO PROGRAM
26 FRAUD, INCLUDING:
27 (1) THE NUMBER OF CASES INVESTIGATED;
28 (2) THE NUMBER OF CASES REFERRED FOR PROSECUTION; AND
4 HOUSE BILL 1402
1 (3) THE PERCENTAGE OF INELIGIBLE PROGRAM RECIPIENTS
2 IDENTIFIED.
3 (F) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT THIS
4 SECTION.
5 Article – Human Services
6 5–501.
7 (a) (1) The Department may implement a Supplemental Nutrition Assistance
8 Program in accordance with the federal Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program.
9 (2) The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program shall include:
10 (i) a Restaurant Meals Program in accordance with § 5–505 of this
11 subtitle; and
12 (ii) a Heat and Eat Program in accordance with § 5–506 of this
13 subtitle.
14 (b) The State shall bear the nonfederal portion of the administrative costs of the
15 Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program for each county.
16 (c) Each local department shall administer the Supplemental Nutrition
17 Assistance Program:
18 (1) under the supervision and control of the Department; and
19 (2) in accordance with the regulations of the Department and federal law.
20 (d) If a household includes an individual who is at least 60 years old and receives
21 a federally funded benefit in an amount less than $50 per month under the Supplemental
22 Nutrition Assistance Program, the State shall provide a supplement to increase the total
23 benefit to $50 per month.
