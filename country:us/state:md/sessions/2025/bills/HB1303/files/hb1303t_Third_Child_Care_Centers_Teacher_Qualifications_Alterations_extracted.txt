1 AN ACT concerning
2 Child Care Centers – Teacher Qualifications – Alterations
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the State Board of Education to set minimal experiential
4 standards as qualifications for child care teachers teaching in a child care center;
5 altering the probationary employment qualifications for a child care teacher
6 employed in a child care center; provide a pathway for certain individuals to attest
7 to qualifying educational credentials to be a child care teacher teaching in a child
8 care center; and generally relating to the qualifications of a child care teacher.

2 HOUSE BILL 1303
1 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
2 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
3 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
4 Article – Education
5 9.5–404.
6 (a) The State Board shall adopt rules and regulations for licensing and operating
7 child care centers.
8 (b) These rules and regulations shall:
9 (13) Subject to subsection [(c)] (D) of this section, establish probationary
10 employment qualifications for an individual who is applying for the first time to be a child
11 care teacher in a child care center in the State that serves preschool or school–age children
12 who are at least 3 years old.
13 (C) (1) THESE RULES AND REGULATIONS SHALL SET MINIMAL
14 EXPERIENTIAL REQUIREMENTS FOR INDIVIDUALS TO QUALIFY AS A CHILD CARE
15 TEACHER THAT REQUIRE A CHILD CARE TEACHER TO:
16 (1) HOLD A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA;
17 (2) HOLD A CERTIFICATE OF HIGH SCHOOL EQUIVALENCY;
18 (3) HAVE SUCCESSFULLY COMPLETED COURSES FOR CREDIT AT AN
19 ACCREDITED INSTITUTION OF HIGHER EDUCATION THAT ARE APPROVED BY THE
20 STATE BOARD AS SUFFICIENT TO QUALIFY AS A PROSPECTIVE CHILD CARE
21 TEACHER;
22 (4) HAVE SUCCESSFULLY BEEN EMPLOYED FOR 2 YEARS IN A
23 POSITION OTHER THAN A CHILD CARE TEACHER FOR A REGISTERED FAMILY CHILD
24 CARE HOME, A REGISTERED LARGE FAMILY CHILD CARE HOME, OR A LICENSED
25 CHILD CARE CENTER; OR
26 (5) MEET THE PROBATIONARY EMPLOYMENT QUALIFICATIONS
27 UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION PROVIDE A PATHWAY FOR AN INDIVIDUAL
28 WHO IS QUALIFIED TO BE A CHILD CARE TEACHER, BUT LACKS PROOF OF
29 NECESSARY EDUCATIONAL ATTAINMENT, TO ATTEST TO THE QUALIFYING
30 EDUCATIONAL CREDENTIALS HELD BY THE INDIVIDUAL.
31 (2) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ESTABLISH A PROCESS TO CARRY OUT
32 THIS SUBSECTION.
HOUSE BILL 1303 3
1 [(c)] (D) (1) The probationary employment qualifications established under
2 subsection (b)(13) of this section shall allow an individual to be employed as a child care
3 teacher during a probationary period if the individual:
4 (i) [1.] Is enrolled in approved pre–service training;
5 [2.] (II) Successfully completes the required 90 hours of the
6 approved pre–service training within 6 months after being hired; and
7 [3. Holds an associate degree or a bachelor’s degree in:
8 A. Early childhood education;
9 B. Elementary education;
10 C. Child development;
11 D. Home economics;
12 E. Nursing;
13 F. Social work;
14 G. Special education; or
15 H. A related field approved by the Department; or
16 (ii) 1. Is enrolled in approved pre–service training;
17 2. Has successfully completed at least 45 hours of the
18 approved pre–service training at the time the child care center hires the individual;
19 3. Successfully completes the remaining hours of the
20 pre–service training within 6 months after being hired; and
21 4. Holds an associate degree or a bachelor’s degree in a field
22 other than a field listed under item (i)3 of this paragraph.]
23 (III) EARNS A CERTIFICATE OF HIGH SCHOOL EQUIVALENCY
24 WITHIN 2 YEARS AFTER BEING HIRED.
25 (2) [If, at the end of the 6–month probationary period,] A CHILD CARE
26 CENTER SHALL TERMINATE FOR CAUSE OR REASSIGN TO A DIFFERENT
27 NONTEACHING POSITION an individual described in paragraph (1) of this subsection [has
4 HOUSE BILL 1303
1 not completed the required pre–service training, the child care center shall, with no further
2 cause, terminate the individual or reassign the individual to a nonteaching position] WHO:
3 (I) AT THE END OF 6 MONTHS AFTER BEING HIRED, HAS NOT
4 COMPLETED THE REQUIRED PRE–SERVICE TRAINING; OR
5 (II) AT THE END OF 2 YEARS AFTER BEING HIRED, HAS NOT
6 EARNED A CERTIFICATE OF HIGH SCHOOL EQUIVALENCY.
7 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
8 1, 2025.
