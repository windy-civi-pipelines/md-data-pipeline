1 AN ACT concerning
2 University of Maryland, College Park Campus – TerpsEXCEED Program –
3 Funding
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the Governor to include in the annual budget bill,
5 beginning in a certain fiscal year, a certain appropriation to the University of
6 Maryland, College Park Campus TerpsEXCEED (EXperiencing College through
7 Education and Employment Discovery) Program to provide educational and
8 employment opportunities to students with intellectual disabilities; and generally
9 relating to funding for the TerpsEXCEED Program.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Education
12 Section 13–107
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – Education
18 13–107.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.

amendment. *sb0603*
2 SENATE BILL 603
1 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
2 INDICATED.
3 (2) “PROGRAM” OR “TERPSEXCEED PROGRAM” MEANS THE
4 UNIVERSITY OF MARYLAND, COLLEGE PARK CAMPUS TERPSEXCEED
5 (EXPERIENCING COLLEGE THROUGH EDUCATION AND EMPLOYMENT DISCOVERY)
6 PROGRAM.
7 (3) “UNIVERSITY” MEANS THE UNIVERSITY OF MARYLAND,
8 COLLEGE PARK CAMPUS.
9 (B) FOR FISCAL YEAR 2027 AND EACH FISCAL YEAR THEREAFTER, THE
10 GOVERNOR MAY INCLUDE IN THE ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION OF
11 $350,000 FOR THE TERPSEXCEED PROGRAM AT THE UNIVERSITY.
12 (C) (1) THE FUNDING PROVIDED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS
13 SECTION MAY SERVE AS A SUSTAINABLE FUNDING SOURCE FOR THE MANAGEMENT
14 OF THE TERPSEXCEED PROGRAM TO PROVIDE EDUCATIONAL AND EMPLOYMENT
15 OPPORTUNITIES TO STUDENTS WITH INTELLECTUAL DISABILITIES AND TO ENABLE
16 THE PROGRAM’S FUTURE EXPANSION.
17 (2) THE FUNDING PROVIDED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS
18 SECTION MAY BE USED FOR:
19 (I) ADVISING AND COUNSELING STUDENTS IN THE PROGRAM
20 TO ASSIST WITH SCHEDULING AND REGISTRATION FOR CLASSES, OUTREACH TO
21 PROFESSORS, DESIGNING COURSE MODIFICATIONS, PROVIDING IN–CLASS SUPPORT
22 AND ACCOMMODATIONS, AND OFFERING MENTOR TUTORING;
23 (II) ADVISING AND COUNSELING STUDENTS IN THE PROGRAM
24 TO ASSIST WITH CALENDAR MANAGEMENT;
25 (III) ADVISING AND COUNSELING STUDENTS IN THE PROGRAM
26 TO FACILITATE STUDENT CLUB AND ACTIVITY CHOICES;
27 (IV) TRAINING AND COMMUNICATING WITH RESIDENT
28 ASSISTANTS AND RESIDENT DIRECTORS IN THE PROGRAM;
29 (V) OFFERING COURSES IN PEER MENTORSHIP IN THE
30 PROGRAM;
31 (VI) ADVISING PEER MENTORS IN THE PROGRAM;
SENATE BILL 603 3
1 (VII) OFFERING COURSES IN CAREER DEVELOPMENT IN THE
2 PROGRAM;
3 (VIII) DEVELOPING INTERNSHIP AND JOB OPPORTUNITIES ON
4 AND OFF CAMPUS, GATHERING SUPERVISOR FEEDBACK, AND PROVIDING DIRECT
5 EMPLOYMENT SUPPORT, INCLUDING GOAL SETTING AND CAREER PLANNING FOR
6 EACH STUDENT IN THE PROGRAM;
7 (IX) CONNECTING STUDENTS IN THE PROGRAM WITH STATE
8 AGENCIES THAT SUPPORT INDIVIDUALS WITH DISABILITIES;
9 (X) MANAGING THE USE OF SELF–DIRECTED WAIVER BUDGETS
10 FOR ON–CAMPUS SUPPORTS IN THE PROGRAM;
11 (XI) MANAGING FINANCIAL AID SYSTEMS FOR THE PROGRAM;
12 (XII) SHARING RESOURCES NEEDED FOR POST–PROGRAM
13 SUPPORT; AND
14 (XIII) FACILITATING A CROSS–AGENCY, INTEGRATED RESOURCE
15 TEAM FOR EACH STUDENT IN THE PROGRAM AND ANY OTHER CUSTOMIZED
16 PROGRAMMING OR DIRECT COUNSELING THAT SUPPORTS THE WELL–BEING OF
17 EACH STUDENT IN THE PROGRAM AND FOSTERS SPECIFIC OPPORTUNITIES FOR
18 SELF–DETERMINATION.
19 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
20 1, 2025.
Approved:
