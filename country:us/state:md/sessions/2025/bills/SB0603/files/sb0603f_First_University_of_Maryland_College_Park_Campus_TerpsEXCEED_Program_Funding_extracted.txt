A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 University of Maryland, College Park Campus – TerpsEXCEED Program –
3 Funding
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the Governor to include in the annual budget bill,
5 beginning in a certain fiscal year, a certain appropriation to the University of
6 Maryland, College Park Campus TerpsEXCEED (EXperiencing College through
7 Education and Employment Discovery) Program to provide educational and
8 employment opportunities to students with intellectual disabilities; and generally
9 relating to funding for the TerpsEXCEED Program.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Education
12 Section 13–107
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – Education
18 13–107.
19 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
20 INDICATED.
21 (2) “PROGRAM” OR “TERPSEXCEED PROGRAM” MEANS THE
22 UNIVERSITY OF MARYLAND, COLLEGE PARK CAMPUS TERPSEXCEED
23 (EXPERIENCING COLLEGE THROUGH EDUCATION AND EMPLOYMENT DISCOVERY)
24 PROGRAM.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0603*
2 SENATE BILL 603
1 (3) “UNIVERSITY” MEANS THE UNIVERSITY OF MARYLAND,
2 COLLEGE PARK CAMPUS.
3 (B) FOR FISCAL YEAR 2027 AND EACH FISCAL YEAR THEREAFTER, THE
4 GOVERNOR MAY INCLUDE IN THE ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION OF
5 $350,000 FOR THE TERPSEXCEED PROGRAM AT THE UNIVERSITY.
6 (C) (1) THE FUNDING PROVIDED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS
7 SECTION MAY SERVE AS A SUSTAINABLE FUNDING SOURCE FOR THE MANAGEMENT
8 OF THE TERPSEXCEED PROGRAM TO PROVIDE EDUCATIONAL AND EMPLOYMENT
9 OPPORTUNITIES TO STUDENTS WITH INTELLECTUAL DISABILITIES AND TO ENABLE
10 THE PROGRAM’S FUTURE EXPANSION.
11 (2) THE FUNDING PROVIDED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS
12 SECTION MAY BE USED FOR:
13 (I) ADVISING AND COUNSELING STUDENTS IN THE PROGRAM
14 TO ASSIST WITH SCHEDULING AND REGISTRATION FOR CLASSES, OUTREACH TO
15 PROFESSORS, DESIGNING COURSE MODIFICATIONS, PROVIDING IN–CLASS SUPPORT
16 AND ACCOMMODATIONS, AND OFFERING MENTOR TUTORING;
17 (II) ADVISING AND COUNSELING STUDENTS IN THE PROGRAM
18 TO ASSIST WITH CALENDAR MANAGEMENT;
19 (III) ADVISING AND COUNSELING STUDENTS IN THE PROGRAM
20 TO FACILITATE STUDENT CLUB AND ACTIVITY CHOICES;
21 (IV) TRAINING AND COMMUNICATING WITH RESIDENT
22 ASSISTANTS AND RESIDENT DIRECTORS IN THE PROGRAM;
23 (V) OFFERING COURSES IN PEER MENTORSHIP IN THE
24 PROGRAM;
25 (VI) ADVISING PEER MENTORS IN THE PROGRAM;
26 (VII) OFFERING COURSES IN CAREER DEVELOPMENT IN THE
27 PROGRAM;
28 (VIII) DEVELOPING INTERNSHIP AND JOB OPPORTUNITIES ON
29 AND OFF CAMPUS, GATHERING SUPERVISOR FEEDBACK, AND PROVIDING DIRECT
30 EMPLOYMENT SUPPORT, INCLUDING GOAL SETTING AND CAREER PLANNING FOR
31 EACH STUDENT IN THE PROGRAM;
SENATE BILL 603 3
1 (IX) CONNECTING STUDENTS IN THE PROGRAM WITH STATE
2 AGENCIES THAT SUPPORT INDIVIDUALS WITH DISABILITIES;
3 (X) MANAGING THE USE OF SELF–DIRECTED WAIVER BUDGETS
4 FOR ON–CAMPUS SUPPORTS IN THE PROGRAM;
5 (XI) MANAGING FINANCIAL AID SYSTEMS FOR THE PROGRAM;
6 (XII) SHARING RESOURCES NEEDED FOR POST–PROGRAM
7 SUPPORT; AND
8 (XIII) FACILITATING A CROSS–AGENCY, INTEGRATED RESOURCE
9 TEAM FOR EACH STUDENT IN THE PROGRAM AND ANY OTHER CUSTOMIZED
10 PROGRAMMING OR DIRECT COUNSELING THAT SUPPORTS THE WELL–BEING OF
11 EACH STUDENT IN THE PROGRAM AND FOSTERS SPECIFIC OPPORTUNITIES FOR
12 SELF–DETERMINATION.
13 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
14 1, 2025.
