A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Transportation Authority – Tolls, Fees, and Other Charges
3 – Temporary Adjustments
4 FOR the purpose of increasing the maximum duration of an emergency status
5 determination authorizing the temporary adjustment of tolls, fees, or other charges
6 by the Maryland Transportation Authority; and generally relating to temporary
7 adjustment of tolls, fees, and other charges by the Maryland Transportation
8 Authority.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
10 Article – Transportation
11 Section 4–312(a)
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
16 Article – Transportation
17 4–312.
18 (a) (1) Notwithstanding the provisions of Section 3, § 20 and Section 4, § 16 of
19 Chapter 608 of the Acts of the General Assembly of 1976, tolls may continue to be charged
20 on the John F. Kennedy Memorial Highway and any project constructed under the
21 provisions of Section 3 (Bridge, Tunnel, and Motorway Revenue Bonds) of Chapter 608 of
22 the Acts of the General Assembly of 1976.
23 (2) As to all or any part of any transportation facilities project, the
24 Authority may:

1 (i) Fix, revise, charge, and collect rentals, rates, fees, tolls, and other
2 charges and revenues for its use or for its services; and
3 (ii) Contract with any person who desires its use for any purpose and
4 fix the terms, conditions, rentals, rates, fees, tolls, or other charges or revenues for this use.
5 (3) (i) Before the Authority adopts an increase in tolls, fees, or other
6 charges on any part of a fixed toll transportation facilities project or adopts an increase in
7 mileage rate ranges, pricing periods, toll zones, fees, or other charges on a variably priced
8 toll transportation facilities project, the Authority shall provide an opportunity for public
9 review and comment on the proposed increase at one or more meetings held at a time and
10 place of convenience to the public in each county in which the increase is proposed to be
11 implemented.
12 (ii) At least 10 working days before the start of the first meeting
13 under subparagraph (i) of this paragraph, the Authority shall provide to the public on the
14 Authority’s official website the proposed increase in tolls, fees, mileage rate ranges, pricing
15 periods, toll zones, or other charges and information and studies used in its analysis to
16 justify the proposed increase.
17 (iii) For a period of at least 10 working days after the last scheduled
18 meeting for public review and comment under subparagraph (i) of this paragraph, the
19 Authority shall provide the public with an opportunity to submit additional written
20 comments on the proposal.
21 (iv) Within 10 days after the close of the written comment period
22 under subparagraph (iii) of this paragraph, the Authority shall provide to the public on its
23 official website and to the members of the Authority a summary and analysis of the
24 comments received from the public on the proposal.
25 (4) Before the Authority votes on any proposal to increase tolls, fees, or
26 other charges on any part of a fixed toll transportation facilities project or votes on an
27 increase in mileage rate ranges, pricing periods, toll zones, fees, or other charges on any
28 part of a variably priced toll transportation facilities project, the Authority shall:
29 (i) Provide in writing to all of the members of the Authority and, on
30 request, to the public any recommendation of the Authority regarding the proposal;
31 (ii) For a period of at least 10 working days after making any
32 recommendation on the proposal, provide an opportunity for public review and written
33 comment on the recommendation;
34 (iii) Provide to the public on its official website:
35 1. Any recommendation of the Authority regarding the
36 proposal; and
SENATE BILL 210 3
1 2. The time, place, and date of the meeting at which the
2 Authority will vote on the proposal; and
3 (iv) Provide to each member of the Authority and provide to the
4 public on its official website a summary and analysis of any public comments received
5 under item (ii) of this paragraph by the Authority regarding the Authority’s
6 recommendation.
7 (5) (i) At any meeting in which a recommendation to increase tolls, fees,
8 or other charges on a fixed toll transportation facilities project or a recommendation to
9 increase mileage rate ranges, pricing periods, toll zones, fees, or other charges on a variably
10 priced transportation facilities project is scheduled for consideration, the Authority shall
11 provide the public a reasonable amount of time to comment on the recommendation before
12 the Authority votes on the recommendation.
13 (ii) If the Authority amends its recommendation after receiving
14 public comment at the meeting under subparagraph (i) of this paragraph and then votes on
15 the amended recommendation at that meeting, the Authority is not required to provide an
16 additional opportunity for public comment under paragraph (4) of this subsection.
17 (6) (i) If the Authority determines that it must increase tolls, fees,
18 mileage rate ranges, pricing periods, toll zones, or other charges within a time period or in
19 a manner that will not permit compliance with paragraphs (3) and (4) of this subsection to
20 remain in compliance with the provisions of any trust agreement, escrow deposit
21 agreement, or resolution that provides for the payment of bonds issued by the Authority,
22 or to ensure that unforeseen circumstances do not adversely affect the continuity of
23 operations at one or more transportation facilities projects, the Authority shall determine
24 that an emergency status exists.
25 (ii) If the Authority determines that an emergency status exists
26 under subparagraph (i) of this paragraph, the Authority shall adopt temporary adjustments
27 to tolls, fees, mileage rate ranges, pricing periods, toll zones, or other charges that shall
28 take effect immediately on adoption by the Authority or on the effective date established
29 by the Authority.
30 (iii) If the Authority adopts any temporary adjustments to tolls, fees,
31 mileage rate ranges, pricing periods, toll zones, or other charges under subparagraph (ii) of
32 this paragraph, the Authority shall:
33 1. Provide notice to the public on the Authority’s official
34 website of any temporary adjustment adopted under subparagraph (ii) of this paragraph;
35 and
36 2. Commence the public notice and comment procedures
37 under paragraphs (3) and (4) of this subsection immediately.
4 SENATE BILL 210
1 (iv) An emergency status determination may not exceed [180] 300
2 days and may be subject to one or more additional conditions imposed by the Authority.
3 (v) When the emergency status expires, the temporary adjustments
4 adopted under subparagraph (ii) of this paragraph shall end.
5 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
6 October 1, 2025.
