A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Constitutional Amendment – Form of County Government – Requirement to
3 Adopt Charter Home Rule
4 FOR the purpose of requiring each county to adopt a charter on or before a certain date
5 using a certain procedure; altering the method by which a county shall adopt a
6 charter under certain circumstances; requiring the charter to provide for the election
7 of members of the county council by the voters of councilmanic districts; and limiting
8 the applicability of certain Constitutional provisions governing code home rule
9 counties.

33 published in the City of Baltimore or County within thirty days after it shall be reported to
34 him. Such charter shall be submitted to the voters of said City or County at the next general
35 or Congressional election after the report of said charter to said Mayor of Baltimore or
36 President of the Board of County Commissioners; and if a majority of the votes cast for and
37 against the adoption of said charter shall be in favor of such adoption, the said charter from
38 and after the thirtieth day from the date of such election shall become the law of said City
39 or County, subject only to the Constitution and Public General Laws of this State, and any
40 public local laws inconsistent with the provisions of said charter and any former charter of
41 the City of Baltimore or County shall be thereby repealed.]

14 [The] BEFORE JANUARY 1, 2026, THE procedure provided in this section for
15 adoption of a charter may be used in any county in lieu of the procedures provided in Section
16 1 of this Article[, and a]. A charter THAT IS adopted pursuant to this section PRIOR TO
17 JANUARY 1, 2026, has the effect of a charter adopted in accordance with the provisions of
18 Section 1. The board of county commissioners of any county at any time may appoint a
19 charter board. Said charter board shall be registered voters and shall consist of an uneven
20 number of members, not fewer than five or more than nine. The board of county
21 commissioners shall appoint a charter board within thirty days after receiving a petition
22 signed by five percent of the registered voters of the county or by ten thousand voters of the
23 county, whichever is the lesser number. If additional charter board members are nominated
24 by petitions signed by three percent of the registered voters of the county or by two
25 thousand registered voters, whichever is the lesser number, delivered to the board of county
26 commissioners within sixty days after the charter board is appointed, the board of county
27 commissioners shall call a special election not less than thirty or more than ninety days
28 after receiving petitions, unless a regular election falls within the designated period. The
29 appointees of the board of county commissioners and those nominated by petitions shall be
30 placed on the ballot in alphabetical order without party designation. The voters may cast
31 votes for, and elect a number of nominees equal to the number of charter board members
32 originally selected by the board of county commissioners, and those so elected are the
33 charter board. The charter board, within 18 months from the date of its appointment, or if
34 there was an election for some of its members, within 18 months from the date of the
35 election, shall present a proposed charter for the county to the board of county
36 commissioners, which shall publish it at least twice in one or more newspapers of general
37 circulation in the county within thirty days after it is presented. The charter shall be
38 submitted to the voters of the county at a special or regular election held not earlier than
39 thirty days or later than ninety days after publication of the charter. If a majority of the
40 votes cast for and against the adoption of the charter are in favor of its adoption, the charter

1 shall become effective as the charter of the county on the thirtieth day after the election or
2 such later date as shall be specified in the charter.
3 3A.
4 The charter for the government of any county governed by the provisions of this
5 Article [may] SHALL provide for the election of members of the county council by the voters
6 of councilmanic districts therein established[, or by the voters of the entire county, or by a
7 combination of these methods of election].
8 Article XI–F – Home Rule for Code Counties
9 1.
10 (A) For the purposes of this Article, (1) “code county” means a county which is not
11 a charter county under Article 11A of this Constitution and has adopted the optional powers
12 of home rule provided under this Article; and (2) “public local law” means a law applicable
13 to the incorporation, organization, or government of a code county and contained in the
14 county’s code of public local laws; but this latter term specifically does not include (i) the
15 charters of municipal corporations under Article 11E of this Constitution, (ii) the laws or
16 charters of counties under Article 11A of this Constitution, (iii) laws, whether or not
17 Statewide in application, in the code of public general laws, (iv) laws which apply to more
18 than one county, and (v) ordinances and resolutions of the county government enacted
19 under public local laws.
20 (B) THE PROVISIONS OF THIS ARTICLE APPLY ONLY UNTIL EACH COUNTY
21 HAS ADOPTED A CHARTER UNDER ARTICLE XI–A OF THIS CONSTITUTION.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the General Assembly
23 determines that the amendment to the Maryland Constitution proposed by Section 1 of this
24 Act affects multiple jurisdictions and that the provisions of Article XIV, § 1 of the Maryland
25 Constitution concerning local approval of constitutional amendments do not apply.
26 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the amendment to the
27 Maryland Constitution proposed by Section 1 of this Act shall be submitted to the qualified
28 voters of the State at the next general election to be held in November 2026 for adoption or
29 rejection in accordance with Article XIV of the Maryland Constitution. At that general
30 election, the vote on the proposed amendment to the Constitution shall be by ballot, and on
31 each ballot there shall be printed the words “For the Constitutional Amendment” and
32 “Against the Constitutional Amendment”, as now provided by law. Immediately after the
33 election, all returns shall be made to the Governor of the vote for and against the proposed
34 amendment, as directed by Article XIV of the Maryland Constitution, and further
35 proceedings had in accordance with Article XIV.
