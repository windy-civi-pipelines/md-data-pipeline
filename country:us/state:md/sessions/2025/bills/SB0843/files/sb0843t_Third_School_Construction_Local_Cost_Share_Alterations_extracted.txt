1 AN ACT concerning
2 School Construction – Local Cost–Share – Alterations
3 FOR the purpose of adding an adjustment to the local cost–share for school construction
4 projects for certain counties under certain circumstances; and generally relating to
5 the local cost–share for school construction projects.
6 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
7 Article – Education
8 Section 5–303(k)
9 Annotated Code of Maryland
10 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
11 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
12 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
13 Article – Education
14 5–303.
15 (k) (1) (I) A county is eligible for an adjustment to the local cost–share for
16 school construction projects under [paragraph (2)] SUBPARAGRAPH (II) of this
17 [subsection] PARAGRAPH if:
18 [(i)] 1. A county’s median household income is in the bottom
19 quartile in the State; and

1 [(ii)] 2. The State and local cost–share formula for the county is
2 50% State and 50% local.
3 [(2) (i)] (II) 1. The local cost–share of a school construction project
4 in a county that is eligible under [paragraph (1)] SUBPARAGRAPH (I) of this [subsection]
5 PARAGRAPH shall be reduced to equal the local cost–share of the adjacent county that is
6 less than 50% but closest to 50%.
7 [(ii)] 2. The State cost–share of a school construction project in the
8 eligible county shall be increased by a percentage that is equal to the reduction under
9 [subparagraph (i) of this paragraph] SUBSUBPARAGRAPH 1 OF THIS SUBPARAGRAPH.
10 (2) (I) A COUNTY IS ELIGIBLE FOR AN ADJUSTMENT TO THE LOCAL
11 COST–SHARE FOR AN ELIGIBLE SCHOOL CONSTRUCTION PROJECT IN THE COUNTY
12 UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS PARAGRAPH IF:
13 1. THE PERCENTAGE OF STUDENTS IN THE COUNTY
14 ELIGIBLE FOR FREE OR REDUCED PRICE MEALS IS GREATER THAN THE STATEWIDE
15 AVERAGE OF STUDENTS ELIGIBLE FOR FREE OR REDUCED PRICE MEALS;
16 2. ALL SCHOOLS IN THE COUNTY PARTICIPATE IN THE
17 U.S. DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE COMMUNITY ELIGIBILITY PROVISION;
18 3. THE STATE AND LOCAL COST–SHARE FORMULA FOR
19 THE COUNTY IS 50% STATE AND 50% LOCAL; AND
20 4. THE ELIGIBLE PROJECT IS A SCHOOL THAT IS THE
21 ONLY AN ELEMENTARY, MIDDLE, OR HIGH SCHOOL IN THE COUNTY; AND
22 5. THE TOTAL COST OF THE PROJECT IS EQUAL TO OR
23 LESS THAN $129,000 PER STATE–ELIGIBLE STUDENT.
24 (II) 1. THE LOCAL COST–SHARE OF A SCHOOL
25 CONSTRUCTION PROJECT IN A COUNTY THAT IS ELIGIBLE UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH
26 (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH SHALL BE REDUCED TO EQUAL THE AVERAGE OF COUNTIES
27 IN WHICH ALL SCHOOLS IN THE COUNTY PARTICIPATE IN THE U.S. DEPARTMENT OF
28 AGRICULTURE COMMUNITY ELIGIBILITY PROVISION.
29 2. THE STATE COST–SHARE OF A SCHOOL
30 CONSTRUCTION PROJECT IN THE ELIGIBLE COUNTY SHALL BE INCREASED BY A
31 PERCENTAGE THAT IS EQUAL TO THE REDUCTION UNDER SUBSUBPARAGRAPH 1 OF
32 THIS SUBPARAGRAPH.
33 (3) A county shall receive:
SENATE BILL 843 3
1 (i) A 10 percentage point increase in the State share of a school
2 construction project if the proposed school construction project, when a local school system
3 submits a project for approval to the Interagency Commission, is at a school with a
4 concentration of poverty level, as defined in § 5–223 of this title, of 80% or greater;
5 (ii) A 5 percentage point increase in the State share of a school
6 construction project if the proposed school construction project, when a local school system
7 submits a project for approval to the Interagency Commission, is at a school with a
8 concentration of poverty level, as defined in § 5–223 of this title, of less than 80% but greater
9 than 55%;
10 (iii) A 5 percentage point increase in the State share of a school
11 construction project if the proposed school construction project is at a school that, in the
12 most recent school maintenance effectiveness assessment by the Interagency Commission,
13 received an assessment rating of:
14 1. Good;
15 2. Superior; or
16 3. Adequate and the school’s current school facility
17 assessment percent of expected useful life is at least 120%; and
18 (iv) A 5 percentage point increase in the State share of a school
19 construction project if the proposed project is to build a net–zero school.
20 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
21 1, 2025. It shall remain effective for a period of 3 years and, at the end of June 30, 2028,
22 this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and
23 of no further force and effect.
Approved:
