1 AN ACT concerning
2 Adult Education – High School Diploma by Examination – Requirements and
3 Study
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Labor to allow an individual to
5 take all components of the General Educational Development Test in the same
6 language of their choice; requiring the Department to submit a certain report to
7 certain committees of the General Assembly on or before a certain date; and
8 generally relating to examination requirements for a high school diploma by
9 examination.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
11 Article – Labor and Employment
12 Section 11–808(a), (b), and (d)
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
16 Article – Labor and Employment
17 Section 11–808(c)
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0451*
2 SENATE BILL 451
1 Article – Labor and Employment
2 11–808.
3 (a) An individual may obtain a high school diploma by examination as provided
4 in this section if the individual:
5 (1) has not obtained a high school diploma;
6 (2) resides in this State; and
7 (3) except as provided in subsection (b) of this section:
8 (i) is not subject to compulsory school attendance under § 7–301 of
9 the Education Article; and
10 (ii) has withdrawn from a regular full–time public or private school
11 program.
12 (b) An individual is not subject to the requirements of subsection (a)(3) of this
13 section if the individual participates in a GED Option Program administered by the State
14 Department of Education that creates a pathway to a high school diploma by examination
15 for currently enrolled high school English language learner students under the age of 21
16 years who have experienced interrupted education and have a lower level of English
17 proficiency than their peers.
18 (c) The Department shall:
19 (1) offer examinations to individuals who are pursuing a high school
20 diploma under this subtitle at least twice each year at places throughout the State that are
21 reasonably convenient for the applicants; AND
22 (2) ALLOW AN INDIVIDUAL TO TAKE ALL COMPONENTS OF THE
23 EXAMINATION IN THE LANGUAGE OF THEIR CHOICE SAME LANGUAGE FROM THE
24 ENGLISH– OR SPANISH–LANGUAGE EXAMINATIONS OFFERED BY THE
25 DEPARTMENT.
26 (d) The examination shall:
27 (1) be offered in appropriate high school subject areas; and
28 (2) be of a comprehensive nature as determined by the State Board of
29 Education.
30 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
SENATE BILL 451 3
1 (a) The Maryland Department of Labor shall:
2 (1) identify other appropriate languages in which to offer the General
3 Educational Development Test for adult learners in the State; and
4 (2) study the feasibility and cost of offering the examination in additional
5 languages.
6 (b) On or before December 1, 2026, the Department shall submit, in accordance
7 with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, a report of its findings and
8 recommendations from the study required under this section to the Senate Finance
9 Committee and the House Committee on Ways and Means.
10 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
11 October July 1, 2025.
Approved:
