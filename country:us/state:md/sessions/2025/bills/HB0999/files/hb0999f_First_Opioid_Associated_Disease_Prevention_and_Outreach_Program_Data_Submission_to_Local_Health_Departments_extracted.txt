J1 5lr3361
By: Delegate R. Lewis
Introduced and read first time: January 31, 2025
Assigned to: Health and Government Operations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Opioid–Associated Disease Prevention and Outreach Program Data –
3 Submission to Local Health Departments
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Health to submit the data
5 reported to the Department by an Opioid–Associated Disease Prevention and
6 Outreach Program to the local health department for each county in which the
7 Program operates; and generally relating to Opioid–Associated Disease Prevention
8 and Outreach Programs.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
10 Article – Health – General
11 Section 24–901(a) and (f) and 24–902(a) and (c)
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – Health – General
16 Section 24–907
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
21 Article – Health – General
22 24–901.
23 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb0999*
2 HOUSE BILL 999
1 (f) “Program” means an Opioid–Associated Disease Prevention and Outreach
2 Program.
3 24–902.
4 (a) (1) A Program may be established by a local health department or a
5 community–based organization, subject to the provisions of this subtitle.
6 (2) (i) A county may cooperate with another county to establish a
7 Program.
8 (ii) A community–based organization may establish a multicounty
9 Program.
10 (3) This subtitle does not apply to the AIDS Prevention Sterile Needle and
11 Syringe Exchange Pilot Program established under Subtitle 8 of this title.
12 (c) If established under subsection (a) of this section, a Program shall:
13 (1) Provide for substance use outreach, education, and linkage to treatment
14 services to participants, including distribution and collection of hypodermic needles and
15 syringes; and
16 (2) Operate in accordance with:
17 (i) The technical assistance of the Standing Advisory Committee;
18 and
19 (ii) The procedures, plans, and protocols approved by:
20 1. The local health officer for each county in which a Program
21 is established; and
22 2. The Department.
23 24–907.
24 (A) A Program shall collect and report at least annually the following data to the
25 Department:
26 (1) The number of participants served by the Program;
27 (2) The number of new participants registered by the Program during the
28 reporting period;
29 (3) Demographic profiles of participants served by the Program, including:
HOUSE BILL 999 3
1 (i) Age;
2 (ii) Gender;
3 (iii) Race;
4 (iv) Zip code; and
5 (v) Types of drugs used;
6 (4) The number of hypodermic needles and syringes distributed and
7 collected;
8 (5) Each location at which hypodermic needles and syringes were
9 distributed; and
10 (6) The number of linkages provided to participants under § 24–903(a)(6)
11 of this subtitle.
12 (B) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL SUBMIT THE DATA REPORTED FROM A
13 PROGRAM TO THE LOCAL HEALTH DEPARTMENT FOR EACH COUNTY IN WHICH THE
14 PROGRAM OPERATES.
15 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
16 October 1, 2025.
