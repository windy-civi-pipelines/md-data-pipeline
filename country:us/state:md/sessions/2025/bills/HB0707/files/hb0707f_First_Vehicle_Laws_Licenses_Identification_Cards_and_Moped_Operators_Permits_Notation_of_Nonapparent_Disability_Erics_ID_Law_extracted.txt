A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Vehicle Laws – Licenses, Identification Cards, and Moped Operator’s Permits
3 – Notation of Nonapparent Disability
4 (Eric’s ID Law)
5 FOR the purpose of requiring that certain applications for a license, an identification card,
6 or a moped operator’s permit allow an applicant to choose to indicate on the
7 document the applicant’s nonapparent disability; requiring the Motor Vehicle
8 Administration to ensure that the license, identification card, or moped operator’s
9 permit of an applicant who indicates that the applicant has a nonapparent disability
10 includes a certain notation; requiring the Administration to establish certain public
11 outreach efforts on the availability of the notation; prohibiting the Administration
12 from disclosing certain information to certain entities about an applicant that
13 indicates that the applicant has a nonapparent disability; and generally relating to
14 nonapparent disability notations on licenses, identification cards, and moped
15 operator’s permits.
16 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
17 Article – Transportation
18 Section 11–128 and 11–135
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
21 BY adding to
22 Article – Transportation
23 Section 12–306
24 Annotated Code of Maryland
25 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Transportation
4 11–128.
5 (a) “License”, as used in reference to the operation of a motor vehicle, means any:
6 (1) Driver’s license; and
7 (2) Any other license or permit to drive a motor vehicle that is issued under
8 or granted by the laws of this State, including:
9 (i) Any temporary license;
10 (ii) A learner’s instructional permit;
11 (iii) A provisional license;
12 (iv) The privilege of any individual to drive a motor vehicle, whether
13 or not that individual is formally licensed by this or any other jurisdiction;
14 (v) Any nonresident’s privilege to drive, as defined in this subtitle;
15 and
16 (vi) A commercial driver’s license.
17 (b) “License” does not include an electronic credential issued under Title 16,
18 Subtitle 10 of this article.
19 11–135.
20 (a) (1) “Motor vehicle” means, except as provided in subsection (b) of this
21 section, a vehicle that:
22 (i) Is self–propelled or propelled by electric power obtained from
23 overhead electrical wires; and
24 (ii) Is not operated on rails.
25 (2) “Motor vehicle” includes a low speed vehicle.
26 (b) “Motor vehicle” does not include:
27 (1) A moped, as defined in § 11–134.1 of this subtitle;

1 (2) A motor scooter, as defined in § 11–134.5 of this subtitle;
2 (3) An electric bicycle, as defined in § 11–117.1 of this subtitle;
3 (4) An electric low speed scooter, as defined in § 11–117.2 of this subtitle;
4 or
5 (5) A personal delivery device, as defined in § 21–104.5 of this article.
6 12–306.
7 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION, “NONAPPARENT DISABILITY” MEANS A
8 DISABILITY OR HEALTH CONDITION THAT IS NOT IMMEDIATELY OBVIOUS OR
9 VISIBLE.
10 (2) “NONAPPARENT DISABILITY” INCLUDES:
11 (I) DEVELOPMENTAL DISABILITIES; AND
12 (II) INTELLECTUAL DISABILITIES.
13 (B) AN INITIAL AND A RENEWAL APPLICATION FOR A LICENSE, AN
14 IDENTIFICATION CARD, OR A MOPED OPERATOR’S PERMIT SHALL ALLOW AN
15 APPLICANT TO CHOOSE TO INDICATE ON THE DOCUMENT THAT THE APPLICANT HAS
16 A NONAPPARENT DISABILITY.
17 (C) THE ADMINISTRATION SHALL:
18 (1) ENSURE THAT THE LICENSE, IDENTIFICATION CARD, OR MOPED
19 OPERATOR’S PERMIT OF AN APPLICANT WHO HAS INDICATED A NONAPPARENT
20 DISABILITY ON AN APPLICATION INCLUDES A NOTATION OF THE NONAPPARENT
21 DISABILITY ON THE BOTTOM OF THE LICENSE, IDENTIFICATION CARD, OR MOPED
22 OPERATOR’S PERMIT;
23 (2) CONSULT WITH DEVELOPMENTAL DISABILITY AND
24 INTELLECTUAL DISABILITY ADVOCACY GROUPS WITH RESPECT TO THE DESIGN OF
25 THE NONAPPARENT DISABILITY NOTATION;
26 (3) NOTIFY AN INDIVIDUAL WITH A NONAPPARENT DISABILITY
27 NOTATION ON A LICENSE, IDENTIFICATION CARD, OR MOPED OPERATOR’S PERMIT
28 THAT TURNS 18 YEARS OF AGE THAT THE INDIVIDUAL MAY REQUEST THAT THE
29 NONAPPARENT DISABILITY NOTATION BE REMOVED; AND
4 HOUSE BILL 707
1 (4) ESTABLISH PUBLIC OUTREACH EFFORTS TO EDUCATE THE
2 GENERAL PUBLIC ON THE AVAILABILITY OF THE NONAPPARENT DISABILITY
3 NOTATION.
4 (D) THERE IS NO FEE FOR A NONAPPARENT DISABILITY NOTATION ON A
5 LICENSE, IDENTIFICATION CARD, OR MOPED OPERATOR’S PERMIT, INCLUDING FOR
6 THE ISSUANCE OF A CORRECTED DOCUMENT IF NO OTHER CORRECTIONS ARE MADE.
7 (E) THE ADMINISTRATION MAY NOT:
8 (1) REQUIRE AN APPLICANT FOR A LICENSE, AN IDENTIFICATION
9 CARD, OR A MOPED OPERATOR’S PERMIT TO PROVIDE PROOF OF THE APPLICANT’S
10 NONAPPARENT DISABILITY;
11 (2) DENY AN APPLICATION FOR A LICENSE, AN IDENTIFICATION
12 CARD, OR A MOPED OPERATOR’S PERMIT BECAUSE THE NONAPPARENT DISABILITY
13 SELECTED BY THE APPLICANT DOES NOT MATCH THE NONAPPARENT DISABILITY
14 INDICATED ON ANOTHER DOCUMENT ASSOCIATED WITH THE APPLICANT;
15 (3) NOTWITHSTANDING § 4–320 OF THE GENERAL PROVISIONS
16 ARTICLE, DISCLOSE INFORMATION RELATED TO AN APPLICANT’S INDICATION OF A
17 NONAPPARENT DISABILITY ON AN APPLICATION FOR A LICENSE, AN
18 IDENTIFICATION CARD, OR A MOPED OPERATOR’S PERMIT FOR ANY USE IN
19 RESPONSE TO A REQUEST FROM AN INSURANCE COMPANY OR THE MARYLAND
20 INSURANCE ADMINISTRATION; OR
21 (4) USE INFORMATION DISCLOSED BY A PERSON UNDER THIS
22 SECTION AS A BASIS FOR REFERRING THE PERSON TO THE MEDICAL ADVISORY
23 BOARD UNDER § 16–118 OF THIS ARTICLE.
24 (F) THE MARYLAND POLICE TRAINING AND STANDARDS COMMISSION, IN
25 CONSULTATION WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF STATE POLICE AND OTHER
26 INTERESTED STAKEHOLDERS, SHALL IMMEDIATELY IMPLEMENT TRAINING
27 CONCERNING INTERACTIONS WITH INDIVIDUALS WHO HAVE A NONAPPARENT
28 DISABILITY NOTATION ON THEIR LICENSE, IDENTIFICATION CARD, OR MOPED
29 OPERATOR’S PERMIT.
30 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
31 October 1, 2025.
