A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Baltimore City – Speed Monitoring System Citations – Late Fees
3 FOR the purpose of requiring that a citation generated by a speed monitoring system
4 located in Baltimore City include certain information regarding late fees; authorizing
5 the Baltimore Police Department to charge a late fee for the failure to pay a civil
6 penalty or contest liability for a citation generated by a speed monitoring system
7 located in Baltimore City; and generally relating to speed monitoring systems.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
9 Article – Transportation
10 Section 21–809(c)
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Transportation
15 Section 21–809(d)(1) and (6)
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 BY adding to
19 Article – Transportation
20 Section 21–809(d)(6)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
23 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
24 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
25 Article – Transportation

1 (xi) Information advising the person alleged to be liable under this
2 section that failure to pay the civil penalty or to contest liability in a timely manner:
3 1. Is an admission of liability;
4 2. May result in the refusal by the Administration to register
5 the motor vehicle; and
6 3. May result in the suspension of the motor vehicle
7 registration; AND
8 (XII) FOR A CITATION GENERATED BY A SPEED MONITORING
9 SYSTEM LOCATED IN BALTIMORE CITY, INFORMATION ADVISING THE PERSON
10 ALLEGED TO BE LIABLE UNDER THIS SECTION THAT FAILURE TO PAY THE CIVIL
11 PENALTY OR TO CONTEST LIABILITY IN ACCORDANCE WITH PARAGRAPH (6) OF THIS
12 SUBSECTION MAY RESULT IN THE ASSESSMENT OF A LATE FEE.
13 (6) (I) THIS PARAGRAPH APPLIES ONLY IN BALTIMORE CITY.
14 (II) AN AGENCY MAY CHARGE A LATE FEE IF A PERSON ALLEGED
15 TO BE LIABLE UNDER THIS SECTION FAILS TO PAY THE CIVIL PENALTY OR TO
16 CONTEST LIABILITY WITHIN A TIMEFRAME DETERMINED BY THE AGENCY.
17 [(6)] (7) To mail the citation or warning notice, an agency shall use:
18 (i) The current mailing address on file with the Administration; or
19 (ii) If a mailing address is unavailable, the current residential
20 address on file with the Administration.
21 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
22 October 1, 2025.
