By: Senator Bailey

Introduced and read first time: January 8, 2025
Assigned to: Education, Energy, and the Environment
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Tidal Fish Licenses – Oyster Authorizations – Administrative Penalties
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Natural Resources to suspend for certain
4 time periods, rather than revoke, a person’s oyster authorization for knowingly
5 committing certain violations in the oyster fishery; requiring the Department to
6 reinstate a person’s revoked oyster authorization under certain circumstances;
7 altering a certain offense related to the use of prohibited gear that serves as grounds
8 for suspension of an oyster authorization; repealing a certain time period within
9 which a certain administrative hearing must be held; and generally relating to
10 administrative penalties for oyster authorizations.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
12 Article – Natural Resources
13 Section 4–1210
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
16 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
17 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
18 Article – Natural Resources
19 4–1210.
20 (a) (1) In addition to any other penalty or fine provided in this title, a person
21 who holds an authorization to catch oysters under § 4–701 of this title and receives a
22 citation for an offense listed under paragraph (2) of this subsection may, IN ACCORDANCE
23 WITH THIS SECTION, have the authorization [revoked in accordance with this section]:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0076*
2 SENATE BILL 76
1 (I) FOR A FIRST VIOLATION, SUSPENDED FOR UP TO 2 YEARS;
2 AND
3 (II) FOR A SECOND OR SUBSEQUENT VIOLATION, SUSPENDED
4 FOR UP TO 10 YEARS.
5 (2) The following offenses, committed in violation of this title or of any
6 regulation adopted under this title, are grounds for [revocation] SUSPENSION of an
7 authorization to catch oysters under this section:
8 (i) Taking oysters located more than 200 feet within a closed or
9 prohibited area;
10 (ii) Taking oysters with gear that is prohibited in that area WHILE
11 MORE THAN 200 FEET WITHIN THE PROHIBITED AREA;
12 (iii) Taking oysters outside of a time restriction for the harvest of
13 oysters by more than 1 hour;
14 (iv) Taking oysters during closed seasons; and
15 (v) Taking oysters from a leased area by a person other than the
16 leaseholder or the leaseholder’s designee.
17 (b) (1) [(i)] Before the [revocation] SUSPENSION of an authorization to
18 catch oysters under this section, the Department shall hold a hearing on the matter in
19 accordance with the Administrative Procedure Act under Title 10, Subtitle 2 of the State
20 Government Article.
21 [(ii) A hearing held under subparagraph (i) of this paragraph shall be
22 held within 90 days after the cited individual commits the offense listed under subsection
23 (a)(2) of this section.]
24 (2) After a hearing is conducted under paragraph (1) of this subsection, if
25 the presiding officer finds or concludes that the person knowingly has committed an offense
26 listed under subsection (a)(2) of this section, the Department shall [revoke] SUSPEND the
27 person’s authorization to catch oysters IN ACCORDANCE WITH SUBSECTION (A)(1) OF
28 THIS SECTION.
29 (c) A person who is aggrieved by the final decision of the Department may obtain
30 judicial review of the decision in accordance with the Administrative Procedure Act under
31 Title 10, Subtitle 2 of the State Government Article.
32 (d) A person whose authorization has been [revoked] SUSPENDED in accordance
33 with this section may not engage or work in the oyster fishery WHILE SUSPENDED whether
34 or not [it] THE WORK requires the use of another license.
SENATE BILL 76 3
1 (E) (1) THIS SUBSECTION APPLIES TO A PERSON’S AUTHORIZATION TO
2 CATCH OYSTERS THAT WAS REVOKED UNDER THIS SECTION BEFORE JULY 1, 2025.
3 (2) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL REINSTATE A PERSON’S
4 AUTHORIZATION TO CATCH OYSTERS IF:
5 (I) 1. THE AUTHORIZATION HAS BEEN REVOKED FOR AT
6 LEAST 2 YEARS; AND
7 2. THE REVOCATION WAS THE PERSON’S FIRST OFFENSE
8 ADJUDICATED AS A KNOWING VIOLATION UNDER THIS SECTION; OR
9 (II) 1. THE AUTHORIZATION HAS BEEN REVOKED FOR AT
10 LEAST 10 YEARS; AND
11 2. THE REVOCATION WAS THE PERSON’S SECOND OR
12 SUBSEQUENT OFFENSE ADJUDICATED AS A KNOWING VIOLATION UNDER THIS
13 SECTION.
14 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
15 1, 2025.
