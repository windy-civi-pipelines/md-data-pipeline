1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Safety – Handgun Permits – Expiration and Renewal Periods for Retired
3 Law Enforcement Officer
4 FOR the purpose of establishing alternative expiration and renewal periods for a handgun
5 permit issued to a certain retired law enforcement officer; and generally relating to
6 handgun permits.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
8 Article – Public Safety
9 Section 5–301(a) and (c) and 5–304(b)(1) and (d)(2)
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Public Safety
14 Section 5–309
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Public Safety

Underlining indicates amendments to bill.

2 SENATE BILL 180
1 5–301.
2 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
3 (c) “Permit” means a permit issued by the Secretary to carry, wear, or transport
4 a handgun.
5 5–304.
6 (b) (1) Subject to subsections (c) and (d) of this section, the Secretary may
7 charge a nonrefundable fee payable when an application is filed for a permit.
8 (d) The Secretary may not charge a fee under subsection (b) of this section to:
9 (2) a retired law enforcement officer of the State or a county or municipal
10 corporation of the State.
11 5–309.
12 (a) Except as provided in [subsection] SUBSECTIONS (d) AND (E) of this section,
13 a permit expires on the last day of the holder’s birth month following 2 years after the date
14 the permit is issued.
15 (b) [Subject] EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (E) OF THIS SECTION
16 AND SUBJECT to subsection (c) of this section, a permit may be renewed for successive
17 periods of 3 years each if, at the time of an application for renewal, the applicant possesses
18 the qualifications for the issuance of a permit and pays the renewal fee stated in this
19 subtitle.
20 (c) A person who applies for a renewal of a permit is not required to be
21 fingerprinted unless the Secretary requires a set of the person’s fingerprints to resolve a
22 question of the person’s identity.
23 (d) The Secretary may establish an alternative expiration date for a permit to
24 coincide with the expiration of a license, certification, or commission for:
25 (1) a private detective under Title 13 of the Business Occupations and
26 Professions Article;
27 (2) a security guard under Title 19 of the Business Occupations and
28 Professions Article; or
29 (3) a special police officer under § 3–306 of this article.
30 (E) A PERMIT ISSUED TO A RETIRED LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER WHO
31 RETIRED IN GOOD STANDING FROM SERVICE WITH A LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCY
SENATE BILL 180 3
1 OF THE STATE OR A COUNTY OR MUNICIPAL CORPORATION OF THE STATE:
2 (1) EXPIRES ON THE LAST DAY OF THE HOLDER’S BIRTH MONTH
3 FOLLOWING 5 YEARS AFTER THE DATE THE PERMIT IS ISSUED; AND
4 (2) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION, MAY BE RENEWED
5 FOR SUCCESSIVE PERIODS OF 5 YEARS EACH IF, AT THE TIME OF AN APPLICATION
6 FOR RENEWAL, THE APPLICANT POSSESSES THE QUALIFICATIONS FOR THE
7 ISSUANCE OF A PERMIT.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
9 October 1, 2025.
Approved:
