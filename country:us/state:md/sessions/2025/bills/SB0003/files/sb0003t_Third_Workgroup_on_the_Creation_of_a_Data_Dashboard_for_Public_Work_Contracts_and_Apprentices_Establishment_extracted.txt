CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Procurement – Public Work Contracts – Data Dashboard
3 (Maryland Public Works and Apprenticeship Transparency Act)
4 Workgroup on the Creation of a Data Dashboard for Public Work Contracts and
5 Apprentices – Establishment
6 FOR the purpose of requiring the Commissioner of Labor and Industry to develop and
7 maintain a data dashboard that includes certain information relating to contractors,
8 subcontractors, employees, and apprentices working under public work contracts;
9 requiring each contractor who is contracted under a public work contract to provide
10 certain information to the Commissioner; requiring the Commissioner to update the
11 data dashboard on a certain basis, post the data dashboard on the Maryland
12 Department of Labor website, and maintain a certain cache; and generally relating
13 to public work contracts establishing the Workgroup on the Creation of a Data
14 Dashboard for Public Work Contracts and Apprentices to study the most efficient
15 and cost–effective method to create a publicly available data dashboard that
16 disaggregates information regarding public work contracts with the State and
17 apprentices working under the public work contracts; and generally relating to the
18 Workgroup on the Creation of a Data Dashboard for Public Work Contracts and
19 Apprentices.
20 BY adding to
21 Article – State Finance and Procurement
22 Section 17–206
23 Annotated Code of Maryland

1 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
2 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
3 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
4 (a) There is a Workgroup on the Creation of a Data Dashboard for Public Work
5 Contracts and Apprentices.
6 (b) The Workgroup consists of the following members:
7 (1) three members of the Senate of Maryland, appointed by the President
8 of the Senate;
9 (2) three members of the House of Delegates, appointed by the Speaker of
10 the House;
11 (3) the Secretary of Labor, or the Secretary’s designee;
12 (4) the Commissioner of Labor and Industry, or the Commissioner’s
13 designee; and
14 (5) the following members, appointed by the Governor:
15 (i) one representative of the Baltimore–DC Building Trades
16 Council, selected by the President of the Council;
17 (ii) one representative of the Mid–Atlantic Pipe Trades Association;
18 (iii) one representative who is an attorney and a member of
19 Steamfitters Union Local 602;
20 (iv) one representative of the Associated Builders and Contractors of
21 Maryland (ABC);
22 (v) one representative of the Independent Electrical Contractors
23 (IEC) Chesapeake; and
24 (vi) one representative of the Maryland Minority Contractors
25 Association (MMCA).
26 (c) The President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House shall designate the
27 co–chairs of the Workgroup.
28 (d) The Commissioner of Labor and Industry shall provide staff for the
29 Workgroup.
30 (e) A member of the Workgroup:
SENATE BILL 3 3
1 (1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Workgroup; but
2 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
3 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.
4 (f) At the first meeting of the Workgroup that occurs on or after July 1, 2025, the
5 Workgroup shall vote to adopt a code of conduct providing for the acceptable conduct of
6 Workgroup members.
7 (g) The Workgroup shall:
8 (1) study the most efficient and cost–effective method to create a publicly
9 available data dashboard that disaggregates information regarding public work contracts
10 with the State, including information on:
11 (i) the name of each contractor and subcontractor working under a
12 public work contract;
13 (ii) wages, including overtime wages, and benefits paid to each
14 employee of a contractor or subcontractor working under a public work contract;
15 (iii) the job classification of each employee employed by a contractor
16 or subcontractor working under a public work contract;
17 (iv) apprentices working under each contractor and subcontractor for
18 public works contracts, including:
19 1. the number of apprentices employed;
20 2. demographic information for each apprentice;
21 3. the date each apprentice registered with the Maryland
22 Department of Labor;
23 4. the trade practiced by each apprentice; and
24 5. the certification status of each apprentice; and
25 (v) each apprenticeship program sponsored by each contractor or
26 subcontractor working under a public work contract; and
27 (2) make recommendations regarding:
28 (i) where the data dashboard shall be located;
4 SENATE BILL 3
1 (ii) necessary costs associated with the development and
2 maintenance of the data dashboard; and
3 (iii) time frames during which the data dashboard can be developed
4 and implemented and the frequency with which the information in the data dashboard can
5 be updated.
6 (h) On or before January 7, 2027, the Workgroup shall report its findings and
7 recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
8 Government Article, the General Assembly.
9 Article – State Finance and Procurement
10 17–206.
11 (A) THE COMMISSIONER SHALL DEVELOP A CLEAR AND
12 EASY–TO–UNDERSTAND GRAPHIC DATA DASHBOARD THAT INCLUDES, FOR EACH
13 ONGOING PUBLIC WORK CONTRACT:
14 (1) THE NAME OF EACH CONTRACTOR AND SUBCONTRACTOR
15 WORKING UNDER THE PUBLIC WORK CONTRACT;
16 (2) THE FOLLOWING INFORMATION REGARDING THE EMPLOYEES OF
17 EACH CONTRACTOR AND SUBCONTRACTOR WORKING UNDER THE PUBLIC WORK
18 CONTRACT:
19 (I) THE PREVAILING WAGE RATES PAID BY EACH CONTRACTOR
20 OR SUBCONTRACTOR;
21 (II) THE NUMBER OF EMPLOYEES EMPLOYED BY EACH
22 CONTRACTOR OR SUBCONTRACTOR;
23 (III) THE REGULAR WAGES AND OVERTIME WAGES PAID BY THE
24 CONTRACTOR OR SUBCONTRACTOR;
25 (IV) ANY FRINGE BENEFITS PROVIDED TO EMPLOYEES BY THE
26 CONTRACTOR OR SUBCONTRACTOR;
27 (V) THE JOB CLASSIFICATION OF EACH EMPLOYEE EMPLOYED
28 BY THE CONTRACTOR OR SUBCONTRACTOR; AND
29 (VI) ANY ADDITIONAL INFORMATION DETERMINED NECESSARY
30 BY THE COMMISSIONER; AND
SENATE BILL 3 5
1 (3) (I) THE NUMBER OF APPRENTICES WORKING FOR EACH
2 CONTRACTOR OR SUBCONTRACTOR;
3 (II) THE TRADE PRACTICED BY EACH APPRENTICE WORKING
4 FOR EACH CONTRACTOR OR SUBCONTRACTOR;
5 (III) THE CERTIFICATION STATUS OF EACH APPRENTICE
6 WORKING FOR EACH CONTRACTOR OR SUBCONTRACTOR;
7 (IV) CONTACT INFORMATION FOR EACH APPRENTICESHIP
8 PROGRAM SPONSOR;
9 (V) DEMOGRAPHIC DATA FOR EACH APPRENTICE WORKING FOR
10 EACH CONTRACTOR OR SUBCONTRACTOR; AND
11 (VI) THE DATE EACH APPRENTICE REGISTERED WITH THE
12 MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF LABOR.
13 (B) EACH CONTRACTOR WHO IS CONTRACTED UNDER A PUBLIC WORK
14 CONTRACT SHALL PROVIDE THE COMMISSIONER WITH THE INFORMATION LISTED
15 UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION ONCE A WEEK.
16 (C) THE COMMISSIONER SHALL:
17 (1) UPDATE THE DATA DASHBOARD DEVELOPED UNDER SUBSECTION
18 (A) OF THIS SECTION AT LEAST ONCE A MONTH;
19 (2) POST THE MOST RECENTLY UPDATED DATA DASHBOARD
20 PROMINENTLY ON THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF LABOR WEBSITE; AND
21 (3) MAINTAIN A CACHE CONTAINING THE INFORMATION POSTED ON
22 THE DATA DASHBOARD FOR AT LEAST THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING 24 MONTHS.
23 (D) THE COMMISSIONER MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT THIS
24 SECTION.
25 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
26 October 1, 2025.
27 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
28 1, 2025. It shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and 7 months and, at the end of
29 January 31, 2027, this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall
30 be abrogated and of no further force and effect.
