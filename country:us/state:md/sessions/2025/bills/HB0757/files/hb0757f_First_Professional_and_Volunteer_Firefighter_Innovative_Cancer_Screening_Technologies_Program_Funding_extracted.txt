Assigned to: Environment and Transportation and Ways and Means
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Professional and Volunteer Firefighter Innovative Cancer Screening
3 Technologies Program – Funding
4 FOR the purpose of altering the fee for a license to transfer oil in the State; requiring the
5 Department of the Environment to transfer a certain amount of the license fee to the
6 Professional and Volunteer Firefighter Innovative Cancer Screening Technologies
7 Program; altering the amount the Governor is authorized to include in the annual
8 budget for the Program; requiring the Secretary of Health to use certain funds
9 exclusively for the Program; authorizing the Secretary to use up to a certain
10 percentage of Program funds to support academic medical centers for certain
11 purposes; and generally relating to the Professional and Volunteer Firefighter
12 Innovative Cancer Screening Technologies Program.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
14 Article – Environment
15 Section 4–411(a) and (b)
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
19 Article – Environment
20 Section 4–411(c)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
23 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
24 Article – Health – General
25 Section 13–4001
26 Annotated Code of Maryland
27 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
8 Article – Environment
9 4–411.
10 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
11 (2) “Barrel” means any measure of petroleum products or its by–products
12 which consists of 42.0 U.S. gallons of liquid measure.
13 (3) “Fund” means the Maryland Oil Disaster Containment, Clean–Up and
14 Contingency Fund.
15 (4) “Transfer” means the offloading or onloading of oil in the State from or
16 to any commercial vessel, barge, tank truck, tank car, pipeline, or any other means used
17 for transporting oil.
18 (b) A person other than a vessel or barge may not transfer oil in the State without
19 a license.
20 (c) (1) A license required under this section shall be secured from the
21 Department of the Environment subject to the terms and conditions set forth in this section.
22 (2) The fee on any barrel shall be imposed only once, at the point of first
23 transfer in the State.
24 (3) The license fee shall be:
25 (i) Credited to the Maryland Oil Disaster Containment, Clean–Up
26 and Contingency Fund and based on:
27 1. Before July 1, 2024, a 7.75 cents per barrel fee for oil
28 transferred in the State; [and]
29 2. On or after July 1, 2024, AND BEFORE JULY 1, 2025, a 5
30 cents per barrel fee for oil transferred in the State; and
31 3. ON OR AFTER JULY 1, 2025, A 9 CENTS PER BARREL
32 FEE FOR OIL TRANSFERRED IN THE STATE; AND
HOUSE BILL 757 3
1 (ii) Until July 1, 2024, based on an additional 0.25 cent per barrel
2 fee for oil transferred in the State and credited to the Oil Contaminated Site Environmental
3 Cleanup Fund as described in Subtitle 7 of this title.
4 (4) (I) BEGINNING IN FISCAL YEAR 2026, THE DEPARTMENT
5 SHALL TRANSFER 4 CENTS OF THE AMOUNT COLLECTED FROM THE PER BARREL FEE
6 IN PARAGRAPH (3)(I)3 OF THIS SUBSECTION TO THE PROFESSIONAL AND
7 VOLUNTEER FIREFIGHTER INNOVATIVE CANCER SCREENING TECHNOLOGIES
8 PROGRAM ESTABLISHED UNDER § 13–4002 OF THE HEALTH – GENERAL ARTICLE.
9 (II) FUNDS TRANSFERRED TO THE PROFESSIONAL AND
10 VOLUNTEER FIREFIGHTER INNOVATIVE CANCER SCREENING TECHNOLOGIES
11 PROGRAM UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH SHALL BE DISTRIBUTED
12 IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 13–4006 OF THE HEALTH – GENERAL ARTICLE.
13 [(4)] (5) (i) The license fee shall be paid quarterly to the Department
14 and on receipt by the Comptroller, credited to the proper fund.
15 (ii) The licensee shall certify to the Department, on forms as may be
16 prescribed by the Department, the number of barrels of oil transferred by the licensee
17 during the fee quarter no later than the last day of the month following the fee quarter.
18 (iii) These records shall be kept confidential by the Department.
19 [(5)] (6) (I) [When] EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBPARAGRAPH (II)
20 OF THIS PARAGRAPH, the balance in the Maryland Oil Disaster Containment, Clean–Up
21 and Contingency Fund from the monthly license fees paid under paragraph (3)(i) of this
22 subsection into the Fund equals or exceeds a maximum limit of $5,000,000, collection of
23 subsequent monthly license fees under paragraph (3)(i) of this subsection shall be abated
24 until:
25 [(i)] 1. The balance in the Fund from the license fees becomes less
26 than or equal to $4,000,000; or
27 [(ii)] 2. There is evidence that the balance in the Fund could be
28 significantly reduced by the recent occurrence of a major discharge or series of discharges.
29 (II) BEGINNING IN FISCAL YEAR 2026, THE FEE ABATEMENT
30 AUTHORIZED UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH MAY NOT EXCEED 5
31 CENTS PER BARREL FOR OIL TRANSFERRED IN THE STATE.
32 [(6)] (7) (i) If a licensee fails to remit the fee and accompanying
33 certification required by this section, the amount of the license fee due shall be determined
34 by the Department from information as may be available.
4 HOUSE BILL 757
1 (ii) Notice of this determination shall be given to the licensee liable
2 for payment of the license fee.
3 (iii) The determination shall finally and irrevocably fix the fee unless
4 the licensee against whom it is assessed, within 30 days after receiving notice of the
5 determination, shall apply to the Department for a hearing or unless the Department, on
6 its own, shall redetermine the fee.
7 [(7)] (8) The Department shall promulgate rules and regulations,
8 establish audit procedures for the audit of licensees, and prescribe and publish forms as
9 may be necessary to effectuate the purposes of this section.
10 Article – Health – General
11 13–4001.
12 In this subtitle, “Program” means the Professional and Volunteer Firefighter
13 Innovative Cancer Screening Technologies Program.
14 13–4006.
15 (a) (1) For fiscal years 2021 through 2024, the Governor shall include at least
16 $100,000 in the annual budget for the Program.
17 (2) For fiscal year 2025 and each fiscal year thereafter, the Governor may
18 include at least [$500,000] $3,000,000 in the annual budget for the Program.
19 (b) Appropriations and expenditures made for the purpose of implementing the
20 Program, including the use of any funds received by a person under any component of the
21 Program, are subject to audit by the Office of Legislative Audits as provided in § 2–1220 of
22 the State Government Article.
23 (C) THE SECRETARY SHALL USE THE FEES TRANSFERRED TO THE
24 PROGRAM UNDER § 4–411(C)(4) OF THE ENVIRONMENT ARTICLE EXCLUSIVELY FOR
25 THE PROGRAM.
26 (D) THE SECRETARY MAY USE UP TO 20% OF PROGRAM FUNDS TO SUPPORT
27 ACADEMIC MEDICAL CENTERS IN THE COLLECTION, ANALYSIS, AND PROCESSING OF
28 PROGRAM OUTCOME DATA FOR THE PURPOSES OF ASSESSING, MAXIMIZING, AND
29 IMPROVING THE EFFECTIVENESS AND CLINICAL UTILITY OF THE CANCER
30 SCREENING FUNDED BY THE PROGRAM.
31 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
32 1, 2025. It shall remain effective for a period of 5 years and, at the end of June 30, 2030,
HOUSE BILL 757 5
1 this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and
2 of no further force and effect.
