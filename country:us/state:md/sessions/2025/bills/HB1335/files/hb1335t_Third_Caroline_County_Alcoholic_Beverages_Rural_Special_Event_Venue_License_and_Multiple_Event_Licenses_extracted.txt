5 as a multiple event license in Caroline County; authorizing the Board of License
6 Commissioners for Caroline County to issue the license to the owner of a venue with
7 a certain zoning classification; authorizing holders of certain multiple event licenses
8 in Caroline County to store alcoholic beverages on the licensed premises for a certain
9 purpose; and generally relating to alcoholic beverages licenses in Caroline County.

26 (A) THERE IS A RURAL SPECIAL EVENT VENUE BEER, WINE, AND LIQUOR
27 LICENSE.
28 (B) THE BOARD MAY ISSUE THE LICENSE:
29 (1) TO THE OWNER OF A VENUE THAT IS ZONED AS A RURAL SPECIAL
30 EVENT VENUE UNDER COUNTY LAW; AND
31 (2) (I) TO THE SAME APPLICANT FOR ALL EVENTS FOR WHICH THE
32 LICENSE IS ISSUED; AND
HOUSE BILL 1335 3
1 (II) FOR USE AT ONLY ONE PREMISES.
2 (C) THE LICENSE AUTHORIZES THE LICENSE HOLDER TO SELL BEER, WINE,
3 AND LIQUOR FOR ON–PREMISES CONSUMPTION.
4 (D) THE LICENSE MAY NOT BE TRANSFERRED TO ANOTHER LOCATION.
5 (E) A LICENSE HOLDER MAY SELL BEER, WINE, AND LIQUOR DURING THE
6 HOURS SET IN COUNTY LAW FOR RURAL SPECIAL EVENT VENUES.
7 (F) A LICENSE HOLDER MAY PURCHASE BEER, WINE, AND LIQUOR ONLY
8 FROM A LICENSED WHOLESALER.
9 (G) A LICENSE HOLDER IS SUBJECT TO THE ALCOHOL AWARENESS
10 TRAINING REQUIREMENTS UNDER § 4–505 OF THIS ARTICLE AND § 15–1902 OF THIS
11 TITLE.
12 (H) THE FEE FOR A RURAL SPECIAL EVENT VENUE LICENSE IS:
13 (1) $250 FOR NOT MORE THAN 10 EVENTS PER YEAR;
14 (2) $500 FOR NOT MORE THAN 20 EVENTS PER YEAR;
15 (3) $750 FOR NOT MORE THAN 30 EVENTS PER YEAR; AND
16 (4) $1,000 FOR NOT MORE THAN 40 EVENTS PER YEAR.
17 15–1308.
18 (a) Instead of issuing individual event licenses, the Board may issue a multiple
19 event license for a particular class of license.
20 (b) The number of days for which a multiple event license may be used by a single
21 applicant may not exceed 40 per calendar year.
22 (c) (1) A multiple event license shall be issued:
23 (i) for one premises only; and
24 (ii) except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, to the
25 same applicant for all events for which the license is issued.
26 (2) The Board may:
27 (i) approve in writing a substitute applicant; and
4 HOUSE BILL 1335
1 (ii) before approving a substitute applicant, hold a hearing.
2 (d) A server who is certified as having completed an alcohol awareness program
3 shall be on the premises for which a multiple event license is issued when alcoholic
4 beverages are served.
5 (E) (1) THE HOLDER OF A MULTIPLE EVENT LICENSE UNDER THIS
6 SECTION MAY STORE ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES ON THE LICENSED PREMISES
7 BETWEEN INDIVIDUAL LICENSED EVENTS IF THE ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES:
8 (I) ARE IN A SPECIALLY IDENTIFIED LOCKED AND SECURED
9 LOCATION; AND
10 (II) ARE NOT SOLD OR CONSUMED EXCEPT DURING LICENSED
11 EVENT HOURS FOR LICENSED EVENT PURPOSES.
12 (2) (I) THE LICENSE HOLDER SHALL KEEP COMPLETE AND
13 ACCURATE RECORDS OF ALL ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES PURCHASED AND SOLD ON
14 THE LICENSED PREMISES.
15 (II) THE RECORDS SHALL BE:
16 1. MAINTAINED ON THE LICENSED PREMISES FOR 2
17 YEARS; AND
18 2. AVAILABLE FOR INSPECTION BY AUTHORIZED
19 PERSONNEL OF THE COMPTROLLER, THE EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR, AND THE BOARD.
20 (III) THE RECORDS SHALL INCLUDE A COMPLETED PRE– AND
21 POST–INVENTORY OF ALL ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES FOR EACH INDIVIDUAL EVENT.
22 (3) AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL OF THE COMPTROLLER, THE
23 EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR, AND THE BOARD MAY INSPECT THE PREMISES OF THE
24 LICENSE HOLDER AS PROVIDED UNDER § 6–202 OF THIS ARTICLE.
25 (4) A LICENSE HOLDER WHO VIOLATES THIS SUBSECTION IS SUBJECT
26 TO:
27 (I) FOR THE FIRST OFFENSE, A FINE OF $100; AND
28 (II) FOR A SUBSEQUENT OFFENSE, A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $500
29 AND DENIAL OF FUTURE REQUESTS FOR A LICENSE FOR AN INDIVIDUAL EVENT OR A
30 SPECIAL MULTIPLE EVENT LICENSE.
31 [(e)] (F) (1) The fee for a multiple event license is:
HOUSE BILL 1335 5
1 (i) $250 for not more than 10 events per year;
2 (ii) $500 for not more than 20 events per year;
3 (iii) $750 for not more than 30 events per year; and
4 (iv) $1,000 for not more than 40 events per year.
5 (2) The Board may not issue a refund if the license holder holds fewer
6 events during the calendar year than the number of events that the license holder is
7 entitled to hold.
8 15–1310. [15–1309.]
9 (a) This section applies only to:
10 (1) volunteer fire companies; AND
11 (2) A HOLDER OF A MULTIPLE EVENT LICENSE UNDER § 15–1308 OF
12 THIS SUBTITLE OR A RURAL SPECIAL EVENT VENUE LICENSE UNDER § 15–1309 OF
13 THIS SUBTITLE.
14 (b) Alcoholic beverages may be stored on the licensed premises between
15 individual licensed events if the alcoholic beverages:
16 (1) are in a specially identified locked and secured location; and
17 (2) are not sold or consumed except during licensed event hours for licensed
18 event purposes.
19 (c) (1) A license holder shall keep complete and accurate records of all
20 alcoholic beverages purchased and sold on the licensed premises.
21 (2) The records shall be:
22 (i) maintained on the licensed premises for 2 years; and
23 (ii) available for inspection by authorized personnel of the
24 Comptroller, the Executive Director, and the Board.
25 (3) The records shall include a completed pre– and post–inventory of all
26 alcoholic beverages for each individual event.
27 (d) Authorized personnel of the Comptroller, the Executive Director, and the
28 Board may inspect the premises of a license holder as provided under § 6–202 of this article.
6 HOUSE BILL 1335
1 (e) A license holder who violates this section is subject to:
2 (1) for the first offense, a fine of $100; and
3 (2) for a subsequent offense, a fine not exceeding $500 and denial of future
4 requests for a license for an individual event or a special multiple event license.
5 SECTION 3. 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
6 July 1, 2025.
