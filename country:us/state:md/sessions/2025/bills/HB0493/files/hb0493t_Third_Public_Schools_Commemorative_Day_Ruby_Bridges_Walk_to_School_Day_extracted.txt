1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Schools – Commemorative Day – Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day
3 FOR the purpose of designating a certain date as Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day;
4 requiring each county board of education to develop and implement a program of
5 education for Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day; requiring the State Department of
6 Education to develop model content for a program of education for Ruby Bridges
7 Walk to School Day; and generally relating to Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Education
10 Section 7–136
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Education
16 7–136.

2 HOUSE BILL 493
1 (A) THE PURPOSE OF THIS SECTION IS TO DESIGNATE NOVEMBER 14 EACH
2 YEAR AS “RUBY BRIDGES WALK TO SCHOOL DAY” TO COMMEMORATE RUBY
3 BRIDGES, WHO, ON NOVEMBER 14, 1960, BECAME THE FIRST AFRICAN AMERICAN
4 STUDENT TO INTEGRATE AN ALL–WHITE ELEMENTARY SCHOOL IN NEW ORLEANS,
5 LOUISIANA.
6 (B) (1) EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL DEVELOP AND IMPLEMENT A
7 PROGRAM OF EDUCATION FOR RUBY BRIDGES WALK TO SCHOOL DAY TO BE HELD
8 ON OR AROUND NOVEMBER 14 EACH YEAR.
9 (2) THE PROGRAM OF EDUCATION MAY INCLUDE:
10 (I) A SYMBOLIC WALK TO SCHOOL, ENCOURAGING STUDENTS
11 TO REFLECT ON THE SIGNIFICANCE OF RUBY BRIDGES’S HISTORIC WALK;
12 (II) INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIALS FOCUSING ON THE HISTORY
13 OF CIVIL RIGHTS, THE IMPACT OF SCHOOL DESEGREGATION, AND THE ONGOING
14 STRUGGLE FOR RACIAL EQUALITY;
15 (III) DISCUSSIONS, ASSEMBLIES, OR PRESENTATIONS THAT
16 HIGHLIGHT RUBY BRIDGES AND THE CIVIL RIGHTS MOVEMENT; AND
17 (IV) INTERACTIVE PROJECTS HIGHLIGHTING THE IMPORTANCE
18 OF DIVERSITY, TOLERANCE, AND JUSTICE, INCLUDING ARTWORK, ESSAYS, OR
19 ARTISTIC PERFORMANCES.
20 (3) IN DEVELOPING A PROGRAM OF EDUCATION IN ACCORDANCE
21 WITH PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, A COUNTY BOARD OR PUBLIC SCHOOL
22 IS ENCOURAGED TO:
23 (I) COLLABORATE WITH PARENTS, COMMUNITY
24 ORGANIZATIONS, LOCAL LEADERS, AND CIVIL RIGHTS GROUPS; AND
25 (II) PARTNER WITH LOCAL MUSEUMS, LIBRARIES, AND
26 HISTORICAL SOCIETIES TO PROVIDE STUDENTS WITH OPPORTUNITIES TO EXPLORE
27 THE HISTORY OF CIVIL RIGHTS.
28 (C) (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP MODEL CONTENT FOR
29 A PROGRAM OF EDUCATION FOR RUBY BRIDGES WALK TO SCHOOL DAY.
30 (2) THE MODEL CONTENT DEVELOPED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN
31 ACCORDANCE WITH PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION:
HOUSE BILL 493 3
1 (I) MAY INCLUDE INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIALS, INCLUDING
2 AGE–APPROPRIATE LESSON PLANS, DISCUSSION GUIDES, HISTORICAL DOCUMENTS,
3 AND MULTIMEDIA RESOURCES; AND
4 (II) SHALL INCLUDE RESOURCES TO ASSIST TEACHERS TO
5 ENGAGE STUDENTS IN DISCUSSIONS ABOUT CIVIL RIGHTS AND SOCIAL JUSTICE.
6 (D) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT THIS
7 SECTION.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
9 1, 2025.
Approved:
