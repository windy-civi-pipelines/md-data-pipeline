Section 1:
F1 5lr0652
By: Delegates Fennell, Harris, Healey, Ivey, Martinez, Pruski, Roberts, Taylor,
Turner, Wilkins, and Williams
Introduced and read first time: January 20, 2025
Assigned to: Ways and Means
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Schools – Commemorative Day – Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day
3 FOR the purpose of designating a certain date as Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day;
4 requiring each county board of education to develop and implement a program of
5 education for Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day; requiring the State Department of
6 Education to develop model content for a program of education for Ruby Bridges
7 Walk to School Day; and generally relating to Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Education
10 Section 7–136
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Education
16 7–136.
17 (A) THE PURPOSE OF THIS SECTION IS TO DESIGNATE NOVEMBER 14 EACH
18 YEAR AS “RUBY BRIDGES WALK TO SCHOOL DAY” TO COMMEMORATE RUBY
19 BRIDGES, WHO, ON NOVEMBER 14, 1960, BECAME THE FIRST AFRICAN AMERICAN
20 STUDENT TO INTEGRATE AN ALL–WHITE ELEMENTARY SCHOOL IN NEW ORLEANS,
21 LOUISIANA.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb0493*
2 HOUSE BILL 493
1 (B) (1) EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL DEVELOP AND IMPLEMENT A
2 PROGRAM OF EDUCATION FOR RUBY BRIDGES WALK TO SCHOOL DAY TO BE HELD
3 ON OR AROUND NOVEMBER 14 EACH YEAR.
4 (2) THE PROGRAM OF EDUCATION MAY INCLUDE:
5 (I) A SYMBOLIC WALK TO SCHOOL, ENCOURAGING STUDENTS
6 TO REFLECT ON THE SIGNIFICANCE OF RUBY BRIDGES’S HISTORIC WALK;
7 (II) INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIALS FOCUSING ON THE HISTORY
8 OF CIVIL RIGHTS, THE IMPACT OF SCHOOL DESEGREGATION, AND THE ONGOING
9 STRUGGLE FOR RACIAL EQUALITY;
10 (III) DISCUSSIONS, ASSEMBLIES, OR PRESENTATIONS THAT
11 HIGHLIGHT RUBY BRIDGES AND THE CIVIL RIGHTS MOVEMENT; AND
12 (IV) INTERACTIVE PROJECTS HIGHLIGHTING THE IMPORTANCE
13 OF DIVERSITY, TOLERANCE, AND JUSTICE, INCLUDING ARTWORK, ESSAYS, OR
14 ARTISTIC PERFORMANCES.
15 (3) IN DEVELOPING A PROGRAM OF EDUCATION IN ACCORDANCE
16 WITH PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, A COUNTY BOARD OR PUBLIC SCHOOL
17 IS ENCOURAGED TO:
18 (I) COLLABORATE WITH PARENTS, COMMUNITY
19 ORGANIZATIONS, LOCAL LEADERS, AND CIVIL RIGHTS GROUPS; AND
20 (II) PARTNER WITH LOCAL MUSEUMS, LIBRARIES, AND
21 HISTORICAL SOCIETIES TO PROVIDE STUDENTS WITH OPPORTUNITIES TO EXPLORE
22 THE HISTORY OF CIVIL RIGHTS.
23 (C) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP MODEL CONTENT FOR A
24 PROGRAM OF EDUCATION FOR RUBY BRIDGES WALK TO SCHOOL DAY.
25 (2) THE MODEL CONTENT DEVELOPED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN
26 ACCORDANCE WITH PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION:
27 (I) MAY INCLUDE INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIALS, INCLUDING
28 AGE–APPROPRIATE LESSON PLANS, DISCUSSION GUIDES, HISTORICAL DOCUMENTS,
29 AND MULTIMEDIA RESOURCES; AND
30 (II) SHALL INCLUDE RESOURCES TO ASSIST TEACHERS TO
31 ENGAGE STUDENTS IN DISCUSSIONS ABOUT CIVIL RIGHTS AND SOCIAL JUSTICE.
HOUSE BILL 493 3
1 (D) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT THIS
2 SECTION.
3 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
4 1, 2025.
