Assigned to: Health and Government Operations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Board of Physical Therapy Examiners – Revisions
3 FOR the purpose of clarifying the definition of “physical therapist assistant” as used in the
4 Maryland Physical Therapy Act; altering the membership of the State Board of
5 Physical Therapy Examiners; requiring the Board to send renewal notices to
6 licensees by electronic means, rather than first–class mail, unless the licensee
7 requests to receive the renewal notice by first–class mail; and generally relating to
8 the State Board of Physical Therapy Examiners.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
10 Article – Health Occupations
11 Section 13–101(a) and 13–311(a)
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – Health Occupations
16 Section 13–101(g), 13–202(a)(1) through (3) and (c), and 13–311(b)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
21 Article – Health Occupations
22 13–101.
23 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 3
1 (g) “Physical therapist assistant” means an individual LICENSED BY THE STATE
2 BOARD OF PHYSICAL THERAPY EXAMINERS who practices limited physical therapy and
3 who has passed the national physical therapy licensing examination administered or
4 accepted by the Board.
5 13–202.
6 (a) (1) The Board consists of [8] 9 members.
7 (2) Of the [8] 9 Board members:
8 (i) Five shall be licensed physical therapists, of which a minimum
9 of four shall be engaged primarily in the clinical practice of physical therapy in this State;
10 (ii) [One] TWO shall be [a] licensed physical therapist [assistant]
11 ASSISTANTS practicing limited physical therapy in this State; and
12 (iii) Two shall be consumer members.
13 (3) The Governor shall appoint the licensed physical therapist members
14 and the licensed physical therapist assistant [member] MEMBERS, with the advice of the
15 Secretary, from a list of names of qualified individuals submitted to the Secretary and the
16 Governor by the American Physical Therapy Association of Maryland. The number of
17 names on the list shall be at least three times the number of vacancies.
18 (c) [The] EACH licensed physical therapist assistant member shall:
19 (1) Have 5 years’ experience in limited physical therapy; and
20 (2) Be engaged in the profession of limited physical therapy in this State.
21 13–311.
22 (a) (1) A license expires on the date set by the Board, unless the license is
23 renewed for an additional term as provided in this section.
24 (2) A license may not be renewed for a term longer than 2 years.
25 (b) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, at least 1 month
26 before the license expires, the Board shall send to the licensee, by [first–class mail]
27 ELECTRONIC MEANS to the last known E–MAIL address of the licensee, a renewal notice
28 that states:
29 (i) The date on which the current license expires;
HOUSE BILL 3 3
1 (ii) The date by which the renewal application must be received by
2 the Board for the renewal to be issued [and mailed] before the license expires; and
3 (iii) The amount of the renewal fee.
4 (2) If requested by a licensee, the Board shall send to the licensee[, at least
5 two times within the month before a license expires,] a renewal notice by [electronic means]
6 FIRST–CLASS MAIL to the last known [electronic mail] address of the licensee.
7 (3) If a renewal notice sent by electronic means under paragraph [(2)] (1)
8 of this subsection is returned to the Board as undeliverable, the Board shall send to the
9 licensee a renewal notice by first–class mail to the last known address of the licensee.
10 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
11 1, 2025.
