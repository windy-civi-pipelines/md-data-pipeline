CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Transportation – Maryland Area Rail Commuter Rail Authority – Establishment
3 (MARC Rail Authority Act of 2025)
4 Workgroup on the Reorganization of the Maryland Transit Administration
5 FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland Area Rail Commuter (MARC) Rail Authority
6 and the powers and duties of the MARC Rail Authority, acting on behalf of the
7 Department of Transportation, has with respect to the supervision, construction,
8 operation, maintenance, and repair of MARC railroad facilities projects; and
9 generally relating to the establishment of the Maryland Area Rail Commuter
10 (MARC) Rail Authority the Workgroup on the Reorganization of the Maryland
11 Transit Administration to study reorganizing the Maryland Transit Administration;
12 and generally relating to the Workgroup on the Reorganization of the Maryland
13 Transit Administration.
14 BY adding to
15 Article – Transportation
16 Section 7–1301 through 7–1310 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 13. Maryland
17 Area Rail Commuter (MARC) Rail Authority”
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
22 Article – Transportation

1 SUBTITLE 13. MARYLAND AREA RAIL COMMUTER (MARC) RAIL AUTHORITY.
2 7–1301.
3 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
4 INDICATED.
5 (B) “MARC” MEANS MARYLAND AREA RAIL COMMUTER RAIL SERVICE.
6 (C) “MARC RAILROAD FACILITIES PROJECT” INCLUDES:
7 (1) (I) THE BRUNSWICK, CAMDEN, OR PENN LINES OF THE
8 MARYLAND AREA RAIL COMMUTER (MARC) SERVICE; AND
9 (II) EXTENDING MARC SERVICE; AND
10 (2) ANY ADDITIONS, IMPROVEMENTS, OR ENLARGEMENTS TO ANY OF
11 THE PROJECTS LISTED UNDER ITEM (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, WHENEVER
12 AUTHORIZED.
13 7–1302.
14 THERE IS A MARC RAIL AUTHORITY.
15 7–1303.
16 (A) (1) THE SECRETARY IS THE CHAIR OF THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY.
17 (2) THE SECRETARY SHALL DESIGNATE AN EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR
18 OF THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY.
19 (B) IN ADDITION TO THE CHAIR, THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY CONSISTS OF
20 THE FOLLOWING MEMBERS:
21 (1) (I) ONE MEMBER WITH A BACKGROUND IN RAIL TRANSIT,
22 APPOINTED BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE; AND
23 (II) ONE MEMBER WITH A BACKGROUND IN RAIL TRANSIT,
24 APPOINTED BY THE SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE; AND
25 (2) AS AN EX OFFICIO MEMBER, THE EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR OF THE
26 MARC RAIL AUTHORITY.
27 (C) (1) THE TERM OF AN APPOINTED MEMBER IS 4 YEARS.
HOUSE BILL 517 3
1 (2) AN APPOINTED MEMBER MAY NOT SERVE MORE THAN THREE
2 CONSECUTIVE TERMS.
3 (3) AT THE END OF A TERM, A MEMBER CONTINUES TO SERVE UNTIL
4 A SUCCESSOR IS APPOINTED AND QUALIFIES.
5 (4) A MEMBER WHO IS APPOINTED AFTER A TERM HAS BEGUN SERVES
6 ONLY FOR THE REST OF THE TERM AND UNTIL A SUCCESSOR IS APPOINTED AND
7 QUALIFIES.
8 (D) AN APPOINTED MEMBER OF THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY IS ENTITLED
9 TO:
10 (1) THE COMPENSATION PROVIDED IN THE STATE BUDGET; AND
11 (2) REIMBURSEMENT FOR EXPENSES UNDER THE STANDARD STATE
12 TRAVEL REGULATIONS, AS PROVIDED IN THE STATE BUDGET.
13 (E) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY IS SUBJECT TO THE OPEN MEETINGS
14 ACT UNDER TITLE 3 OF THE GENERAL PROVISIONS ARTICLE.
15 7–1304.
16 (A) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY IS ENTITLED TO THE STAFF PROVIDED IN
17 THE STATE BUDGET.
18 (B) THE SECRETARY SHALL PROVIDE THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY WITH
19 THE PERSONNEL OF THE DEPARTMENT THAT THE SECRETARY CONSIDERS
20 NECESSARY FOR PERFORMANCE OF THE MAINTENANCE AND OTHER FUNCTIONS
21 REQUIRED OF THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY TO MEET ITS OBLIGATIONS WITH
22 RESPECT TO ITS MARC RAILROAD FACILITIES PROJECTS.
23 7–1305.
24 (A) (1) ACTING ON BEHALF OF THE DEPARTMENT, THE MARC RAIL
25 AUTHORITY HAS THOSE POWERS AND DUTIES RELATING TO THE SUPERVISION,
26 FINANCING, CONSTRUCTION, OPERATION, MAINTENANCE, AND REPAIR OF MARC
27 RAILROAD FACILITIES PROJECTS AS ARE GRANTED TO IT BY THIS SUBTITLE.
28 (2) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY MAY:
29 (I) DELEGATE PROJECT OPERATIONS, MAINTENANCE, AND
30 REPAIR TO THE ADMINISTRATION; AND
4 HOUSE BILL 517
1 (II) USE FARE REVENUE TO PROVIDE THE ADMINISTRATION
2 WITH PAYMENTS FOR PROJECT OPERATIONS, MAINTENANCE, AND REPAIR.
3 (B) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY HAS GENERAL SUPERVISION OF MARC
4 RAILROAD FACILITIES PROJECTS.
5 (C) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY SHALL FINANCE, CONSTRUCT, OPERATE,
6 REPAIR, AND MAINTAIN IN GOOD ORDER THE MARC RAILROAD FACILITIES.
7 (D) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY SHALL, TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE,
8 ADHERE TO THE TRANSIT PLANS PREPARED UNDER SUBTITLE 3 OF THIS TITLE.
9 7–1306.
10 (A) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY MAY ACQUIRE, HOLD, AND DISPOSE OF
11 PROPERTY IN THE EXERCISE OF ITS POWERS AND PERFORMANCE OF ITS DUTIES.
12 (B) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY MAY MAKE ANY CONTRACTS AND
13 AGREEMENTS NECESSARY OR INCIDENTAL TO THE EXERCISE OF ITS POWERS AND
14 PERFORMANCE OF ITS DUTIES.
15 (C) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY MAY APPLY FOR AND RECEIVE GRANTS
16 FROM ANY FEDERAL AGENCY FOR THE PLANNING, CONSTRUCTION, OPERATION, OR
17 FINANCING OF ANY PASSENGER RAIL OR TRANSIT PROJECT AND MAY RECEIVE AID
18 OR CONTRIBUTIONS OF MONEY, PROPERTY, LABOR, OR OTHER THINGS OF VALUE
19 FROM ANY SOURCE, TO BE HELD, USED, AND APPLIED FOR THE PURPOSES FOR
20 WHICH THE GRANTS, AID, AND CONTRIBUTIONS ARE MADE.
21 (D) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY MAY ADOPT RULES AND REGULATIONS TO
22 CARRY OUT THE PROVISIONS OF THIS SUBTITLE.
23 (E) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY MAY DO ANYTHING ELSE NECESSARY OR
24 CONVENIENT TO CARRY OUT THE POWERS GRANTED IN THIS SUBTITLE.
25 7–1307.
26 SUBJECT TO TITLE 12 OF THE REAL PROPERTY ARTICLE AND CHAPTER 608
27 OF THE ACTS OF THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF 1976, THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY
28 MAY CONDEMN PROPERTY FOR ANY MARC RAILROAD FACILITIES PROJECT.
29 7–1308.

1 THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY MAY CONTRACT WITH THE ADMINISTRATION
2 TO AUTHORIZE ADMINISTRATION POLICE OFFICERS TO EXERCISE THE POWERS
3 DESCRIBED UNDER § 7–207 OF THIS TITLE ON PROPERTY OWNED, LEASED, OR
4 OPERATED BY OR UNDER THE CONTROL OF THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY.
5 7–1309.
6 (A) THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY SHALL MAKE PUBLICLY AVAILABLE ON
7 ITS WEBSITE:
8 (1) EACH OPEN MEETING AGENDA:
9 (I) AT LEAST 48 HOURS IN ADVANCE OF EACH MEETING; OR
10 (II) IF THE MEETING IS BEING HELD DUE TO AN EMERGENCY, A
11 NATURAL DISASTER, OR ANY OTHER UNANTICIPATED SITUATION, AS FAR IN
12 ADVANCE OF THE MEETING AS PRACTICABLE;
13 (2) MEETING MINUTES FROM THE PORTIONS OF A MEETING THAT
14 WERE HELD IN OPEN SESSION, NOT MORE THAN 2 BUSINESS DAYS AFTER THE
15 MINUTES ARE APPROVED;
16 (3) LIVE VIDEO STREAMING OF EACH OPEN MEETING OF THE MARC
17 RAIL AUTHORITY THAT IS HELD AT:
18 (I) THE HEADQUARTERS OF THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY; OR
19 (II) ANY OTHER LOCATION WHERE THE MARC RAIL
20 AUTHORITY HAS HELD AT LEAST 10 MEETINGS DURING THE IMMEDIATELY
21 PRECEDING CALENDAR YEAR; AND
22 (4) A COMPLETE AND UNEDITED ARCHIVED VIDEO RECORDING OF
23 EACH OPEN MEETING FOR WHICH LIVE VIDEO STREAMING WAS MADE AVAILABLE
24 UNDER ITEM (3) OF THIS SUBSECTION FOR A MINIMUM OF 5 YEARS AFTER THE DATE
25 OF THE MEETING.
26 (B) THE DEPARTMENT OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY SHALL PROVIDE TO
27 THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY THE TECHNICAL STAFF, SUPPORT, AND EQUIPMENT
28 NECESSARY TO LIVESTREAM THE OPEN MEETINGS OF THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY.
29 7–1310.

1 (A) ON AN ANNUAL BASIS, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ALLOCATE TO THE
2 MARC RAIL AUTHORITY THE REVENUE NEEDED TO SUPPORT THE MARC
3 OPERATING AND CAPITAL BUDGETS.
4 (B) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION,
5 MARC FARES AND ANY OTHER REVENUE FROM CHARGES IMPOSED FOR MARC
6 SERVICE MAY BE USED ONLY TO SUPPORT THE MARC RAIL AUTHORITY.
7 (2) TO THE EXTENT REQUIRED UNDER FINANCING AGREEMENTS
8 UNDER TITLE 4, SUBTITLE 3 OF THIS ARTICLE ENTERED INTO ON OR BEFORE
9 OCTOBER 1, 2024, MARC FARES AND ANY OTHER REVENUE FROM CHARGES
10 IMPOSED FOR MARC SERVICE SHALL CONTINUE TO SUPPORT THOSE EXISTING
11 INSTRUMENTS AND OBLIGATIONS.
12 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Maryland Department of
13 Transportation shall transition all MARC operations and contracts to the MARC Rail
14 Authority to the extent permissible by such agreements.
15 (a) There is a Workgroup on the Reorganization of the Maryland Transit
16 Administration.
17 (b) The Workgroup consists of the following members:
18 (1) the Chair of the Appropriations Committee, or the Chair’s designee;
19 (2) the Chair of the Environment and Transportation Committee, or the
20 Chair’s designee;
21 (3) the Chair of the Budget and Taxation Committee, or the Chair’s
22 designee;
23 (4) the Chair of the Budget and Taxation Committee’s Public Safety,
24 Transportation, and Environment Subcommittee, or the Chair’s designee;
25 (5) the Secretary of Transportation, or the Secretary’s designee;
26 (6) the Maryland Transit Administrator, or the Administrator’s designee;
27 (7) the Chair of the Baltimore Region Transit Commission, or the Chair’s
28 designee; and
29 (8) a representative of the local metropolitan planning organization for
30 Baltimore City, designated by the organization.
31 (c) The Secretary of Transportation shall designate the chair of the Workgroup.
HOUSE BILL 517 7
1 (d) The Department of Legislative Services shall provide staff for the Workgroup.
2 (e) A member of the Workgroup:
3 (1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Workgroup; but
4 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
5 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.
6 (f) The Workgroup shall:
7 (1) study issues relating to a reorganization of the Maryland Department
8 of Transportation and the Maryland Transit Administration, including:
9 (i) options for allowing the Administration to continue providing
10 local Baltimore City transit services, including light rail, subway, and bus services and the
11 creation of a new unit or reorganization of the Office of the Secretary to provide statewide
12 transit services;
13 (ii) the current contractual obligations and agreements of the
14 Administration and the process to transfer the obligations and agreements as part of a
15 reorganization of the Department and the Administration; and
16 (iii) the governance changes to the Administration necessary to
17 ensure that Baltimore City has the appropriate oversight and input into local Baltimore
18 City transit service; and
19 (2) make recommendations regarding the reorganization of the
20 Department and the Administration to ensure that:
21 (i) local Baltimore City transit service, including light rail, subway,
22 and bus service, remain as a single unit within the Department and any other functions of
23 the Administration are reorganized into a separate unit within the Department or the
24 Office of the Secretary;
25 (ii) the workforce and personnel of the Administration necessary to
26 operate local Baltimore City transit services remain within the Administration and the
27 workforce and personnel of the Administration necessary to operate statewide transit
28 services are reorganized into a separate unit within the Department or the Office of the
29 Secretary; and
30 (iii) the funding for local Baltimore City transit service is not
31 reduced.
32 (g) On or before December 1, 2025, the Workgroup shall report its findings and
33 recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
34 Government Article, the General Assembly.
8 HOUSE BILL 517
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That it is the intent of the General
2 Assembly that:
3 (1) beginning with the fiscal year 2027 budget, all local Baltimore City
4 transit services, including light rail, subway, and bus service, be included in the Maryland
5 Transit Administration budget and all statewide transit service, including MARC rail
6 service, the construction and operation of the Purple Line, and commuter bus service, be
7 included in the Office of the Secretary’s budget; and
8 (2) on or before December 1, 2025, the Department of Transportation shall
9 submit draft legislation to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
10 Government Article, the General Assembly providing for the reorganization of the
11 Department to ensure that the Maryland Transit Administration provides local Baltimore
12 City transit services, including light rail, subway, and bus service, and a separate unit
13 within the Department provides statewide transit services including MARC rail service,
14 the construction and operation of the Purple Line, and commuter bus service.
15 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
16 1, 2025. It shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and, at the end of June 30, 2026, this
17 Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no
18 further force and effect.
