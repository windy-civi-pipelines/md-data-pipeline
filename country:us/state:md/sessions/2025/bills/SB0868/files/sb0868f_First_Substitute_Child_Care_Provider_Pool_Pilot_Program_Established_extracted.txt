By: Senator Henson
Introduced and read first time: January 28, 2025
Assigned to: Education, Energy, and the Environment
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Substitute Child Care Provider Pool Pilot Program – Established
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Substitute Child Care Provider Pool Pilot Program in
4 the State Department of Education; providing for the purpose, administration, and
5 funding for the Program; and generally relating to the Substitute Child Care
6 Provider Pool Pilot Program.
7 BY adding to
8 Article – Education
9 Section 9.5–118
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Education
15 9.5–118.
16 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
17 INDICATED.
18 (2) “CHILD CARE FACILITY” MEANS:
19 (I) A LICENSED CHILD CARE CENTER;
20 (II) A REGISTERED FAMILY CHILD CARE HOME; OR
21 (III) A REGISTERED LARGE FAMILY CHILD CARE HOME.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0868*
2 SENATE BILL 868
1 (3) “CHILD CARE PROVIDER” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL WHO PROVIDES
2 CARE FOR A CHILD IN A CHILD CARE FACILITY.
3 (4) “PROGRAM” MEANS THE SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE PROVIDER
4 POOL PILOT PROGRAM.
5 (5) “SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE PROVIDER POOL” MEANS ONE OR
6 MORE CHILD CARE PROVIDERS WHO ARE AVAILABLE TO PROVIDE TEMPORARY CARE
7 TO CHILDREN AS A SUBSTITUTE FOR A REGULAR CHILD CARE PROVIDER IN A CHILD
8 CARE FACILITY AND WHO:
9 (I) HAVE THE REQUIRED CREDENTIALS, TRAINING,
10 EXPERIENCE, AND CRIMINAL HISTORY VETTING TO WORK IN A CHILD CARE
11 FACILITY; AND
12 (II) ARE LOCATED IN THE SAME REGION OF THE STATE AS THE
13 CHILD CARE FACILITY PARTICIPATING IN THE PROGRAM.
14 (B) (1) THERE IS A SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE PROVIDER POOL PILOT
15 PROGRAM IN THE DEPARTMENT.
16 (2) THE PURPOSE OF THE PROGRAM IS TO FACILITATE THE
17 CONTINUOUS OPERATION OF CHILD CARE FACILITIES IN THE STATE BY ENSURING
18 THAT QUALIFIED SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE PROVIDERS ARE AVAILABLE TO FILL
19 SHORT–TERM STAFFING NEEDS OF CHILD CARE FACILITIES ON A TEMPORARY BASIS.
20 (3) THE PROGRAM SHALL BEGIN JULY 1, 2026, AND END JUNE 30,
21 2030.
22 (C) (1) (I) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL SELECT A NONPROFIT
23 ORGANIZATION TO ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM THAT MEETS THE REQUIREMENTS
24 OF THIS SUBSECTION.
25 (II) FOR EACH OF FISCAL YEARS 2027 THROUGH 2030, THE
26 DEPARTMENT SHALL AWARD A GRANT OF $350,000 TO THE NONPROFIT
27 ORGANIZATION SELECTED TO ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM.
28 (2) TO BE ELIGIBLE TO ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM AND RECEIVE A
29 GRANT UNDER THIS SECTION, A NONPROFIT ORGANIZATION SHALL:
30 (I) BE A PUBLIC BENEFIT CORPORATION THAT IS EXEMPT
31 FROM TAXATION AS PROVIDED BY LAW;
SENATE BILL 868 3
1 (II) OPERATE THROUGHOUT THE STATE;
2 (III) HAVE EXPERIENCE WORKING WITH CHILD CARE FACILITIES
3 OF ALL TYPES; AND
4 (IV) HAVE EXPERIENCE HELPING INDIVIDUALS MEET THE
5 LEGAL REQUIREMENTS TO WORK AS CHILD CARE PROVIDERS.
6 (D) THE NONPROFIT ORGANIZATION SELECTED BY THE DEPARTMENT
7 SHALL USE THE GRANT TO:
8 (1) ORGANIZE SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE PROVIDER POOLS IN AT
9 LEAST THREE REGIONS IN THE STATE, INCLUDING A RURAL, SUBURBAN, AND URBAN
10 AREA;
11 (2) RECRUIT CREDENTIALED CHILD CARE PROVIDERS TO PROVIDE
12 SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE SERVICES IN MULTIPLE CHILD CARE FACILITIES
13 PARTICIPATING IN THE PROGRAM;
14 (3) ASSIST A SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE PROVIDER WITH MEETING THE
15 REQUIREMENTS TO WORK IN A CHILD CARE FACILITY, INCLUDING BY HELPING THE
16 SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE PROVIDER:
17 (I) OBTAIN A CRIMINAL HISTORY RECORDS CHECK, AS
18 REQUIRED UNDER § 5–551 OF THE FAMILY LAW ARTICLE, AS AN EMPLOYEE, A
19 CONTRACTOR, OR A SUBCONTRACTOR OF THE CHILD CARE FACILITY;
20 (II) MEET ANY EXPERIENTIAL OR EDUCATIONAL
21 REQUIREMENTS; AND
22 (III) COMPLETE ANY REQUIRED TRAINING, INCLUDING HEALTH
23 AND SAFETY TRAINING AND FIRST AID TRAINING;
24 (4) TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE, ASSIST A SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE
25 PROVIDER IN COMPLETING A REGISTERED EARLY EDUCATION APPRENTICESHIP;
26 (5) IDENTIFY AND ORGANIZE, INTO REGIONAL AREAS, CHILD CARE
27 FACILITIES THAT AGREE TO WORK WITH SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE PROVIDERS FROM
28 A POOL ESTABLISHED BY THE PROGRAM;
29 (6) KEEP ANY RECORDS REQUIRED BY THE DEPARTMENT; AND
4 SENATE BILL 868
1 (7) COMPLY WITH ANY OTHER REQUIREMENTS ESTABLISHED BY THE
2 DEPARTMENT.
3 (E) THE NONPROFIT ORGANIZATION SELECTED BY THE DEPARTMENT MAY
4 USE THE GRANT TO:
5 (1) CREATE A STATEWIDE DIGITAL PLATFORM TO FACILITATE THE
6 MATCHING OF SUBSTITUTE CHILD CARE PROVIDERS FROM THE SUBSTITUTE CHILD
7 CARE PROVIDER POOL WITH PARTICIPATING CHILD CARE FACILITIES, INCLUDING
8 PARTNERING WITH A THIRD–PARTY VENDOR; AND
9 (2) INCENTIVIZE PARTICIPATION IN THE PROGRAM BY QUALIFIED
10 CHILD CARE PROVIDERS AND CHILD CARE FACILITIES.
11 (F) FOR EACH OF FISCAL YEARS 2027 THROUGH 2030, THE GOVERNOR
12 SHALL INCLUDE IN THE ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION OF $375,000 FOR
13 THE PROGRAM.
14 (G) EACH YEAR THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP AND PUBLISH GOALS
15 FOR THE PROGRAM.
16 (H) ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 1, 2029, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL REPORT
17 TO THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY, IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE
18 GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, ON THE STATUS OF THE PROGRAM, INCLUDING:
19 (1) AN EVALUATION OF THE PROGRAM; AND
20 (2) WHETHER THE PROGRAM HAS MET THE GOALS SET BY THE
21 DEPARTMENT.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
23 1, 2025. It shall remain effective for a period of 5 years and, at the end of June 30, 2030,
24 this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and
25 of no further force and effect.
