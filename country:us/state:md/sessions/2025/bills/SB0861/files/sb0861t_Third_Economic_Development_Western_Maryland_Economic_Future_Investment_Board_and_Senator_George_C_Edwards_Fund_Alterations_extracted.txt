1 AN ACT concerning
2 Economic Development – Western Maryland Economic Future Investment
3 Board and Senator George C. Edwards Fund – Alterations
4 FOR the purpose of altering the composition of the Western Maryland Economic Future
5 Investment Board; providing that the Executive Director serves as a nonvoting
6 member of the Board, except under a certain circumstance; altering the purpose of
7 the Senator George C. Edwards Fund; extending the fiscal years in which the
8 Governor is required to may include a certain appropriation in the annual budget
9 bill to the Fund; altering the criteria that the Board must consider when awarding
10 certain grant and loan funding from the Fund; and generally relating to the Western
11 Maryland Economic Future Investment Board and the Senator George C. Edwards
12 Fund.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Economic Development
15 Section 13–737, 13–739, and 13–740
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
20 Article – Economic Development

5 (2) (I) In each of fiscal years 2024 through [2026] 2031, the Governor
6 shall include in the annual operating or capital budget bill an appropriation of at least
7 $10,000,000 to the Fund.
8 (II) IN EACH OF FISCAL YEARS 2027 THROUGH 2031, THE
9 GOVERNOR MAY INCLUDE IN THE ANNUAL OPERATING OR CAPITAL BUDGET BILL AN
10 APPROPRIATION OF AT LEAST $10,000,000 TO THE FUND.
11 (e) Money in the Fund may be used only to:
12 (1) award grants and provide loans [to capital infrastructure projects and
13 business development projects to improve the economic conditions in the region] IN
14 ACCORDANCE WITH § 13–740 OF THIS SUBTITLE; and
15 (2) pay the costs necessary to administer the Fund, including paying the
16 salary of the Executive Director.
17 (f) (1) The Fund is a special, nonlapsing fund that is not subject to reversion
18 under § 7–302 of the State Finance and Procurement Article.
19 (2) The State Treasurer shall hold the Fund separately, and the
20 Comptroller shall account for the Fund.
21 (3) The State Treasurer shall invest the money in the Fund in the same
22 manner as other State money may be invested.
23 (4) Any investment earnings of the Fund shall be credited to the Fund.

25 (a) A member county or local entity within a member county may submit a
26 proposal for funding under this part.
27 (b) To qualify for funding, a project shall be:
28 (1) a capital infrastructure project that:

1 (i) is linked to economic development in the region, as approved by
2 the Board; and
3 (ii) receives 20% matching funds from the member county where the
4 project is located, another local entity, a private business, or a combination thereof;
5 (2) a business development project that:
6 (i) encourages and promotes downtown neighborhood
7 revitalization, small business tourism, or other areas as approved by the Board; and
8 (ii) receives 10% matching funds from the member county where the
9 project is located, another local entity, a private business, or a combination thereof; or
10 (3) any other project that the Board determines is appropriate to promote
11 economic development in the region.
12 (c) (1) (i) The Board shall develop criteria to review, evaluate, and rate
13 project proposals for funding under this part.
14 (ii) The criteria in subparagraph (i) of this paragraph shall ensure
15 that the project:
16 1. EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN ITEM 2 OF THIS
17 SUBPARAGRAPH, creates or retains AT LEAST 25 jobs;
18 2. creates or retains [businesses] AT LEAST 10 JOBS EACH
19 WITH WAGES THAT MEET OR EXCEED 150% OF THE MEDIAN HOUSEHOLD INCOME
20 FOR THE MEMBER COUNTY IN WHICH THE PROJECT IS LOCATED, AS DETERMINED
21 BY THE MOST RECENT AVAILABLE DATA FROM THE STATE; OR
22 3. [increases wages; or
23 4.] creates [new State or local tax revenue] SIGNIFICANT
24 POPULATION GROWTH IN THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY IN WHICH THE PROJECT IS
25 LOCATED, AS CERTIFIED IN WRITING TO THE BOARD BY THE COUNTY
26 COMMISSIONERS FOR THE MEMBER COUNTY OR THE MUNICIPAL GOVERNMENT FOR
27 THE MUNICIPALITY IN WHICH THE PROJECT IS LOCATED.
28 (2) (i) The Board shall choose which projects to award grants and loans
29 to based on the criteria developed in accordance with paragraph (1) of this subsection.
30 (ii) The Board or the Executive Director shall distribute grants and
31 loans to projects based on the decisions made by the Board in accordance with
32 subparagraph (i) of this paragraph.
6 SENATE BILL 861
1 (d) (1) The Board or the Executive Director shall monitor and track the
2 progress of each project that receives funding under this part.
3 (2) If the Board determines that sufficient progress is not being made
4 toward achieving the requirements of this section, the Board may reclaim the awarded
5 funds.
6 (e) The funding provided in this section shall be used to supplement and not
7 supplant any funds that would otherwise be provided to the Board or the member counties.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
9 1, 2025.
