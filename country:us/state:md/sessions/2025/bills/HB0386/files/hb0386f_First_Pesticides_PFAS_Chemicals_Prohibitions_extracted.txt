HB 1190/24 – HGO CF SB 345
By: Delegates Ruth, Allen, Bagnall, Bartlett, Ebersole, Embry, Fair, Feldmark,
Foley, Guyton, Guzzone, Kaufman, Lehman, R. Lewis, J. Long, McCaskill,
Pasteur, Phillips, Ruff, Simpson, Terrasa, Woods, and Woorman
Introduced and read first time: January 16, 2025
Assigned to: Health and Government Operations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Pesticides – PFAS Chemicals – Prohibitions
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Agriculture to develop and maintain a list
4 of certain registered pesticides that list PFAS chemicals as an active ingredient on
5 the labeling accompanying the pesticide; prohibiting a person from using, for certain
6 purposes and beginning on certain dates, certain PFAS pesticides; prohibiting,
7 beginning on a certain date, the Department from registering certain PFAS
8 pesticides for sale in the State; and generally relating to pesticides and PFAS
9 chemicals.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
11 Article – Agriculture
12 Section 5–101(a) and (b) and 5–201(a), (c), (g), and (l)
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
15 BY adding to
16 Article – Agriculture
17 Section 5–210.6
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
22 Article – Agriculture
23 5–101.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 386
1 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
2 (b) “Active ingredient” means:
3 (1) In the case of a pesticide other than a plant regulator, defoliant, or
4 desiccant, an ingredient which prevents, destroys, repels, or mitigates insects, nematodes,
5 fungi, rodents, weeds, bacteria, or other pests;
6 (2) In the case of a plant regulator, an ingredient which, through
7 physiological action, accelerates or retards the rate of growth or rate of maturation or
8 otherwise alters the behavior of ornamental or crop plants or their produce;
9 (3) In the case of a defoliant, an ingredient which causes the leaves or
10 foliage to drop from a plant; and
11 (4) In the case of a desiccant, an ingredient which artificially accelerates
12 the drying of plant tissue.
13 5–201.
14 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
15 (c) “Certified applicator” means a person who is certified by the Secretary under
16 this subtitle.
17 (g) “Labeling” means all written, printed, or graphic matter:
18 (1) Accompanying the pesticide or device at any time; or
19 (2) To which reference is made on the label or in literature accompanying
20 the pesticide or device, except to current official publications of the:
21 (i) Environmental Protection Agency;
22 (ii) United States Departments of Agriculture, Interior, and Health
23 and Human Services;
24 (iii) State experiment stations;
25 (iv) State agricultural colleges; or
26 (v) Other similar federal or State institutions or agencies authorized
27 by law to conduct research in the field of pesticides.
28 (l) “Pesticide” means any substance or mixture of substances intended for:
29 (1) Preventing, destroying, repelling, or mitigating any pest;
HOUSE BILL 386 3
1 (2) Use as a plant regulator, defoliant, or desiccant; or
2 (3) Use as a spray adjuvant such as a wetting agent or adhesive.
3 5–210.6.
4 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
5 INDICATED.
6 (2) “ACTIVE INGREDIENT” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 5–101 OF
7 THIS TITLE.
8 (3) “PFAS CHEMICALS” MEANS A CLASS OF FLUORINATED
9 CHEMICALS THAT CONTAIN AT LEAST ONE FULLY FLUORINATED CARBON ATOM,
10 INCLUDING PERFLUOROALKYL AND POLYFLUOROALKYL SUBSTANCES.
11 (4) “PFAS PESTICIDE” MEANS A PESTICIDE THAT HAS PFAS
12 CHEMICALS LISTED AS AN ACTIVE INGREDIENT ON THE LABELING.
13 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP AND MAINTAIN A LIST OF
14 EACH PFAS PESTICIDE THAT IS REGISTERED WITH THE SECRETARY IN
15 ACCORDANCE WITH SUBTITLE 1 OF THIS TITLE.
16 (2) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1, 2026, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL:
17 (I) POST THE LIST DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION ON
18 ITS WEBSITE; AND
19 (II) DISTRIBUTE THE LIST TO ALL CERTIFIED APPLICATORS.
20 (C) (1) BEGINNING JUNE 1, 2026, A PERSON MAY NOT USE A PFAS
21 PESTICIDE LISTED BY THE DEPARTMENT UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION:
22 (I) AT A HEALTH CARE FACILITY;
23 (II) AT A SCHOOL;
24 (III) AT A DAY CARE OPERATION;
25 (IV) FOR RESIDENTIAL LAWN CARE; OR
26 (V) FOR COMMERCIAL MOSQUITO SPRAYING OPERATIONS.
4 HOUSE BILL 386
1 (2) BEFORE JUNE 1, 2026, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL NOTIFY ALL
2 CERTIFIED APPLICATORS OF THE PROHIBITION IN PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
3 SUBSECTION.
4 (D) BEGINNING JUNE 1, 2027, THE DEPARTMENT MAY NOT REGISTER A
5 PFAS PESTICIDE FOR SALE IN THE STATE.
6 (E) BEGINNING JUNE 1, 2028, A PERSON MAY NOT USE A PFAS PESTICIDE
7 IN THE STATE.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
9 October 1, 2025.
