18 Article – Election Law
19 8–202.
20 (a) A principal political party, as determined by the statement of registration
21 issued by the State Board:
22 (1) shall use the primary election to:
23 (i) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN § 8–402 OF THIS TITLE, nominate its
24 candidates for public office; and
25 (ii) elect all members of the local central committees of the political
26 party; and
27 (2) may use the primary election in the year of a presidential election to
28 elect delegates to a national presidential nominating convention.
29 8–401.
30 (a) A special primary election [and] OR a special general election may be held at
31 a time other than the date of a regular primary election [and] OR a regular general election:
32 (1) to fill a vacancy in the office of Representative in Congress; or
SENATE BILL 262 3
1 (2) to fill a vacancy in the county council or in the office of chief executive
2 officer or county executive if the charter of that county provides for special elections.
3 (b) (1) Special elections to fill a vacancy in the office of Representative in
4 Congress shall be held at the time specified in Subtitle 7 of this title.
5 (2) (i) [Special] SUBJECT TO SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS
6 PARAGRAPH, SPECIAL elections to fill vacancies in a county council or in the office of chief
7 executive officer or county executive shall be held as provided in the county charter.
8 (ii) The date set for the special election to fill a vacancy under this
9 paragraph [shall]:
10 1. SHALL BE AT LEAST 90 DAYS AFTER THE ADOPTION OF
11 THE RESOLUTION OF THE COUNTY COUNCIL ESTABLISHING THE DATE OF THE
12 SPECIAL ELECTION;
13 2. MAY NOT BE WITHIN 120 DAYS OF BEFORE OR AFTER
14 A REGULARLY SCHEDULED ELECTION, BUT MAY BE CONCURRENT WITH A
15 REGULARLY SCHEDULED ELECTION; AND
16 3. SHALL allow at least 45 days between the date a local
17 board makes an absentee ballot available to an absent uniformed services voter or overseas
18 voter, as defined under the Uniformed and Overseas Citizens Absentee Voting Act, and the
19 date of the special election.
20 8–402.
21 (A) THE RESOLUTION OF A COUNTY COUNCIL ESTABLISHING THE DATE OF
22 A SPECIAL ELECTION TO FILL A VACANCY IN THE COUNTY COUNCIL OR IN THE
23 OFFICE OF CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER OR COUNTY EXECUTIVE MAY DIRECT THAT:
24 (1) A SPECIAL PRIMARY ELECTION NOT BE HELD; AND
25 (2) THE VACANCY BE FILLED SOLELY THROUGH A SPECIAL GENERAL
26 ELECTION.
27 (B) IF THE COUNTY COUNCIL DIRECTS THAT A SPECIAL PRIMARY ELECTION
28 NOT BE HELD, THE RESOLUTION OF THE COUNTY COUNCIL ESTABLISHING THE DATE
29 OF THE SPECIAL ELECTION SHALL SPECIFY ONE OF THE FOLLOWING METHODS FOR
30 CONDUCTING THE SPECIAL GENERAL ELECTION:
31 (1) A PARTISAN ELECTION IN WHICH THERE ARE NO PARTY NOMINEES
32 BUT EACH FILED CANDIDATE APPEARS ON THE BALLOT WITH THE CANDIDATE’S
33 PARTY AFFILIATION, IF ANY; OR
4 SENATE BILL 262
1 (2) A NONPARTISAN ELECTION IN WHICH THERE ARE NO PARTY
2 NOMINEES AND EACH FILED CANDIDATE APPEARS ON THE BALLOT WITHOUT THE
3 CANDIDATE’S PARTY AFFILIATION.
4 8–403.
5 IF THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR DETERMINES THAT A CONFLICT EXISTS
6 BETWEEN A COUNTY CHARTER OR OTHER COUNTY LAW RELATING TO THE CONDUCT
7 OF A SPECIAL ELECTION AND THE PROVISIONS OF THIS ARTICLE OR THE CODE OF
8 MARYLAND REGULATIONS, THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR SHALL CONDUCT THE
9 SPECIAL ELECTION ENTIRELY IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS ARTICLE AND THE CODE
10 OF MARYLAND REGULATIONS.
11 9–502.
12 (a) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, a local board
13 shall mail by nonforwardable mail a vote–by–mail ballot to each registered voter who is
14 eligible to vote in a special election.
15 (2) A local board is not required to mail a vote–by–mail ballot to a voter if
16 the voter has requested that the ballot be sent by other means.
17 (3) A LOCAL BOARD IS NOT REQUIRED TO MAIL SPECIMEN BALLOTS
18 TO THE REGISTERED VOTERS OF THE COUNTY UNDER § 8–102 OF THIS ARTICLE.
19 (d) A local board shall send a vote–by–mail ballot to each eligible voter at least
20 [14] 30 days before the day of a special election.
21 9–504.
22 (a) A voter may return a vote–by–mail ballot to a local board:
23 (1) by mail;
24 (2) in person during regular office hours; [or]
25 (3) THROUGH A BALLOT DROP BOX; OR
26 (4) through a duly authorized agent in accordance with subsection (b) of
27 this section.
28 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act is an emergency
29 measure, is necessary for the immediate preservation of the public health or safety, has
30 been passed by a yea and nay vote supported by three–fifths of all the members elected to
SENATE BILL 262 5
1 each of the two Houses of the General Assembly, and shall take effect from the date it is
2 enacted.
