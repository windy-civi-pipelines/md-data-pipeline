2 SENATE BILL 294
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – State Government
4 9–2901.
5 (b) There is a Maryland Cybersecurity Council.
6 (c) The Council consists of the following members:
7 (1) the Attorney General, or the Attorney General’s designee;
8 (2) the Secretary of Information Technology, or the Secretary’s designee;
9 (3) the Secretary of State Police, or the Secretary’s designee;
10 (4) the Secretary of Commerce, or the Secretary’s designee;
11 (5) the Adjutant General, or the Adjutant General’s designee;
12 (6) the State Administrator of Elections, or the State Administrator’s
13 designee;
14 (7) the Executive Director of the Governor’s Office of Homeland Security,
15 or the Executive Director’s designee;
16 (8) the Director of the Maryland Coordination and Analysis Center, or the
17 Director’s designee;
18 (9) the Secretary of Emergency Management, or the Secretary’s designee;
19 (10) the Chief Executive Officer of the Maryland Technology Development
20 Corporation, or the Chief Executive Officer’s designee;
21 (11) the Chair of the Tech Council of Maryland, or the Chair’s designee;
22 (12) the President of the Fort Meade Alliance, or the President’s designee;
23 (13) the President of the Army Alliance, or the President’s designee; and
24 (14) the following members appointed by the [Attorney General]
25 GOVERNOR:
26 (i) five representatives of cybersecurity companies located in the
27 State, with at least three representing cybersecurity companies with 50 or fewer employees;
SENATE BILL 294 3
1 (ii) four representatives from statewide or regional business
2 associations;
3 (iii) up to ten representatives from institutions of higher education
4 located in the State;
5 (iv) one representative of a crime victims organization;
6 (v) four representatives from industries that may be susceptible to
7 attacks on cybersecurity, including at least one representative of a bank, whether or not
8 State–chartered, that has a branch in the State;
9 (vi) two representatives of organizations that have expertise in
10 electronic health care records; and
11 (vii) any other stakeholder that the [Attorney General] GOVERNOR
12 determines appropriate.
13 (f) The [Attorney General] GOVERNOR also shall invite, as appropriate, the
14 following representatives of federal agencies to serve on the Council:
15 (1) the Director of the National Security Agency, or the Director’s designee;
16 (2) the Secretary of Homeland Security, or the Secretary’s designee;
17 (3) the Director of the Defense Information Systems Agency, or the
18 Director’s designee;
19 (4) the Director of the Intelligence Advanced Research Projects Activity, or
20 the Director’s designee; and
21 (5) any other federal agency that the Attorney General determines
22 appropriate.
23 [(g) The Attorney General, or the Attorney General’s designee, shall chair the
24 Council.]
25 (G) BEGINNING OCTOBER 1, 2025, AND EVERY 2 YEARS THEREAFTER, THE
26 COUNCIL SHALL ELECT A CHAIR FROM AMONG THE MEMBERS OF THE COUNCIL.
27 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
28 October 1, 2025.
