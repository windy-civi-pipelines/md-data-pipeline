CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Cybersecurity Council – Membership – Alterations
3 FOR the purpose of altering the selection of the membership and chair of the Maryland
4 Cybersecurity Council; requiring the Council, working with certain entities, to assess
5 and address cybersecurity threats and associated risks from artificial intelligence
6 and quantum computing; and generally relating to the Maryland Cybersecurity
7 Council membership.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
9 Article – State Government
10 Section 9–2901(b)
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

14 Article – State Government
15 Section 9–2901(c) and, (f), and (j)
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 BY repealing
19 Article – State Government
20 Section 9–2901(g)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland

20 BEGINNING OCTOBER 1, 2025, AND EVERY 2 YEARS THEREAFTER, THE COUNCIL
21 SHALL ELECT A CHAIR AND VICE CHAIR FROM AMONG THE MEMBERS OF THE
22 COUNCIL.
23 (2) ONE SHALL BE A STATE EMPLOYEE AND ONE SHALL BE A
24 NON–STATE EMPLOYEE.
25 (j) The Council shall work with the National Institute of Standards and
26 Technology and other federal agencies, private sector businesses, NONPROFITS, and
27 private cybersecurity experts to ASSESS AND ADDRESS CYBERSECURITY THREATS AND
28 ASSOCIATED RISKS FROM ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE AND QUANTUM COMPUTING
29 TO:
30 (1) for critical infrastructure [not covered by federal law or the Executive
31 Order], review and conduct risk assessments to determine which local infrastructure
6 SENATE BILL 294
1 sectors are at the greatest risk of cyber attacks and need the most enhanced cybersecurity
2 measures;
3 (2) use federal guidance to identify categories of critical infrastructure as
4 critical cyber infrastructure if cyber damage or unauthorized cyber access to the
5 infrastructure could reasonably result in catastrophic consequences, including:
6 (i) interruption in the provision of energy, water, transportation,
7 emergency services, food, or other life–sustaining services sufficient to cause a mass
8 casualty event or mass evacuations;
9 (ii) catastrophic economic damage; or
10 (iii) severe degradation of State or national security;
11 (3) assist infrastructure entities that are not covered by the Executive
12 Order in complying with federal cybersecurity guidance;
13 (4) assist private sector cybersecurity businesses in adopting, adapting,
14 and implementing the National Institute of Standards and Technology cybersecurity
15 framework of standards and practices;
16 (5) examine inconsistencies between State and federal laws regarding
17 cybersecurity;
18 (6) recommend a comprehensive State strategic plan to ensure a
19 coordinated and adaptable response to and recovery from cybersecurity attacks; [and]
20 (7) ADDRESS SENSITIVE PRIVACY INTERESTS OF STATE RESIDENTS
21 RELATED TO CYBERSECURITY AND ASSOCIATED RISKS;
22 (8) ADDRESS EMERGING THREATS POSED BY ARTIFICIAL
23 INTELLIGENCE, INCLUDING:
24 (I) ADVERSARIAL ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE;
25 (II) CYBER ATTACKS;
26 (III) DEEPFAKE TECHNOLOGIES;
27 (IV) UNETHICAL USE; AND
28 (V) FRAUD; AND
29 [(7)] (9) recommend any legislative changes considered necessary by the
30 Council to address cybersecurity issues.

1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That it is the intent of the General
2 Assembly that the Maryland Cybersecurity Council reviews and adjusts its subcommittee
3 structure, if necessary, and implements appropriate bylaws of operation consistent with
4 State law by December 1, 2025.
5 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
6 October 1, 2025.
