By: Senator Simonaire
Requested: May 18, 2024
Introduced and read first time: January 8, 2025
Assigned to: Judicial Proceedings
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Boating Accidents – Duty to Remain at the Scene, Render Assistance, and
3 Provide Information – Penalties
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the operator of a vessel involved in a collision, accident, or
5 other casualty to return to and remain at the scene of the collision, accident, or
6 casualty until the operator has rendered certain assistance and provided certain
7 information; establishing criminal penalties for the operator of a vessel involved in
8 a collision, accident, or other casualty who does not take certain actions; and
9 generally relating to the operator of a vessel involved in a collision, accident, or other
10 casualty.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
12 Article – Natural Resources
13 Section 8–724
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
16 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
17 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
18 Article – Natural Resources
19 8–724.
20 (a) (1) (I) THE OPERATOR OF A VESSEL INVOLVED IN A COLLISION,
21 ACCIDENT, OR OTHER CASUALTY THAT RESULTS IN BODILY INJURY TO ANOTHER
22 PERSON IMMEDIATELY SHALL STOP THE VESSEL AS CLOSE AS POSSIBLE TO THE
23 SCENE OF THE COLLISION, ACCIDENT, OR OTHER CASUALTY.

1 (II) THE OPERATOR OF A VESSEL INVOLVED IN A COLLISION,

3 PERSON IMMEDIATELY SHALL RETURN TO AND REMAIN AT THE SCENE OF THE

8 PERSON IMMEDIATELY SHALL STOP THE VESSEL AS CLOSE AS POSSIBLE TO THE
9 SCENE OF THE COLLISION, ACCIDENT, OR OTHER CASUALTY.
10 (II) THE OPERATOR OF A VESSEL INVOLVED IN A COLLISION,
11 ACCIDENT, OR OTHER CASUALTY THAT RESULTS IN THE DEATH OF ANOTHER
12 PERSON IMMEDIATELY SHALL RETURN TO AND REMAIN AT THE SCENE OF THE
13 COLLISION, ACCIDENT, OR OTHER CASUALTY UNTIL THE OPERATOR HAS COMPLIED
14 WITH SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
15 (3) (I) IN THIS PARAGRAPH, “SERIOUS BODILY INJURY” MEANS AN
16 INJURY THAT:
17 1. CREATES A SUBSTANTIAL RISK OF DEATH;
18 2. CAUSES SERIOUS PERMANENT OR SERIOUS
19 PROTRACTED DISFIGUREMENT;
20 3. CAUSES SERIOUS PERMANENT OR SERIOUS
21 PROTRACTED LOSS OF THE FUNCTION OF ANY BODY PART, ORGAN, OR MENTAL
22 FACULTY; OR
23 4. CAUSES SERIOUS PERMANENT OR SERIOUS
24 PROTRACTED IMPAIRMENT OF THE FUNCTION OF ANY BODY PART OR ORGAN.
25 (II) 1. EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBPARAGRAPH (III) OF
26 THIS PARAGRAPH, A PERSON CONVICTED OF A VIOLATION OF PARAGRAPH (1) OF
27 THIS SUBSECTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 1 YEAR OR A FINE
28 NOT EXCEEDING $3,000 OR BOTH.
29 2. EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBPARAGRAPH (III) OF
30 THIS PARAGRAPH, A PERSON CONVICTED OF A VIOLATION OF PARAGRAPH (2) OF
31 THIS SUBSECTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 5 YEARS OR A
32 FINE NOT EXCEEDING $5,000 OR BOTH.
33 (III) 1. A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SUBSECTION AND

1 WHO KNEW OR REASONABLY SHOULD HAVE KNOWN THAT THE COLLISION,
2 ACCIDENT, OR OTHER CASUALTY MIGHT RESULT IN SERIOUS BODILY INJURY TO
3 ANOTHER PERSON AND SERIOUS BODILY INJURY ACTUALLY OCCURRED TO
4 ANOTHER PERSON IS GUILTY OF A FELONY AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO
5 IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 5 YEARS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $5,000 OR
6 BOTH.
7 2. A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SUBSECTION AND
8 WHO KNEW OR REASONABLY SHOULD HAVE KNOWN THAT THE COLLISION,
9 ACCIDENT, OR OTHER CASUALTY MIGHT RESULT IN THE DEATH OF ANOTHER
10 PERSON AND DEATH ACTUALLY OCCURRED TO ANOTHER PERSON IS GUILTY OF A
11 FELONY AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 10
12 YEARS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $10,000 OR BOTH.
13 (B) (1) The operator of a vessel involved in a collision, accident, or other
14 casualty shall [render]:
15 (I) RENDER all practical and necessary assistance to persons
16 affected by the collision, accident, or casualty to save the persons from danger caused by
17 the collision, accident, or casualty, to the extent the operator can do so without serious
18 danger to the operator’s own vessel, or persons aboard[. The operator also shall give]; AND
19 (II) GIVE the operator’s name, address, and the identification of the
20 operator’s vessel to any person injured and to the owner of any property damaged.
21 (2) The duties imposed by this subsection are in addition to any duties
22 otherwise imposed by law.
23 (3) A PERSON CONVICTED OF A VIOLATION OF THIS SUBSECTION IS
24 SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 2 MONTHS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING
25 $500 OR BOTH.
26 [(b)] (C) If an accident involves any vessel subject to this subtitle while on the
27 waters of the State and results in the death, disappearance, or injury of any person or in
28 property damage of $2,000 or more, or there is complete loss of the vessel, the operator shall
29 file with the Department a full description of the accident, including any information the
30 Department requires by regulation. If the operator is unable to file the report, the vessel
31 owner shall file the report. If the accident caused the death or disappearance of any person
32 or a person receives medical treatment beyond first aid, the report shall be made within 48
33 hours. Any other accident resulting in personal injury or property damage shall be reported
34 within 10 days. This subsection does not apply to a vessel required to have a certificate of
35 inspection under Chapter 1, Title 46, Code of Federal Regulations.
36 [(c)] (D) If any vessel numbered in the State is involved in an accident on waters
37 outside of the State, and the accident results in the death, disappearance, or injury of any
4 SENATE BILL 123
1 person or in property damage of $2,000 or more, the operator or owner, within 30 days,
2 shall file a complete report with the Department including any information the Department
3 requires by regulation. This subsection does not exempt or excuse any operator or owner
4 from the requirements of any federal or other state law or rule or regulation having
5 jurisdiction over the waters in which the accident occurs. Furthermore, any
6 accident–reporting requirement in the federal or other state law or rule or regulation may
7 not exonerate or excuse any failure of the operator or owner of a vessel numbered in the
8 State to report the accident in the State.
9 [(d)] (E) The required report of a boating accident may not be referred to during
10 any judicial proceeding. It is not subject to subpoena or admissible as evidence in any
11 proceeding. Subject to these restrictions, information contained in a boating accident report
12 and any statistical information based on the report is available on request for official
13 purposes to the U.S. Coast Guard and its successor agency.
14 [(e)] (F) Any person who complies with subsection [(a)] (B) of this section or who
15 gratuitously and in good faith renders or attempts to render assistance to any vessel in
16 distress on any waters of the State without objection from any person assisted, is not liable
17 for any civil damage as the result of any act or omission by the person in rendering
18 assistance, if the act or omission does not amount to gross negligence.
19 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
20 October 1, 2025.
