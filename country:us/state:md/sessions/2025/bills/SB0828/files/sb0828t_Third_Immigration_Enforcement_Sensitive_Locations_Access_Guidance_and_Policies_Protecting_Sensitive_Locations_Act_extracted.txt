P1 EMERGENCY BILL 5lr3006
By: Senator Smith
Introduced and read first time: January 28, 2025
Assigned to: Judicial Proceedings
Committee Report: Favorable with amendments
Senate action: Adopted
Read second time: February 26, 2025
CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Immigration Enforcement – Sensitive Locations – Guidelines Access, Guidance,
3 and Policies
4 (Protecting Sensitive Locations Act)
5 FOR the purpose of prohibiting certain schools, libraries, and units of State government
6 that operate at certain sensitive locations from allowing certain federal personnel to
7 access certain areas, subject to certain exceptions; requiring the Attorney General to
8 develop guidelines relating to guidance regarding immigration enforcement at
9 certain sensitive locations; requiring the Attorney General to develop guidance
10 regarding individuals interacting with certain federal personnel; requiring State
11 agencies certain schools, libraries, and units of State government that operate at
12 certain sensitive locations to adopt certain policies or provide certain written notice
13 of a decision not to adopt certain policies; to adopt policies consistent with certain
14 guidance; and generally relating to immigration enforcement.
15 BY adding to
16 Article – Criminal Procedure
17 Section 2–104.2
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2018 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
20 BY adding to
21 Article – State Government
22 Section 6–111
23 Annotated Code of Maryland

2 SENATE BILL 828
1 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
2 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
3 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
4 Article – Criminal Procedure
5 2–104.2.
6 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “SENSITIVE LOCATION” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN
7 § 6–111 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE.
8 (B) (1) THIS SUBSECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO A STATE OR LOCAL
9 CORRECTIONAL FACILITY.
10 (2) A PUBLIC SCHOOL, A PUBLIC LIBRARY, OR A UNIT OF THE
11 EXECUTIVE BRANCH OF STATE GOVERNMENT THAT OPERATES AT A SENSITIVE
12 LOCATION SHALL DENY ACCESS TO ANY PORTION OF THE SENSITIVE LOCATION THAT
13 IS NOT ACCESSIBLE TO THE GENERAL PUBLIC TO ANY INDIVIDUAL WHO IS SEEKING
14 ACCESS FOR THE PURPOSE OF ENFORCING FEDERAL IMMIGRATION LAW, UNLESS:
15 (I) THE INDIVIDUAL PRESENTS A VALID WARRANT ISSUED BY A
16 FEDERAL COURT OR A COURT OF THE STATE; OR
17 (II) EXIGENT CIRCUMSTANCES EXIST.
18 Article – State Government
19 6–111.
20 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “SENSITIVE LOCATION” MEANS:
21 (1) A SCHOOL, INCLUDING A PRESCHOOL, PRIMARY OR SECONDARY
22 SCHOOL, VOCATIONAL OR TRADE SCHOOL, OR COLLEGE OR UNIVERSITY;
23 (2) A MEDICAL OR MENTAL HEALTH CARE FACILITY, INCLUDING A
24 HOSPITAL, A DOCTOR’S OFFICE, A HEALTH CLINIC, A VACCINATION OR TESTING
25 SITE, AN URGENT CARE CENTER, A SITE THAT SERVES PREGNANT INDIVIDUALS, OR
26 A COMMUNITY HEALTH CENTER;
27 (3) A PLACE OF WORSHIP OR RELIGIOUS STUDY, WHETHER IN A
28 STRUCTURE DEDICATED TO ACTIVITIES OF FAITH OR A TEMPORARY FACILITY OR
29 LOCATION WHERE ACTIVITIES OF FAITH ARE TAKING PLACE;
SENATE BILL 828 3
1 (4) A PLACE WHERE CHILDREN GATHER, INCLUDING A PLAYGROUND,
2 RECREATION CENTER, CHILD CARE CENTER, BEFORE– OR AFTER–SCHOOL CARE
3 CENTER, FOSTER CARE FACILITY, GROUP HOME FOR CHILDREN, OR SCHOOL BUS
4 STOP;
5 (5) A SOCIAL SERVICES ESTABLISHMENT, INCLUDING A CRISIS
6 CENTER, DOMESTIC VIOLENCE SHELTER, VICTIMS SERVICES CENTER, CHILD
7 ADVOCACY CENTER, SUPERVISED VISITATION CENTER, FAMILY JUSTICE CENTER,
8 COMMUNITY–BASED ORGANIZATION, FACILITY THAT SERVES DISABLED PERSONS,
9 HOMELESS SHELTER, DRUG OR ALCOHOL COUNSELING AND TREATMENT FACILITY,
10 OR FOOD BANK OR PANTRY OR OTHER ESTABLISHMENT DISTRIBUTING FOOD OR
11 OTHER ESSENTIALS OF LIFE TO PEOPLE IN NEED;
12 (6) A PLACE WHERE DISASTER OR EMERGENCY RESPONSE AND
13 RELIEF IS BEING PROVIDED, INCLUDING ALONG EVACUATION ROUTES, WHERE
14 SHELTER OR EMERGENCY SUPPLIES, FOOD, OR WATER ARE BEING DISTRIBUTED, OR
15 REGISTRATION FOR DISASTER–RELATED ASSISTANCE OR FAMILY REUNIFICATION IS
16 UNDERWAY;
17 (7) A PLACE WHERE A FUNERAL, GRAVESIDE CEREMONY, ROSARY,
18 WEDDING, OR OTHER RELIGIOUS OR CIVIL CEREMONY OR OBSERVANCE OCCURS;
19 (8) A PLACE WHERE THERE IS AN ONGOING PARADE,
20 DEMONSTRATION, OR RALLY;
21 (9) A COURTHOUSE; OR
22 (10) ANY OTHER LOCATION DESIGNATED BY THE ATTORNEY
23 GENERAL.
24 (B) (1) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL, IN CONSULTATION WITH
25 APPROPRIATE STAKEHOLDERS, SHALL DEVELOP GUIDELINES FOR ENTITIES
26 OPERATING AT SENSITIVE LOCATIONS THAT LIMIT IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT AT
27 SENSITIVE LOCATIONS TO THE FULLEST EXTENT POSSIBLE CONSISTENT WITH
28 FEDERAL AND STATE LAW.
29 (2) A STATE AGENCY THAT OPERATES AT A SENSITIVE LOCATION
30 SHALL:
31 (I) ADOPT POLICIES CONSISTENT WITH THE GUIDELINES
32 DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION; OR
4 SENATE BILL 828
1 (II) PROVIDE WRITTEN NOTICE TO THE ATTORNEY GENERAL OF
2 THE AGENCY’S DECISION NOT TO ADOPT POLICIES CONSISTENT WITH THE
3 GUIDELINES DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION, INCLUDING:
4 1. AN EXPLANATION OF THE REASONS FOR NOT
5 ADOPTING POLICIES; AND
6 2. COPIES OF ANY EXISTING POLICIES RELATING TO
7 IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT AT THE SENSITIVE LOCATION.
8 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “SENSITIVE LOCATION” MEANS:
9 (1) A PUBLIC SCHOOL;
10 (2) A PUBLIC LIBRARY;
11 (3) A HEALTH CARE FACILITY OPERATED BY A UNIT OF STATE
12 GOVERNMENT;
13 (4) A FACILITY OPERATED BY THE COMPTROLLER;
14 (5) A COURTHOUSE; OR
15 (6) ANY OTHER LOCATION THAT:
16 (I) PROVIDES STATE–FUNDED SERVICES RELATED TO:
17 1. PHYSICAL OR MENTAL HEALTH;
18 2. EDUCATION; OR
19 3. SHELTER CARE; AND
20 (II) AS DETERMINED BY THE ATTORNEY GENERAL, REQUIRES
21 SPECIAL CONSIDERATION FOR IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT ACTIVITIES.
22 (B) (1) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL SHALL DEVELOP AND PUBLISH
23 GUIDANCE REGARDING IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT AT SENSITIVE LOCATIONS
24 THAT INFORMS THE PUBLIC AND RELEVANT STATE AGENCIES ABOUT:
25 (I) THE LIMITATIONS ON FEDERAL IMMIGRATION
26 ENFORCEMENT ACTIVITIES AT SENSITIVE LOCATIONS; AND
27 (II) THE RESPONSIBILITIES AND RESTRICTIONS ON STATE
28 AGENCIES THAT AFFECT THE AGENCIES’ ABILITY TO ENSURE COMPLIANCE WITH
SENATE BILL 828 5
1 FEDERAL AND STATE LAW WHILE MAINTAINING PUBLIC SAFETY AND
2 ACCESSIBILITY.
3 (C) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL SHALL DEVELOP AND PUBLISH GUIDANCE
4 REGARDING THE RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES OF INDIVIDUALS WHO INTERACT
5 WITH FEDERAL LAW ENFORCEMENT PERSONNEL ACTING TO ENFORCE FEDERAL
6 IMMIGRATION LAW.
7 (D) ON OR BEFORE OCTOBER 1, 2025, EACH PUBLIC SCHOOL, PUBLIC
8 LIBRARY, AND UNIT OF THE EXECUTIVE BRANCH OF STATE GOVERNMENT THAT
9 OPERATES AT A SENSITIVE LOCATION SHALL IMPLEMENT A POLICY CONSISTENT
10 WITH THE GUIDANCE ISSUED BY THE ATTORNEY GENERAL UNDER THIS SECTION.
11 (E) THE GUIDANCE AND POLICIES REQUIRED BY THIS SECTION ARE NOT
12 SUBJECT TO TITLE 10, SUBTITLES 1, 2, AND 3 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT
13 ARTICLE.
14 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act is an emergency
15 measure, is necessary for the immediate preservation of the public health or safety, has
16 been passed by a yea and nay vote supported by three–fifths of all the members elected to
17 each of the two Houses of the General Assembly, and shall take effect from the date it is
18 enacted shall take effect June 1, 2025.
