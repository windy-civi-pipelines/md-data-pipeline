2 State Procurement – Minority Business Enterprise Program – Extension and
3 Reports
4 FOR the purpose of altering the termination date for certain provisions of law governing
5 the Minority Business Enterprise Program and its application to offshore wind
6 projects and video lottery terminals; altering the date by which certain reports,
7 studies, and guidelines must be submitted; and generally relating to the Minority
8 Business Enterprise Program.

SENATE BILL 829 3
1 Chapter 553 of the Acts of 2020, as amended by Chapters 137 and 138 of the Acts
2 of 2023
3 SECTION 5. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the certification agency
4 designated by the Board of Public Works under § 14–303(b) of the State Finance and
5 Procurement Article to certify and decertify minority business enterprises, in consultation
6 with the Office of the Attorney General and the Governor’s Office of Small, Minority, and
7 Women Business Affairs, shall initiate an analysis of the disparity study submitted in
8 accordance with this Act to determine if it applies to the type of work that will be likely to
9 be performed by an approved applicant with respect to an offshore wind project under §
10 7–704.1(i) of the Public Utilities Article as enacted by Section 2 of this Act and submit a
11 report on the analysis to the Legislative Policy Committee of the General Assembly, in
12 accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, on or before September 30,
13 [2024] 2025.
14 Chapter 728 of the Acts of 2021, as amended by Chapters 137 and 138 of the Acts
15 of 2023
16 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That on or before July 1, [2025]
17 2026, the Special Secretary for the Office of Small, Minority, and Women Business Affairs,
18 in consultation with the Secretary of Transportation and the Attorney General, shall
19 establish policies and guidelines for the implementation of this Act.
20 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before December 31,
21 [2025] 2026, the Governor’s Office of Small, Minority, and Women Business Affairs shall
22 report to the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government
23 Article, on the establishment of minority business enterprise subgoals in accordance with
24 this Act, including:
25 (1) whether the establishment of minority business enterprise subgoals
26 was practicable based on the type of capital project; and
27 (2) whether any changes should be made to the capital minority business
28 enterprise subgoal framework established by this Act.
29 Chapter 154 of the Acts of 2012, as amended by Chapters 200 and 201 of the Acts
30 of 2013, Chapter 340 of the Acts of 2017, Chapter 117 of the Acts of 2022, and
31 Chapters 137 and 138 of the Acts of 2023
32 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Certification Agency, in
33 consultation with the General Assembly and the Office of the Attorney General, shall
34 initiate a study of the Minority Business Enterprise Program to evaluate the Program’s
35 continued compliance with the requirements of the Croson decision and any subsequent
36 federal or constitutional requirements. In preparation for the study, the Board of Public
37 Works may adopt regulations authorizing a unit of State government to require bidders
38 and offerors to submit information necessary for the conduct of the study. The Board of
39 Public Works may designate that certain information received in accordance with
4 SENATE BILL 829
1 regulations adopted under this section shall be confidential. Notwithstanding that certain
2 information may be designated by the Board of Public Works as confidential, the
3 Certification Agency may provide the information to any person that is under contract with
4 the Certification Agency to assist in conducting the study. The study shall also evaluate
5 race–neutral programs and other methods that can be used to address the needs of minority
6 businesses. The final report on the study shall be submitted to the Legislative Policy
7 Committee of the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government
8 Article, on or before September 30, [2024] 2025, so that the General Assembly may review
9 the report before the [2025] 2026 Session.
10 Chapter 641 of the Acts of 2022, as amended by Chapters 137 and 138 of the Acts
11 of 2023
12 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
13 (b) The certification agency and the Governor’s Office of Small, Minority, and
14 Women Business Affairs shall submit the findings of the study required under subsection
15 (a) of this section to the Legislative Policy Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the
16 State Government Article, on or before December 31, [2025] 2026, so that the General
17 Assembly may review the findings before the [2026] 2027 Session.
18 (d) The Governor’s Office of Small, Minority, and Women Business Affairs, in
19 consultation with the certification agency and the Office of the Attorney General, shall
20 develop race– and gender–neutral approaches to address the needs of minority and
21 women–owned businesses in the brokerage and investment management services industry
22 and market and submit its report to the Legislative Policy Committee, in accordance with
23 § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, on or before December 31, [2025] 2026, so that
24 the General Assembly may review, consider, and adopt race– and gender–neutral
25 alternatives to remedial measures that are not race and gender neutral.
26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
27 1, 2025.
