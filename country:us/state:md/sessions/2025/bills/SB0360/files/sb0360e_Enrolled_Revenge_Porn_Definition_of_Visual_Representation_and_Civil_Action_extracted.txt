1 AN ACT concerning
2 Revenge Porn – Definition of Visual Representation and Civil Action and
3 Criminal Offense
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing a person to bring and maintain a civil action for defamation
5 under certain circumstances; prohibiting a person from knowingly distributing a
6 certain computer–generated visual representation of another under certain
7 circumstances defining the term “visual representation” as it relates to the crime of
8 revenge porn; authorizing a person to bring a certain civil action for revenge porn;
9 and generally relating to a civil action and criminal offense for revenge porn.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
12 Section 3–505
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

1 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
2 Article – Criminal Law
3 Section 3–809
4 Annotated Code of Maryland

6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
8 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
9 3–505.
10 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
11 INDICATED.
12 (2) “COMPUTER–GENERATED VISUAL REPRESENTATION” INCLUDES:
13 (I) A VISUAL REPRESENTATION CREATED WITHOUT USING
14 OTHER EXISTING VISUAL REPRESENTATIONS OF A PERSON; AND
15 (II) A VISUAL REPRESENTATION CREATED USING OTHER
16 EXISTING VISUAL REPRESENTATIONS OF A PERSON WITHOUT THE PERSON’S
17 CONSENT.
18 (3) “DISTRIBUTE” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 3–809 OF THE
19 CRIMINAL LAW ARTICLE.
20 (4) (I) “INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM AN ACTUAL VISUAL
21 REPRESENTATION OF THE PERSON” MEANS THAT AN ORDINARY PERSON WOULD
22 CONCLUDE THAT THE VISUAL REPRESENTATION IS AN ACTUAL VISUAL
23 REPRESENTATION OF THE PERSON.
24 (II) “INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM AN ACTUAL VISUAL
25 REPRESENTATION OF THE PERSON” INCLUDES A COMPUTER–GENERATED VISUAL
26 REPRESENTATION THAT HAS BEEN CREATED, ADAPTED, OR MODIFIED TO APPEAR
27 GENUINE.
28 (III) “INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM AN ACTUAL VISUAL
29 REPRESENTATION OF THE PERSON” DOES NOT INCLUDE IMAGES OR ITEMS
30 DEPICTING A PERSON THAT ARE:
31 1. DRAWINGS;
SENATE BILL 360 3
1 2. CARTOONS;
2 3. SCULPTURES; OR
3 4. PAINTINGS.
4 (5) “INTIMATE PARTS” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 3–809 OF THE
5 CRIMINAL LAW ARTICLE.
6 (6) “SEXUAL ACTIVITY” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 3–809 OF THE
7 CRIMINAL LAW ARTICLE.
8 (B) A PERSON MAY BRING AND MAINTAIN A CIVIL ACTION FOR DEFAMATION
9 PER SE AGAINST ANOTHER WHO DISTRIBUTES A COMPUTER–GENERATED VISUAL
10 REPRESENTATION THAT IS INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM AN ACTUAL VISUAL
11 REPRESENTATION OF THE PERSON AND FALSELY DEPICTS THE PERSON WITH HIS OR
12 HER INTIMATE PARTS EXPOSED OR ENGAGED IN SEXUAL ACTIVITY.
13 (C) IN ADDITION TO OTHER RELIEF, THE COURT MAY AWARD REASONABLE
14 ATTORNEY’S FEES TO A PREVAILING PLAINTIFF.
15 Article – Criminal Law
16 3–809.
17 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
18 (2) “COMPUTER–GENERATED VISUAL REPRESENTATION” INCLUDES:
19 (I) A VISUAL REPRESENTATION CREATED WITHOUT USING
20 OTHER EXISTING VISUAL REPRESENTATIONS OF A PERSON; AND
21 (II) A VISUAL REPRESENTATION CREATED USING OTHER
22 EXISTING VISUAL REPRESENTATIONS OF A PERSON WITHOUT THE PERSON’S
23 CONSENT.
24 (3) (2) “Distribute” means to give, sell, transfer, disseminate, publish,
25 upload, circulate, broadcast, make available, allow access to, or engage in any other form
26 of transmission, electronic or otherwise.
27 [(3)] (4) “Harm” means:
28 (i) physical injury;
29 (ii) serious emotional distress; or

1 (iii) economic damages.
2 (5) (I) “INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM ANOTHER ACTUAL AND
3 IDENTIFIABLE PERSON” MEANS THAT AN ORDINARY PERSON WOULD CONCLUDE
4 THAT THE VISUAL REPRESENTATION IS OF AN ACTUAL AND IDENTIFIABLE PERSON.
5 (II) “INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM ANOTHER ACTUAL AND
6 IDENTIFIABLE PERSON” INCLUDES A COMPUTER–GENERATED VISUAL
7 REPRESENTATION THAT HAS BEEN CREATED, ADAPTED, OR MODIFIED TO APPEAR
8 AS AN ACTUAL AND IDENTIFIABLE PERSON.
9 (III) “INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM ANOTHER ACTUAL AND
10 IDENTIFIABLE PERSON” DOES NOT INCLUDE VISUAL REPRESENTATIONS OR ITEMS
11 DEPICTING A PERSON THAT ARE:
12 1. DRAWINGS;
13 2. CARTOONS;
14 3. SCULPTURES; OR
15 4. PAINTINGS.
16 [(4)] (6) “Intimate parts” means the naked genitals, pubic area, buttocks,
17 or female nipple.
18 [(5)] (7) “Sexual activity” means:
19 (i) sexual intercourse, including genital–genital, oral–genital,
20 anal–genital, or oral–anal;
21 (ii) masturbation; or
22 (iii) sadomasochistic abuse.
23 (6) (I) “VISUAL REPRESENTATION” OF AN IDENTIFIABLE PERSON
24 MEANS:
25 1. AN UNALTERED IMAGE OF THE PERSON; OR
26 2. AN IMAGE CREATED WITH OR WITHOUT USING OTHER
27 EXISTING DEPICTIONS OF THE PERSON THAT IS INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM THE
28 PERSON, FROM THE PERSPECTIVE OF AN ORDINARY PERSON.

1 (II) “VISUAL REPRESENTATION” INCLUDES A
2 COMPUTER–GENERATED IMAGE.
3 (III) “VISUAL REPRESENTATION” DOES NOT INCLUDE AN IMAGE
4 OR DEPICTION THAT IS:
5 1. A DRAWING;
6 2. A CARTOON;
7 3. A SCULPTURE; OR
8 4. A PAINTING.
9 (b) (1) This section does not apply to:
10 (i) lawful and common practices of law enforcement, the reporting
11 of unlawful conduct, or legal proceedings; or
12 (ii) situations involving voluntary exposure in public or commercial
13 settings.
14 (2) An interactive computer service, as defined in 47 U.S.C. § 230(f)(2), is
15 not liable under this section for content provided by another person.
16 (c) (1) A person may not knowingly distribute a visual representation of
17 another identifiable person that displays the other person with his or her intimate parts
18 exposed or while engaged in an act of sexual activity:
19 [(1)] (I) with the intent to harm, harass, intimidate, threaten, or coerce
20 the other person;
21 [(2) (i)] (II) 1. under circumstances in which the person knew that
22 the other person did not consent to the distribution; or
23 [(ii)] 2. with reckless disregard as to whether the person
24 consented to the distribution; and
25 [(3)] (III) under circumstances in which the other person had a reasonable
26 expectation that the image would remain private.
27 (2) A PERSON MAY NOT KNOWINGLY DISTRIBUTE A
28 COMPUTER–GENERATED VISUAL REPRESENTATION THAT IS INDISTINGUISHABLE
29 FROM ANOTHER ACTUAL AND IDENTIFIABLE PERSON THAT DISPLAYS THE OTHER

1 PERSON WITH HIS OR HER INTIMATE PARTS EXPOSED OR WHILE ENGAGED IN AN ACT
2 OF SEXUAL ACTIVITY:
3 (I) WITH THE INTENT TO HARM, HARASS, INTIMIDATE,
4 THREATEN, OR COERCE THE OTHER PERSON; AND
5 (II) 1. UNDER CIRCUMSTANCES IN WHICH THE PERSON
6 KNEW THAT THE OTHER PERSON DID NOT CONSENT TO THE DISTRIBUTION; OR
7 2. WITH RECKLESS DISREGARD AS TO WHETHER THE
8 PERSON CONSENTED TO THE DISTRIBUTION.
9 (d) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and on conviction
10 is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 2 years or a fine not exceeding $5,000 or both.
11 (e) A person who violates this section is subject to § 5–106(b) of the Courts Article.
12 (f) (1) A PERSON WHOSE VISUAL REPRESENTATION WAS DISTRIBUTED IN
13 VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION HAS A CIVIL CAUSE OF ACTION FOR DEFAMATION PER
14 SE OR INVASION OF PRIVACY AGAINST ANY PERSON WHO DISTRIBUTED THE VISUAL
15 REPRESENTATION.
16 (2) IN ADDITION TO OTHER RELIEF, THE COURT MAY AWARD
17 REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES TO A PREVAILING PLAINTIFF UNDER THIS
18 SUBSECTION.
19 (G) A visual representation AND A COMPUTER–GENERATED VISUAL
20 REPRESENTATION of a victim that is part of a court record for a case arising from a
21 prosecution under this section:
22 (1) subject to item (2) of this subsection, may not be made available for
23 public inspection; and
24 (2) except as otherwise ordered by the court, may only be made available
25 for inspection in relation to a criminal charge OR CIVIL ACTION under this section to:
26 (i) court personnel;
27 (ii) a jury in a criminal case brought under this section;
28 (iii) the State’s Attorney or the State’s Attorney’s designee;
29 (iv) the Attorney General or the Attorney General’s designee;
30 (v) a law enforcement officer;

1 (vi) THE PLAINTIFF OR THE PLAINTIFF’S ATTORNEY;
2 (VII) the defendant or the defendant’s attorney; or
3 (vii) (VIII) the victim or the victim’s attorney.
4 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, if any provision of this Act or
5 the application thereof to any person or circumstance is held invalid for any reason in a
6 court of competent jurisdiction, the invalidity does not affect other provisions or any other
7 application of this Act that can be given effect without the invalid provision or application,
8 and for this purpose the provisions of this Act are declared severable.
9 SECTION 3. 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
10 July 1, 2025.
