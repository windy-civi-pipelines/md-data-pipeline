CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Artificial Intelligence – Guidelines, Professional Development, and
3 Task Force and Professional Development
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the State Department of Education, in consultation with the
5 State Board of Education, and the Task Force on Artificial Intelligence in K–12
6 Education relevant stakeholders, to develop or and periodically update certain
7 guidance and strategies on artificial intelligence for county boards of education;
8 requiring the State Department of Education, in consultation with the Department
9 of Information Technology, to develop and update annually a list of approved
10 artificial intelligence tools; requiring each county board to designate an employee to
11 facilitate the use of artificial intelligence in the local school system and to conduct
12 an annual inventory of systems that employ artificial intelligence; requiring the
13 Career and Technical Education Committee to ensure that artificial intelligence
14 literacy is a component of workforce preparation; requiring the State Department of
15 Education, in consultation with certain entities, to develop and administer a certain
16 artificial intelligence needs assessment; requiring the State Department of
17 Education to promote training and workforce development in artificial intelligence;
18 establishing the Task Force on Artificial Intelligence in K–12 Education; requiring
19 the Career and Technical Education Committee and the CTE Skills Standards
20 Advisory Committee to use the Department’s guidance and, if necessary, consult
21 with the Department before incorporating the use of artificial intelligence into
22 certain CTE–based actions; and generally relating to artificial intelligence in
23 primary and secondary education.

9 SUBTITLE 22. ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN EDUCATION.

11 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
12 INDICATED.
13 (B) “ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 3.5–801
14 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.
15 (C) “CTE ADVISORY COMMITTEE” MEANS THE CTE SKILLS STANDARDS
16 ADVISORY COMMITTEE ESTABLISHED UNDER § 21–210 OF THIS ARTICLE.
17 (C) (D) “CTE COMMITTEE” MEANS THE CAREER AND TECHNICAL
18 EDUCATION COMMITTEE ESTABLISHED UNDER § 21–209 OF THIS ARTICLE.
19 7–2202.
20 (A) (1) ON OR BEFORE AUGUST 1, 2026, THE DEPARTMENT, IN
21 CONSULTATION WITH THE STATE BOARD AND THE TASK FORCE ON ARTIFICIAL
22 INTELLIGENCE IN K–12 EDUCATION RELEVANT STAKEHOLDERS, SHALL DEVELOP
23 OR UPDATE K–12 GUIDANCE ON CLASSROOM USE AND IMPLEMENTATION OF
24 ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE THAT:
25 (I) PROMOTES THE SAFE, RESPONSIBLE, AND ETHICAL USE OF
26 ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE;
27 (II) EMPHASIZES STUDENTS AND TEACHERS AT THE CENTER OF
28 EDUCATIONAL TECHNOLOGY; AND
29 (III) PRIORITIZES THE EDUCATIONAL NEEDS OF STUDENTS
30 USING EVIDENCE–BASED APPROACHES AND METHODOLOGIES.

2 STAKEHOLDERS, SHALL PUBLISH ON ITS WEBSITE SEPARATE GUIDANCE FOR
3 STUDENTS, TEACHERS, AND ADMINISTRATORS.
4 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP STRATEGIES TO ASSIST
5 COUNTY BOARDS AND COUNTY SUPERINTENDENTS TO IMPLEMENT THE

7 UNDER THIS SECTION.

9 DEPARTMENT OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY, SHALL DEVELOP AND ANNUALLY
10 UPDATE A LIST OF COMMONLY AVAILABLE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOOLS
11 APPROVED FOR USE IN SCHOOLS.
12 (II) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ENSURE THAT ARTIFICIAL
13 INTELLIGENCE TOOLS APPROVED UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH
14 SAFEGUARD PRIVACY AND SECURITY.
15 (C) EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL DESIGNATE AN EMPLOYEE TO FACILITATE
16 THE PRODUCTIVE AND ETHICAL USE OF SYSTEMS OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN
17 THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM.
18 (D) ON OR BEFORE OCTOBER 1, 2025, THE CTE COMMITTEE SHALL
19 ENSURE THAT ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE LITERACY IS A COMPONENT OF
20 WORKFORCE PREPARATION.
21 (E) (1) THE DEPARTMENT, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE MARYLAND
22 ASSOCIATION OF BOARDS OF EDUCATION, THE MARYLAND STATE EDUCATION
23 ASSOCIATION, AND THE PUBLIC SCHOOL SUPERINTENDENTS’ ASSOCIATION OF
24 MARYLAND, SHALL DEVELOP AND ADMINISTER A STATEWIDE ARTIFICIAL
25 INTELLIGENCE NEEDS ASSESSMENT TO DETERMINE THE CAPACITY OF LOCAL
26 SCHOOL SYSTEMS TO INTEGRATE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE.
27 (2) THE NEEDS ASSESSMENT SHOULD IDENTIFY WHETHER LOCAL
28 SCHOOL SYSTEMS NEED SUPPORT IN INTEGRATING ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE,
29 INCLUDING THE TYPE AND LEVEL OF SUPPORT.

31 COUNTY BOARD SHALL REPORT TO THE DEPARTMENT ON:
32 (1) PRODUCTS THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM HAS PURCHASED IN THE
33 PRIOR SCHOOL YEAR THAT INCLUDE SIGNIFICANT USE OF ARTIFICIAL
34 INTELLIGENCE;

2 (3) USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO ASSIST STUDENTS;

4 THE USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN THE CLASSROOM; AND
5 (5) COMPLIANCE WITH STATE DATA PRIVACY LAWS.

8 GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, ON THE RESULTS OF THE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

12 ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, AND THE NEEDS OF STUDENTS, PARENTS, EDUCATORS,
13 AND ADMINISTRATORS.
14 (2) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL CONTINUE TO CONSULT WITH
15 REGULAR STAKEHOLDERS IN UPDATING THE GUIDANCE.

23 POST–CCR CTE PATHWAY THE CTE ADVISORY COMMITTEE RECOMMENDS TO THE
24 CTE COMMITTEE.

27 DEVELOPMENT IN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE.
28 (B) PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT IN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE FOR

30 (1) COURSES ON ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE;

2 TRAIN OTHER TEACHERS AND ADMINISTRATORS RELATED TO ARTIFICIAL
3 INTELLIGENCE; AND
4 (3) MONTHLY WEBINARS.
5 (C) FUNDS FOR PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT ON ARTIFICIAL
6 INTELLIGENCE FOR TEACHERS AND ADMINISTRATORS SHALL BE AS PROVIDED IN
7 THE STATE BUDGET.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
9 (a) There is a Task Force on Artificial Intelligence in K–12 Education.
10 (b) The Task Force consists of the following members:
11 (1) the State Superintendent, or the State Superintendent’s designee;
12 (2) the Executive Director of the Maryland Association of Boards of
13 Education, or the Executive Director’s designee;
14 (3) the President of the Maryland State Education Association, or the
15 President’s designee;
16 (4) the President of the Maryland Association of School Personnel
17 Administrators, or the President’s designee;
18 (5) the Executive Director of the Public School Superintendents’
19 Association of Maryland, or the Executive Director’s designee;
20 (6) a teacher, student, or parent from each local school system, appointed
21 by the county superintendent; and
22 (7) the employees designated by the county boards under § 7–2202(c) of the
23 Education Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act.
24 (c) It is the intent of the General Assembly that the teachers appointed or
25 designated to the Task Force shall represent diverse subject areas.
26 (d) The State Superintendent of Schools shall designate the chair of the Task
27 Force from the Department and may establish subcommittees of the Task Force.
28 (e) The Department:
29 (1) shall provide staff for the Task Force; and
6 HOUSE BILL 1391
1 (2) may partner or contract with an organization to support the Task Force.
2 (f) A member of the Task Force:
3 (1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Task Force; but
4 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
5 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.
6 (g) The Task Force shall:
7 (1) study the uses of artificial intelligence in each local school system,
8 including a review of innovation by industry and philanthropic support of schools in the
9 use of artificial intelligence; and
10 (2) make findings and recommendations regarding:
11 (i) the development or update of guidance and best practices for
12 county boards under § 7–2202 of the Education Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act;
13 (ii) the implementation of best practices for relevant professional
14 development;
15 (iii) the development and administration of the statewide artificial
16 intelligence needs assessment; and
17 (iv) the adoption of policies and procedures regarding the
18 development, procurement, implementation, utilization, and ongoing assessment of
19 systems that employ artificial intelligence by a county board.
20 (h) The Task Force shall coordinate with the Department, the CTE Committee,
21 and the CTE Skills Standards Advisory Committee to provide recommendations for
22 integrating artificial intelligence into the college and career readiness standards
23 established by the State Board under § 7–205.1 of the Education Article.
24 (i) On or before January 1, 2026, based on best practices from across the country,
25 the Task Force shall create a document with examples of artificial intelligence integrated
26 into curriculum, including examples for:
27 (1) elementary, middle, and high school curriculum; and
28 (2) all subject areas represented in the courses required for graduation.
29 (j) On or before December 1, 2026, the Task Force shall report its findings and
30 recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
31 Government Article, the General Assembly.
