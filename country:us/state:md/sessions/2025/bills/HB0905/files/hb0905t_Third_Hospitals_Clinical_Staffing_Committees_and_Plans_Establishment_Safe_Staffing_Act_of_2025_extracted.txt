CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Hospitals – Clinical Staffing Committees and Plans – Establishment
3 (Safe Staffing Act of 2025)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring certain hospitals licensed in the State to establish and
5 maintain a clinical staffing committee and to implement a clinical staffing plan;
6 requiring each clinical staffing committee to develop a clinical staffing plan; and
7 generally relating to hospitals and clinical staffing committees and plans.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Health – General
10 Section 19–388 through 19–390 to be under the new part “Part XII. Clinical Staffing
11 Committees and Plans”
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
16 Article – Health – General
17 19–386. RESERVED.
18 19–387. RESERVED.

2 HOUSE BILL 905
1 PART XII. CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEES AND PLANS.
2 19–388.
3 (A) (1) IN THIS PART THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
4 INDICATED.
5 (2) (I) “ANCILLARY MEMBER OF THE FRONTLINE TEAM” MEANS AN
6 INDIVIDUAL WHOSE PRIMARY DUTIES INCLUDE SUPPORTING INDIVIDUALS WHO
7 PROVIDE DIRECT PATIENT CARE.
8 (II) “ANCILLARY MEMBER OF THE FRONTLINE TEAM”
9 INCLUDES:
10 1. A DIETARY WORKER;
11 2. A PATIENT CARE TECHNICIAN; AND
12 3. ANY OTHER NONLICENSED STAFF ASSISTING WITH
13 PATIENT CARE.
14 (3) “CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE” MEANS A COMMITTEE
15 ESTABLISHED BY A HOSPITAL IN ACCORDANCE WITH SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS
16 SECTION.
17 (4) “CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN” MEANS A PLAN DEVELOPED UNDER
18 SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION.
19 (B) THIS PART DOES NOT APPLY TO STATE HOSPITALS.
20 (C) (1) EACH HOSPITAL LICENSED UNDER THIS TITLE SHALL ESTABLISH
21 AND MAINTAIN A CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE THAT HAS EQUAL MEMBERSHIP
22 FROM MANAGEMENT AND EMPLOYEES.
23 (2) EACH CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE ESTABLISHED UNDER
24 THIS PART SHALL INCLUDE AT LEAST ONE OF THE FOLLOWING INDIVIDUALS:
25 (I) A CERTIFIED NURSING ASSISTANT;
26 (II) A DIETARY AIDE;
27 (III) AN EMERGENCY ROOM NURSE;
28 (IV) AN ENVIRONMENTAL SERVICE WORKER;
HOUSE BILL 905 3
1 (V) 1. A RESIDENT, IF THE COMMITTEE IS IN A TEACHING
2 HOSPITAL; OR
3 2. (VI) A STAFF PHYSICIAN, IF THE COMMITTEE IS IN A
4 HOSPITAL THAT IS NOT A TEACHING HOSPITAL WHO IS NOT A HOSPITAL EMPLOYEE
5 OR ADMINISTRATOR; AND
6 (VI) (VII) A TECHNICIAN.
7 (3) A CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE ESTABLISHED UNDER
8 PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION MAY INCLUDE A PATIENT ADVOCATE.
9 (D) EACH CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE SHALL DEVELOP A CLINICAL
10 STAFFING PLAN FOR THE HOSPITAL THAT ESTABLISHES THE APPROPRIATE NUMBER
11 OF CLINICIANS NEEDED TO ADMINISTER QUALITY HEALTH CARE BY SETTING THAT
12 MEETS PATIENT NEEDS.
13 (E) (1) WHEN DEVELOPING A CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN, THE CLINICAL
14 STAFFING COMMITTEE SHALL ESTABLISH, BASED ON THE PATIENT POPULATION OF
15 THE HOSPITAL:
16 (I) SUGGESTED GUIDELINES OR RATIOS, MATRICES, OR GRIDS
17 INDICATING HOW MANY PATIENTS SHOULD BE ASSIGNED TO EACH REGISTERED
18 NURSE BY UNIT OR SETTING; AND
19 (II) THE NUMBER OF REGISTERED NURSES, LICENSED
20 PRACTICAL NURSES, CERTIFIED NURSING ASSISTANTS, CERTIFIED MEDICINE
21 TECHNICIANS, ENVIRONMENTAL SERVICE WORKERS, AND ANCILLARY MEMBERS OF
22 THE DIRECT CARE TEAM THAT SHOULD BE PRESENT ON EACH UNIT DURING EACH
23 SHIFT.
24 (2) TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE, THE GUIDELINES ESTABLISHED
25 UNDER PARAGRAPH (1)(I) OF THIS SUBSECTION SHALL BE BASED ON NATIONAL
26 EVIDENCE–BASED STANDARDS WHEN DEVELOPING A CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN,
27 THE CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE SHALL CONSIDER:
28 (1) EXISTING STAFFING LEVELS;
29 (2) METHODS TO SECURE COVERAGE NEEDS AS NECESSARY;
30 (3) EXISTING GAPS IN STAFFING AND HOW TO ADDRESS THE GAPS;
31 (4) ANY AVAILABLE EVIDENCE–BASED STAFFING STANDARDS; AND
4 HOUSE BILL 905
1 (5) A SCHEDULE FOR PERIODIC REVIEW OF THE STAFFING PLAN.
2 (F) ON OR BEFORE JULY 1 EACH YEAR, EACH HOSPITAL, THROUGH THE
3 CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE, SHALL CONDUCT A REVIEW OF THE CLINICAL
4 STAFFING PLAN TO:
5 (1) EVALUATE THE EFFECTIVENESS OF THE CLINICAL STAFFING
6 PLAN DURING THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING YEAR;
7 (2) UPDATE THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN TO ENSURE THAT THE
8 CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN CONTINUES TO BE APPROPRIATE AND EFFECTIVE; AND
9 (3) DEVELOP A PROCESS FOR RECEIVING, RESOLVING, AND
10 TRACKING COMPLAINTS RELATED TO THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN.
11 (G) IN DEVELOPING THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN, THE CLINICAL
12 STAFFING COMMITTEE SHALL CONSIDER:
13 (1) THE AVERAGE NUMBER OF PATIENTS ON EACH UNIT ON EACH
14 SHIFT DURING THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING YEAR AND RELEVANT INFORMATION
15 REGARDING PATIENT DISCHARGES, POTENTIAL ADMISSIONS, AND TRANSFERS;
16 (2) THE AVERAGE LEVEL OF ACUITY FOR PATIENTS ON EACH UNIT ON
17 EACH SHIFT DURING THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING YEAR AND THE
18 CORRESPONDING LEVEL OF NURSING CARE REQUIRED; AND
19 (3) AN ESTIMATE OF THE APPROPRIATE COMBINATION OF SKILL,
20 EXPERIENCE LEVEL, AND SPECIALTY CERTIFICATION OR TRAINING OF STAFF FOR
21 EACH UNIT ON EACH SHIFT THAT IS REQUIRED TO ADEQUATELY PROVIDE CARE.
22 19–389.
23 (A) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1 EACH YEAR, BEGINNING IN 2026 2027, EACH
24 HOSPITAL SHALL:
25 (1) IMPLEMENT THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN ADOPTED UNDER §
26 19–388 OF THIS SUBTITLE; AND
27 (2) ASSIGN PERSONNEL TO EACH PATIENT CARE UNIT IN
28 ACCORDANCE WITH THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN.
29 (B) A REGISTERED NURSE, A LICENSED PRACTICAL NURSE, AN ANCILLARY
30 MEMBER OF THE FRONTLINE TEAM, OR AN APPLICABLE EXCLUSIVE
HOUSE BILL 905 5
1 REPRESENTATIVE MAY SUBMIT A COMPLAINT TO THE CLINICAL STAFFING
2 COMMITTEE REGARDING ANY VARIATION WHERE PERSONNEL ASSIGNMENT IN A
3 PATIENT CARE UNIT IS NOT IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE ADOPTED CLINICAL
4 STAFFING PLAN.
5 (C) THE CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE SHALL DETERMINE, BY A
6 MAJORITY VOTE, WHETHER A COMPLAINT HAS BEEN ADEQUATELY RESOLVED.
7 19–390.
8 (A) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, ON OR BEFORE
9 JANUARY 1 EACH YEAR, EACH HOSPITAL SHALL:
10 (1) POST IN A PUBLICLY ACCESSIBLE AND CONSPICUOUS AREA ON
11 EACH PATIENT UNIT THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN FOR THE UNIT AND THE ACTUAL
12 DAILY STAFFING FOR EACH SHIFT ON THE UNIT; AND
13 (2) ENSURE THAT A COPY OF THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN IS
14 AVAILABLE, ON REQUEST, ON EACH PATIENT UNIT; AND
15 (3) POST THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN ON THE HOSPITAL’S
16 WEBSITE.
17 (B) IF A CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN FOR A UNIT IS AMENDED AFTER IT IS
18 IMPLEMENTED, THE HOSPITAL SHALL POST OR PROVIDE THE AMENDED CLINICAL
19 STAFFING PLAN FOR THE UNIT IN THE MANNER REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (A)
20 OF THIS SECTION, IN A TIMELY MANNER.
21 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That it is the intent of the General
22 Assembly that, beginning on or before July 1 each year, beginning in 2029, the Maryland
23 Hospital Association shall:
24 (1) compile reports provided by each hospital that summarize how the
25 hospital’s clinical staffing committee has addressed safe hospital staffing through the
26 hospital’s clinical staffing plan during the immediately preceding year; and
27 (2) provide the reports in a single transmittal, in accordance with § 2–1257
28 of the State Government Article, to the Senate Finance Committee and the House Health
29 and Government Operations Committee.
30 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That each hospital shall
31 establish a clinical staffing committee as required under § 19–388 of the Health – General
32 Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act, on or before January 1, 2026.
6 HOUSE BILL 905
1 SECTION 3. 4. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That each clinical staffing
2 committee shall develop a clinical staffing plan as required under § 19–388 of the
3 Health – General Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act, on or before July 1, 2026.
4 SECTION 4. 5. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
5 October 1, 2025.
