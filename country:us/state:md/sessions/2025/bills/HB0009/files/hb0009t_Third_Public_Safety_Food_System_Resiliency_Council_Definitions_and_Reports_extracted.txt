2 Public Safety – Food System Resiliency Council – Definitions and Reports
3 FOR the purpose of defining “healthy food priority areas” and “wasted food” as they relate
4 to the Food System Resiliency Council; repealing certain outdated reporting
5 requirements and altering certain ongoing reporting requirements; and generally
6 relating to the Food System Resiliency Council.

15 Section 14–1102(a)

17 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
19 Article – Public Safety
20 Section 14–1103

2 HOUSE BILL 9
1 Annotated Code of Maryland
2 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)
3 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
4 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
5 Article – Public Safety
6 14–1101.
7 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
8 (b) “Council” means the Maryland Food System Resiliency Council.
9 (c) “Food council organization” means a local food council organization that is
10 established in the State.
11 (d) “HEALTHY FOOD PRIORITY AREAS” MEANS PREDOMINANTLY
12 LOWER–INCOME AREAS THAT HAVE LIMITED ACCESS TO RETAIL OUTLETS THAT
13 SELL AFFORDABLE, HEALTHY FOODS.
14 (E) “Secretary” means the Secretary of Emergency Management.
15 (F) “WASTED FOOD” MEANS FOOD NOT USED FOR ITS INTENDED PURPOSE
16 THAT IS MANAGED IN A VARIETY OF WAYS, INCLUDING:
17 (1) DONATIONS TO FEED PEOPLE;
18 (2) THE CREATION OF ANIMAL FEED;
19 (3) COMPOSTING;
20 (4) ANAEROBIC DIGESTION; OR
21 (5) DISPOSAL IN LANDFILLS OR COMBUSTION FACILITIES.
22 14–1102.
23 (a) There is a Maryland Food System Resiliency Council in the Office of Resilience
24 in the Department.
25 14–1103.
26 (a) The Council shall:
HOUSE BILL 9 3
1 (1) meet regularly for a period of at least 2 years to address food insecurity
2 in the State; and
3 (2) work toward the following goals:
4 (i) to address the food insecurity crisis in the State resulting from
5 the COVID–19 pandemic and resulting economic crisis by:
6 1. coordinating State and local level food insecurity services
7 to support residents of the State;
8 2. tracking and analyzing data to create a comprehensive
9 map of food insecurity across the State and identify the gaps in service;
10 3. leveraging federal and private sector grants and other
11 resources in order to address food insecurity needs;
12 4. advising the State on how best to allocate resources and
13 increase efficiency; and
14 5. A. exploring the role of and potential for the federal
15 Community Eligibility Provision to ensure all students in the State are fed; and
16 B. making recommendations to the Maryland State
17 Department of Education and the Maryland General Assembly to implement relevant
18 findings under item A of this item;
19 (ii) to develop[, on or before November 1, 2021,] equity and
20 sustainability policy recommendations to increase the long–term resiliency of the food
21 system, including:
22 1. addressing and eliminating racial inequities in the food
23 system;
24 2. addressing and eliminating diet–related public health
25 disparities;
26 3. addressing and eliminating [food deserts] LIMITED FOOD
27 OPTIONS AND FOOD SCARCITY THAT EXIST IN HEALTHY FOOD PRIORITY AREAS; and
28 4. reducing WASTED food [waste], increasing recycling, and
29 encouraging other relevant environmental impacts;
30 5. IN URBAN, SUBURBAN, AND RURAL SETTINGS,
31 INCREASING THE QUANTITY, QUALITY AGGREGATION, MARKETING, AND
32 DISTRIBUTION OF LOCAL FOOD;
4 HOUSE BILL 9
1 6. INCREASING THE PROCUREMENT OF LOCAL FOOD
2 THROUGH SCHOOLS, INSTITUTIONS OF HIGHER EDUCATION, AND OTHER
3 INSTITUTIONS;
4 7. CREATING ADDITIONAL MARKET OPPORTUNITIES
5 FOR MARYLAND FOOD BUSINESSES; AND
6 8. EXPANDING ACCESS TO SMALL SCALE
7 MANUFACTURING AND FOOD PRODUCTION INFRASTRUCTURE; AND
8 (iii) to expand the impact of existing food council organizations by:
9 1. providing coordination and facilitation of knowledge
10 exchange at the State level; and
11 2. supporting identification and application of grants to
12 operating funds to support existing and new food council organizations as needed; and
13 (iv) to develop[, on or before November 1, 2021,] AND
14 PERIODICALLY UPDATE a strategic plan to increase the production and procurement of
15 Maryland certified food, including:
16 1. increasing the quality and quantity of production, as well
17 as aggregation, marketing, and distribution of local food in urban, suburban, and rural
18 settings;
19 2. increasing procurement of local food through schools,
20 universities, and other institutions;
21 3. creating additional market opportunities for Maryland
22 food businesses; and
23 4. expanding access to small scale manufacturing and food
24 production infrastructure.
25 (b) To advance the goals of the Council, the Council shall engage and collaborate
26 with interested stakeholders, including:
27 (1) residents of the State with lived experience of food insecurity; and
28 (2) food council organizations and similar local level food system convening
29 organizations.
HOUSE BILL 9 5
1 (c) [(1) On or before November 1, 2021, the Council shall submit an interim
2 report to the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government
3 Article, on key findings and activities undertaken by the Council.
4 (2) On or before November 1, 2022 the Council shall submit a report to the
5 General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, on the
6 Council’s recommendations for a long term structure for a statewide food policy council
7 including providing recommendations on:
8 (i) appropriate policy and legislative changes;
9 (ii) potential ways to restructure the Council such as:
10 1. placing the Council within a different agency or
11 organization; and
12 2. enacting a sunset provision for the Council; and
13 (iii) any other recommendations of the Council.
14 (3)] On or before November 1, 2023, and each year thereafter, the Council
15 shall submit a report to the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
16 Government Article, on key findings, APPROPRIATE POLICY AND LEGISLATIVE
17 CHANGES, and activities undertaken by the Council.
18 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
19 October 1, 2025.
Approved:
